# Bloodlines World Scale Doctrine Addendum - 2026-04-14

## Purpose

This addendum records the world-scale implications of the 2026-04-14 master doctrine.

## Core Rule

Bloodlines does not exist only at local battle scale. The world includes:

- zoomed-out strategic geography
- multiple continents
- meaningful water separation
- delayed contact between powers
- real maritime distance and route pressure

## World Implications

- some dynasties may develop for substantial periods before first direct military contact
- continents and oceans must remain strategically meaningful rather than decorative map padding
- water separation affects trade, faith spread, diplomacy timing, and coalition formation
- the world must feel larger than any single battlefield inside it

## Strategic Consequence

Continental architecture supports multiple kinds of stories:

- isolated civilizational buildup before collision
- maritime-first empires
- delayed invasions
- intercontinental faith and trade spread
- different timing windows for military and diplomatic awareness

The doctrine therefore reinforces world size and ocean separation as identity-defining Bloodlines features rather than optional map variants.
