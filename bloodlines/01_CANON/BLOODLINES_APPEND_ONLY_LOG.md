WORLD AND LORE FOUNDATIONS
Bloodlines is a medieval real-time strategy game designed in the spirit of older Command & Conquer style RTS games, with strong emphasis on base building, offense and defense, resource gathering, strategic freedom, and broad viability across multiple playstyles. The project direction explicitly values the artistic and structural lesson of Command & Conquer Generals in that the game does not need to be "fancier" than that in visual ambition if balance, playability, faction balance, and experimentation value are strong. The project is built around the belief that what makes a strategy game endure is not visual excess alone, but deep and balanced systems that allow players to repeatedly return and succeed through different approaches.
The game is intended to feel like a medieval kingdom RTS while still maintaining the core strategic readability and base-building clarity associated with classic real-time strategy design. It is intended to support aggressive openings, defensive expansion, economic scaling, ideological consolidation, and other varied play patterns without collapsing into a single dominant strategy. The game is intended to stand the test of time and be balanced and enjoyable in a way that keeps players hooked and experimenting.
The project later evolved from an early title direction of Crown and Conquest into the locked project title Bloodlines. The game now centers not just on kingdoms and armies, but on hereditary power, dynastic identity, ideological divergence, moral trajectory, family continuity, and the social character of a civilization.
The foundational world logic includes noble lineages, ancient faiths, population loyalty, and the possible re-emergence of ancient blood-based legitimacy in the form of the Trueborn return. The world is not just a battlefield. It is a living strategic society in which war, belief, inheritance, governance, and territory all interact.
The lore foundation includes the existence of founding Houses, lesser houses, ancient faith traditions, bloodline heritage, and a later-game possibility that ancient legitimacy and buried dynastic power may return to alter the course of history. The world supports conflict not only between factions but within ideologies, within families, and within the moral direction of each civilization.

THE FOUR ANCIENT FAITHS
The canonical project memory records that the game includes 4 ancient Faiths chosen at the end of Level 1 or the end of the founding stage. These faiths are:
Old Light / Covenant of the First Flame
Blood Dominion / The Red Covenant
The Order / Covenant of the Sacred Mandate
The Wild / Covenant of the Thorned Elder Roots
These names must be preserved exactly as historical project language because both paired naming forms have appeared in the project memory and later prompt outputs. The faith system is one of the central pillars of Bloodlines and is not a decorative or secondary layer. It is a major strategic axis.
Covenant of the First Flame, also called the Old Light, is described as centered around enlightenment, unity, order, societal cohesion, disciplined governance, and defensive stability.
The Red Covenant, also called Blood Dominion, is described as darker in tone and centered around sacrifice, power, domination, and strong military benefits, but it carries moral cost and conviction consequences.
Covenant of the Sacred Mandate, also called The Order, is described as emphasizing authority, rigid doctrine, hierarchy, and structured governance or administrative control.
Covenant of the Thorned Elder Roots, also called The Wild, is described as a primal or ancient nature-tied belief system emphasizing adaptive survival, environmental strength, and ancient rooted power.
The project later added a critical clarification that faith should not only have intensity, but also a sliding scale of light and dark interpretation. The user stated that faith should have a sliding scale in addition to "intensity," and that a faith like Blood Dominion could be heavily practiced but on a lighter side that renounces human sacrifice in favor of something like paralyzed animals or otherwise lesser ritual substitutes. The user said this difference could be relatively minor but provides the opportunity for a "light vs dark faith" battle that could bring about conflict. This is a major design clarification because it means faith identity is not binary and the same faith can internally diverge.

CONVICTION SYSTEM
The canonical project memory states that Conviction is a behavioral morality spectrum shaped permanently by actions. This means conviction is not a cosmetic alignment label chosen in a menu. It is produced by what the player actually does during the match.
Later session outputs elaborated that conviction represents the moral trajectory of the kingdom or civilization and that actions influencing it include warfare practices, treatment of prisoners, sacrifice, governance decisions, and other behavioral choices. Conviction is permanent and reflects the historical character of the civilization.
Conviction is distinct from Faith. Faith is chosen. Conviction is lived. Faith determines doctrine and worldview. Conviction determines what kind of civilization the player has actually become through action. This distinction is foundational to Bloodlines and should remain explicit in future work.
Conviction ties directly into ideological consequences, moral identity, player decision consequences, cultural memory, and likely future diplomacy, loyalty, and possibly event or faction reactions. Although not all systemic downstream effects have been fully designed, conviction is already canonically positioned as a long-term behavioral morality spectrum shaped by actions and permanently attached to the civilization's identity.

POPULATION SYSTEM
The canonical project memory states that population is a single pool driven by food and water. This has been consistently repeated in later outputs as a unified population pool representing the civilians of the kingdom.
Population growth depends on food supply and water availability. Population is not just a cap. It is a living strategic resource. It supplies the labor, military, civic, and ideological body of the civilization.
Population can be converted into workers, soldiers, specialists, or religious participants. The later player guide and design prompts repeatedly reinforce that population is the foundation of workers, soldiers, specialists, and faith participation.
Population is directly tied to housing. Housing replaces Command & Conquer style power plant concepts in the Bloodlines design. Instead of generating electrical grid power, the player expands social and logistical capacity through housing, which in turn raises population capacity and supports social stability.
Population is also implied to be central to loyalty and territory consolidation because a territory is not truly controlled by military occupation alone. Population acceptance matters. Therefore population is not just a labor pool. It is also a political body.

RESOURCE ECONOMY
The canonical project memory states that the core resources are:
gold
food
water
stone
Gold is used for administration, military support, advanced structures, and broader civilizational expenses.
Food is required for population growth and likely army support and sustainability.
Water is required for population sustainability and is a defining economic constraint, not an assumed background resource. This is a major point of distinction because water is elevated to one of the game's four primary resources rather than treated as a secondary terrain effect.
Stone is used for construction of major structures and likely long-term fortified development.
The economy is therefore intentionally grounded and legible. The user emphasized appreciation for classic RTS clarity, and these four resources reflect a desire for strong core simplicity with deep interaction rather than bloated complexity for its own sake.

TERRITORY CONTROL
The canonical design repeatedly states that territory control in Bloodlines requires both territorial control and loyalty. The project memory explicitly says: territory control and loyalty both required.
Later outputs elaborated this into a two-part system:
Military control
Population loyalty
A region held only by force is unstable. It may revolt. A region becomes truly controlled only when the population accepts the player's rule. This is a defining design departure from many RTS games where a structure or outpost marker is enough to claim land.
Territory therefore involves questions of occupation, legitimacy, stability, loyalty, and governance. It also implies that expansion without consolidation can create internal weakness. This opens space for strategic playstyles built around fast conquest but unstable administration versus slower expansion but durable legitimacy.
Methods of loyalty maintenance were later described in broad terms including governance structures, faith influence, economic prosperity, and dynasty reputation. While not yet fully systematized into a final mechanical model, these relationships are already canonical.

BLOODLINE MEMBERS
The canonical project memory states that bloodline members are a major gameplay system and that there are 20 active bloodline members, with additional members beyond 20 becoming dormant. This must remain exactly preserved because it was explicitly saved into project memory in that form: "bloodline members 20 active (at 20, beyond dormant), with training paths and dynastic roles, capture/enslavement/marriage diplomacy."
Bloodline members are not ordinary military units. They are family members within the ruling dynasty. They can occupy strategic roles, may have training paths, and influence the house and wider kingdom.
Later outputs described possible bloodline roles such as military commanders, governors, diplomats, and ideological leaders. The family tree interface should allow the player to understand family structure, head of family, specialization, heritage, and public love or hatred by the population.
The bloodline system is therefore not just a hero system. It is a dynastic management layer integrated into politics, reputation, leadership, and inheritance.

DYNASTIC SYSTEMS
The dynastic system is one of the four foundational design pillars of Bloodlines, alongside territory, population, and faith.
The player controls a ruling noble house that produces family members and develops a living family tree. The family tree should always be accessible and informative. The player should always understand who leads the house, who belongs to it, what each person specializes in, what heritage they carry, and how the population perceives them.
Bloodline members have training paths and dynastic roles. This indicates specialization and developmental progression, not just static identity. Although specific training trees were not fully designed in this session history, their existence is already part of the design memory and must remain.
Dynastic systems include capture, enslavement, marriage, diplomatic leverage, and inheritance-style tracking. This makes the dynasty a gameplay object that can be threatened, exploited, or strengthened in ways beyond direct combat. It also turns the player's house into an evolving strategic institution rather than a static faction label.

ARMY STRUCTURE
The canonical project memory states that Bloodlines is intended to support Command & Conquer style real-time strategy structure with emphasis on base building, offense and defense, resource gathering, and viable varied playstyles such as blitz-heavy openings, defensive base building, and other strategic approaches.
The detailed army structure has not yet been fully designed. However, several components are already canonically present:
There will be armies built from population.
The game supports offensive and defensive playstyles.
The game includes elite army-forging through the Born of Sacrifice system.
The game includes bloodline members who may fill military command roles.
The game includes lesser houses and likely subordinate or AI military bodies.
The game's design direction values balanced faction design and enjoyable varied playstyles.
The user explicitly stated that the game will likely be an award winner if seen through because it has all the elements of strategy games that keep people hooked. This statement matters as creative intent: army and combat structure are expected to contribute to repeated experimentation and varied strategic success, not just spectacle.

ELITE UNITS AND BORN OF SACRIFICE
The canonical project memory explicitly records an "elite army-forging mechanic called Born of Sacrifice with player naming or random generator."
This is one of the most distinct named systems in the game. It must remain verbatim in the design archive.
Born of Sacrifice is an elite unit or elite warrior forging system. Through rituals, achievements, or some as-yet not fully finalized transformation process, rare and extremely powerful warriors may be created. These units can be named by the player or assigned names through a random generator.
Later outputs described them as rare, extremely powerful, and often becoming legendary figures within a match. This indicates that the mechanic is not just a stat upgrade. It is intended to create identity-rich, memorable battlefield units tied to the ideological and dynastic character of the civilization.

RELIGION AND FAITH INTENSITY
The canonical project memory explicitly states: "Faith has intensity that must be maintained and costs resources and scales with population participation."
This is a critical system statement and must remain exactly preserved in design intent. Faith is not a passive permanent buff. It is active, costly, population-scaled, and maintenance-dependent.
This means:
Faith has intensity.
Faith intensity is not permanent without upkeep.
Maintaining strong faith costs resources.
Faith scales with population participation.
Later outputs elaborated that faith intensity grows through participation, rituals, structures, and engagement. Even if some of those exact implementation details are not yet final, the population-linked, resource-costing, maintenance-based nature of faith intensity is already canonical.
The project also added the light versus dark interpretation layer, making faith internally divergent. Two kingdoms may follow the same faith and yet split on moral practice. This may later support intra-faith civil wars, doctrinal fractures, soft and hard orthodoxy, ritual reform, or other ideological conflicts.

LEVEL PROGRESSION AND DIVERGENCE
The canonical project memory records that the four ancient faiths are chosen at the end of Level 1 and that Level 4 is major irreversible divergence unlocking grand faith structures and late-game apex.
Later outputs rephrased this structure as four broad match stages:
Stage 1 — Founding
Stage 2 — Consolidation
Stage 3 — Ideological Expansion
Stage 4 — Irreversible Divergence
These two framings must both remain in the archive because they are historical design expressions of the same or related structural progression and no explicit instruction was given to discard either wording. One uses "Level" terminology. The later one uses "Stage" terminology. Both are part of project history.
The critical canonical design points are:
The player begins with settlement formation and population/resource establishment.
At the end of early development, the player selects one of the four ancient faiths.
As the match progresses, ideology increasingly influences society, conflict, and civilization identity.
Late in the match the civilization reaches a major irreversible divergence point.
This divergence unlocks grand faith structures and late-game apex power.
This is one of the strongest pieces of the game's progression identity. It means a match is not just bigger armies over time. It is a civilizational arc.

GRAND FAITH STRUCTURES
The canonical project memory explicitly states that Level 4 is a major irreversible divergence unlocking grand faith structures and late-game apex. This means grand faith structures are a confirmed canonical system.
The specific structures for each faith have not yet been fully designed in the session content currently archived here. However, their existence is already canon.
They are tied to late-game ideological culmination. They likely represent each civilization's final spiritual, political, or metaphysical institutional expression. They are one of the clearest signs that Bloodlines intends to tie macro-progression, ideology, and late-game power together in a uniquely themed way.

AI KINGDOM SYSTEMS
The canonical project memory states that the game can support up to 10 players with AI kingdoms and minor tribes. It also states that lesser houses may be optional AI-autonomous or player-controlled.
This establishes that AI kingdoms are a major part of the game ecosystem, not mere placeholders. They occupy the world alongside players and minor entities and participate in a living political landscape.
Later outputs described multiplayer and world structure as including multiple players, AI kingdoms, and tribal factions in a dynamic political environment.
Specific AI behavior models, doctrine systems, diplomacy logic, faith prioritization logic, or economy styles have not yet been fully defined in this session archive, but the existence of AI kingdoms as active kingdom-level participants is canonical.

LESSER HOUSES AND MINOR TRIBES
The canonical project memory explicitly states that lesser houses are optional AI-autonomous or player-controlled. This is a distinctive system because it means lesser houses are not fixed to one implementation mode. They may act independently as AI entities or be directly controlled by a player in some capacity.
Minor tribes are also part of the match ecosystem. The project memory states that matches can include AI kingdoms and minor tribes. This means the world has a layered political map, not just equal-sized major player empires.
Later outputs referred to lesser houses as additional noble houses in the world, functioning as autonomous AI factions or subordinate player-controlled houses. This implies subordination, vassal-like relationships, or secondary command structures may eventually matter.

MATCH STRUCTURE AND SCALE
The canonical project memory states: "optional long matches 2 to 10+ hours, up to 10 players with AI kingdoms and minor tribes."
This exact range must be preserved because it strongly defines the intended scale of Bloodlines. The game is designed to support both relatively long RTS matches and very long epic matches. It is not restricted to short-ladder match pacing.
The later player guide described Bloodlines as a game in which large matches may extend well beyond ten hours in epic campaigns. This later phrasing should be preserved as explanatory expansion, but the exact canonical memory phrase "2 to 10+ hours" remains the direct archival form.
The game is therefore structurally intended for large, slow-burn, civilization-shaping play, not just skirmish bursts. This is a major identity distinction.

This implies that the game's pacing, economy, dynasty systems, ideology systems, and population systems are meant to sustain long-term meaning and not peak too early. Long match mode supports the fantasy of watching a civilization truly evolve over time.

Conviction is permanently shaped by actions.
Faith intensity must be maintained and costs resources.
Faith has light and dark moral interpretation.

Bloodlines is a real-time strategy game centered on the rise, evolution, and survival of dynastic houses.
Players control a ruling dynasty known as a Bloodline.
The world is structured around territorial expansion, ideological conflict, religious development, and generational political evolution.
The game combines traditional RTS combat mechanics with deep dynastic and ideological systems.
The core fantasy of the game is not controlling a faction but controlling a dynasty.
Matches can last several hours and represent the rise and fall of kingdoms over time.
Players must balance warfare, governance, religion, economy, and dynastic leadership.
The world evolves through alliances, wars, religious movements, and political disputes.

THE FOUR ANCIENT FAITHS
Faith represents the religious covenant that a dynasty aligns with.
Faith is selected after the early exploration phase when dynasties encounter the wider world.
The dynasty does not create a faith. It aligns with an existing covenant.
The four covenants are:
The Old Light
(Covenant of the First Flame)
A faith centered on protection, mercy, divine guardianship, and the defense of innocents.
The Blood Dominion
(The Red Covenant)

The Order

The Wild
(Covenant of the Thorned Elder Roots)
A primal faith rooted in nature mysticism, ancient spirits, and the balance of the natural world.

RELIGION AND FAITH INTENSITY
Faith is governed by three interacting components.
Covenant
The religion the dynasty follows.
Faith Intensity
The degree to which the population actively practices the faith.
Faith Alignment
A sliding scale representing how the covenant is practiced along a light vs dark spectrum.
