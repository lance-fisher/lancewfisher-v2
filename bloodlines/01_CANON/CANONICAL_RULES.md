# Bloodlines — Canonical Rules

This document tracks which design elements are considered **settled canon** versus **open for exploration**. It serves as a quick reference to prevent accidental contradiction of established design decisions.

## How This Document Works

- **SETTLED**: This design element has been decided. It should not be contradicted in future sessions without explicit revisitation and authorization. If a change is made, the original decision remains in the archive alongside the new one.
- **OPEN**: This design element is still being explored. Multiple ideas may coexist. Future sessions may freely propose alternatives.
- **PROPOSED**: A specific idea has been put forward but not yet confirmed as canon. It may be adopted, modified, or set aside.

## Sixth Ingestion Canon — 2026-03-18

| Element | Status | Decision | Date |
|---------|--------|----------|------|
| Faction asymmetry model | SETTLED | Hybrid asymmetry. All nine houses share the full tech tree and unit roster. Each house has subtle base stat variations on shared units (Off/Def starting ratings) and one or more unique units accessible only to that house. Differences must be subtle — no house is dominant at match start. Unique unit assignments are a design gap pending full house-by-house mapping. At minimum: one house (axe-iconography, likely Ironmark) has access to a unique Axeman unit; at least one house has a base Pikeman with a defensively stronger starting stat. | 2026-03-18 |
| Base unit Off/Def stat system | SETTLED | All base units have an offensive and defensive rating. The neutral baseline is Off 5 / Def 5. House asymmetry applies small per-house deviations to shared units: examples include 5/6 (more defensive), 6/5 (more offensive), 4/6 (weaker offense, stronger defense). Deviations are intentionally subtle to prevent early-game dominance. Full stat assignments per house pending. | 2026-03-18 |
| Map structure | SETTLED | All three map structures layered. Massive continuous terrain (RTS movement, Warcraft 3-style direct unit control) forms the base experience. Province regions overlay the terrain and define territory/loyalty zones for governance, conviction, and diplomatic systems. Underlying tile/hex structure handles territory boundary calculations. Map scale is roughly ten times a typical RTS map with no upper ceiling imposed. | 2026-03-18 |
| Bloodline member aging and death | SETTLED | Aging out is very rare under normal conditions. Bloodline members die primarily through combat, assassination, illness events, sacrifice, and other in-game actions — not automatically from time. Exception: the initial starting leader options (Father, Uncle, or equivalent) who were born before or during The Great Frost are genuinely old at match start. These figures will die within the match timeframe. Their age is a feature, not a flaw — it makes the starting choice meaningful and their death narratively significant. No other bloodline members age to natural death except through extraordinary circumstances. | 2026-03-18 |
| Faith victory — Divine Right declaration mechanic | SETTLED | Faith victory is declared, not passively accumulated. The dynasty must formally declare Divine Right — their bloodline as blood descendants of the covenant's highest power. The declaration spreads across the world over a defined time window before victory can be claimed. All milestone requirements and the declaration clock are visible to all players at all times from the moment of declaration. Rival dynasties may form coalitions and mount a military challenge during the spread window. If the dynasty survives and faith thresholds are maintained, the victory is recognized. If the coalition defeats the dynasty before the window closes, the claim fails. Pre-declaration requirements (faith intensity, global faith share, specific structures) are also publicly known in advance so opposition can begin before a declaration is ever made. | 2026-03-18 |
| Divine Right milestone transparency | SETTLED | All milestone progression toward and during a Divine Right claim is fully visible to all players. Opacity is rejected. The drama is the race, not the surprise. This applies both to pre-declaration threshold requirements and to the spread clock after declaration. | 2026-03-18 |
| Trueborn City as world knowledge hub | SETTLED | The Trueborn City is the primary conduit for global information. Trade routes passing through the city carry news from across the world — population sentiment, faith distribution, political events, and faction movements. This is how the player observes global faith tracking data and world population conviction posture. Dynasties with active trade relationships with the city receive accurate, timely world-state information. Dynasties cut off from trade (blockaded, diplomatically isolated, or at war with the city's trade partners) see degraded or delayed information. Conquering the city grants information dominance in addition to economic and diplomatic advantages. | 2026-03-18 |
