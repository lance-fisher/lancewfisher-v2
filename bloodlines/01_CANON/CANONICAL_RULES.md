# Bloodlines — Canonical Rules

This document tracks which design elements are considered **settled canon** versus **open for exploration**. It serves as a quick reference to prevent accidental contradiction of established design decisions.

## How This Document Works

- **SETTLED**: This design element has been decided. It should not be contradicted in future sessions without explicit revisitation and authorization. If a change is made, the original decision remains in the archive alongside the new one.
- **OPEN**: This design element is still being explored. Multiple ideas may coexist. Future sessions may freely propose alternatives.
- **PROPOSED**: A specific idea has been put forward but not yet confirmed as canon. It may be adopted, modified, or set aside.

| Element | Status | Decision | Date |

| Faction asymmetry model | SETTLED | Hybrid asymmetry. All nine houses share the full tech tree and unit roster. Each house has subtle base stat variations on shared units (Off/Def starting ratings) and one or more unique units accessible only to that house. Differences must be subtle — no house is dominant at match start. Unique unit assignments are a design gap pending full house-by-house mapping. At minimum: one house (axe-iconography, likely Ironmark) has access to a unique Axeman unit; at least one house has a base Pikeman with a defensively stronger starting stat. | 2026-03-18 |
| Base unit Off/Def stat system | SETTLED | All base units have an offensive and defensive rating. The neutral baseline is Off 5 / Def 5. House asymmetry applies small per-house deviations to shared units: examples include 5/6 (more defensive), 6/5 (more offensive), 4/6 (weaker offense, stronger defense). Deviations are intentionally subtle to prevent early-game dominance. Full stat assignments per house pending. | 2026-03-18 |
| Map structure | SETTLED | All three map structures layered. Massive continuous terrain (RTS movement, Warcraft 3-style direct unit control) forms the base experience. Province regions overlay the terrain and define territory/loyalty zones for governance, conviction, and diplomatic systems. Underlying tile/hex structure handles territory boundary calculations. Map scale is roughly ten times a typical RTS map with no upper ceiling imposed. | 2026-03-18 |
| Bloodline member aging and death | SETTLED | Aging out is very rare under normal conditions. Bloodline members die primarily through combat, assassination, illness events, sacrifice, and other in-game actions — not automatically from time. Exception: the initial starting leader options (Father, Uncle, or equivalent) who were born before or during The Great Frost are genuinely old at match start. These figures will die within the match timeframe. Their age is a feature, not a flaw — it makes the starting choice meaningful and their death narratively significant. No other bloodline members age to natural death except through extraordinary circumstances. | 2026-03-18 |
| Faith victory — Divine Right declaration mechanic | SETTLED | Faith victory is declared, not passively accumulated. The dynasty must formally declare Divine Right — their bloodline as blood descendants of the covenant's highest power. The declaration spreads across the world over a defined time window before victory can be claimed. All milestone requirements and the declaration clock are visible to all players at all times from the moment of declaration. Rival dynasties may form coalitions and mount a military challenge during the spread window. If the dynasty survives and faith thresholds are maintained, the victory is recognized. If the coalition defeats the dynasty before the window closes, the claim fails. Pre-declaration requirements (faith intensity, global faith share, specific structures) are also publicly known in advance so opposition can begin before a declaration is ever made. | 2026-03-18 |
| Divine Right milestone transparency | SETTLED | All milestone progression toward and during a Divine Right claim is fully visible to all players. Opacity is rejected. The drama is the race, not the surprise. This applies both to pre-declaration threshold requirements and to the spread clock after declaration. | 2026-03-18 |
| Trueborn City as world knowledge hub | SETTLED | The Trueborn City is the primary conduit for global information. Trade routes passing through the city carry news from across the world — population sentiment, faith distribution, political events, and faction movements. This is how the player observes global faith tracking data and world population conviction posture. Dynasties with active trade relationships with the city receive accurate, timely world-state information. Dynasties cut off from trade (blockaded, diplomatically isolated, or at war with the city's trade partners) see degraded or delayed information. Conquering the city grants information dominance in addition to economic and diplomatic advantages. | 2026-03-18 |

## Fourteenth Session Canon — Defensive Fortification Doctrine — 2026-04-14

Source: Lance W. Fisher direction, session of 2026-04-14. See full doctrine in `01_CANON/DEFENSIVE_FORTIFICATION_DOCTRINE.md`. Defender-side specification in `04_SYSTEMS/FORTIFICATION_SYSTEM.md`. Attacker-side specification in `04_SYSTEMS/SIEGE_SYSTEM.md`.

| Element | Status | Decision | Date |
|---------|--------|----------|------|
| Defensive fortification as strategic pillar | SETTLED | Bloodlines treats defensive fortification as a major and fully viable strategic path. Not cosmetic. Not merely delaying. Meaningful investment into fortification produces real security, real deterrence, and real military difficulty for the attacker. Canonical strategic pillar on par with offensive warfare. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Layered fortification architecture | SETTLED | Fortified strongholds are composed of three concentric layers: outer works (first contact surface), inner ring (main defensive enceinte), and final defensive core (keep proper, bloodline seat). The fall of one layer must not automatically render the entire base tactically meaningless. Breach of each layer is a separate achievement. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Defensive ecosystem | SETTLED | Walls, gates, towers, defensive emplacements, garrisons, chokepoints, kill zones, signal systems, reserve mustering points, and inner fallback positions function as a connected ecosystem. Not isolated health-bar objects. Cutting any connection weakens the whole. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Defensive leverage forms | SETTLED | Investment in fortification produces measurable leverage beyond raw hit points: attritional cost multiplier on attackers, tempo drag on attacker campaigns, siege requirement escalation, operational risk imposition on the besieger, accelerated defender recovery after repulsed assaults. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Bloodline keep as apex defensive structure | SETTLED | Primary dynastic keeps housing key bloodline figures, succession lines, or dynastic command are canonically among the hardest targets in the game when fully developed. Bloodline presence in the keep confers defensive bonuses (garrison cohesion, reserve availability, loyalty surge, commander aura). The keep that holds its bloodline fights differently from a keep emptied of bloodline. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Wave-spam denial | SETTLED | The simulation must refuse to reward repeated reckless assaults as a reliable path to structural collapse of developed fortifications. Failed assaults inflict real losses on the attacker. Repeated reckless assault compounds penalties (unit loss, morale degradation, cohesion loss, supply drain, tempo penalty, political consequence). Wave-spam is a canonically losing branch against developed fortifications. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Siege as earned military operation | SETTLED | Breaking elite fortifications requires real preparation. Plural commitments are required for serious siege: siege engines, engineer specialists, logistics and supply continuity, scouting and intelligence, breach planning, elite breaching units, faith powers, sabotage operations, coordinated multi-front timing, isolation from relief. Assaulting a major fortress is a campaign objective, not a casual click action. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Late-game defensive relevance | SETTLED | Offensive scaling must not render defensive investment meaningless in the late game. Apex fortifications match apex offense. High-tier defensive development unlocks stronger and more specialized forms of fortification (grand wall projects, citadel keeps, shielded inner cores, faith-warded inner sanctums, specialized garrison classes, layered resistance mechanisms). LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Fortification tradeoffs | SETTLED | Heavy investment in fortification accepts real opportunity costs (smaller field army, slower expansion, reduced economy tempo, stone/iron/wood biased toward fortification, commander commitment to defensive governance). In exchange the realm gets measurable siege-denial, coalition-defiance capability, and dynasty preservation under siege. Both paths are canonically viable; neither is dominant. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Settlement class hierarchy | SETTLED | Settlement classes have distinct defensive ceilings: border settlement (warn and retreat), military fort (hold against raids and modest sieges), trade town (protect economic core), regional stronghold (force a real siege), primary dynastic keep (bloodline seat, apex resistance), fortress-citadel (actively-invested primary keep, the hardest target in the realm). Investment deepens within-class; class promotion requires major material and political commitment. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Felt difference — fortified vs neglected | SETTLED | The player must experience measurable difference between an unfortified realm and a fortified one: visual presentation of the keep and walls, auditory cues when audio lands (horns, bells, chants), behavioral signals (NPC and AI treat a fortified realm with more caution), numerical effects in combat logs and debug overlays, and narrative / political weight. A neglected seat of power invites opportunism; a disciplined fortified capital commands respect. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| AI rejection of insufficient-force siege | SETTLED | Attacking AI must be capable of recognizing that elite fortifications require preparation. Throwing line infantry at a fortress-citadel must be an explicit losing branch in AI decision-making, reinforced by simulation penalties. AI attacking developed fortifications must stage siege engines, recruit engineers, protect supply lines, scout, and commit elites. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Defensive specialist population categories | SETTLED | Fortification demands dedicated specialist populations: garrison troops (rotating duty, reserve yards), engineers and sappers (construction, repair, counter-mining, traps), signal keepers (watchtowers, bells, horn relay), wall wardens (specialist infantry for wall combat), tower artillerists (late-game specialist crews). These are distinct from field army populations and have their own recruitment and maintenance pipelines. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Faith integration inside fortifications | SETTLED | Faith buildings placed inside fortified enceintes alter the defensive equation. Each covenant contributes a canonical defensive expression: Old Light pyre wards (vigilance, counter-sabotage), Blood Dominion blood-altar reserves (morale surge at sacrifice cost), The Order edict wards (garrison discipline and reserve cohesion), The Wild root wards (entangling terrain advantage). Doctrine path modifies behavior. Apex-tier faith buildings in a keep produce canonically powerful defensive effects. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Siege conviction consequences | SETTLED | Siege conduct feeds the four-bucket conviction ledger: committing a serious siege adds ruthlessness proportional to scale; massacre after breach adds strong ruthlessness and often desecration; sparing the defender adds oathkeeping and stewardship; wave-spam failure adds minor desecration (dishonor of the fallen by reckless waste); victory through sabotage alone adds desecration under most doctrines; victory through honorable siege adds oathkeeping. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
| Fortification doctrine non-negotiability | SETTLED | The Defensive Fortification Doctrine is a canonical strategic pillar, not optional flavor. No session may reduce, dilute, or contradict the doctrine's ten pillars without explicit authorization from Lance. Implementation milestones may schedule when the code expresses the canon, but the canon is in force as design authority immediately. LOCKED. | 2026-04-14 |
