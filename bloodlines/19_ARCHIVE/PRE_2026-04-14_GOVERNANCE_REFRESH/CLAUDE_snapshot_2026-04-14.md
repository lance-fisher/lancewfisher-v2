## Archival Rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
1. Nothing may be deleted without explicit authorization
2. Nothing may be summarized or shortened
3. New information is always appended, never replaced
4. Conflicting ideas are both preserved
5. Project memory grows continuously
6. Historical context remains intact

## Design Notes
- Seven core systems identified: Conviction, Faith, Population, Resource, Territory, Dynastic, Born of Sacrifice
- All systems are interconnected (population feeds resources and armies, conviction is shaped by faith, etc.)
- The Born of Sacrifice system is the moral core: extraordinary power at extraordinary cost
- The dynasty is the emotional core: personal stakes in a strategy game

## Scope Directive — NON-NEGOTIABLE
Bloodlines has no size or scope ceiling. There is no point at which the project is too big. Conventional game development scope constraints (feature cuts, scope reduction, feasibility trimming) do not apply here. Depth is the goal. Every session should make the design richer, not smaller. If something can go deeper, it goes deeper. The risk is building too little, not too much.
