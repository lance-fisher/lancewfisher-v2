## THE AGE BEFORE (400+ Years Ago — Unknown Duration)

The Age Before is defined by what was lost. No living person remembers it. What survives comes in three forms: ruins buried in frost-scarred earth, fragments of doctrine preserved by the four ancient faiths, and the nine bloodlines themselves — genetic memory carried by families who remember, even without knowing why.

**Key features of the Age Before:**
- Iron-age civilizations connected by trade roads and coastal shipping
- The four ancient faiths predate every dynasty — they emerged from human encounters with forces that predated organized civilization
- The Trueborn bloodline occupied the city at the geographic center of the known world
- The nine founding houses were secondary houses — significant but not dominant powers
- The dominant civilizations of the Age Before are gone; only their ruins remain

## THE GREAT FROST — DESCENT (~370-350 Years Before Current)

**Year -370 (approximate): First signs**
- The sky dims subtly in daylight; observers note a "thinning of light"
- Crops grow, but not vigorously; animals born smaller than expected
- No widespread concern — a single poor year is not unknown

**Year -368 (approximate): First hard winters**
- Winters arrive three weeks early; depart two weeks late
- Stores run short; mountain passes close earlier than living memory records
- General reassurance: a cold period, not a catastrophe

**Year -360 (approximate): The pattern becomes undeniable**
- Growing season contracts significantly
- First famine events in northern territories
- Faith leaders of all four covenants begin providing competing explanations
- Population movement begins from highland territories to lowlands

---

## THE GREAT FROST — PEAK SEVERITY (~350-200 Years Before Current)

**Year -350 (approximate): Global collapse begins**
- Rivers freeze solid in regions that had never seen ice
- Coastal zones experience ice that had never been recorded
- Growing season in temperate regions: six weeks maximum, zero in bad years

**Year -350 to -200: The Long Dark**
- Global population falls by more than half, concentrated in the first 50 years
- Cities abandoned — not stormed, not burned, simply left when food production collapsed
- Trade road networks buried under ice, then eroded, then grown over in the eventual partial thaw
- Foundries go cold permanently; iron-working knowledge partially lost
- Ships rot in harbors or burned for heat
- Institutional structures of the Age Before — governments, courts, guilds, military organizations — dissolve within a single generation; institutions require surplus, and the Frost eliminated surplus

**Surviving structures:**
- The four ancient faiths survive, though their relationships with populations transform entirely during this period
- The Trueborn City survives by maintaining absolute neutrality — all factions knew attacking it destroyed the only universal trade point
- The nine bloodlines survive through a combination of geographic luck, resource management, and each other's occasional cooperation in the direst periods

**Iron Ridges formed:**
- Glacial action during the peak Frost exposes deep iron deposits in highland territories
- These Iron Ridge formations will become critical strategic resources in the Age of Reclamation
- At the time, they are simply evidence of how deep the cold has gone

## THE GREAT FROST — THAW (~200-170 Years Before Current)

**Year -200 (approximate): The thaw begins**
- Uneven, partial, inconsistent — not a single moment of relief but a slow and unpredictable return of warmth
- Southern territories thaw first; northern and highland regions remain frozen
- Tundra terrain is the permanent expression of regions the thaw has never fully reached
- Frost Ruin territories are the thaw's incomplete work — reached by warmth but not healed

**Year -200 to -170: Age of Survival begins**
- The nine bloodlines begin establishing territorial bases
- Initial period is pure survival competition — no formal alliances, no stable governance, constant resource pressure
- The four ancient faiths become more important, not less — in extreme scarcity, meaning is sought

---

## AGE OF SURVIVAL (~170-80 Years Before Current)

**Year -170 to -110: Scattered communities**
- The nine bloodlines each anchor communities in territories they will eventually claim as home
- No formal kingdoms yet — governance is at the community level
- Rivalries that will define the match begin forming: territorial friction, resource competition, faith incompatibilities
- Iron Ridges begin to be identified and exploited; iron becomes a differentiating resource for communities that can access it

**Historical rivalries established during this period (PROPOSED — CB004):**
- Hartvale and Westland: border friction over frontier territories
- Trueborn and Oldcrest: competing claims to historical legitimacy
- Highborne and Goldgrave: cultural friction between aristocratic legacy and commercial identity
- Stonehelm and Hartvale: contested valley territories; eventual accommodation rather than defeat

**Year -110 to -80: Territory consolidation**
- Formal house identities emerge — each bloodline begins to define what it is and what it stands for
- The distinction between founding houses becomes a political reality, not just a biological one
- Lesser house formation begins — war heroes and capable administrators are granted land and title
- The Trueborn City is rebuilt as a formal neutral hub, with the Founding Compact establishing its governance structure

---

## AGE OF RECLAMATION — YEAR 0 (80 Years Before Current)

**Year 0: The Founding Compact**
- The nine founding houses collectively agree to hold the Trueborn City in trust rather than contest it
- The agreement is part philosophical (the Trueborn name creates moral gravity that makes claiming it feel wrong) and part practical (every dynasty that wants it would have to fight every other dynasty)
- The Compact establishes the city as sovereign in itself, governed by a rotating council of dynasty representatives
- Year 0 is counted from the signing of this compact — the official beginning of the Age of Reclamation

---

## AGE OF RECLAMATION — YEARS 0-80

**Year 0-20: Early consolidation**
- Each dynasty establishes formal territorial boundaries
- First formal trade agreements through the Trueborn City
- Faith affiliations begin hardening from the fluid survival-era practice into political identities
- Iron infrastructure becomes a significant differentiator between rapidly developing and struggling dynasties

**Year 20-50: The first major conflicts**
- Border wars between dynasties over resource territory
- The first Trueborn City diplomatic incidents — and the first demonstrations of the city's neutral-ground guarantee
- Lesser houses begin playing independent roles in regional politics
- Faith competition escalates in territories where multiple covenants have significant presence

**Year 50-80: Political maturation**
- Alliances form and break; the diplomatic landscape is now as complex as the military one
- The four ancient faiths have settled into their post-Frost doctrinal forms
- Several Trueborn claimants have appeared and been investigated; none confirmed
- The balance of power has reached the kind of unstable equilibrium that precedes large-scale conflict

---

## YEAR 80 — MATCH START

The game begins in Year 80 of the Age of Reclamation. The world is stable enough to have real kingdoms, real armies, real political stakes. It is not stable enough to have peace.

The conditions that define match start:
- Nine founding dynasties, each with established territory and governance
- Four ancient faiths in active competition for population allegiance
- The Trueborn City at the center of world trade and information
- Iron as the defining military resource of the era
- The Trueborn question unanswered — the city's legitimacy depends on the answer remaining open
- Something has shifted. The balance is unstable. Every player on the map is moving at once.
