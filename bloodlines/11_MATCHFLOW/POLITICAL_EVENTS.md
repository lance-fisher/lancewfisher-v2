## OVERVIEW

### What Political Events Are

Political events are the game's voice. They are the moment when the match's accumulated history speaks directly to the players who made it. Every named event in this system fires because something has been building — a pattern of behavior, a threshold crossed, a pressure sustained past its limit. No event is random. No event is a punishment dispensed without cause. Every single one can be traced backward through the match's history to specific decisions specific players made, and players who are paying attention can see most of them coming before they arrive.

Events are not punishment dispensers. They are pressure systems that reward attentive play and punish neglect. Every event creates an actor and a reactor. The actor had choices that led here. The reactor has choices about what to do next. The world's history within the match is the engine that makes events feel earned rather than arbitrary.

### How Events Are Structured

Every political event has the following components:

**Trigger condition.** The precise game-state configuration that causes the event to fire. Triggers are always player-generated — they are the result of decisions, not random timers. A player who understands trigger conditions can prevent most events from firing against them, and can engineer conditions that cause events to fire for their benefit.

**Recipients.** Who receives the primary event notification and who receives secondary notifications. Some events are private (the trigger player only). Some are announced globally. The recipient list is itself a design tool — a global announcement of Alliance Betrayal is a different kind of pressure than a private notification of army revolt.

**Effect.** What mechanically changes when the event fires. Effects always have duration, always affect specific systems (conviction, faith intensity, resource generation, diplomatic states, military stats), and always leave the recipient with agency — something to respond to, not just absorb.

**Resolution paths.** How the event ends. Most events are not permanent states. They are pressure conditions with explicit resolution mechanics. A Succession Crisis resolves when an heir is designated. A Holy War ends when its conditions are met. Understanding resolution paths is as important as understanding trigger conditions.

**Counter-play.** What options exist for the receiving player, for opposing players who were not the trigger, and for third parties who might benefit from the situation. Counter-play is a first-class design requirement. An event that only affects one player with no response options is not an event — it is a penalty.

### How Events Interact with the Three Pillars

Conviction, faith, and dynasty are not score systems in this context. They are pressure systems. Events interact with the Three Pillars to shape what options are available and what costs must be paid.

A player who has maintained Moral conviction throughout the match has access to options a Cruel player simply does not: they can absorb an insult with grace and earn conviction credit, they can trigger Divine Favor, they can accept refugees and receive the integration outcome. A Cruel player has traded those options for different ones: they can execute without standard conviction cost, suppress dissent, rule through fear. The design goal is not to make Moral play obviously superior but to make each conviction orientation coherent and internally consistent. Both paths have genuine strategic value. Both paths have genuine costs.

Faith intensity shapes what events the player is eligible for, which events fire against them, and what bonus effects are available during events. A Fervent player has access to Holy War, to the Holy Pilgrimage, to Covenant Test eligibility. An Apex player can no longer trigger a Pilgrimage because they have transcended it — but they can defend against the Trueborn Legion with a Rallying Cry.

Dynasty — the bloodline, the heirs, the members — is the substrate on which Dynastic Events operate. A dynasty that has not designated a succession line is vulnerable to Succession Crisis. A dynasty with an unguarded Bloodline Member in the field is vulnerable to capture. The dynastic system generates most of the match's most emotionally significant events because it is where the game's named characters live and die.

### World Events vs. Dynasty-Specific Events

Most events are dynasty-specific: they fire for one or two players based on their specific game state. World events are different. They fire for every player simultaneously and change the conditions of the entire match. The Great Reckoning, The Long Winter, The Great Exhaustion, The Legitimacy Erosion — these are not local pressures. They are match-defining shifts in the global environment that every dynasty must respond to at once.

World events are also calibrated to fire no more than once per category per match. The Reckoning fires once. The Long Winter fires once. This calibration is intentional: world events should feel exceptional, not procedural. They mark the match's major turning points, not its daily conditions.

## CATEGORY 1: FAITH EVENTS

Faith events fire based on the faith intensity, doctrine path, and faith-related actions of player dynasties. They are the most numerous category because the faith system generates pressure across every stage of the match, from early shrine construction through the Apex Covenant Test. Faith events interact constantly with the conviction system — the two are independent but read each other continuously.

---

### [THE SCHISM]

**Category:** Faith

**Trigger condition:** Two players share the same faith but have diverged to opposite ends of its doctrine path — both follow Blood Dominion, for example, but one has built toward the Light Doctrine branch and the other toward the Dark Doctrine branch. Faith intensity for both must be at Devout (40%) or higher. The divergence must persist for 15+ minutes of match time without either player moving toward reconciliation: no Faith Alliance formed, no shared faith buildings constructed cooperatively.

**Who receives it:** Both diverged players receive the primary event notification. All other players who share that faith receive a secondary notification indicating that a schism is underway within the faith.

**What the player sees:** A notification that the faith has fractured. Two players who share the covenant have pushed its doctrine to irreconcilable extremes. The faith community is in crisis. The Trueborn City registers the fracture. Minor tribes of the parent faith are watching.

**Effect:** A third doctrine path unlocks — the Splinter Path — available only to players inside the schism. The Splinter Path offers faster faith intensity gains but at the cost of permanent isolation from the parent faith's diplomatic bonuses. Both schism players receive a conviction pressure event: their faith-aligned population begins generating unrest proportional to how far faith intensity has climbed. Ecclesiastical buildings generate 15% less faith intensity per structure until the schism resolves or hardens. A global diplomatic notification fires to all players indicating which faith has fractured. Neutral minor tribes whose faith alignment matches the parent faith become diplomatically destabilized — they will not enter agreements with either schism player unless one player renounces the Splinter Path.

**Resolution paths:** Three conditions resolve the schism: (a) one schism player is eliminated; (b) one player formally renounces their doctrine path, losing all doctrine-specific bonuses and returning to base faith intensity; (c) both players form a Faith Alliance, which suppresses the schism and unlocks the Reunification bonus — faith intensity gains +10% for both players for 20 minutes.

**Counter-play:** The non-schism player can attempt to recruit both schism players against a common enemy by proposing a Military Alliance. A third player who follows the same faith and has higher faith intensity than either schism player can position themselves as arbiter — proposing mediation, which forces a Cordial diplomatic floor between the schism parties for 10 minutes.

**Conviction interaction:** Entering the Splinter Path costs 8 conviction points on first entry. The act of formal doctrinal separation is a moral statement. Renouncing the Splinter Path returns 4 of those points.

**Faith interaction:** Core driver. Requires Devout+ faith intensity for both parties. Schism resolution via Reunification grants the fastest faith intensity boost available outside the Covenant Test.

**Frequency:** Once per faith per match globally. If the Old Light faith schisms, it cannot schism again. A second doctrinal divergence between Old Light players produces only minor diplomatic unrest, not a full Schism event.

### [HOLY PILGRIMAGE]

**Category:** Faith

**Trigger condition:** Any player reaches Fervent faith intensity (60-80%) and has held that intensity for 10+ continuous minutes without entering a War state. The player's dynasty must have at least one Bloodline Member alive and at least one faith structure constructed.

**Who receives it:** The trigger player receives the primary event. The Trueborn City receives a secondary notification that a Pilgrimage delegation is en route.

**What the player sees:** The dynasty's faith has reached a fervor that demands expression beyond its borders. The faith calls the dynasty to send a delegation to the Trueborn City — the neutral ground that predates all covenants — as an act of witness. A non-combat caravan unit spawns at the trigger player's primary faith structure.

**Effect:** The Pilgrimage caravan moves toward the Trueborn City along roads. While en route, all territories the caravan passes through receive a passive faith intensity boost of +5% per territory per minute of caravan presence, affecting any player who controls those territories regardless of faith alignment. If the caravan reaches Trueborn City: the trigger player receives +15% faith intensity, a one-time conviction bonus of +10, and a Diplomatic Reputation buff making the trigger player appear Cordial to all currently Neutral parties for 20 minutes. If the caravan is attacked or destroyed by any player, that player receives the same consequence as attacking a Trueborn Representative — automatic Trueborn Summons fires against the attacker, plus -20 conviction.

**Counter-play:** Enemies cannot attack the caravan without catastrophic diplomatic consequence, but they can close roads (destroy road structures) to slow or reroute it. A competing player who is also Fervent or higher can launch their own Pilgrimage simultaneously. If both caravans reach Trueborn City within the same 5-minute window, both players receive the faith intensity bonus but neither receives the Diplomatic Reputation buff — the Trueborn cannot honor two pilgrims simultaneously.

**Conviction interaction:** Launching a Pilgrimage is a Moral act. The trigger player gains +5 conviction on caravan spawn. Destroying a caravan is a major Cruel act with full consequences.

**Faith interaction:** Apex faith players (80%+) cannot trigger a Pilgrimage — they have already transcended the need for external validation and have access to Apex mechanics that supersede this event's rewards.

**Frequency:** Once per player per match. A player who has completed a Pilgrimage cannot trigger another.

### [FAITH PERSECUTION]

**Category:** Faith

**Trigger condition:** A player deliberately destroys or captures a faith structure belonging to a different faith — the structure must be a dedicated faith building, not merely a military building in a contested territory. Alternatively, fires if a player executes a Bloodline Member whose faith alignment differs from their own.

**Who receives it:** All players who share the victimized faith receive the notification. The trigger player receives a private consequence notification.

**What the player sees:** If you are the Persecutor, you receive a private notification that the world has taken notice. If you follow the victimized faith, you receive a call: one of your covenant's houses of prayer has been destroyed, or one of those who carried your faith has been killed for it.

**Effect:** The trigger player is declared a Persecutor by the global diplomatic system. Every player who shares the victimized faith receives: +5% faith intensity, a passive combat bonus of +8% damage against the Persecutor's units, and the option to declare Holy War immediately without the standard conviction cost. Minor tribes of the victimized faith become permanently hostile to the Persecutor regardless of prior agreements. The Trueborn City issues a formal denunciation: all diplomatic agreements between the Persecutor and Trueborn-aligned minor factions are suspended for 15 minutes.

**Resolution:** Persecutor status persists for 20 minutes or until: (a) the Persecutor constructs a faith structure for the victimized faith in their own territory as a symbolic act of restitution, which costs conviction to commission; or (b) the Persecutor surrenders a Bloodline Member to the victimized faith's player as hostage.

**Counter-play:** The Persecutor can attempt to divide the responding coalition by offering Trade Agreements to the weaker members of the victimized-faith bloc. The Persecutor's own faith may rally to their defense if faith intensity is high enough — Fervent+ players of the Persecutor's faith receive a Mirror Holy War option to counter-declare.

**Conviction interaction:** Each faith structure destroyed costs -12 conviction. Each Bloodline Member executed for faith costs -20 conviction. If the Persecutor is already in the Cruel band, the conviction costs are halved (cruelty normalized) but the diplomatic consequence is doubled — the world notices the pattern.

**Frequency:** Can fire multiple times per match per player. A player who systematically destroys faith structures will accumulate compounding Persecutor status durations.

### [DIVINE FAVOR]

**Category:** Faith

**Trigger condition:** A player completes three faith-aligned actions within a single 20-minute window without any interruption by Cruel conviction acts. Faith-aligned actions include: completing a faith structure, reaching a new faith intensity tier, completing a faith doctrine upgrade, or offering tribute to the Trueborn City in the name of their faith.

**Who receives it:** The trigger player only.

**What the player sees:** A private notification that the faith has recognized the dynasty's sustained devotion. A visible world notification fires simultaneously — all players see that a dynasty has achieved Divine Favor, but the notification does not reveal the favored player's position or territory.

**Effect:** A Divine Favor state activates for 15 minutes. During this state: faith structures produce +20% intensity, Bloodline Members with faith alignment gain a passive +15% to all stats, and any Trade Agreement or Faith Alliance proposed by this player during the window carries a reputation bonus that makes neutral parties 30% more likely to accept. The world notification creates natural diplomacy: other players will investigate who achieved it.

**Counter-play:** There is no direct mechanic to cancel another player's Divine Favor. The indirect response is to accelerate your own faith actions to achieve it simultaneously — two players can hold Divine Favor concurrently, creating an interesting competitive dynamic as both race to capitalize on the window.

**Conviction interaction:** Divine Favor cannot fire if the player is currently in the Cruel band. A player who achieves Neutral band and holds it for 10 minutes before the three faith actions complete causes Divine Favor to fire with a bonus: +5 additional minutes of duration.

**Faith interaction:** Requires Active (20%+) faith intensity as a floor. At Apex (80%+) faith intensity at time of trigger, the stat bonuses on Bloodline Members increase to +25%.

**Frequency:** Once per player per 45-minute match window. In long matches (4+ hours), this can fire twice per player.

### [THE HOLY WAR DECLARATION]

**Category:** Faith

**Trigger condition:** A player in Active faith intensity or higher declares war on another player who shares their faith, OR a player explicitly announces Holy War through the diplomatic interface against a player of a different faith who has committed Faith Persecution against them. Holy War can also be triggered proactively by any Fervent+ player against a declared Persecutor.

**Who receives it:** All players receive the global notification. Players of the declaring player's faith receive a secondary notification with the option to join as co-belligerents.

**What the player sees:** A global announcement that a dynasty has declared Holy War. The name of the declaring dynasty and the declared enemy are both visible. The faith that sanctioned it is identified. This is not a private matter — it is a statement the world registers.

**Effect:** The declaring player enters a Holy War state. All military units receive +10% damage against the declared enemy. Faith intensity cannot decrease for the duration — it is locked at current level. Any Bloodline Member who dies in combat against the declared enemy generates a Martyr bonus: +8% faith intensity added to the declaring player's total on each Bloodline Member death (enemy deaths, not friendly deaths). Co-belligerent players who join receive the same combat bonus but not the Martyr mechanic. Neutral players who share the declared enemy's faith receive pressure to respond — their minor faction allies will generate unrest if the neutral player does not intervene within 15 minutes.

**Resolution:** Holy War state persists until: (a) the declared enemy is eliminated; (b) the declaring player achieves their stated war objective (captured at declaration — a faith structure, a territory threshold); or (c) the declaring player's faith intensity drops below Active (20%) due to prolonged war without faith maintenance.

**Counter-play:** The target of a Holy War can seek a Counter-Faith Alliance — any player of their faith or a compatible faith who is currently Cordial or higher can offer a defensive Faith Alliance. A declared target who achieves Divine Favor during the Holy War receives temporary immunity to the Martyr mechanic for 10 minutes.

**Conviction interaction:** Declaring Holy War against a player of your own faith costs -15 conviction. Declaring against a genuine Persecutor costs 0 conviction. If the declared enemy has a higher Moral conviction score than the declaring player, the Holy War declaration costs an additional -5 conviction — the world's moral accounting is precise.

**Frequency:** Once per player per match. Holy War is a statement of purpose, not a repeatable tactic.

### [FAITH REFORMATION]

**Category:** Faith

**Trigger condition:** A player who previously reached Devout faith intensity (40%+) experiences a faith intensity collapse — dropping from Devout or higher to Latent (below 20%) within a 15-minute window. The collapse must be caused by game-state events (failed Covenant Test, extended war against a faith ally, conviction slide into Cruel band) rather than deliberate player abandonment of faith buildings.

**Who receives it:** The trigger player receives the Reformation option. All players of the same faith receive a notification that a dynasty has entered spiritual crisis.

**What the player sees:** The dynasty's faith has collapsed under the weight of its contradictions. The faith community is fracturing. A 5-minute decision window opens. Three paths are presented with their full costs visible.

**Effect and choices:**
- **(a) Recant** — formally abandon the faith. Lose all doctrine bonuses but permanently remove faith-related event vulnerabilities. Conviction cost: -10.
- **(b) Recommit** — begin a 20-minute faith rebuilding sequence. Faith intensity gains are doubled but all diplomatic bonuses from faith are suspended during the rebuild. No conviction cost.
- **(c) Heresy** — formally declare against the faith's doctrine, creating a splinter identity. Unlock unique Heretic units with unconventional ability sets. Faith intensity locks at current collapsed level but cannot decline further. Conviction cost: -15, but gain a Defiance bonus that adds +12% to all Bloodline Member stats for 10 minutes.

**Counter-play:** Other players of the same faith can attempt to influence the reforming player's choice by sending diplomatic messages during the 5-minute window. A Faith Alliance proposal sent during the window creates a mechanical bias toward Recommit — the receiving player still chooses, but the support is felt.

**Conviction interaction:** Path-dependent as described. Heresy generates a strong signal across all three Pillars — the dynasty's faith collapse and defiance affects conviction trajectory, bloodline stability, and population trust simultaneously.

**Frequency:** Once per player per match. A player who has already Reformed cannot Reform again — their spiritual identity is set for the rest of the match.

## CATEGORY 2: DYNASTIC EVENTS

Dynastic events operate on the bloodline — the named members of the dynasty, their relationships, their succession, their presence in the world. These are the match's most emotionally significant events because they involve the characters the player has invested in. The death of an heir, the capture of a champion, the emergence of a powerful child who threatens the parent's authority — these events generate stories. They are the mechanism by which Bloodlines fulfills its design goal of producing a narrative worth retelling.

---

### [SUCCESSION CRISIS]

**Category:** Dynastic

**Trigger condition:** A Bloodline Member who is the current dynasty's designated Heir dies in combat, and no secondary Bloodline Member has been designated as heir. The dynasty must have been in the match for 30+ minutes — the time threshold prevents this from firing in early-game skirmishes where designation hasn't yet occurred.

**Who receives it:** The trigger player receives the full event. All neighboring dynasties receive a notification that the dynasty is in succession crisis.

**What the player sees:** The heir is dead. The throne is empty. The dynasty's allies and enemies both know it. A 10-minute window opens — the dynasty must name a new heir before the uncertainty hardens into fracture.

**Effect:** The dynasty enters a 10-minute Interregnum state. Resource generation drops 15% across all categories. Military unit production time increases 20%. All diplomatic agreements become fragile — any player can attempt to break a Trade Agreement with the crisis dynasty without the normal diplomatic cost. Neighboring dynasties who were Hostile may attempt opportunistic expansion — territorial border checks are relaxed during Interregnum. If designation occurs within the window, a Restoration bonus fires: +10% resource generation for 15 minutes.

**Dynastic Fracture variant:** If no Heir is designated within 10 minutes, the dynasty's secondary military commander attempts to seize power. The player retains control but loses all Bloodline Member bonuses for 20 minutes. The usurper unit gains a permanent +20% stat bonus but cannot grow through the Bloodline progression tree.

**Counter-play:** Players with an Allied relationship to the crisis dynasty can offer a Bloodline Marriage during the Interregnum to shore up succession — this does not resolve the crisis mechanically but adds one available Bloodline Member candidate to the trigger player's pool.

**Conviction interaction:** A Moral dynasty that loses their Heir in battle receives +5 conviction from the Martyr's Legacy — the Heir died fighting, not hiding. A Cruel dynasty that loses their Heir receives an additional -5 conviction — the dynasty's cruelty contributed to the Heir's circumstances.

**Faith interaction:** If the deceased Heir was faith-aligned at Devout+, the faith structure associated with their doctrine generates +10% intensity for 5 minutes as the faith community mourns.

**Frequency:** Can fire multiple times per player per match — every qualifying Heir death is a potential Succession Crisis.

### [THE BLOODLINE CAPTURE]

**Category:** Dynastic

**Trigger condition:** A Bloodline Member from any dynasty is captured in combat. Health reduced to zero when the capturing player has a Capture-equipped unit present, and the downed Bloodline Member is in controlled territory at time of downing.

**Who receives it:** The capturing player and the captured player both receive the event notification. All players with diplomatic relationships to either party receive a secondary notification.

**What the player sees:** If you are the captor, a named member of another dynasty is now yours to use or release. If you are the one who lost them, someone whose name you know, whose progression you built, is sitting in an enemy's hold. Every subsequent diplomatic action is shadowed by this fact.

**Effect:** The captured Bloodline Member is held as a Hostage at the capturing player's hold structure. The captured player loses access to all abilities and progression of the captured Bloodline Member for the duration of captivity. The capturing player gains a Leverage state with four options:
- **(a) Ransom** — demand resources. The capturing player sets terms, the captured player can counter-offer.
- **(b) Execution** — kill the hostage. Grants +30% military production speed for 20 minutes. Costs -25 conviction. Triggers a permanent diplomatic reputation hit with all Cordial and Allied parties.
- **(c) Trade** — exchange the hostage for a diplomatic concession: Hostile to Neutral, or Neutral to Cordial.
- **(d) Retain** — keep the hostage as ongoing leverage. The captured player cannot declare war on the capturing player while their Bloodline Member is held, without executing their own hostage first — which costs conviction. Retaining without resolution generates slow Cruel drift: -2 conviction per 10 minutes.

**Rescue missions:** The captured player can attempt a covert military operation to free the hostage — a small elite unit assault on the hold structure. Success is based on unit composition and territorial control, not random.

**Conviction interaction:** Execution is among the largest single-action conviction losses in the game outside of genocide. Ransom and Trade are conviction-neutral. Indefinite retention without resolution generates compounding Cruel drift.

**Faith interaction:** If the captured Bloodline Member is the dynasty's primary faith figure — the member who has been performing faith actions — their captivity halts all faith intensity gains for the duration. The faith community will not honor a faith that imprisons those who carry it.

**Frequency:** Can fire multiple times per match — each Bloodline Member capture is a separate event.

### [THE MARRIAGE PROPOSITION]

**Category:** Dynastic

**Trigger condition:** Two dynasties have been at Cordial diplomatic status for 20+ continuous minutes, and both have at least one eligible Bloodline Member — not currently designated as primary Heir, not currently captured, and not already in a prior Marriage Agreement.

**Who receives it:** Both dynasties receive the event notification simultaneously.

**What the player sees:** The two dynasties have built a relationship close enough that the possibility of formal alliance through bloodline becomes real. A Marriage Proposition appears in both diplomatic interfaces. Both players must decide — accept, counter-offer, or decline.

**Effect on acceptance:** Both parties designate one Bloodline Member each. The Bloodline Members enter a paired state — they cannot be assigned to the same military operation as a safety measure. Both dynasties receive a Bloodline Merge bonus: each player gains access to one passive ability from the other player's Bloodline progression tree (the highest-tier ability the partner has already unlocked). Both dynasties receive a Diplomatic Stability bonus: transitions from Cordial to Hostile now require a 10-minute cooling-off period, blocking immediate escalation. If either dynasty later breaks the Marriage Agreement by declaring war, the breaking dynasty loses both Bloodline Members from the paired state — not to death, but to a 20-minute estrangement during which they are non-functional.

**Counter-play:** A third player who opposes the alliance can attempt to manufacture a pretense for conflict between the two parties — attacking border territories to provoke misattribution. This is high-risk if discovered.

**Conviction interaction:** Proposing and accepting a Marriage is a Moral act (+4 conviction each). Breaking a Marriage Agreement for war is a Cruel act (-18 conviction) — the world regards it as a betrayal of sacred covenant.

**Faith interaction:** If both dynasties share the same faith, the Marriage Agreement generates a joint faith intensity bonus (+8% for both) as the faith recognizes the union as doctrinally significant. Mixed-faith marriages are politically neutral — no faith bonus, no faith penalty.

**Frequency:** One active Marriage Agreement per dynasty at any time. Sequential marriages are permitted after a prior agreement dissolves.

### [HEIR EMERGENCE]

**Category:** Dynastic

**Trigger condition:** A dynasty's Bloodline Member who has been designated as Heir reaches Level 3 progression while the current dynasty leader — the primary Bloodline Member — is still alive and active at Level 4+ progression. The generational gap creates tension: a powerful heir and a powerful leader in the same dynasty at the same time.

**Who receives it:** The trigger player receives the event. All players with Cordial or Allied relationships to the trigger dynasty receive a secondary notification.

**What the player sees:** The heir has grown into something formidable. The match has given them battles, decisions, losses, and victories. They are not the child they were at founding. And the dynasty's current leader is watching, calculating whether this is a threat or a resource. An 8-minute decision window opens.

**Effect and choices:**
- **(a) Crown the Heir** — the Heir becomes primary leader. The current leader becomes Elder Advisor, retaining all abilities but losing primary command of military units. The Heir gains +15% to all stats immediately but the Elder Advisor generates ongoing tension (-5% resource generation for 15 minutes while the transition settles).
- **(b) Suppress the Heir** — the current leader retains primacy. The Heir's progression is capped at Level 3 until the leader dies or is incapacitated. Costs -8 conviction.
- **(c) Declare Co-Regency** — both operate as equals. Combined stat bonuses are 80% of what each would receive individually, but the dynasty gains access to dual-command military operations.

After 8 minutes without a choice, Suppression is automatically applied.

**Counter-play:** Rivals can attempt to create a crisis during the 8-minute window to force hasty decision-making. Allies can offer diplomatic recognition of the new leader, biasing the trigger player toward Crowning.

**Conviction interaction:** Suppression costs conviction. Crowning is conviction-neutral. Co-Regency is a Moral act (+6 conviction) — sharing power is recognized as generous governance.

**Faith interaction:** If the Heir has higher faith intensity alignment than the current leader, Crowning generates a faith intensity bonus (+10%) as the faith community welcomes a more devout ruler.

**Frequency:** Once per dynasty per match. Heir Emergence represents a singular generational moment.

---

### [DYNASTIC RIVALRY]

**Category:** Dynastic

**Trigger condition:** Two dynasties have been in a War or Hostile diplomatic state for 30+ continuous minutes without either achieving decisive military advantage. Neither has eliminated a Bloodline Member from the other. Neither has taken more than 2 territories from the other during this period. The prolonged standoff creates institutional rivalry.

**Who receives it:** Both rival dynasties receive the event. Minor tribes in contested border territories receive a secondary notification.

**What the player sees:** The two dynasties have been fighting long enough that the conflict has developed its own character. The soldiers on both sides know the enemy's formations. The commanders know each other's tendencies. A formal state of Dynastic Rivalry is recognized — which changes what each dynasty is capable of.

**Effect:** Both dynasties receive a Rivalry Bonus — +12% to all military unit stats when fighting each other specifically, not against other players. This represents the adaptive learning of prolonged conflict against a known enemy. Minor tribes in contested territories become unreliable for both parties — their loyalty can shift based on which rival most recently performed a positive action in their territory. The Rivalry creates a Prestige mechanic: the first rival to eliminate a Bloodline Member from the other during the Rivalry state receives a global prestige notification and a +10% diplomatic reputation boost with all Neutral parties.

**Resolution:** Rivalry state persists until one party is eliminated, or both parties agree to a formal Peace. A Peace signed during Rivalry grants both parties a +10% resource generation bonus for 20 minutes — the end of a cold war releases productive capacity.

**Counter-play:** A third party can attempt to break the Rivalry by offering both parties a common threat — proposing a trilateral conflict elsewhere. If both rivals accept an external Military Alliance against a common enemy, the Rivalry state suspends until the common threat is resolved.

**Conviction interaction:** A Moral player who achieves the Prestige moment by defending a minor tribe rather than striking the rival directly receives +5 conviction bonus on the Prestige notification.

**Faith interaction:** If both rivals share the same faith, the Rivalry generates ongoing faith community unrest. Faith intensity for both parties loses 3% every 10 minutes while Rivalry persists between same-faith dynasties.

**Frequency:** One active Dynastic Rivalry per dynasty at any time. A dynasty cannot be in simultaneous declared Rivalries with two parties.

## CATEGORY 3: ECONOMIC EVENTS

Economic events fire based on the health, scale, and behavior of player economies. They register when trade routes collapse, when resource systems fail under stress, when dynasties compete for the same markets, and when one dynasty's financial position becomes large enough to distort everyone else's. Economic events are quieter than faith or dynastic events — they often fire without global announcement, affecting only the relevant parties — but their downstream consequences on military and diplomatic positioning are substantial.

---

### [TRADE ROUTE COLLAPSE]

**Category:** Economic

**Trigger condition:** An active Trade Route Establishment is severed because the territory through which the route passes is captured by a third party, or the originating or receiving trade building is destroyed. Alternatively, fires if a player with 3+ active trade routes simultaneously loses 2 of them within a 5-minute window.

**Who receives it:** Both parties in the severed Trade Route receive the primary event. The player who caused the severance receives a secondary notification indicating they have triggered economic disruption.

**What the player sees:** A trade route you depended on is gone. The income is missing. The goods you were exchanging are backing up. The Trueborn City has noticed — their trade volume is briefly absorbing the disruption. A market opportunity has opened for anyone paying attention.

**Effect:** Both trade route parties experience an immediate resource generation drop of 20% across all traded resource types for 10 minutes. The route's specific traded resources drop 35% during that same window. The Trueborn City registers the collapse and briefly increases its own trade volume — Trueborn-allied minor factions become more willing to enter new Trade Agreements for 5 minutes.

**Counter-play:** Both affected parties can independently pursue replacement trade routes. A player who caused the collapse should expect the two affected parties to seek each other and form a defensive economic alliance — they now have a common grievance.

**Conviction interaction:** Deliberately destroying a trade building to collapse a rival's route costs -8 conviction. Losing a route to military action is conviction-neutral.

**Frequency:** Fires each time a qualifying collapse occurs. No cap.

---

### [RESOURCE SHORTAGE]

**Category:** Economic

**Trigger condition:** Any player falls below 100 units of two or more of the six primary resources simultaneously for 10+ continuous minutes. This represents genuine economic crisis, not momentary expenditure.

**Who receives it:** The trigger player receives the event. Players who are Cordial or Allied receive a secondary notification that the dynasty is struggling.

**What the player sees:** The granaries are low. The smithies are idle. The population is beginning to ask questions that don't have comfortable answers. Unit production halts. Hunger Unrest begins building. The window to resolve this before it becomes something worse is measured in minutes.

**Effect:** The trigger player enters Scarcity State. Unit production halts for any unit requiring deficient resources. Population growth stops. Existing population begins generating Hunger Unrest — if unresolved for another 10 minutes, a Population Revolt fires. All players can offer Emergency Trade to the struggling dynasty: a one-time resource transfer without a formal Trade Agreement. Emergency Trade earns +5 Diplomatic Reputation with the receiver and moves the diplomatic state one step toward Cordial if currently Neutral.

**Scarcity resolution:** Both deficient resources must rise above 200 units simultaneously. Hunger Unrest persists for 5 minutes after Scarcity resolves — the community remembers.

**Counter-play:** The trigger player can prioritize resource recovery through aggressive expansion, Emergency Trade requests, or population sacrifice if their faith doctrine supports it. Rivals can exploit Scarcity by timing military pressure to coincide with the production halt.

**Conviction interaction:** Deliberately neglecting resource distribution in a territory you control is tracked — if Scarcity is detected in a Cruel dynasty, the conviction cost for the subsequent unrest doubles.

**Frequency:** Can fire multiple times per player per match — each qualifying scarcity period generates the event independently.

---

### [THE CURRENCY WAR]

**Category:** Economic

**Trigger condition:** Two players who both have active Trade Agreements with a third party (a "neutral hub" — a minor tribe or neutral faction) begin competing for that hub's exclusive trade relationship. Detected when both players have offered Trade Route Establishment to the same minor tribe or neutral faction within the same 10-minute window, and the minor faction can only accept one.

**Who receives it:** Both competing players and the neutral hub receive the event. The Trueborn City receives a notification.

**What the player sees:** Two dynasties want the same trade partner. The tribe or faction is now in the position to extract maximum value from the competition. A bidding window opens. The bid is not just in gold — it is in reputation, in faith, in the story of who this minor power wants to align with.

**Effect:** The neutral hub enters a Bidding State for 8 minutes. Both competing players can escalate their trade offers by committing resources from their stockpile — minimum bid escalation: 50 gold per round. The minor faction's acceptance threshold rises 10% with each competing escalation. At the end of 8 minutes, the highest committing player wins the exclusive trade relationship. The losing player does not receive their committed resources back but receives a Consolation Relationship — the minor tribe remains Neutral. The winning player receives the trade route plus a Market Dominance bonus: +8% gold generation for 15 minutes. If the bid escalation exceeded 500 total gold committed, the Trueborn City issues an Economic Interest declaration and sends a trade delegation to the winning player's territory within 10 minutes.

**Counter-play:** A third player who is uninvolved can attempt to poach the minor tribe by offering Military Protection during the bidding window. Military Protection makes the minor tribe Cordial to the third player and suspicious of both bidders — the tribe may withdraw from bidding entirely, leaving both bidders without the route.

**Conviction interaction:** Currency War is conviction-neutral in itself. Bid escalation tactics that involve resource deception (promising resources you don't have and defaulting) cost -10 conviction when the default is detected.

**Faith interaction:** If the minor tribe has faith alignment, a bid from a player of the same faith receives a 20% discount on bid escalation — the tribe values faith commonality and requires less gold to be persuaded.

**Frequency:** Once per minor tribe per match. A tribe that has been through a Currency War will not accept a second bidding process — their relationship with the winner is locked.

---

### [MARKET MANIPULATION]

**Category:** Economic

**Trigger condition:** A player with 4+ active Trade Agreements simultaneously requests renegotiation of all agreements within a 5-minute window, leveraging market position to extract value from existing partners.

**Who receives it:** All affected trade partners receive the renegotiation request.

**What the player sees:** If you are the manipulator, you have decided that your market position is strong enough to rewrite the rules in your favor — and you are betting that your partners will accept rather than walk away. If you are a partner, you have received a demand where previously there was an agreement.

**Effect:** Each trade partner must independently decide to Accept, Counter-Offer, or Refuse within 5 minutes. Refusals do not immediately break the trade agreement but move the relationship one step toward Hostile. If two or more partners refuse within the same window, a Trade Bloc forms: the refusing partners automatically become Cordial to each other and share economic intelligence — each bloc member can see the manipulating player's resource totals. The Trade Bloc persists for 20 minutes and creates a diplomatic pressure entity — the manipulating player's agreements with bloc members are all flagged as Fragile. If all renegotiations succeed, the manipulating player receives The Monopolist title: +15% gold generation but a permanent -10% diplomatic receptivity from all Neutral parties for the rest of the match.

**Counter-play:** The manipulating player can attempt to divide the Trade Bloc by offering preferential terms to the strongest bloc member privately — a visible side deal that may fracture bloc solidarity.

**Conviction interaction:** Market Manipulation is a mild Cruel act (-5 conviction per successful forced renegotiation). If partners were coerced through military positioning during the negotiation window, the conviction cost doubles.

**Frequency:** Once per player per match. The Monopolist reputation, once earned, is not escapable through repetition.

---

## CATEGORY 4: MILITARY EVENTS

Military events fire from the conditions of warfare itself — prolonged campaigns without rest, sieges that grind on past their expected resolution, territories captured but not governed, armies worn down past the point of loyalty. These events do not punish military players for being military. They punish military players for ignoring the human cost of their decisions. Every trigger condition in this category could have been prevented by a player who was managing their army's welfare, governing their conquests, or ending their sieges before attrition took hold.

---

### [VETERAN ARMY REVOLT]

**Category:** Military

**Trigger condition:** A player's military forces have been continuously deployed in War state for 40+ minutes without returning to a garrison or rest state, AND the player's conviction is in the Cruel band, AND food resources have been below 150 units for 15+ minutes. All three conditions must be simultaneously true.

**Who receives it:** The trigger player receives the event. No external notification — revolt is an internal affair until the defectors join another faction.

**What the player sees:** The soldiers have had enough. They have been in the field long enough that they remember what a bed feels like. They are hungry. And they are led by someone who has not shown them mercy. The revolt is not surprise — it is the inevitable end of a long accumulation. 30% of non-Bloodline military units enter Mutiny State.

**Effect:** Mutinous units stop accepting player commands but do not immediately attack — they form a stationary force demanding resolution within 8 minutes. Three resolution paths:
- **(a) Pay the Troops** — commit 200 gold immediately. Units return to service. Conviction: neutral.
- **(b) Purge the Mutiny** — send Bloodline Members to suppress. Suppression costs 10% of the Bloodline Member's current health. Units are disbanded. Conviction: -10.
- **(c) Negotiate** — reduce conviction to Neutral band (if possible within 8 minutes through faith actions or moral acts) and the units return automatically.

If no resolution occurs within 8 minutes, the mutinous units defect to the nearest Neutral minor tribe, joining that tribe's defensive force. They will fight against the trigger player if attacked.

**Counter-play:** Rivals who observe prolonged war without food production can pre-position to capitalize on the defection outcome — capturing the minor tribe the defectors join, or recruiting those defectors through a diplomatic overture.

**Frequency:** Once per conviction descent into Cruel per match. A player who resolves a revolt and slides back into Cruel can trigger it again — but the second revolt requires 50% of the army to mutiny.

---

### [GARRISON DEFECTION]

**Category:** Military

**Trigger condition:** A player captures a territory and places garrison units there but does not perform any positive stability action within 20 minutes of capture. The conquered population becomes restless. Positive stability actions include: constructing a building, stationing a Bloodline Member, or initiating trade with the territory's resident population.

**Who receives it:** The capturing player receives the event notification. The original territory owner receives a notification that resistance is forming in the lost territory.

**What the player sees:** You took the land. You did not take the people. 25% of the garrison unit strength has defected to a local Resistance cell. The original owner knows about it. The lesson is visible in the landscape: conquest without governance creates its own army against you.

**Effect:** A Resistance cell occupies a corner of the captured territory — not controlled by the original owner, not controlled by the capturing player. The Resistance harasses garrison units with periodic 8% damage ticks on adjacent garrison forces but cannot capture territory itself. The original territory owner can re-ignite the Resistance into a full rebellion by making contact (sending a diplomatic unit or Bloodline Member), converting the Resistance into a partial recapture force. The capturing player can eliminate the Resistance cell by stationing a Bloodline Member in the territory for 10 minutes.

**Counter-play:** Prevention is the intended lesson. Performing a stability action within the 20-minute window prevents the event from firing entirely. Speed of governance is the answer.

**Conviction interaction:** A Moral capturing player who performs a stability action within 5 minutes receives a Benevolent Conqueror bonus: +8% resource generation in the new territory for 20 minutes. Ignoring the territory costs -5 conviction — neglect is a form of cruelty.

**Faith interaction:** If the captured territory has a faith structure of a different faith, and the capturing player destroys it within the 20-minute window before performing stability actions, Garrison Defection fires immediately without the delay.

**Frequency:** Fires for each qualifying captured territory. No cap. A player who captures many territories rapidly and governs none of them will face multiple simultaneous Resistance cells.

---

### [SIEGE ATTRITION]

**Category:** Military

**Trigger condition:** A siege operation has persisted for 20+ minutes without either side achieving resolution. Military units stationed adjacent to an enemy fortification without breaching it — neither the besieger pressing through nor the besieged breaking out.

**Who receives it:** Both the besieging player and the besieged player receive the event simultaneously.

**What the player sees:** This siege has stopped being a military operation and started being a way of life. The besieging army's supplies are running thin. The besieged city's food stores are being rationed. Neither side is winning. Both sides are paying. The world has noticed — Mercenary Availability offers will begin arriving.

**Effect — for the besieger:** Food and water consumption rates for siege units increase 25%. Gold generation drops 10%. For the besieged: food and water generation inside the besieged territory drops 30%. However, the besieged player receives a Defender's Resolve bonus — all garrison and defense units gain +15% combat stats. The event fires a global notification that a major siege is underway. After 10 additional minutes of unresolved siege (30 total), a Secondary Siege Attrition fires: both sides lose 5% of their total food stores per 5 minutes until the siege resolves.

**Counter-play:** The besieger's best response is breakthrough — committing Bloodline Members to a direct assault rather than waiting. The besieged's best response is calling for allied relief — an Allied player who breaks through the siege line from outside receives the Siege Breaker title: a diplomatic prestige bonus moving all Neutral parties one step toward Cordial for 15 minutes.

**Faith interaction:** If the besieged fortification contains a faith structure and the besieging player is of a different faith, the faith community of the besieged player generates a Holy War call — a passive 5% faith intensity gain per 10 minutes of siege for the besieged player, representing spiritual rallying under threat.

**Frequency:** One active Siege Attrition event per siege operation. Can fire multiple times if multiple sieges are underway simultaneously.

---

### [MERCENARY AVAILABILITY]

**Category:** Military

**Trigger condition:** Any active war state has persisted for 25+ minutes globally — any war between any two players, not necessarily involving the player receiving the event. Extended conflict creates demand for independent military contractors. Alternatively, fires immediately when a Siege Attrition event reaches the 20-minute threshold.

**Who receives it:** All players not currently in an active war state receive the availability notification. Players in war states receive it 5 minutes later.

**What the player sees:** A Mercenary Company has formed at a designated neutral location on the map's edge. Eight standard units and one elite commander, available to whoever can pay and get there first.

**Effect:** The Mercenary Company is purchasable for gold — base cost 150 gold in the first hour, +50 gold per additional 30-minute increment. Once hired, the Company operates for 20 minutes under player control before disbanding. If no player hires the Company within 10 minutes of availability, the Company attacks the most recently contested territory on the map, acting as a chaotic third force.

**Counter-play:** Multiple players can bid for the Mercenary Company simultaneously — the first player to send gold to the Mercenary location wins. A player who suspects a rival will hire the Company can attempt to intercept the mercenaries' path with military units to delay deployment.

**Conviction interaction:** Hiring mercenaries is conviction-neutral. Using mercenaries to commit atrocities carries the normal conviction cost — they are your proxy, and their actions are your responsibility.

**Faith interaction:** If the Mercenary Company's elite commander has a faith alignment that conflicts with your own faith at Fervent+, hiring them costs 5% faith intensity — the faith community disapproves of employing commanders whose convictions contradict the doctrine.

**Frequency:** Fires once per qualifying trigger condition per match phase. Maximum three Mercenary Availability events per match.

---

### [CONQUERED TERRITORY RISING]

**Category:** Military

**Trigger condition:** A player who has captured 5+ territories from a single enemy dynasty has held those territories for 30+ minutes without the conquered dynasty being eliminated. The surviving enemy dynasty's population in captured territories reaches a tipping point.

**Who receives it:** The conquering player receives the primary event. The surviving conquered dynasty receives a notification that their people are rising.

**What the player sees:** The five territories you took are not pacified. They are occupied. There is a difference. The people in them remember who their lord was before you arrived, and someone is organizing. A Liberation Movement spawns in the most recently captured territory, visible to all players on the map.

**Effect:** The Liberation force is proportional to territories held by the conquering player — 1 unit per captured territory, up to 8 units. The Liberation force moves toward the nearest territory the conquered dynasty still controls. Along the way, it can free captured territories: if the Liberation force passes through a captured territory with only garrison units, it has a 40% chance of reclaiming the territory for the conquered dynasty. A Bloodline Member presence in the territory drops the reclaim chance to 5%. A global notification fires — the Liberation Movement is visible on the map. Its progress is public.

**Counter-play:** The conquering player's response is to station Bloodline Members in threatened territories. The most effective long-term counter is governance — a Moral conquering player who has constructed governance buildings in conquered territories reduces the Liberation chance to 15% and prevents the event from firing entirely if buildings are present in all 5 territories.

**Faith interaction:** If the Liberation force passes through a territory with a faith structure of the conquered dynasty's faith, it gains +15% combat stats — the faith becomes a rallying force for the liberation.

**Frequency:** Once per conquering dynasty per pair at the 5-territory threshold. A second Liberation Movement spawns at the 10-territory threshold if the conquered dynasty is still alive.

---

## CATEGORY 5: DIPLOMATIC EVENTS

Diplomatic events fire at the intersections between dynasties — when relationships are violated, when refugees need shelter, when trade wars are declared, when embassies become battlegrounds. They are the category that most directly models the political life between the founding houses. Every diplomatic event involves at least two parties and creates a visible change in the diplomatic landscape that all players can see and respond to.

---

### [ALLIANCE BETRAYAL]
*(Settled Canon — Full Specification)*

**Category:** Diplomatic

**Trigger condition:** A player moves their diplomatic relationship from Allied to War in a single step without an intermediate Hostile period, OR breaks a Non-Aggression Pact by attacking a party they have a NAP with, OR uses diplomatic cover (maintains Cordial or Allied status) while actively positioning military forces for an attack that is later executed. All three forms are recognized as Alliance Betrayal by the game system.

**Who receives it:** The betrayed player receives the primary event notification. All players receive a global notification indicating which dynasty has committed Alliance Betrayal.

**What the player sees:** The announcement is unambiguous. The name of the betrayer is attached to the act. Every dynasty on the map sees it at the same moment. The betrayed party receives sympathy. The betrayer receives something they will spend the next 30 minutes trying to repair.

**Effect:** The betraying player receives: -25 conviction immediately, a permanent Betrayer Reputation flag visible to all players in diplomatic interfaces (replaced with a Rehabilitated flag after 30 minutes of Moral-band conviction acts), and a diplomatic receptivity penalty — all players are 40% less likely to accept any new agreement with the betraying player for 20 minutes. The betrayed player receives: automatic Cordial diplomatic status with all Neutral parties for 15 minutes, a 15% military defense bonus for 10 minutes, and the right to immediately declare war without any diplomatic cost. Minor tribes who observed the betrayal become permanently Hostile to the betraying dynasty.

**Resolution:** The betraying player's only recovery path is consistent Moral-band behavior for 30 minutes — diplomatic outreach, non-aggressive actions, completing faith acts. There is no shortcut to Rehabilitated status. The diplomatic cost of betrayal is designed to be genuinely painful.

**Conviction interaction:** The -25 conviction is the single largest one-time conviction cost available through a diplomatic action.

**Faith interaction:** Faith communities of the betraying player's faith generate unrest — betrayal is contrary to most faith doctrines' stated values. Faith intensity drops 8% on betrayal.

**Frequency:** Can fire for each qualifying betrayal act. A player who commits Alliance Betrayal twice receives compounding reputation damage — the second Betrayer flag reduces diplomatic receptivity by 60% and adds an additional 10-minute duration.

---

### [EMBASSY INSULT]

**Category:** Diplomatic

**Trigger condition:** A player who has an active diplomatic representative in another dynasty's territory issues a public diplomatic declaration that is hostile, dismissive, or accusatory toward the host dynasty. This includes: refusing a public arbitration request at Trueborn City, publicly rejecting a Marriage Proposition in a hostile manner, or sending a military unit into the diplomatic zone around an embassy building.

**Who receives it:** The insulted dynasty receives the primary event. All players receive a secondary notification of the diplomatic incident.

**What the player sees:** If you are the insulted party, you have a choice: respond publicly with a formal Protest and expel the diplomat, or swallow the insult and let the world see your restraint. Both choices carry consequences. Neither is costless.

**Effect:** The insulted dynasty may issue a formal Protest within 5 minutes. If a Protest is issued, the insulting dynasty's diplomatic representative is expelled, removing any infrastructure bonuses the Diplomatic Mission provided. The insulting dynasty receives a Diplomatic Contempt flag — all players in Neutral or Cordial state move one step toward Hostile automatically. If no Protest is issued, the insulted dynasty receives +4 conviction for restraint, but the insulter's conviction drops -5 regardless.

**Resolution:** The insulting player can issue a formal Apology through the diplomatic interface — this costs 100 gold as a gesture of sincerity and moves the insulted dynasty's diplomatic state back one step toward its prior position. If accepted, the Contempt flag is lifted early. If rejected, the flag remains and the gold is lost.

**Conviction interaction:** Formal Apology with Acceptance earns +6 conviction for the insulting player. The gamble has a real downside.

**Faith interaction:** If both dynasties share the same faith, the insult generates additional faith community unrest — intra-faith disrespect is theologically problematic. Faith intensity drops 5% for the insulting player.

**Frequency:** Can fire multiple times per relationship pair per match. A dynasty that has committed Embassy Insult twice against the same target cannot issue an Apology — the second offense is beyond repair.

---

### [NEUTRAL PARTY APPEAL]

**Category:** Diplomatic

**Trigger condition:** Any player who has been in a War state for 20+ minutes without making material progress may issue a Ceasefire Appeal to the Trueborn City, requesting neutral arbitration. Alternatively, the Trueborn City may issue an unsolicited Arbitration Demand if two players have been in war for 40+ minutes and the war has generated significant civilian disruption — multiple captures and recaptures of the same territories, indicating a grinding stalemate.

**Who receives it:** Both warring parties receive the Appeal or Demand. All players receive a global notification that arbitration is underway.

**What the player sees:** The Trueborn City has noticed the war. They have proposed that both sides sit down in front of a neutral party and consider the cost. Attacking during the arbitration window does not cost military resources — it costs conviction.

**Effect:** Both parties enter a 10-minute negotiation window. Military actions are not halted (there is no forced ceasefire) but attacking during arbitration costs -8 conviction per military engagement initiated. The Trueborn City proposes terms based on the current territorial state — the party with more territory at arbitration initiation is asked to yield one territory as a peace gesture. The other party is asked to pay a resource tribute (50 gold, 50 food). If both parties accept, they enter a Mediated Peace — they cannot re-enter War state for 15 minutes. If either party refuses, the refusing party receives the Intransigent flag — -15% diplomatic receptivity from all Neutral parties for 10 minutes.

**Counter-play:** A warring player who is winning the war has no incentive to accept arbitration — they should refuse. The Intransigent flag is an acceptable cost if the military campaign is progressing. The design intention is that arbitration is genuinely valuable to losing players and genuinely useless to winning players, which makes it an accurate signal of relative military position.

**Conviction interaction:** Accepting arbitration and honoring the peace is a Moral act (+6 conviction for both parties). Accepting and then attacking within the 15-minute Mediated Peace window triggers Alliance Betrayal.

**Frequency:** Once per war pair per conflict instance.

---

### [REFUGEE CRISIS]

**Category:** Diplomatic

**Trigger condition:** A player's territory is captured or destroyed at a sufficient scale to displace a significant portion of their simulated population. Specifically: if 3+ territories are captured within a 15-minute window, the dispossessed population flows toward the nearest stable dynasty.

**Who receives it:** The player who has lost territory receives the displacement event. The player whose territory the refugees are flowing toward receives the refugee arrival notification.

**What the player sees:** A Refugee Column is on the move. They are real — they have names in the game's tracking system, they have faith alignments, they have loyalty to the dynasty that lost them. They need somewhere to go. And the receiving dynasty must decide what kind of civilization they are.

**Effect — three choices, 8-minute window:**
- **(a) Accept Refugees** — population integrates. +10% food and water consumption for 15 minutes (feeding the displaced), then +8% population growth rate. The displaced dynasty becomes Cordial to the accepting player permanently as a baseline.
- **(b) Reject Refugees** — Refugee Column turned away. Rejected population becomes a Minor Tribe — a neutral, homeless faction that aggressively contests unoccupied resource territories. The rejecting dynasty receives -12 conviction and a Cruel Reputation flag visible to all Cordial and Allied parties for 10 minutes.
- **(c) Exploit Refugees** — offer settlement in exchange for labor conscription. +15% resource generation for 10 minutes but -8 conviction and the displaced dynasty's diplomatic attitude moves to Hostile — they recognize the exploitation.

**Counter-play:** The displaced dynasty can attempt to recapture their lost territories before the Refugee Column reaches another player. If territories are retaken, the Refugee Column turns back and the event resolves without reaching the decision window.

**Conviction interaction:** Accepting refugees is the only major diplomatic event where a Moral act carries a real short-term cost — increased consumption. The design is intentional. Morality should have real costs, not just benefits.

**Faith interaction:** If the refugees share the accepting player's faith, Accept Refugees grants +8% faith intensity. Exploit Refugees always costs 5% faith intensity regardless of shared faith — the faith community does not approve of exploiting the vulnerable.

**Frequency:** Can fire multiple times per match — each qualifying displacement event generates a Refugee Column.

---

### [TRADE WAR DECLARATION]

**Category:** Diplomatic

**Trigger condition:** A player who has 3+ active Trade Agreements unilaterally cancels all of them simultaneously and publicly announces trade sanctions against a specific named target dynasty. Alternatively, fires if a player deliberately routes a competing Trade Route through another player's territory without permission, cutting off their existing trade flow.

**Who receives it:** The declaring player and all sanctioned parties receive the event. The Trueborn City receives a notification.

**What the player sees:** Economic warfare has been declared openly. The sanctioning dynasty has chosen to use their market position as a weapon. All Mercenary Availability events in the match are triggered early — the economic disruption destabilizes military markets.

**Effect:** The sanctioning player becomes a Trade War Actor — all their existing trade agreements are suspended for 10 minutes. The sanctioned dynasty receives: a 15% boost to independent resource generation for 20 minutes (the pressure of sanctions forces internal development), and automatic Sympathy relationship with all Neutral parties (they move to Cordial for 10 minutes). All players who had active trade agreements with the sanctioning player may form a Trade Bloc automatically — no bid requirement, just automatic Cordial status among bloc members.

**Counter-play:** The sanctioning player's strategic goal should be to isolate the target before the sympathy period expires. If the target can form a Trade Bloc during the sympathy period, the sanctioning player will face a united economic front.

**Conviction interaction:** Trade War Declaration is conviction-neutral if announced openly. Using it as a prelude to military action without declaring intent — attacking during the suspension period — costs -15 conviction. Economic warfare as military cover is treated as a form of Alliance Betrayal.

**Frequency:** Once per player per match as an initiating action. A player can be the target of Trade War multiple times.

---

## CATEGORY 6: WORLD EVENTS

World events are distinct from all other event categories. They fire for every player simultaneously. They change the conditions of the entire match — not for one dynasty, not for two competing parties, but for everyone who is still alive when they fire. They mark the match's major turning points. They are the moments when the game's own systems speak directly to all players at once about the state of the world they have been building together.

World events are calibrated to fire no more than once per category per match. Their rarity is their weight. When the Great Reckoning fires, every player on the map knows that the match has changed permanently.

---

### [THE COVENANT TEST]
*(Settled Canon — Full Specification)*
*(Cross-listed: Faith Category and World Category — fires as a personal test visible to all)*

**Category:** World / Faith

**Trigger condition:** Any dynasty reaches Level 4 progression in their Bloodline development tree. The Covenant fires specifically for the dynasty that achieved Level 4.

**Who receives it:** The trigger dynasty receives the Test mandate. All other players receive a global notification that a Covenant Test has been issued for that dynasty. They know the stakes but not the specific requirement.

**What the player sees:** The faith has noticed your power. Level 4 is not a threshold you crossed silently — the covenant recognized it. Now it is demanding something in return. Every other dynasty on the map has just been told that you are being tested. What the test requires is yours alone to know.

**Effect:** The trigger dynasty's faith makes a demand appropriate to its doctrine path:
- **Sacrifice doctrines** demand a specific sacrifice act — a Bloodline Member sacrificed, or a specified number of enemy units offered at a faith structure.
- **Construction doctrines** demand a grand structure completed within the test window.
- **War doctrines** demand the capture of a specific named territory within the test window.

The test window is 20 minutes from notification.

**Success:** Faith intensity rises one full tier (capped at Apex). Apex mechanics for the dynasty's faith doctrine unlock permanently — these are the match's most powerful faith abilities, unavailable by any other path. A global notification fires that the dynasty has passed the Covenant.

**Failure:** Faith intensity drops one full tier. The Bloodline Member who triggered Level 4 enters a Doubt State — 20% stat reduction for 10 minutes.

**Counter-play:** Opposing players who receive the global notification can attempt to disrupt the test — attacking the dynasty to prevent completion of a war objective, destroying a structure required for the sacrifice, contesting the demanded territory. Disrupting another dynasty's Covenant Test earns +15 diplomatic prestige from the tested dynasty's rivals but costs -8 conviction — interfering with sacred obligations is morally questionable.

**Conviction interaction:** The specific act demanded by the Covenant Test carries its own conviction weight. The Test cannot waive these costs. Completing the Covenant faithfully means bearing the conviction consequences honestly.

**Frequency:** Once per dynasty per match. The Covenant fires exactly once — when Level 4 is first achieved. There is no second chance.

---

### [THE GREAT RECKONING]
*(Settled Canon — Full Specification)*

**Category:** World

**Trigger condition:** Any single dynasty controls 70% or more of the match's total territorial area simultaneously.

**Who receives it:** All players receive the Reckoning notification simultaneously. The Trueborn City issues a formal declaration.

**What the player sees:** A dynasty has taken too much. The world recognizes it. Every surviving dynasty receives the Coalition Mandate simultaneously. The Trueborn mobilize. The match has changed.

**Effect:** The hegemon dynasty is designated The Ascendant. All other surviving dynasties immediately receive the Coalition Mandate:
- +20% to all military unit stats
- +15% to resource generation
- A forced diplomatic floor — all non-hegemon dynasties are locked at Neutral or higher with each other. They cannot enter War state with each other for the duration of the Reckoning.

The Trueborn Summons fires automatically. The Reckoning ends when the hegemon drops below 60% territorial control. When Reckoning ends: all Coalition bonuses expire, the forced diplomatic floor lifts, and Coalition members return to their prior diplomatic states. If the hegemon is eliminated during Reckoning, a Victory Condition check fires — the match ends if 60%+ of surviving players vote for match conclusion.

**Counter-play:** The Ascendant dynasty's only path through the Reckoning is to eliminate Coalition members faster than the Coalition can coordinate. Reducing player count before Coalition coordination solidifies is the core strategic problem. The Reckoning is designed to be solvable but genuinely difficult — not a guaranteed game-ending event, but the defining challenge of the match's final phase.

**Conviction interaction:** The Reckoning does not change the Ascendant's conviction — it is a consequence of success, not a moral judgment. However, Coalition members who commit atrocities during Reckoning defense still suffer their normal conviction costs. The Reckoning does not morally license all behavior in the Coalition's name.

**Frequency:** Once per match globally. Once the Reckoning fires and the hegemon returns below 60%, it cannot fire again for the same dynasty.

---

### [THE TRUEBORN SUMMONS]

**Category:** World

**Trigger condition:** Fires in two scenarios: (1) automatically as part of The Great Reckoning when the 70% threshold is reached, or (2) independently when any player kills or captures a Trueborn Representative in Trueborn City territory.

**Who receives it:** All players receive the Summons notification. The triggering player receives a specific Judgment notification indicating they are the target.

**What the player sees:** The Trueborn City has mobilized its military reserve. A Trueborn Legion has spawned at the city and is moving toward the source of the transgression. It is not a threat. It is a fact.

**Effect:** The Trueborn Legion is a powerful, independent, non-controllable military force:
- Higher individual unit stats than most player armies at equivalent development stage
- No morale or supply chain vulnerability — they do not tire, revolt, or suffer siege attrition
- A specific target objective — they do not wander or attack bystanders
- If a unit from the Legion is eliminated, two regenerate over the next 5 minutes

The Legion can be neutralized by: (a) offering a tribute of 500 gold and 200 of each primary resource diplomatically to the Trueborn City; (b) returning a captured Trueborn Representative if that was the trigger; or (c) eliminating 80% of the Legion's total strength within 10 minutes without regeneration — this is extremely difficult and intended as a last resort. If the Trueborn Legion reaches the target player's territory and the player has not neutralized it, the Legion besieges the target's primary hold structure for 30 minutes. During this siege, the target player cannot receive new diplomatic agreements or trade from any party. If the siege completes and the hold falls, the match ends for the besieged player.

**Counter-play:** Allies of the targeted player cannot assist in neutralizing the Trueborn Legion without becoming secondary targets — Trueborn judgment is not a coalition problem, it is a specific reckoning. Rivals of the targeted player may intercept the Legion to slow it and give the target time to neutralize through tribute.

**Faith interaction:** If the targeted player's faith has Apex intensity (80%+), their faith community generates a Rallying Cry — +15% to the targeted player's military defense stats for the duration of the Legion's approach.

**Frequency:** Can fire twice in a match maximum — once as part of the Great Reckoning, and once for the assassination trigger. Subsequent attacks on Trueborn Representatives escalate the existing Legion rather than spawning a second one.

---

### [THE LONG WINTER]

**Category:** World

**Trigger condition:** Food resources across the entire map — aggregate total of all player stockpiles and resource nodes — drop below 40% of starting capacity simultaneously. This requires significant sustained warfare destroying food-producing territories. Cannot happen through normal play without deliberate large-scale agricultural destruction. Fires at the earliest in the Ideological Expansion stage.

**Who receives it:** All players receive the global notification simultaneously.

**What the player sees:** Someone has burned enough farmland, poisoned enough wells, and broken enough supply chains that the world is running out of food. Every dynasty feels it at once. The question now is who had the foresight to stockpile, and what everyone else is willing to do to survive.

**Effect:** A 20-minute famine period begins. During The Long Winter:
- Food generation from all sources drops an additional 25% globally
- Population growth halts universally
- Food consumption by military units doubles

Players who had been maintaining food surpluses are shielded. Players running food deficits before the Winter hits enter immediate Scarcity. Two additional mechanics activate:
- **Famine Diplomacy** — all trade proposals involving food receive a 50% receptivity bonus. Everyone wants food; everyone is willing to negotiate.
- **Survival Coalition pressure** — players whose food drops below 50 units during the Winter receive pressure from their population to seek alliance, generating a natural diplomatic convergence among the weakest players.

**Counter-play:** Players who anticipate the Long Winter can stockpile food before the trigger threshold. Players who have diversified into faith-based resource production are partially insulated. Agricultural players should be aware they are pulling the trigger on this event if they have been destroying farmland.

**Conviction interaction:** A player who deliberately destroys enemy food systems to trigger the Long Winter receives -15 conviction — weaponizing famine is among the game's cruelest recognizable actions. A player who offers food to struggling dynasties during the Winter receives +8 conviction per Emergency Trade completed.

**Faith interaction:** Faith doctrines that include resource stewardship generate a Faith Response to the Long Winter — affected players whose faith is Active or higher receive a Pastoral bonus: food generation from faith structures increases 30% during the Winter.

**Frequency:** Once per match globally. Agricultural recovery after the Winter prevents a second collapse.

---

### [THE GREAT EXHAUSTION]

**Category:** World — Late Match Escalation

**Trigger condition:** Fires at the late-match threshold — approximately 70-80% of estimated match duration. Represents the cumulative strain of decades of war on the game world's population base.

**Who receives it:** All players receive the global notification simultaneously.

**What the player sees:** The world is tired. Not the armies — the people behind the armies. The farmers who feed soldiers they never chose. The craftsmen replacing equipment they did not break. The populations who have lived under war flags for longer than anyone planned. The world's productivity begins to reflect the cost.

**Effect:** Permanent penalties applied globally that cannot be reversed through standard means:
- All armies lose a permanent -10% loyalty modifier (reversible only through specific late-game doctrine or faith intervention)
- Food production in all territories drops by 15%
- Gold income from trade drops by 20%
- Revolt risk in all held territories with any unresolved loyalty issue increases by 10%

The Great Exhaustion makes continued military stalemate progressively more expensive and forces dynasties to make choices about which fronts they can sustain.

**Frequency:** Once per match globally. Part of the late-game escalation sequence.

---

### [THE SUCCESSION CRISIS] *(Late-Match World Event)*

**Category:** World — Late Match Escalation

*Note: Distinct from the Dynastic Event of the same name. This is a global pressure event that fires for dynasties with unresolved succession lines at the late-match threshold. The Dynastic event fires when a specific heir dies. This event fires when succession has never been formalized.*

**Trigger condition:** Fires at a slightly delayed late-match threshold — after The Great Exhaustion, creating a one-two punch. Fires for any dynasty that has not formalized their succession line: no Bloodline Member heir designated with a functioning succession framework.

**Who receives it:** Each qualifying dynasty receives the event privately.

**What the player sees:** The lords and lesser houses within your territory have begun watching your bloodline's lines of succession and finding them unsatisfying. Nobody has been declared. Nobody knows who rules if the head of the bloodline falls tomorrow. Major factions within the dynasty's territories begin positioning around this uncertainty.

**Effect:** Loyalty events fire in core territories as lords hedge their commitments. If unresolved for fifteen match-minutes, a Succession Fragmentation event fires: two or more internal factions claim the throne simultaneously. The player must choose which faction to back — choosing one triggers hostility from the other, potentially creating an internal civil conflict.

**Frequency:** Once per dynasty per match. Part of the late-game escalation sequence targeting dynasties that have focused entirely on external expansion without managing internal dynastic continuity.

---

### [THE LEGITIMACY EROSION]

**Category:** World — Late Match Escalation

**Trigger condition:** Fires at the latest stage of match duration — approximately 85-90% of estimated match duration — if no dynasty has reached any victory threshold.

**Who receives it:** All players receive the global notification simultaneously.

**What the player sees:** The world can no longer sustain the present situation. Every legitimacy framework — military dominance, economic control, faith authority, dynastic prestige — has been contested long enough that the populations themselves are losing faith in the idea of a sovereign resolution. The ground beneath every dynasty's authority is shifting.

**Effect:**
- All loyalty tiers globally begin a slow downward drift — 1-2 points per match-minute, regardless of governance quality
- Faith intensity in all territories loses one tier over the course of ten match-minutes
- The Trueborn City, if not yet conquered and if already activated, doubles its military output — the old authority's urgency escalates
- All dynasties receive a global notification: the world cannot wait indefinitely for resolution

The Legitimacy Erosion creates a clock toward total governance collapse if the match is not resolved. It is the game's final forcing function. Players who do not find a resolution — through victory threshold completion or Alliance Victory negotiation — will eventually find their empires ungovernable regardless of skill or resource levels.

**Frequency:** Once per match globally. Final stage of the late-game escalation sequence.

## SPECIAL EVENTS — VICTORY PATH TRIGGERS

These events fire in direct relation to specific victory conditions. They are not standalone political events in the same sense as the categories above — they are the political expression of a dynasty approaching or crossing a specific victory threshold. Each one is publicly visible to all players. Each one announces that the match has entered a new phase and that all remaining dynasties must decide how to respond.

---

### [DIVINE RIGHT DECLARATION]
*(Faith Divine Right Victory Path)*

**What triggers this event:** A dynasty that has met all pre-declaration requirements (Apex faith intensity in core territories, High Synod endorsement at Reverential or above, Moral conviction firmly held, Grand Temple at Apex Tier in capital, Faith Council and Faith Academy operational) formally announces the pursuit of Divine Right Declaration to the High Synod. This announcement is publicly visible the moment it is made.

**What the player sees:** The world sees a dynasty declaring that their bloodline is descended from the divine. The Declaration Intent appears in the diplomatic interface for all players. From this moment, the dynasty is the explicit target of every rival who does not want a Divine Right victor.

**How the event unfolds:** The Declaration Clock begins from the moment the High Synod formally accepts the Declaration Intent. The clock is public. Every player sees it. The Declaration Clock is disrupted — reset — if a military attack reaches the capital or a major faith institution is destroyed. The coalition that forms post-announcement splits its efforts between military assault on faith infrastructure and diplomatic pressure on Synod relationships.

**Victory condition:** The Declaration Clock completes without interruption. Faith intensity remains at Apex in core territories, at Fervent or above in 70% of held territories. Moral conviction holds throughout. Population faith acceptance at 55% of own population at Devout or above. Two external faith ally kingdoms survive and maintain alliance at Declaration completion.

**Role in political events system:** The Declaration Intent announcement is the Faith path's analog to The Great Reckoning. It does not trigger the Reckoning coalition bonuses, but it accomplishes the same effect diplomatically — every dynasty that is not the declaring dynasty now has a shared interest in stopping the clock.

---

### [CURRENCY DOMINANCE THRESHOLD]
*(Economic Dominance Victory Path)*

**What triggers this event:** A dynasty pursuing the Currency Standard path reaches the Currency Adoption Marker — the moment when at least three other kingdoms are formally trading in the dynasty's currency as their accepted medium of exchange. This is a visible event in the diplomatic interface — all players see when the currency begins spreading.

**What the player sees:** The economic player receives a notification that the Currency Adoption Marker has been crossed and the final push to the victory threshold has begun. All other players see the adoption counter move in the diplomatic interface. This is the economic player's first major visibility spike.

**How the event unfolds:** From the currency adoption marker forward, opponents with economic sophistication attempt to break the adoption by destabilizing trade routes, spreading Cruel-conviction damage against the dynasty (reducing trust), or launching military campaigns that force the economic player to choose between Conviction maintenance and defense. The adoption threshold is 65%: killing two or three medium-sized partner kingdoms drops the count below viability and forces the economic player to find new adoption partners while the clock resets.

**Victory condition:** 65% of all active non-Trueborn kingdoms have formally adopted the dynasty's currency. Total trade volume through the dynasty's network exceeds 50% of map total trade activity. At least three kingdoms carry outstanding debt instruments to the dynasty's Banking House. All core territories at 70% stability or above. Conviction remains at Moral or Neutral throughout the sustained window.

---

### [ALLIANCE VICTORY FORMATION]
*(Alliance Victory Path)*

**What triggers this event:** Two or more dynasties formalize a Coalition Agreement through the Alliance Council interface — one agreed supreme ruler is named, all coalition partners formally subordinated in the agreement text. The formalization is broadcast publicly to all players and factions simultaneously. The Population Acceptance Clock begins immediately.

**What the player sees:** A public announcement that two or more dynasties have agreed to shared rule with a named supreme ruler. The Population Acceptance Clock is visible in the diplomatic interface to all players. From this moment, the military assault on both coalition members begins — every external bloodline now has a defined mutual interest in preventing the acceptance threshold from being reached.

**How the event unfolds:** The Population Acceptance Clock is not a timer that counts upward automatically. It is a contested score that grows through diplomatic missions, faith alignment events, economic stability, and formal recognition events — and falls through active revolts, military losses, coalition partner behavior violations, and competing diplomatic missions from external dynasties. If one coalition partner breaks the agreement before the acceptance threshold is reached, the Clock resets to zero and both dynasties receive an Alliance Betrayal consequence.

**Victory condition:** 65% of total living world population recognizes the agreed supreme ruler's authority. All coalition partners remain active and in compliance throughout the sustained window. The agreed supreme ruler is alive and in control of their capital. At least six distinct population groups have each individually crossed the recognition threshold. Coalition territory combined covers at least 45% of the total map.

---

### [TERRITORIAL GOVERNANCE RECOGNITION]
*(Territorial Governance Victory Path)*

**What triggers this event:** A dynasty pursuing the Territorial Governance path achieves Loyal loyalty tier in every held territory simultaneously for the first time. This is a visible milestone in the territory interface — all players who have active intelligence networks can see the dynasty's loyalty map.

**What the player sees:** Every corner of your empire, from the old core territories to the tribal lands you brought in last season, has stopped resisting you. They have not accepted you warmly — but they have accepted you. The loyalty map shows green across the board. The clock is running: you need to hold this for the full sustained window without any territory falling below threshold.

**How the event unfolds:** Late-game disruptions are maximum at this moment. Revolt generation from rivals targeting territories close to Loyal threshold but not yet secure. Assassination of governors removing administrative infrastructure. Faith provocation creating instability events that loyalty progress cannot survive. The governance player cannot afford any territory falling below threshold — and cannot be everywhere at once.

**Victory condition:** Every held territory at Loyal or Devoted loyalty tier — not 80%, not 90%. Every territory. No territory below 65% stability during the sustained window. Active Governor's House in every held territory throughout the window. No active revolt in any held territory during the window. No kingdoms at War state during the sustained window. Must hold at least 35% of total map territory.

---

### [DYNASTIC PRESTIGE GRAND WAR]
*(Dynastic Prestige Victory Path)*

**What triggers this event:** A dynasty pursuing Dynastic Prestige reaches a Prestige Tier threshold in the late match and the game engine identifies a Defining Moment opportunity — a high-stakes battle at a critical juncture that carries multiplied prestige weight. The dynasty must commit Bloodline Members to this engagement for maximum prestige generation.

**What the player sees:** The world has been watching this bloodline long enough to judge them. A Defining Moment has arrived — a battle that will be remembered. Multiple Bloodline Members on the field simultaneously is the maximum prestige generation scenario. It is also the maximum Bloodline extinction risk scenario. The risk is complete. The choice is yours.

**How the event unfolds:** A Defining Moment victory dominates the final prestige ledger. A loss strips prestige rather than just preventing gain. The best counter is to force the Prestige player into a no-win Defining Moment — an engagement large enough to demand their Bloodline Members' presence but designed to fail, so that presence generates maximum loss rather than maximum gain.

**Victory condition:** Total dynastic prestige score exceeds 10,000 points. At least seven of the nine founding houses have witnessed at least three prestige events from the dynasty. At least two living Bloodline Members active at time of prestige declaration. No Defining Moment loss during the sustained declaration period. At least three major dynastic monuments standing and undesecrated. Active heralds or ambassadors deployed in at least five foreign kingdoms.

## EVENT DESIGN PRINCIPLES

**Events are consequences, not punishment.** Every event in this system is the legible result of choices players made during the match. The Succession Crisis fires because the player did not designate a secondary heir. The Veteran Army Revolt fires because the player ran their army into the ground without food or mercy. The Long Winter fires because someone has been burning farms. Players who are paying attention can see every event coming before it arrives — and players who are not paying attention discover that the world has been keeping score. The design explicitly rejects the randomized disaster card model, where a player might lose progress through no fault of their own. In Bloodlines, every event has a reason, and every reason can be found somewhere in the match's history.

**Every event creates an actor and a reactor, and the reactor has real options.** A political event that only affects the receiving player and offers no response path is not an event — it is a penalty. Every event in this system was designed with counter-play as a first-class requirement. The Siege Attrition pressures the besieger and the besieged simultaneously, with each having meaningful options. The Alliance Betrayal gives the betrayed party a sympathy window and diplomatic momentum. The Refugee Crisis gives the receiving player three genuinely different paths with distinct downstream consequences. Reactive decision-making is strategy, not suffering.

**Conviction and faith are not score systems — they are pressure systems.** Events interact with the Three Pillars not to reward good players and punish bad players, but to create pressure that shapes behavior and opens or closes options. A player who has maintained Moral conviction for the entire match has access to options that a Cruel player simply does not have. A Cruel player is not losing — they have traded those options for different ones. The design goal is not to make Moral play obviously superior, but to make each conviction orientation coherent and internally consistent.

**Scale and timing are designed to prevent event flooding.** Events that can repeat have repeat restrictions calibrated to their impact. The Schism fires once per faith globally. The Covenant Test fires once per dynasty. The Great Reckoning fires once per match. Events that can fire repeatedly are calibrated to feel earned each time rather than mechanically automatic. In long matches (4+ hours with up to 10 players), the event system can generate dozens of events, but the distribution across all players and all categories ensures that no single player is overwhelmed while others experience nothing.

**Coalition-forming events are intentional diplomatic design.** The game benefits from moments where multiple players are pulled toward the same decision simultaneously. The Great Reckoning does this explicitly — all non-hegemons are pushed toward coalition. The Long Winter does it subtly — resource scarcity makes all weakened players interested in the same conversations. The Holy War Declaration does it through shared identity — faith allies respond without being asked. These coalition-forming events are the mechanism by which the game's political landscape shifts organically from competitive to cooperative and back.

**The world has memory, and events reflect that memory.** The Dynastic Rivalry event fires because two players have been fighting for 30 minutes without resolution — the game noticed. The Currency War fires because two players converged on the same minor tribe in the same window — the game noticed that too. The Conquered Territory Rising fires because a player held five territories for 30 minutes without governing them — the neglect was tracked. This is the core design philosophy of the entire event system: the match generates its own history, and the events are the history speaking back.
