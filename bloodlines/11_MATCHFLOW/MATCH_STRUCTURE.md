# Bloodlines — Match Structure

How a match begins, progresses, escalates, and resolves.

### Content Added 2026-03-15

## Match Phases

Bloodlines matches progress through four strategic stages. Two naming conventions exist in the archive (Level and Stage terminology). Both are preserved.

### Stage 1 — Founding (Level 1)
The player establishes their initial settlement, gathers resources, and begins population growth. At the end of this stage the player selects one of the four ancient faiths. The dynasty encounters tribes, ruins, and sacred sites during exploration.

### Stage 2 — Consolidation (Level 2)
The kingdom expands infrastructure and military capability while stabilizing territory. Building construction, population growth, and territorial loyalty become primary concerns.

### Stage 3 — Ideological Expansion (Level 3)
Faith systems begin influencing society and shaping political identity. Ideology increasingly influences society, conflict, and civilization identity. Dynastic politics and religious development intensify.

### Stage 4 — Irreversible Divergence (Level 4)
The civilization reaches its ideological apex and unlocks powerful late-game structures. This is the major irreversible divergence point (point of no return). Dynasties commit to strategic directions such as faith dominance or military supremacy.

**Level 5 — Apex (beyond the four stages):**
Grand endgame apex structure tier. Apex capabilities. Level 5 represents the culmination of the civilization's development.

## Victory Conditions

**Military Conquest** — Conquest with instability risk. Reckless warfare lowers morale, causes mutinies and defections. Military conquest is not simply "destroy everything."

**Economic / Currency Dominance** — Dynasty introduces own currency into the global economy. Economic victory occurs when the world economy becomes dependent on that dynasty's currency.

**Faith Victory / Divine Right** — Faith-based victory path through religious influence and dominance.

**Territorial Governance Attraction** — Smaller territories choose to join the dynasty willingly due to prosperity and infrastructure. Victory through attraction rather than conquest.

**Dynastic Prestige** — Prestige as victory path with bloodline members on battlefield in prestige dispute wars.

**Note:** Population loyalty is not favored as a standalone victory path.

## Pacing and Escalation

Early game focuses on expansion and survival. Mid game introduces dynastic politics and religious development. Late game introduces ideological wars and victory escalation.

Matches represent civilizational arcs, not just military escalation. A match is not just bigger armies over time. It is the evolution of a civilization's identity, faith, governance, and dynasty.

Late game events may include global coalitions against dominant dynasties via the Trueborn coalition response system.

## Short Match vs Long Match

Optional long matches run from 2 to 10+ hours. Large matches may extend well beyond ten hours in epic campaigns.

Long match mode supports extended strategic campaigns emphasizing territory development, dynastic expansion, faith influence, and long-term strategic planning.

The game's pacing, economy, dynasty systems, ideology systems, and population systems are meant to sustain long-term meaning and not peak too early.

## Multiplayer Considerations

Up to 10 players with AI kingdoms and minor tribes. Different strategic playstyles remain viable. The world functions as a living political landscape.

**Trueborn Coalition Response System:**
If the Trueborn city remains unconquered, it can intervene when one dynasty approaches victory too quickly. The city raises the Trueborn banner and recruits dynasties into a coalition against the emerging hegemon. Contribution history to the Trueborn city matters.

Detailed network rules, lobby systems, team structures, diplomacy toggles, and multiplayer matchmaking rules have not yet been fully defined.

### Player Manual Match Flow Expansion (Added 2026-03-15)

## Match Scale and Duration

Design target assumes extreme stress test games lasting six to ten hours that still feel coherent, balanced, and fair in the Command and Conquer sense. Map scale is roughly ten times the scale of typical RTS maps. Matches can be as short as two hours but are intentionally built for long sessions.

## Ten-Hour Match Rhythm

1. **Opening phase:** Securing survival, reading the world, laying foundations
2. **Early expansion phase:** First real commitments to economy, bloodline structure, scouting-led strategic identity
3. **Covenant decision phase:** Transforms exploration into spiritual direction
4. **Developing mid-game:** Reveals whether realm is structurally real or theatrically fragile
5. **Pre-specialization phase:** Multiple futures still possible but not indefinitely
6. **Level 4 divergence phase:** Broad contest becomes struggle among increasingly defined powers
7. **Late game:** Currency wars, divine claims, prestige crises, territorial attraction, demonic or holy manifestations, major hostage politics, Trueborn coalition response

The game changes genre texture as it progresses: begins as a severe large-scale RTS and gradually becomes something closer to a dynastic civilizational struggle.

## Early Game Philosophy

The early game establishes the identity of the dynasty, stability of the realm, tempo of expansion, and conditions under which later specialization choices will flourish or break down.

First phase simultaneous jobs:
1. Secure food and water (before almost everything else)
2. Establish housing at pace matching ambitions
3. Scout the world (not optional — faith choice depends on it)
4. Begin forming internal dynastic plan

Level 1 is not merely the first rung of a tech ladder. It is the phase in which the dynasty is still becoming itself in relation to the world.

## Mid-Game Transition

Begins when dynasty stops merely surviving and starts revealing its direction. Early decisions are tested for structural soundness. Bloodline management becomes visibly important. Player must stop thinking like conventional RTS player and start thinking like a ruler.

## Victory Condition Late-Game Escalation

Each victory condition has specific late-game escalation mechanics that reshape the world and serve as a climax. The game pushes toward late-game ideological escalation rather than simple bigger armies.

- **Economic:** Currency adoption forcing wars, timer for resolution
- **Faith:** Divine bloodline claim accepted by the world
- **Territorial:** Voluntary integration through governance attraction
- **Prestige:** Grand wars requiring 5-10 bloodline members per side to the death
- **Military:** Stability requirement, mutiny/defection risk for reckless conquest

## End-of-Match Rewards

XP earned even for non-winning placements. Being second, third, or fourth remains rewarding. XP contributes to progression system unlocking starting advantages, variations, cosmetics, and early bonuses for replayability.

## Recovery as Gameplay

Recovery from severe defeats is a real part of gameplay. Dynasties can bend without immediately breaking. After defeat: diagnose, stabilize (water, food, population, housing, morale, recruitment, faith, family tree), then consider revenge. A player who learns recovery will outperform a player who only knows how to snowball.

### Full Match Structure Expansion — Sessions 6-9 (Added 2026-03-19)

## The Sovereignty Seat — What Every Victory Path Is Pursuing

Every victory condition in Bloodlines is a different route to the same destination: recognition as the supreme authority over all lands. Not the largest dynasty. Not the most powerful military. The primary ruler — the bloodline (or agreed coalition with an agreed head) recognized by the world as the legitimate, supreme governing authority.

No bloodline wins by being second. No bloodline wins by being the most respected or the most feared without the world's recognition of ultimate authority. The sovereignty seat is the Iron Throne equivalent in mechanical terms — it represents the answer to the question every match is asking: which bloodline, or coalition with an agreed head, the world of this match will recognize as its ruler.

This framing governs why every victory path is extremely difficult. It is not difficult because the design wants to punish players. It is difficult because legitimate supreme authority is rare in the real world, and the systems are designed to reflect that reality. The game naturally trends toward stalemate, coalition formation, and political resolution rather than clean solo conquest. A match that ends in a single bloodline's absolute sovereignty is remarkable. A match that ends in an Alliance Victory or a Divine Right claim is equally remarkable. Both are correct endings.

## Six Victory Paths — Expanded

### 1. Military Conquest

The most direct path. Destroy enemy armies, capture enemy territory, eliminate enemy governance. When enough territory has been held with sufficient stability — not just captured, but governed — the conquest qualifies as completion.

The critical mechanic: reckless conquest causes Legitimacy Erosion. Territory held by force without governance infrastructure generates loyalty collapse events. Armies that fight without moral conviction begin experiencing attrition, mutiny, and defection. A dynasty that has captured 70% of the map but is governing it at 40% stability is not approaching Military Conquest — it is approaching internal collapse.

The path requires: sustained military capacity, governance development pacing military acquisition, sufficient conviction to prevent army deterioration, and enough diplomatic management to prevent coalition formation.

**Late-game escalation:** The Great Reckoning. When any dynasty holds 70%+ of map territory, all remaining dynasties receive a coalition bonus and the Trueborn mobilizes. The Reckoning is the structural immune response of the match to imminent military domination.

### 2. Economic Dominance — Currency Standard

Full specification in RESOURCE_SYSTEM.md. The dynasty replaces gold with its own currency as the world's economic standard, achieving the capacity to purchase loyalty and starve rival economies.

**Late-game escalation:** Resolution Battle trigger. When currency dominance threshold approaches, dynasties that refuse adoption may demand a military confrontation to resolve the question of whose economic standard governs the world.

### 3. Faith Divine Right

The dynasty formally declares that its bloodline descends from the covenant's highest divine power and has the right to rule all lands. The declaration initiates a spread window during which the claim must propagate through the world's faith population.

Full specification in FAITH_SYSTEM.md. The declaration is public from the moment it is made. All milestones are visible before and after declaration. The drama is the race, not the surprise.

**Late-game escalation:** Coalition formation against the declaring dynasty. Every dynasty whose faith covenant is different — and some whose faith is the same — have reasons to prevent one bloodline from claiming divine sovereignty.

### 4. Territorial Governance

Every province under the dynasty's control — including all tribal territories — must reach loyalty above 80%, infrastructure at settlement tier or higher, and active economic production. The population must have had time to develop organic attachment to the dynasty's rule.

This path requires the consent of the governed. It is not military occupation. It is development. Revolts are constant. Newly acquired territories resist. Tribal lands are the hardest — their populations have managed their own affairs for decades and do not transfer loyalty easily.

**Late-game escalation:** Voluntary integration events. At a threshold of development excellence, smaller territories begin approaching the dynasty unprovoked, seeking prosperity. This is both confirmation that the path is working and acceleration toward the victory threshold.

### 5. Dynastic Prestige

The dynasty accumulates sufficient prestige through bloodline member performance on the battlefield in formal prestige disputes. Grand Prestige Wars require approximately five to ten bloodline members per side on the battlefield, with outcomes determined by their performance and deaths. The prestige accumulated through these deaths — and the recognition of what was sacrificed — constitutes the dynasty's claim to supreme authority.

**Late-game escalation:** Grand Prestige Wars become the primary match conflict at late stage for dynasties pursuing this path. The death of a Legendary Bloodline Member in a Prestige War is a match-announced event with diplomatic and conviction consequences beyond the immediate prestige outcome.

### 6. Alliance Victory (Added — Seventh Ingestion)

Two or more bloodlines formally agree on terms of shared rulership with one agreed-upon ultimate ruler. The sovereign holder's identity, the coalition partners' obligations, and the territory arrangements must be ratified before the victory mechanism activates.

Once terms are ratified, sixty-five percent of the world's total population must come to accept the coalition's authority. This is not automatic. Active governance work is required: faith infrastructure deployment, economic investment, military presence. Revolt continues throughout the acceptance clock.

The internal dynamics of maintaining the coalition are a political game within the political game. The dynasty holding the sovereignty seat has the strongest incentive for the victory clock to proceed. Coalition partners may calculate that using the coalition to reach dominance and then defecting is more advantageous than completing the Alliance Victory. Managing this pressure while simultaneously managing the acceptance clock is the core challenge.

**Difficulty standard:** Alliance Victory is VERY VERY VERY difficult — the same standard as every other path. It is not a consolation path. It is a complex coordination problem under adversarial conditions.

## The Trueborn Rise Arc — Match Timing

The Trueborn City is not passive throughout the match. If no dynasty has claimed the city by the activation threshold — a point in match time calculated from match length and current territorial development — the Trueborn raise their banners and become an active NPC faction pursuing their own reconquest.

**Before activation threshold:** The Trueborn City functions as the neutral trade hub, information network, and diplomatic guarantee holder described in the lore. All dynasties have interest in maintaining access to the city.

**At activation threshold (if unclaimed):** The Trueborn rise arc activates. The Trueborn house declares their intent to reassert the ancient authority they held before The Great Frost. They begin actively expanding territory, seeking alliances with dynasties that have contributed to the city's stability, and opposing dynasties that have not.

**Why this matters:** A dynasty that has been ignoring the Trueborn City suddenly faces a powerful, historically legitimate rival with institutional gravity that no bloodline can simply dismiss. The Trueborn rise arc creates a forced pressure mechanic — the city cannot be ignored indefinitely, and the cost of ignoring it is facing the city as an adversary with the legitimacy advantage.

**Conquering the city:** Holding the Trueborn City is not a victory condition. It is a strategic objective that provides significant bonuses to whatever victory path the conquering dynasty is pursuing. The information network, trade amplification, and diplomatic influence of a conquered city are substantial. But conquest does not end the match — it accelerates the path toward ending it.

## AI Kingdoms and Minor Tribes

**AI Kingdoms:** Matches at 1-10 players can include AI-controlled dynasties that fill the remaining founding house slots. AI kingdoms pursue viable victory paths, respond to diplomatic overtures, form coalitions, and create the political landscape pressure that keeps the match interesting even at low player counts. AI kingdoms are not targets to farm — they are participants in the match's politics with genuine strategic goals.

**Minor Tribes:** Ten archetype types distributed across the map. Minor tribes are not founding houses but they are not passive either. They have territory, military capability, and diplomatic responses. A dynasty that mistreats nearby tribes faces armed resistance. A dynasty that invests in tribal relationships can form alliances that provide military support, resource access, or buffer zones against rival dynasties. The Trueborn house is classified as a special tribe (not a founding house) until the rise arc activates or they are selected as a player bloodline.

**Minor tribe conquest outcomes:** Four options — assimilation (population joins dynasty, territory integrated), tributary arrangement (tribe remains semi-autonomous but pays tribute), alliance (tribe maintains autonomy, provides military support when called), conquest and dissolution (tribe is eliminated, surviving population absorbed or exiled). Each outcome has conviction effects depending on how it is handled.

## Stage 1 — Founding: Detailed Match Design

Stage 1 is the period in which the dynasty is still becoming itself in relation to the world. Every resource decision in Stage 1 constrains Stage 2. Every bloodline member commitment made in Stage 1 shapes the dynasty's identity for the rest of the match.

**Priority sequencing:** Food and water before everything. Housing before military. Scouting before faith commitment. The player who skips any of these in favor of aggressive military expansion in Stage 1 is building an army on an unstable foundation.

**Faith selection timing:** At the end of Stage 1, the dynasty selects one of the four ancient faiths. This selection is informed by the scouting that happened during Stage 1 — what terrain is accessible (Sacred Ground nearby favors faith investment), what rival dynasties appear to be doing (a rival committing heavily to Blood Dominion early changes the calculus for committing to Old Light), and what victory path the player is gravitating toward.

**Starting leader significance:** The founding bloodline member who leads Stage 1 sets the dynasty's initial conviction trajectory. Their presence or absence in combat, their governance decisions, and their interactions with early events all generate conviction points before the player has full system literacy. Stage 1 ends with the dynasty having already begun to acquire a conviction character.

## Stage 2 — Consolidation: Detailed Match Design

Stage 2 is where structural soundness reveals itself. A dynasty that built Stage 1 correctly enters Stage 2 with a stable population, adequate resource flow, and a faith selection that aligns with the territory it occupies and the victory path it is leaning toward. A dynasty that overextended in Stage 1 enters Stage 2 with a military it cannot sustain and a realm that has more land than loyalty.

**The iron transition:** Stage 2 is when iron demand begins rising. The shift from spear-and-bow armies to swordsman and cavalry units marks the transition from Stage 2 early to Stage 2 late. Dynasties that secured iron node territory in Stage 1 have a compounding advantage here. Dynasties that did not face their first significant strategic decision: contest iron territory now (militarily costly) or find workarounds (limiting army modernization).

**Bloodline management becomes visible:** By mid-Stage 2, the bloodline has multiple active members with different attributes. Commitment roles become a real decision — which member goes to War commitment, which to Trade/Economic, which to Defense. The implications of these choices start manifesting in concrete outputs rather than in theoretical planning.

## Stage 3 — Ideological Expansion: Detailed Match Design

Stage 3 is the stage in which the game changes genre texture. It begins as an RTS and gradually becomes something closer to dynastic civilizational struggle. Faith mechanics move from early investment decisions to active gameplay systems. Bloodline family politics become match-level events. Other dynasties are now fully developed rivals with recognizable identities.

**Faith units become central:** Level 3 faith units (the first doctrine-specific military) become available at Stage 3. A dynasty that has maintained consistent faith investment enters Stage 3 with access to units that are qualitatively different from the Stage 1-2 roster. A dynasty that neglected faith investment finds its military options narrowing to the non-faith unit progression.

**Political event density rises:** More events fire in Stage 3 than in any other stage. Succession crises, diplomatic betrayals, faith conflicts, territorial disputes, and coalitions all accelerate in frequency as the match's political landscape becomes fully populated and all dynasties are in contact with each other.

## Stage 4 — Irreversible Divergence: Detailed Match Design

Stage 4 is the irreversible divergence point. Decisions made in Stage 4 cannot be walked back. Faith paths become fully committed. Conviction character has been established and is now compounding through pattern amplification. The victory path the dynasty has been developing becomes the only viable victory path for the rest of the match — pivoting to a different path at Stage 4 requires beginning that path's requirements from near-zero while Stage 4's pressure is fully active.

**Apex mechanics unlocked:** Level 4 and Level 5 faith units, Grand Sanctuary structures, apex military units, and the Covenant Test all become possible in Stage 4. The match reaches its highest-stakes period.

**The Great Reckoning, Covenant Test, Trueborn Rise:** These three world events can all potentially fire in Stage 4. If the Reckoning fires and the Covenant Test fires simultaneously for different dynasties, the match enters a period of maximum intensity with multiple world-scale mechanics running concurrently.

## World Events Reference

| Event | Trigger | Effect |
|-------|---------|--------|
| The Great Reckoning | Any dynasty holds 70%+ territory | All remaining dynasties receive coalition bonus; Trueborn mobilizes; coalition persists until hegemon drops to 60% |
| The Covenant Test | Any dynasty reaches Level 4 faith progression | Specific grand act demanded by the faith; failure costs faith intensity; success unlocks apex mechanics |
| Trueborn Rise Arc | Activation threshold reached without city conquest | Trueborn become active NPC faction pursuing reconquest |
| Dark Extremes Coalition | Extreme dark conviction + unbeliever sacrifice at scale | Irreversible population decline begins; other dynasties observe through Trueborn information network; coalition formation becomes world defense mechanism |
| The Succession Crisis | Dynastic health indicators drop while heir selection is inadequate | Window of institutional weakness; specific to timing — endgame succession crisis is match-altering |
| Trueborn Summons | Any dynasty kills or captures a representative at neutral ground | Automatic coalition summons against perpetrator; most serious diplomatic violation in game |

### Early Game Design Philosophy (Added 2026-03-26)

A large part of the early game should be battling or taking over as ruler many of the NPC tribes in the area. Conflict between houses should be extremely minimal for the early stages of the game.

Cheesing (blitz rushes, mass force buildups with abandonment of every other factor necessary to build a successful bloodline) should be actively discouraged by game design. The early game rewards dynasty-building, territorial consolidation against NPC tribes, and economic/faith foundation, not premature house-versus-house warfare.

## 2026-04-14 — Fortification and Siege Pacing (Defensive Fortification Doctrine Integration)

Source: `01_CANON/DEFENSIVE_FORTIFICATION_DOCTRINE.md` (locked 2026-04-14). This section integrates the doctrine into match flow without altering any prior canonical stage or phase lock.

The fortress-path realm and the offensive-path realm experience the five canonical stages (Founding, Expansion and Identity, Encounter/Establishment/Neutral City, War and Turning of Tides, Final Convergence) with distinct pacing inflections.

**Stage 1 (Founding).** Keep siting and initial defensive posture (outer palisade, stockade, watch tower) begin in the Founding stage. Class decisions implicit: where will the primary dynastic keep sit? Which border settlements warrant investment? Fortress-path realms commit early to stone-and-wood biased production. Offensive-path realms postpone fortification beyond basic walls.

**Stage 2 (Expansion and Identity).** The fortress-path decision becomes material. Regional strongholds begin development. Inner ring construction starts at the primary keep. Signal chain planning begins. Fortress-path realms accept slower map expansion; offensive-path realms push borders faster and carry fortification debt.

**Stage 3 (Encounter / Establishment / Neutral City).** Trade and diplomatic architecture weaves through fortifications. Signal chains link to Trueborn City trade routes for information flow. Reserve networks form between fortified settlements. Faith infrastructure migrates into keeps as covenant commitment deepens. Fortress-path realms may now coordinate multi-settlement defensive responses.

**Stage 4 (War and Turning of Tides).** Siege operations begin in earnest. Fortified realms face real sieges that take campaign-scale commitment to reduce. Unfortified realms face fast collapse under any serious military contest. The doctrine's core payoff happens here: the player who invested in fortification sees the attacker bleed on the outer works while reserves cycle, signal chains trigger relief, and covenant wards activate. The player who neglected fortification sees their realm reduce to retreat.

**Stage 5 (Final Convergence).** Apex fortifications are targeted by apex sieges. The final convergence of a match often plays out as a siege of a primary keep or fortress-citadel. Apex-tier siege engines, faith-apex breaching rites, coordinated multi-front timing, and coalition siege operations converge on the most developed defensive structures in the realm. The match may end on breach of a final core or on successful repulsion that forces a negotiated settlement.

**Declared time under siege (integration with Declared Time Model, canon 2026-04-07).** The declared in-world time for siege campaigns already canonically spans 3-5 years. The Fortification Doctrine anchors this: a serious siege against a developed fortress is a multi-year operation, even as the battle-clock plays out in tens of real minutes. The declaration narrative describes the siege as a campaign, not a skirmish.

**Directive integration (canon 2026-04-07).** The existing Siege directive category (Encircle and Starve, Assault When Ready, Siege and Negotiate) now operates against tiered fortification targets. "Assault When Ready" against an unfortified settlement produces fast resolution. Against a fortress-citadel it requires the full siege preparation set. Commanders executing Siege directives must respect the doctrine's preparation requirements or incur the wave-spam penalties.

**Attitude modifier interaction.** Aggressive, Relentless, and Opportunistic attitudes applied to a siege against a developed fortress produce higher attacker losses than the corresponding Measured, Cautious, or Disciplined modifiers. The doctrine reinforces the existing attitude system: careful preparation wins sieges; raw aggression burns armies against walls.

**Commitment phase decisions.** In the commitment phase between battles, a player allocating resources to fortification signals the fortress-path choice. That choice constrains other commitments: fewer field armies, slower expansion, commander time biased toward defensive governance. The canonical Bloodlines scope directive (no ceiling, depth is the goal) means both paths deepen over the course of a long match.

**No-skip guarantee.** Fortified realms cannot be skipped by offensive-path realms looking to rush victory. The doctrine explicitly denies wave-spam and suicide erosion as reliable structural collapse strategies. An offensive-path realm that ignores fortification must either invest in siege capability or accept that fortress realms are campaign obstacles, not speed bumps.

**Late-game stalemate tilt.** The canonical Victory Difficulty standard (all paths VERY difficult; solo victory VERY VERY VERY difficult; game trends toward stalemate / coalition) is strengthened by the Fortification Doctrine. Apex fortifications raise the cost of unilateral conquest. Coalition intervention is easier to form against an overextended hegemon whose apex fortresses have not yet fallen. This aligns with the Great Reckoning trigger (canon 2026-03-18) and Trueborn coalition mobilization.

**Fortification doctrine is canonical.** Match flow implementations must honor the doctrine without reducing its pillars.

## 2026-04-15 Session 82: Canonical Five-Stage Lock, Declared Time Model, and Multiplayer Engagement Doctrine

Source: `02_SESSION_INGESTIONS/SESSION_2026-04-15_match-structure-time-system-multiplayer-doctrine_RAW.md` (Canonical Prompt Insert v1.0, April 2026). This section is additive. Nothing above is removed.

### Five-Stage Structure (Locked)

The canonical match structure is now definitively five stages with condition-based advancement (no timer):

1. **Stage One: Founding.** Intimate, localized opening. Home seat, food/water security, basic borders, geography exploration, minor tribal diplomacy. Faith commitment should NOT be rushed during Stage One.
2. **Stage Two: Expansion and Identity.** Territorial expansion beyond founding perimeter. Faith covenant commitment arrives here. Economy compounds. First rival contact. Identity hardens.
3. **Stage Three: Encounter, Establishment, and the Neutral City.** First significant rival-dynasty military contact. Forward bases, trade across dynasty lines, Trueborn City as strategic variable, alliance/rivalry hardening.
4. **Stage Four: War and the Turning of Tides.** Sustained campaigns, siege operations central, faith divergence visible, Great Reckoning possible, Trueborn City fate decided, fortification doctrine payoff.
5. **Stage Five: Final Convergence.** Apex systems active, Divine Right windows, coalition resolution, final sovereignty determination.

Three overlay phases: Emergence (S1 through early S2), Commitment (late S2 through S4), Resolution (S5).

Stage transition gates are readiness conditions. No fixed timers. See the raw ingestion for the full condition list per stage.

**Naming note:** The older "four strategic stages" with Level 5 Apex in the sections above is superseded by this five-stage naming. Both are preserved for historical continuity.

**Faith commitment timing note:** The older "end of Stage 1" faith selection is superseded. Faith commitment now canonically belongs to Stage Two.

### Declared Dual-Clock Time Model (Locked)

Bloodlines uses two clocks joined by a declaration seam:

- **Clock One (Battle Clock):** real-time RTS combat. Major engagement: 20-40 real minutes.
- **Clock Two (Dynasty Clock):** in-world historical time. Aging, succession, operations, diplomacy. Does not tick at a fixed ratio.
- **Declaration Seam:** when battle ends, the game declares elapsed dynasty-clock time. Canonical ranges: 3-6 months (skirmish), 1-2 years (major battle), 3-5 years (sustained siege). Later stages produce larger declarations to support 2-3 visible generations.

Session rhythm: Battle, Declaration, Events Queue, Commitment Phase, Next Battle.

The 90-second realm cycle is the "internal processing heartbeat" running underneath this architecture. The dual-clock `inWorldDays` field already exists in runtime.

Time is mode-based temporal context, not a speed slider. Tactical, Declaration, and Strategic are layers of play, not speed settings.

### Live Strategic Layer (Locked)

The world remains active at zoomed-out scale. Armies move as icons. Date display is prominent. The strategic layer is a core play layer, not a pause menu.

Single-player pacing: normal, slow, pause-for-orders, auto-pause on major events.
Multiplayer: shared live calendar. See below.

### Multiplayer Time and Engagement Doctrine (Locked)

Shared match calendar. No individual player may pause or alter global speed.

Permitted pause forms (only these): unanimous, majority vote, host/admin in private lobby, emergency tokens (limited, non-chainable).

**Imminent Engagement Warning System:** when hostile engagement becomes inevitable, the defending player receives an Immediate alert with a short live countdown (10-30 seconds by battle type). The countdown provides a pre-battle command panel. The world does not pause. Player choices: enter, delegate, change posture, swap commander, commit reinforcements, protect bloodline members. Timer expiry starts battle under current conditions. Countdown is NOT a planning chamber.

Modifiers increase warning time: watchtowers, patrols, scouting, fortifications, loyalty, vigilant commander.
Modifiers decrease warning time: ambush terrain, fog, deception, low scouting.

Bloodline-priority elevations when significant dynasty members are at risk.
