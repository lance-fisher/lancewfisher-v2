# Bloodlines Master Doctrine Matchflow Integration - 2026-04-14

## Purpose

This addendum integrates the 2026-04-14 master doctrine into Bloodlines matchflow, pacing, and naval interpretation.

## Match Structure

The doctrine reaffirms the Bloodlines match as a civilizational arc rather than a simple escalation ladder.

This means the five-stage match structure must continue to support:

- realm maturation
- bloodline development
- water and food infrastructure growth
- fortification investment
- faith deepening
- late-game divergence
- exhaustion, recovery, and overextension

The state of the realm remains as important as the size of the army.

## Timing

The doctrine uses "determined time" wording, but the active settled mechanic remains the `declared time model` / `dual-clock architecture`.

Operational rule:

- preserve the current timing mechanic
- preserve the doctrine's wording in its source file
- do not split the project into competing time models

## Naval Strategy

The doctrine confirms that naval play is a complete strategic expression, not a supporting transport layer.

Naval posture must continue to matter for:

- blockade
- denial
- route control
- delayed intercontinental contact
- amphibious projection
- maritime defense
- coastal sustainment

Defensive maritime dynasties and offensive maritime dynasties remain equally canonical.

## Supply and Siege Pressure

The doctrine hardens the link between:

- supply pressure
- blockades
- siege duration
- negative upkeep
- desertion risk

This means matchflow cannot treat siege, blockade, and sustainment as side noise. They are pacing drivers and late-game resolution tools.

## Replayability

The doctrine confirms that replayability comes from systemic depth, different development arcs, and interacting pressures rather than from making the match structure smaller or flatter.

Future matchflow work should therefore preserve:

- fortress-path vs expansion-path pacing
- different continental contact timings
- recovery windows
- coalition pressure
- multiple viable doctrinal identities
