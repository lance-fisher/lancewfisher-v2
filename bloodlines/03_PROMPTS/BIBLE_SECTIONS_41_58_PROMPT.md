## PART VIII — MATCH STRUCTURE AND VICTORY (Sections 41-48)

**Section 41: The Four Match Stages**
Founding / Consolidation / Ideological Expansion / Irreversible Divergence.
For each stage: define what changes (resources, units, faith access, diplomacy options), what the player is building, what the typical threats are, what the strategic goals are. The Irreversible Divergence stage is the hardest — all units become faith-specific, no going back.

**Section 42: Match Scale, Pacing, and Recovery Mechanics**
2-10+ hour session design. 90-second game cycle. Up to 10 players + AI + minor tribes. Recovery mechanics: how dynasties survive disasters (famine, military defeat, succession crisis, Trueborn Summons). The exile state (hobbled but not eliminated). Late-game Great Exhaustion, Succession Crisis, Legitimacy Erosion pressure mechanics. The design intent: matches should trend toward negotiated resolution or Alliance Victory, not clean solo conquest.

**Section 43: Political Events System**
25+ named events across 6 categories: Faith, Dynastic, Economic, Military, Diplomatic, World. They fire based on game state conditions, not scripted sequence. The Covenant Test (Level 4 progression trigger). The Great Reckoning (70% territory hegemon trigger). Key events from CB002 (The Blood Moon, The Trade War, The Succession Scandal, etc.). Events as story generators.

**Section 44: Diplomatic System**
Five states: War, Hostile, Neutral, Cordial, Allied. Seven agreement types: Trade Agreement, Non-Aggression Pact, Military Alliance, Faith Alliance, Marriage Agreement, Tributary Arrangement, Trade Route Establishment. Alliance Betrayal event (moving Allied to War in one step — major conviction cost). War declaration process and recognized grounds. Trueborn City diplomatic guarantee. How conviction affects diplomatic reception.

**Section 45: Victory Conditions Overview**
All six paths to the Sovereignty Seat. The structural design philosophy: individual victory is designed to be rare. Why the game trends toward stalemate and coalition. The asymmetry between paths — each path resists in different ways. The design expectation that most matches resolve through Alliance Victory, Trueborn-pressure resolution, or prolonged stalemate, not clean solo conquest. Exile as a state. Match resolution mechanics.

**Section 46: Military Conquest and Territorial Governance**
Military Conquest: what it means (governing what you take, not just taking it), prerequisites, stage-by-stage progression, victory threshold (75% territory, 60% stability floor, held for scaled time window), counter-play options, The Great Reckoning response. Garrison math. The Cruel conquest variant.

Territorial Governance: near-impossible difficulty stated plainly. Must develop ALL owned territory including tribal lands. Constant revolts, loyalty crises, population resistance. What it means to actually achieve it (every corner of the land governed into willing acceptance).

**Section 47: Economic Dominance and Dynastic Prestige**
Economic/Currency Standard: getting the world to adopt the dynasty's currency, displacing gold. Threshold mechanics (purchase enemy loyalty, starve rivals through currency access denial, force economic dependency). Counter-play: resolution battle trigger. Requires Moral or Neutral conviction for trade network trust.

Dynastic Prestige: the bloodline as historical achievement. Accumulating prestige through bloodline depth, political marriages, famous battles, faith alignment, and the legacy of notable Bloodline Members. The escalation mechanic: grand wars requiring ~5-10 Bloodline Members per side to the death. Prestige as a public resource tracked globally.

**Section 48: Faith Divine Right and Alliance Victory**
Faith Divine Right: declaration mechanic (formal Declaration of Divine Right spreads across the world over a defined time window). All milestone requirements and the declaration clock visible to all players from moment of declaration. Pre-declaration requirements publicly known. Coalition response during spread window. Success/failure conditions.

Alliance Victory (sixth path): two or more bloodlines agree on shared rulership with one ultimate ruler. Population acceptance clock: 65% of world population must accept the coalition's authority. Contested — revolts continue, coalition partners have incentives to defect. VERY VERY VERY difficult. The design outlet for matches where solo dominance is impossible.

Late-Game Escalation: The Great Exhaustion, The Succession Crisis, The Legitimacy Erosion. Why they exist. What they do. The Trueborn rise arc as the ultimate late-game pressure (in Section 7, reference back to it here in context of match endgame).

## PART IX — THE NINE FOUNDING HOUSES (Sections 49-57)

Each of the nine founding houses gets a dedicated section. These are NOT the quick-reference profiles in Section 6 — those are overview summaries. These sections are the full treatment: lore, mechanical identity, rivalry context, bloodline member archetypes, strategic narrative, and what it feels like to play this house or face it.

Structure for each house section:

**[HOUSE NAME]**
- Historical Identity (Pre-Frost / Frost / Post-Frost — full paragraphs, not bullet points)
- Identity Statement (the design specification sentence)
- Mechanical Identity (advantage + disadvantage + unit values + unique unit + squad trait)
- Strategic Narrative (early/mid/late game as a narrative arc, not a bullet list)
- Victory Path Affinities (strongest and weakest paths, and why)
- Conviction Axis (what Moral and Cruel playthroughs look like for this house)
- Key Rivalries and Relationships (who they distrust, who they naturally align with, and why)
- Legendary Member Archetype (one iconic character type this house produces — what makes them legendary, how other dynasties react to them, what their eventual fate tends to be)
- Flavor Paragraph (rich sensory prose about what this house feels like — smell, bearing, psychology)

**Section 49: House Trueborn**
The house that was once the world and is trying to remember how to be a player again. Silver hair. Diplomatic broker. The Mediator. The house everyone tolerates and nobody fully trusts.

**Section 50: House Highborne**
The house that believes it was chosen before the war began. Darker gold hair. Faith anchor. The Cantor. The house that fights with belief when it fights at all.

**Section 51: House Ironmark**
"Ironmark reaches power first and pays for it in blood." Charcoal Iron + Ember Red. The Axe with five variants. Ferocity Under Loss squad trait. The house that smells like forge-smoke.

**Section 52: House Goldgrave**
The house whose wars are fought on ledgers before they're fought on fields. Bright gold hair. Economic powerhouse. The Coin Sergeant. The house whose bright gold is a brand.

**Section 53: House Stonehelm**
The house that wins sieges — the ones it fights and the ones it never has to. Brown hair. Defensive fortress. The Rampart Spear. The house that was building walls when other houses were still naming themselves.

**Section 54: House Westland**
The house from the frontier where law was three days' ride away. Vivid red hair. Military aggressor. The Outrider. The house whose lords want to be seen across the battlefield.

**Section 55: House Hartvale**
The house that nearly died and built an obsession with life out of the trauma. Orange hair. Population engine. The Verdant Warden or Hearthmaster. The house that counts every person.

**Section 56: House Whitehall**
The house other houses underestimate and then study. Neutral brown hair. The Liaison Officer. The house that reads the situation and becomes what it needs to be.

**Section 57: House Oldcrest**
The house that has been there the longest and remembers everything. Black/gray/pepper hair. The Oathkeeper. The house whose age is a weapon.

## PART X — CLOSING (Section 58)

**Section 58: Design Philosophy — What Bloodlines Must Always Be**

The closing manifesto. Not a summary — a statement of identity. What this game is for. What it must always feel like. The four pillars as existential questions. The scope directive: no ceiling, deeper is better, the risk is building too little. The tonal standard: not a game for children, not cleaned up, genuine consequence. The narrative generation goal: every match should produce a story worth retelling. What it means for a player to finish a Bloodlines match and describe it as a story rather than a score. Why the dynasty lives longer than the game session.
