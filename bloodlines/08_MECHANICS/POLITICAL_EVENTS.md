# Bloodlines — Political Events System

Events are triggered by game state conditions, not scripted timelines. Every event in this system is the world responding to what is actually happening — economically, militarily, religiously, diplomatically. Events are visible to the players they affect and may be partially visible to others depending on the event type and the Whitehall espionage mechanics.

### Added 2026-03-18, Fifth Session

---

## Design Philosophy

Events serve three functions:

**Narrative punctuation:** They break the continuous flow of resource management and military planning with moments that require attention and decision. They create the feeling that the world is alive and responding.

**Strategic disruption:** Predictable games favor the strongest economy. Events introduce disruption that forces adaptive play and can reverse momentum in ways that raw military and economic power cannot.

**Moral testing:** Many events present a clear choice between a moral and a pragmatic response. These choices accumulate as conviction record. A player's response to events over time is one of the primary drivers of their conviction posture.

---

## Event Categories

Events are organized into six categories. Each category has a different trigger logic and a different relationship to game systems.

---

## Category 1 — Faith Events

These events trigger when faith system conditions reach thresholds. They involve the spiritual state of the world.

---

### THE SCHISM

**Trigger:** Two or more dynasties follow the same covenant but have adopted opposing doctrine paths (one Light, one Dark) and have both reached moderate-to-high faith intensity.

**What happens:** The faith splits visibly. The two expressions of the same covenant are now in active doctrinal conflict. Both dynasties take a temporary faith intensity penalty as their populations process the conflict between what they believe and what they see the other claiming. Populations globally with that covenant begin to choose — populations in neutral or minor territories begin drifting toward one of the two doctrinal expressions.

**Player choices:** Intensify their own doctrine expression (double down on Light or Dark) to attract followers of that expression, or reach out diplomatically to the rival dynasty to negotiate a doctrinal coexistence framework (temporary faith intensity stabilization).

**If unresolved:** The Schism escalates to a Holy War Declaration eventually. The world cannot sustain two incompatible versions of the same covenant indefinitely.

---

### THE CALLING

**Trigger:** Any dynasty reaches 80% or higher faith intensity sustained for a full game cycle.

**What happens:** The covenant's influence radiates beyond the dynasty's borders. Surrounding neutral populations feel drawn toward that faith. Minor tribal communities in adjacent unclaimed territories may declare for that covenant unprompted. Populations in neighboring dynasties with low faith intensity begin reporting spiritual restlessness.

**Other dynasties' experience:** Dynasties adjacent to a dynasty experiencing The Calling receive a notification that their border populations are experiencing unusual religious interest. They may increase their own faith investment to counter it, attempt diplomacy to limit its spread, or use covert operations to disrupt the source dynasty's faith infrastructure.

**Duration:** Ongoing as long as the source dynasty maintains 80%+ intensity.

---

### THE HERESY

**Trigger:** A dynasty following Light Doctrine commits an extreme cruel action (conviction shift of -8 or more from a single action) while at moderate-to-high faith intensity.

**What happens:** A segment of the dynasty's own population recognizes the contradiction between what is professed and what is done. A Heresy Movement forms — an internal faith resistance group that believes the ruling dynasty has betrayed the covenant and must be replaced or reformed.

**Mechanical effects:** The Heresy Movement reduces faith intensity gradually from within. Covert operations from other dynasties can amplify or exploit the Heresy Movement. The dynasty must choose: suppress the movement (costly in conviction and resources, temporary solution), reform publicly (costly in capability, permanent resolution), or ignore it (the movement grows until it triggers a population revolt or territorial fracture).

**Severity:** Scales with how extreme the triggering action was and how high the faith intensity was when it occurred. A minor heresy can be resolved quickly. A major heresy at peak faith intensity is a civilizational crisis.

---

### THE HOLY WAR DECLARATION

**Trigger:** One covenant reaches 60% or more of global population share, and at least one dynasty of that covenant has reached Level 4 progression.

**What happens:** The dominant faith's most advanced Bloodline leader may declare a Holy War — a formal call to faith that transforms the conflict from dynastic competition to religious obligation. All dynasties following the declaring faith receive a morale bonus. All dynasties following opposing faiths receive a morale penalty (they know what is coming). Neutral populations begin choosing sides faster.

**Special property:** A Holy War declaration changes the diplomatic landscape fundamentally. Alliances between dynasties of different faiths are strained — populations on both sides understand that their rulers are now formally at odds with allied rulers at a level beyond politics.

**Defensive mechanic:** Other dynasties can respond to a Holy War Declaration with a Counter-Covenant — formally unifying around a competing faith to resist the dominant covenant. If two or more other covenants form a Counter-Covenant, the Holy War's morale bonus is negated and replaced with an escalating global conflict.

---

### THE COVENANT TEST

**Trigger:** Any dynasty reaches Level 4 progression in their faith development.

**What happens:** The covenant presents a Grand Test — a specific, demanding act of faith commitment that is unique to the doctrine path. The test is not optional — the covenant will not advance the dynasty to apex faith mechanics without it. The test is also not private — other dynasties know a Covenant Test is underway, though not necessarily the specifics.

**Old Light Light Doctrine Test:** Protect a population currently under siege or threat, at military cost to the protecting dynasty.

**Old Light Dark Doctrine Test:** Purge a specific faith heresy within your own population — locate and suppress a heterodox group — within a time limit.

**Blood Dominion Dark Doctrine Test:** Conduct the Great Offering — a mass ritual sacrifice within a Grand Sanctuary, consuming significant population and resources.

**Blood Dominion Light Doctrine Test:** Complete three Covenant Priest rituals in a single Level of play without combat losses during those rituals.

**The Order Light Doctrine Test:** Establish and enforce a legal code across at least three territories simultaneously — a formal governance declaration.

**The Order Dark Doctrine Test:** Subject an enemy dynasty to total institutional submission — capture their primary governance structure and hold it for a sustained period.

**The Wild Light Doctrine Test:** Establish a Grove network spanning at least three territories — connected Wild sacred sites that form a living network.

**The Wild Dark Doctrine Test:** Release the Wildfire — deliberately trigger a natural disaster or mass animal event in enemy territory without covert operations cover.

**Failure consequences:** Failed Covenant Tests reduce faith intensity significantly and lock the dynasty out of apex faith mechanics for one full Level period before they can re-attempt.

## Category 2 — Dynastic Events

These events trigger within the dynasty itself — within the bloodline, the family structure, the personal history.

---

### SUCCESSION CRISIS

**Trigger:** The head of a dynasty's Bloodline dies without a mature heir (under adult age threshold) or with multiple heirs of equal claim.

**What happens:** The dynasty enters a formal succession contest. Population loyalty drops in all territories as the political future is uncertain. Other dynasties may attempt marriage offers, diplomatic positioning, or military aggression during the window of instability. The succession must be resolved — by the player choosing an heir and consolidating authority — before full military and economic efficiency returns.

**Severity scale:**
- Minor: One heir, slightly young — brief instability, moderate loyalty drop
- Moderate: Two equal heirs — factional split risk within the dynasty; covert operations can amplify it
- Major: No biological heirs — requires adopting a successor, which carries legitimacy questions that persist for the rest of the match
- Catastrophic: The head dies in battle simultaneously with multiple heirs — political near-collapse requiring immediate and decisive action

**Other dynasties' options:** Marriage proposals during a succession crisis carry unusual weight — the offering dynasty gains both a diplomatic relationship and a stake in the successor's loyalty.

---

### THE CHAMPION EMERGES

**Trigger:** A non-bloodline unit survives five major engagements, each against a significantly larger force.

**What happens:** The unit's history becomes known. Their name spreads. The population knows who they are. The dynasty receives a prompt to recognize the Champion formally.

**Recognition options:**
- Grant land and title: The Champion forms a new Lesser House under the dynasty's banner. They become a permanent political entity within the dynasty's structure.
- Appoint as General: The Champion leads armies with significant battlefield bonuses. They do not become a Bloodline Member but gain equivalent battlefield presence.
- Retire with honor: Full conviction bonus (Moral). The Champion becomes a civilian figure whose reputation continues to generate population loyalty.
- Ignore: Minor conviction penalty. The Champion's legend continues without the dynasty's endorsement, which can lead to an Independent Champion event.

**Independent Champion:** If the dynasty ignores a Champion, that Champion may be recruited by another dynasty through marriage or diplomacy — creating a rival force with institutional knowledge of the original dynasty's military tactics.

---

### THE ELDER'S COUNSEL

**Trigger:** Any Bloodline Member reaches elder status (70+ years of in-game age) having survived at least three major military engagements.

**What happens:** The elder calls a formal counsel — a private meeting with the player that functions as an intelligence and strategy event. Based on the elder's lifetime of accumulated experience, the player receives one of the following (varies by the elder's specialization and life history):

- Intelligence on one opponent's military composition
- Economic assessment revealing an opponent's resource vulnerability
- A diplomatic insight — the elder's read on whether a neighboring dynasty's alliance offer is genuine
- A military recommendation — the elder identifies a tactical opening the player may not have seen

**Conviction interaction:** How the player treats the elder's counsel affects conviction. Dismissing an elder's counsel (ignoring the information entirely) is a minor cruel action. Acting on it and acknowledging the debt publicly is a minor moral action.

---

### THE PRODIGY

**Trigger:** Random rare event on any Bloodline Member birth. Probability increases with Moral conviction (parents' environment encourages exceptional development).

**What happens:** A child is born with exceptional natural aptitude in one specialization:

- Military Prodigy: Develops combat capability 50% faster than normal; reaches the maximum specialization tier earlier in their life
- Diplomatic Prodigy: Produces faith and diplomatic effects at twice the normal rate for their role
- Economic Prodigy: Generates resource bonuses in any territory they govern
- Faith Prodigy: Faith intensity increases faster in any territory where they hold a religious role
- Intellectual Prodigy: Speeds the research and development of all faith and building upgrades dynasty-wide

**Design note:** A Prodigy is not guaranteed to become a great Bloodline Member — they have exceptional potential, but that potential requires proper investment in their training and proper assignment. A Military Prodigy left in an administrative role develops administrative skills, not military ones.

---

### THE BLOOD BETRAYAL

**Trigger:** A Bloodline Member is captured and held for more than a full game cycle without ransom.

**What happens:** The captured member's continued captivity creates a wound in the dynasty. Their home territory — the one they governed or were associated with — suffers a loyalty collapse. Other Bloodline Members' morale is reduced. Recruitment in the affected territory slows.

**Resolution options:**
- Pay the ransom: The member returns. Loyalty recovers over time. A ransom paid quickly (within the first cycle) carries no conviction implications.
- Rescue by force: High-risk covert operation. If successful, major morale bonus to entire dynasty and significant conviction boost. If it fails, the captured member is executed.
- Leave them: The dynasty's population eventually accepts the loss and moves on, but the Bloodline Member's home territory's loyalty never fully recovers while the dynasty rules under the same head.
- Conduct a trade: Exchange a captured enemy Bloodline Member for your own. The cleanest resolution diplomatically, but requires having an enemy member to trade.

## Category 3 — Economic Events

Events triggered by resource system conditions and economic states.

---

### THE IRON DROUGHT

**Trigger:** A dynasty's iron production drops below minimum army sustenance levels for two consecutive game cycles, or a dynasty's iron territory is entirely captured.

**What happens:** Army modernization halts. No new iron-consuming units can be recruited. Existing iron-equipped units gradually lose equipment condition, reducing their combat effectiveness over time. Iron-holding territories within reach become immediately contested — other dynasties receive intelligence that someone is iron-poor.

**Recovery:** Retake iron territory, establish a trade route for iron with a willing ally, or disband iron-consuming units to reduce iron demand (Born of Sacrifice cycle — the recycled troops are a resource response, not just a tactical one).

**Strategic note:** The Iron Drought is often more valuable as a threat than as an actual event — the credible ability to deny iron to an enemy can be more powerful than the denial itself.

---

### THE CURRENCY CONTEST

**Trigger:** Two dynasties are both pursuing the Economic Victory path and both reach 40% or higher currency adoption.

**What happens:** The world economy cannot sustain two competing currency standards at high adoption simultaneously. A formal Currency Contest is declared — a time-limited period during which both dynasties actively compete for the final global adoption threshold. If one dynasty reaches 60% within the contest window, the other is forced to retreat to gold standard.

**Military escalation mechanic:** If the Currency Contest is unresolved at the end of the window, it triggers a Currency Resolution War — a formal military conflict where the outcome determines economic dominance. Other dynasties may side with either competitor or attempt to exploit the conflict to advance their own position.

---

### THE ABUNDANT HARVEST

**Trigger:** A dynasty's food production exceeds population needs by 50% or more for three consecutive game cycles.

**What happens:** A genuine food surplus generates. Population growth accelerates beyond its normal rate. A recruitment window opens — extra population is available for military service at reduced conscription cost during the window. Trade partners offer better terms for food.

**Optional action:** The dynasty may choose to share the surplus with a neighboring dynasty or with the Trueborn City. Sharing with a neighbor is a diplomatic gesture with relationship consequences. Sharing with the Trueborn City generates significant moral conviction and Trueborn trust.

---

### THE GREAT FAMINE

**Trigger:** Food production falls below population minimum for two consecutive game cycles.

**What happens:** Population decline begins. Faith intensity drops as spiritual engagement falls to survival priority. Conviction shifts slightly toward Cruel if no player action is taken (the population perceives inaction as indifference). Military morale drops across all units.

**Response options:**
- Emergency rationing: Reduces population decline rate but imposes happiness penalty
- Emergency recruitment freeze: No new soldiers; all available food prioritized for civilians
- Trade for food: Diplomatic option requiring a willing partner and gold
- Allow decline: Conviction cost; eventual population stabilization at lower level
- Take food by force from a neighbor: Major conviction cost, starts a war, solves the food problem

**Conviction interaction:** How the dynasty responds to famine is one of the most visible conviction moments in the game. The population is watching.

---

### THE MARKET COLLAPSE

**Trigger:** A dynasty's gold income drops below operating costs for one full game cycle while having outstanding diplomatic commitments that require gold payment.

**What happens:** The dynasty defaults on its diplomatic commitments. Alliance agreements become strained or broken. Mercenary contracts (if active) are void. The dynasty's reputation for financial reliability is damaged — future trade negotiations are less favorable.

**Recovery:** Restore gold production, sell assets through the Trueborn City market, or renegotiate diplomatic commitments through Whitehall mediators.

---

## Category 4 — Military Events

Events triggered by battlefield conditions and army states.

---

### THE ROUT

**Trigger:** An army is destroyed with 90% or higher casualties in a single engagement.

**What happens:** The survivors, if any, carry a Rout status. Their morale is severely reduced and recovers at half the normal rate. More significantly, the news of the rout spreads — enemy dynasties learn of the defeat, and the losing dynasty's military reputation takes a temporary penalty affecting recruitment willingness.

**Recovery:** A Bloodline Member must formally address the survivors and the population — a gameplay event where the player chooses the framing (inspire with vengeance, honor the fallen, pledge to do better). The choice has minor conviction implications and determines how quickly morale recovers.

---

### THE STAND

**Trigger:** An army holding a defensive position successfully repels an attacking force with at least twice its numbers.

**What happens:** The defending units immediately gain Veteran status (if they don't already have it). The dynasty's military reputation receives a significant positive signal — populations in adjacent territories take notice. Conviction shifts slightly toward Moral (defense against overwhelming odds resonates morally with the population).

**Named event:** The battle is named. The player may name it, or the game assigns a name based on the location. Named battles accumulate in the dynasty's history log and become part of the Prestige Dynastic Victory calculation.

---

### THE DESERTION WAVE

**Trigger:** Dynasty conviction crosses into Cruel threshold while an army is on extended campaign (away from home territory for two or more game cycles).

**What happens:** A percentage of active troops desert during the night phase. The percentage scales with how deeply into Cruel conviction the dynasty is. Troops closer to home territory desert at lower rates than troops on extended campaign. Units led by Bloodline Members desert at lower rates than units with appointed generals.

**Prevention:** Bloodline Member presence, morale investment, shorter campaigns, regular rotation of troops back to home territory.

**Escalation:** A single Desertion Wave is survivable. Repeated Desertion Waves strip armies bare. A dynasty that reaches extreme Cruel conviction and maintains extended campaigns simultaneously is militarily destabilizing itself.

---

### THE SIEGE BREAKS

**Trigger:** A dynasty successfully defends a territory against a siege that lasted three or more game cycles.

**What happens:** The defending dynasty's population in that territory gains a permanent loyalty bonus — they held, and holding means something. The territory's faith intensity increases (hardship deepens faith). The defending units gain accelerated experience from the sustained defense.

**Other dynasties' awareness:** The successful defense is known. The besieging dynasty's reputation for siege capability takes a temporary penalty. Dynasties considering allying with the besieging house reconsider the alliance value.

---

### THE CHAMPION'S DUEL

**Trigger:** Two Bloodline Members from opposing dynasties are both present on the same battlefield.

**What happens:** The game offers both players the option to call a formal Champion's Duel — a one-on-one engagement between the two Bloodline Members that determines the battle outcome without full engagement. Both players must agree. If only one agrees, the duel does not occur (but the agreement offer is visible and refusing a duel is a minor reputation event).

**Duel outcome:** Determined by the Bloodline Members' accumulated experience, specialization, and current health. A Duel is not purely randomized — a Military-specialized Bloodline Member at full health against a Faith-specialized Bloodline Member will win with high probability. But upsets are possible.

**Duel stakes:** The losing Bloodline Member is captured (not killed, unless the winner's dynasty has specific conviction and faith mechanics that mandate execution). The winning side's army receives a massive morale bonus for the remainder of the engagement.

---

## Category 5 — Diplomatic Events

Events triggered by the relationships between dynasties.

---

### THE MARRIAGE PROPOSAL

**Trigger:** Any dynasty reaches Level 2 progression and has a marriageable Bloodline Member.

**What happens:** Other dynasties may send formal marriage proposals. The player receives the proposal with available information about the proposing dynasty and the proposed arrangement.

**Marriage outcomes:**
- Full alliance: Both dynasties share intelligence and cannot declare war on each other without a formal Betrayal (major conviction cost)
- Trade alliance: Favorable trade terms exchanged; military independence maintained
- Political arrangement: Symbolic alliance with no mechanical binding
- Rejection: No mechanical penalty if declined politely; diplomatic damage if declined insultingly

**Cross-faith marriages:** A marriage between dynasties of different faiths creates a mixed household. The child of such a marriage may follow either parent's faith, have an unusual relationship with both, or in rare cases develop a personal faith expression that does not fit either parent's covenant neatly.

---

### THE TRUEBORN SUMMONS

**Trigger:** Any dynasty approaches military hegemony — holding more than 55% of total map territory or having eliminated three or more other dynasties.

**What happens:** The Trueborn City sends a formal summons to all remaining dynasties. The Trueborn Coalition window opens. Dynasties may join the coalition voluntarily. The hegemon receives a formal notification that a coalition is forming against them.

**Coalition mechanics:** Dynasties that join the coalition share military intelligence and receive a small morale bonus against the hegemon specifically. The coalition does not merge the dynasties — they remain independent actors with their own victory conditions. The coalition is focused specifically on reducing the hegemon's territory below the hegemony threshold.

**Hegemon response options:**
- Accept limitation: Voluntarily reduce military presence in contested territories; the coalition dissolves
- Negotiate with individual coalition members: Break the coalition through diplomacy, trade, or marriage
- Fight the coalition: Win decisively or face eventual attrition against multiple opponents
- Accelerate victory: Push harder and faster before the coalition can fully coordinate

---

### THE ALLIANCE BETRAYAL

**Trigger:** A dynasty declares war on a dynasty it has a formal alliance with.

**What happens:** Major conviction penalty (Cruel shift). The betraying dynasty's diplomatic relationships with every other dynasty are damaged — they have demonstrated they will break formal agreements when convenient. The betrayed dynasty's population receives a significant loyalty boost (outrage at the betrayal unifies them). The Trueborn City records the betrayal and the betraying dynasty's trust with the City drops significantly.

**Mechanical note:** This event exists to make the formal alliance meaningful. Without a cost to betrayal, alliances are just words. With a significant cost, alliances become genuine commitments.

---

### THE NEUTRAL ARBITRATION

**Trigger:** Two dynasties are in sustained conflict (three or more game cycles of continuous war) and at least one of them has built up sufficient Trueborn City trust.

**What happens:** The Trueborn City offers to arbitrate. Both dynasties may agree or decline. If both agree, the arbitration produces a formal peace framework with specific terms (territory, resources, prisoner exchange). Both dynasties receive a conviction bonus for accepting arbitration (the willingness to pursue peace through legitimate means is morally coded).

**If one declines:** The declining dynasty loses Trueborn trust. The accepting dynasty gains trust. This asymmetry creates pressure toward arbitration even when the military situation might favor continuing the conflict.

## Category 6 — World Events

Events that affect the entire game world, triggered by macro-level conditions.

---

### THE FROST ECHO

**Trigger:** Late game (Level 4 or higher), any dynasty occupies Tundra territory during a winter cycle with low overall world development index.

**What happens:** A miniature climate event. Frost conditions temporarily extend south from Tundra into adjacent Badlands and occasionally into Stone Highlands territories. This is not the Great Frost returning — it is a weather echo, lasting one game cycle. During the Frost Echo:

- Frost Ruin territories in the affected zone become accessible with unique resource caches (preserved goods surviving from the Age Before, not yet thawed)
- Armies in the affected zone take attrition damage
- Population in affected territories loses happiness
- The Wild faith units are unaffected by the Echo attrition

**World event visibility:** All dynasties are aware a Frost Echo is occurring. It is a shared experience. The Trueborn City makes a formal statement about it.

---

### THE OLD LIGHT RETURNS

**Trigger:** A Trueborn dynasty emerges (if Trueborn is a player-controlled dynasty that has reached Level 3 and publicly revealed their identity claim).

**What happens:** A signal event. All faith intensities in the world rise slightly — the Trueborn re-emergence resonates with every covenant's ancient memory. The Trueborn City's council convenes a special session. Other dynasties must formally respond: acknowledge the claim, challenge it, or remain silent. Silence has diplomatic implications. Acknowledgment changes the political landscape significantly.

**Mechanical effects:** If the claim is broadly acknowledged, the Trueborn dynasty gains significant faith intensity in all territories and a global diplomatic aura that makes war against them politically costly for any who declared acknowledgment.

---

### THE GREAT RECKONING

**Trigger:** One dynasty holds 70% or more of total map territory.

**What happens:** All remaining dynasties receive a global coalition bonus against the hegemon specifically — morale, recruitment capacity, and faith intensity are boosted for dynasties in formal opposition. The Trueborn City mobilizes its neutral military forces for the first time in the match — not as an army that attacks, but as a defensive guarantor for any territory that requests Trueborn City protection.

**This event marks the final phase of the match:** Either the hegemon completes the conquest before the coalition can coordinate, or the coalition reduces the hegemon's territory below the trigger threshold and the match continues.

---

### THE FAITH CONVERGENCE

**Trigger:** Three or more dynasties follow the same covenant at high intensity simultaneously.

**What happens:** The faith in question gains a world presence that exceeds what any individual dynasty represents. The faith begins operating partially independently — populations in neutral territories convert without any dynasty's direct involvement. Sacred Ground affiliated with the convergent faith begins generating higher intensity spontaneously.

**Competitive reaction:** Other faiths experience this as existential pressure. Dynasties following minority faiths receive a "Defender of the Faith" bonus — increased faith intensity and morale — as their populations recognize they are in the minority and commit more deeply.

**Endgame implication:** A Faith Convergence can accelerate a Faith Victory path or can trigger the most significant Holy War the match has seen.
