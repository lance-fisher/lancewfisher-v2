# Bloodlines — Diplomacy and War System

Diplomacy in Bloodlines is not a sidebar to the game. It is a primary strategic dimension that interacts with every other system: conviction, faith, population loyalty, economic victory, and the Trueborn City's coalition mechanics. A dynasty that manages its diplomatic relationships well can achieve outcomes that superior military force alone cannot.

### Added 2026-03-18, Fifth Session

---

## Design Philosophy

**Diplomacy is not safe.** Every formal agreement carries a moral weight. Breaking it has a cost. Honoring it when it would be more convenient to break it is how moral conviction is built. Diplomacy is where conviction is tested most visibly.

**Diplomacy is not free.** Maintaining alliances costs gold, political attention, and occasionally military deployment. A dynasty that overextends its diplomatic commitments will find itself unable to honor them when it matters.

**Diplomacy is information.** Every diplomatic interaction reveals something about the other dynasty's priorities, their resource state, their faith posture, and their likely next move. A skilled diplomat is a skilled intelligence officer.

## Diplomatic States

Every pair of dynasties exists in one of five formal diplomatic states at any given time:

| State | Description | Key Properties |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **War** | Active military conflict | Full military engagement permitted; no trade; no communication except ransom |
| **Hostile** | Formal hostility without active war | No trade; military skirmishes permitted; diplomatic channels exist but are strained |
| **Neutral** | Default state for unknown or unengaged dynasties | No special bonuses or penalties; trade possible but not guaranteed |
| **Cordial** | Positive relationship with ongoing communication | Trade terms improved; military intelligence sharing optional |
| **Allied** | Formal alliance agreement | Intelligence sharing required; war on an ally is an Alliance Betrayal event; trade is at favorable terms |

Transitioning between states requires specific actions and carries specific costs. Moving from Allied to War in a single step is an Alliance Betrayal and carries the full conviction and diplomatic consequences of that event.

## Diplomatic Actions

### Sending an Envoy

The foundational diplomatic action. An envoy is a Bloodline Member or appointed diplomat dispatched to another dynasty's territory. The envoy can:

- Open formal communication channels (moves from Neutral toward Cordial)
- Propose specific agreements (see Agreement Types below)
- Gather intelligence (passive while present — the envoy observes the host dynasty's territory)
- Deliver ultimatums (moves from Cordial toward Hostile if refused)

**Envoy vulnerability:** An envoy can be held hostage, expelled, or in extreme cases killed. Holding an envoy hostage is a major diplomatic incident. Killing an envoy triggers an automatic Hostile state and a significant conviction penalty for the killing dynasty (universally condemned regardless of faith).

---

### Agreement Types

**Trade Agreement:** Both dynasties exchange a negotiated quantity of specific resources each game cycle. Terms are set at agreement time and may be renegotiated with mutual consent. Breaking a Trade Agreement moves the relationship toward Hostile and carries a minor conviction cost.

**Non-Aggression Pact:** Both dynasties formally agree not to attack each other's territories for a defined duration. Breaking a NAP triggers an Alliance Betrayal-equivalent event (major conviction cost, diplomatic reputation damage to all other dynasties).

**Military Alliance:** Full alliance. Both dynasties share active military intelligence (each can see the other's army positions). Neither may declare war on the other without Alliance Betrayal. Both are expected to provide military assistance if the other is attacked — the expectation is not absolute but declining to assist weakens the alliance.

**Faith Alliance:** Two dynasties of the same covenant form a religious bond beyond politics. Faith Alliance carries a shared faith intensity bonus to both parties. If either pursues the Holy War path, the Faith Alliance partner is automatically included in the declaration. Breaking a Faith Alliance carries more conviction damage than a political alliance betrayal — it is a spiritual betrayal, which the population feels differently.

**Marriage Agreement:** Formalizes a marriage between Bloodline Members of two dynasties. The most binding diplomatic agreement — the families are physically joined. Marriage agreements create shared lineage consequences: children of the marriage may form new dynastic branches, inherit from both families, or become political tools of either dynasty.

**Tributary Arrangement:** One dynasty formally submits to the other as a tributary — paying resources each cycle in exchange for military protection and political recognition. This is not slavery — it is a formal hierarchy that both parties enter willingly. Tributaries retain their internal autonomy but cannot conduct independent foreign policy without the patron's awareness.

**Trade Route Establishment:** Specific agreement to open a dedicated trade route between territories. More granular than a general Trade Agreement — applies to specific resource nodes and generates ongoing income for both parties. Disrupting an established Trade Route (physically attacking the route) is a hostile act even outside of war.

### Ultimatums

An ultimatum is a formal demand delivered through diplomatic channels. It specifies an action the receiving dynasty must take and a deadline for compliance. If the deadline passes without compliance, the ultimatum-sending dynasty may escalate state without the diplomatic cost of initiating hostility unilaterally.

**Effective ultimatum uses:**
- Demand the return of a captured Bloodline Member
- Demand withdrawal from a contested territory
- Demand that a specific Faith Action cease
- Demand tribute as an alternative to war

**Ultimatum mechanics:**
- The receiving dynasty may comply (avoids war, may carry conviction and pride costs)
- The receiving dynasty may counter-propose (opens negotiation)
- The receiving dynasty may refuse (triggering escalation on the sending dynasty's terms)
- The receiving dynasty may ignore (treated as refusal after the deadline)

**Conviction interaction:** A dynasty that uses ultimatums as a tool of routine coercion accumulates a Cruel signal — the population perceives it as bullying. A dynasty that issues an ultimatum in response to a genuine violation (broken agreement, captured member) carries no conviction cost.

## War Declaration

War is a formal state, not just an action. Declaring war has specific steps with specific implications.

### Grounds for War

A dynasty may declare war at any time. However, declaring war without formal grounds carries a diplomatic reputation penalty with all other dynasties and a minor conviction cost.

**Recognized grounds for war:**
- Territorial violation (the other dynasty's forces entered your territory without permission)
- Alliance betrayal (the other dynasty broke a formal agreement)
- Bloodline Member capture or assassination
- Faith persecution (the other dynasty is suppressing your covenant within its borders)
- Trade route disruption
- Defense of an ally under attack
- Formal Trueborn Coalition war (special case — coalition wars have the most legitimate grounds of all)

**Unrecognized grounds (diplomatically costly):**
- Expansion desire (wanting someone's territory)
- Preemptive strike against a growing power
- Religious disagreement alone (without persecution)

### War Declaration Process

1. The declaring dynasty issues a Formal Declaration of War — visible to all players, identifying grounds
2. The defending dynasty has one game cycle to respond (may accept peace terms preemptively, strengthening their diplomatic position)
3. After the cycle, war state is active and military operations may proceed
4. Neutral dynasties assess the declaration — those with alliances to either party must respond to their alliance obligations

### Peace Resolution

War ends through one of four mechanisms:

**Unconditional surrender:** The losing dynasty formally surrenders. The winning dynasty dictates terms. The losing dynasty must comply or continue the war from a weakened position.

**Negotiated peace:** Both dynasties agree on terms through an envoy or Trueborn Arbitration. Terms are formal and binding. Either party breaking peace terms within a defined window is an automatic Alliance Betrayal equivalent.

**Exhaustion peace:** Both dynasties have been at war long enough that population morale and economic capacity demand resolution. An Exhaustion Peace is less formally binding — it carries no immediate convention cost to resume conflict, but both populations lose faith in their dynasty's governance judgment.

**Trueborn Arbitration peace:** The cleanest form of resolution. Both dynasties accept the City's terms. The terms are enforced by the City's implicit coalition threat — breaking a Trueborn Arbitration peace triggers an immediate Trueborn Summons against the breaking dynasty.

## The Trueborn City's Diplomatic Role

The Trueborn City is the world's permanent neutral diplomatic platform. It exists outside the dynamics of any individual dynasty and provides services that no single dynasty can provide.

### City Services

**Neutral ground for negotiation:** Any two dynasties may request a formal meeting at the Trueborn City. The City guarantees the safety of all participants. Killing or capturing a dynasty representative at the City's ground is the most serious diplomatic violation possible — it triggers an automatic and immediate Trueborn Summons against the perpetrator regardless of other circumstances.

**Market access:** The Trueborn City maintains a market where any dynasty may buy and sell resources at standardized rates. The rates are less favorable than direct bilateral trade, but the City market is always open regardless of diplomatic state. A dynasty at war with every other dynasty can still access the City market.

**Intelligence brokerage:** The City collects and trades information. Dynasties with high Trueborn trust may purchase intelligence about other dynasties — army movements, resource levels, faith intensity — that the City has gathered through its neutral position. The City never reveals the buyer's identity to the subject.

**Arbitration services:** Formal neutral arbitration for disputes, as described in the Political Events system.

**Coalition coordination:** When the Trueborn Summons is active, the City coordinates the coalition's military intelligence sharing and formal communication.

### Building Trueborn Trust

Trust with the Trueborn City is a separate resource from diplomatic relationships with individual dynasties. It is built through:

- Contribution to the City's market (selling at below-market rates occasionally)
- Participating in Trueborn Arbitration and honoring its results
- Restraining from attacking Frost Ruins that the City has flagged as protected archaeological sites
- Responding to Trueborn requests for military assistance in specific protection operations
- Maintaining moral conviction generally (the City prefers moral dynasties as trading partners)
- House Whitehall: natural bonus at match start
- House Trueborn: significant natural bonus at match start

Trust is lost through:

- Ignoring Trueborn Summons
- Breaking Trueborn Arbitration peace terms
- Attacking City-protected territories
- Extreme cruel conviction (the City monitors and reacts)
- Refusing multiple trade requests over time

## Espionage and Covert Operations (Overview)

Full covert operations are documented in COVERT_OPS_SYSTEM.md. Key diplomatic interactions:

**Intelligence gathering through diplomacy** is passive — an envoy present in another dynasty's territory observes and reports. The information quality scales with how long the envoy is present and the envoy's specialization.

**Covert operations through diplomatic cover** are possible — an envoy with covert operation specialization can conduct sabotage under diplomatic cover. If discovered, it is a major diplomatic incident that justifies war from the victim's perspective regardless of prior state.

**Whitehall espionage advantage** is not a covert operation — it is a passive intelligence network that functions independently of envoy presence. Whitehall's information comes from relationships that have been built over generations, not from active operations.

## Faith and Diplomacy

Faith creates the most complex diplomatic situations in the game because it creates in-group and out-group dynamics that political agreements cannot fully override.

**Same-faith dynasties** have a natural diplomatic warmth — populations on both sides recognize shared spiritual identity. This does not prevent war (same-faith wars happen and can be the most brutal), but it means the path to alliance is shorter and the cost of betrayal is higher.

**Cross-faith alliances** are possible and sometimes strategically essential, but they require more maintenance investment. Populations on both sides need convincing that the arrangement is beneficial rather than a spiritual compromise. A cross-faith alliance that persists through multiple game cycles becomes culturally normalized within both populations — but early cross-faith alliances are always slightly fragile.

**Faith Alliance at Holy War:** When a Holy War Declaration occurs, it tests every existing diplomatic arrangement. Faith Alliances override political alliances in the population's perception — a dynasty with a political alliance to the enemy of their covenant faces internal pressure to abandon the political alliance in favor of the faith one.

## Conviction and Diplomacy

Conviction shapes every diplomatic interaction through the lens of reputation.

**Moral conviction dynasties:**
- Other dynasties trust their agreements more (fewer verification mechanics required)
- Populations of neutral territories are more willing to voluntarily integrate
- Trade partners offer better initial terms
- The Trueborn City extends more trust by default

**Cruel conviction dynasties:**
- Other dynasties demand stronger guarantees before formalizing agreements
- Alliance partners are less likely to respond to assistance calls (fear of being associated)
- Populations of conquered territories require more governance investment to stabilize
- Ultimatums carry more weight (fear of consequences) but alliances are harder to form

**Neutral conviction dynasties:**
- The most diplomatically flexible — no bonus, no penalty in either direction
- Most useful for the economic victory path, which requires relationships with everyone
- Goldgrave's natural state — the pragmatic position
