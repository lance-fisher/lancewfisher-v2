# Bloodlines — Unit Index

Registry of all unit types, army composition rules, and elite unit specifications.

### Content Added 2026-03-15

## Standard Units — Level 1 (Earliest Military Stage)

Level 1 represents the earliest military stage. Armies are small and drawn from the population.

### Militia
Basic soldiers drawn directly from the population. Very cheap, weak combat ability, low morale. Used for emergency defense and early expansion.

### Swordsmen
The first organized professional soldiers. Moderate attack and defense. Faster movement speed. Increased line of sight. Serve as both frontline infantry and reconnaissance units.

**Design Decision:** Scouts are intentionally removed as a unit type. Swordsmen replace the scouting role. This was an explicit design change made during Session 2.

### Spearmen
Defensive infantry with higher defense than swordsmen. Slower movement. Effective at holding the line. Strong against cavalry.

### Hunters
Light ranged units drawn from the population. Used for harassment and ambush. Weak in melee combat.

### Bowmen
Upgraded ranged units trained from hunters. Bowmen can specialize in two roles:
- **Offensive Bowmen** — Higher attack damage
- **Defensive Bowmen** — Increased range and improved defensive accuracy

Bowmen remain weak in close combat.

## Standard Units — Levels 2-5

---

### Level 2 Units — The Iron Transition (Added 2026-03-18, Fifth Session)

Level 2 represents the shift from survival to consolidation. Iron begins flowing from mines into the armory. The army gains specialization for the first time. Cavalry appears on the field.

**Design principle:** Level 2 units are still available to all dynasties regardless of faith. Faith has been chosen by the end of Level 1 but has not yet reshaped the military. These units represent the first age of real professional military organization.

---

#### Men-at-Arms
Full iron armor on the body of a Swordsman. Slower movement, dramatically more durable. The backbone of mid-game army formations. Where Swordsmen are versatile and mobile, Men-at-Arms are immovable anchors.

**Role:** Heavy frontline infantry
**Strength:** Exceptional durability under sustained attack; morale anchor for adjacent units
**Weakness:** Slower movement reduces ability to pursue or retreat; iron cost is ongoing
**Countered by:** Crossbowmen (armor-piercing), flanking maneuvers, siege weapons
**Requirement:** Armory (upgraded), iron supply

---

#### Pike Square
An upgraded formation of Spearmen reorganized into a disciplined pike formation. Devastating against cavalry charges. Largely immobile — they hold a position, not advance on one.

**Role:** Anti-cavalry anchor, defensive line
**Strength:** Negates cavalry charges completely; high defense when stationary; forces enemies to route around
**Weakness:** Immobile in formation; vulnerable to ranged fire; useless when flanked without adjacent infantry support
**Countered by:** Crossbowmen, flanking infantry, siege weapons
**Requirement:** Training Grounds (upgraded)
**Formation bonus:** Two or more adjacent Pike Square units form a continuous wall with escalating defense bonus — each additional unit in the wall increases the wall's resistance

---

#### Scout Rider
The first cavalry unit. Light, fast, built for harassment and intelligence gathering rather than sustained combat. Dies quickly against formed infantry. Invaluable for disrupting supply lines and forcing repositioning.

**Role:** Light cavalry, raid and reconnaissance
**Strength:** Exceptional speed; destroys undefended resource nodes and Lumber Camps; forces enemies to respond to threats rather than executing their plan
**Weakness:** Paper-thin against formed infantry; countered by Pike Squares immediately
**Countered by:** Any formed infantry unit; Pike Square especially
**Requirement:** Stable (horses), iron (horseshoes and equipment)
**Special:** Can capture undefended resource nodes without siege, forcing the enemy to divert forces

---

#### Crossbowmen
The most important ranged upgrade of the mid-game. Slower rate of fire than Bowmen but dramatically more effective against armored targets. Changes how siege and battle formations work.

**Role:** Armor-piercing ranged suppression
**Strength:** Pierces Men-at-Arms armor; effective against fortified positions from medium range; steady-state suppression during sieges
**Weakness:** Slow reload cycle; weak in melee; needs infantry screen
**Countered by:** Fast cavalry closing quickly; flanking; shield wall infantry
**Requirement:** Armory, Training Grounds
**Note:** The introduction of Crossbowmen is the primary reason Men-at-Arms are not unbeatable — the design creates an ongoing arms race between armor and armor-piercing capability

---

#### Siege Engineer
Not a combat unit. Tears down walls, builds field fortifications, and enables siege operations. A force with Siege Engineers can take fortified positions. A force without them cannot.

**Role:** Siege and field construction
**Strength:** Only unit capable of actively dismantling walls and gates; can construct field barricades and rudimentary fortifications
**Weakness:** Completely defenseless; must be screened by combat units; slow movement
**Countered by:** Any combat unit if unescorted
**Requirement:** Armory + Training Grounds
**Special:** Can sabotage enemy resource infrastructure (slower than raiding cavalry, but more thorough — disables rather than just damages)

---

### Level 3 Units — Ideological Expansion Era (Added 2026-03-18, Fifth Session)

Level 3 is where faith begins reshaping the military. All dynasties retain access to base unit types, but faith investment unlocks specialized forces that are qualitatively different from standard military units. Iron demand peaks at Level 3. The armies that emerge at this level will carry civilizational identity visibly.

---

#### Base Units Available to All Dynasties

---

##### Knight
The defining cavalry unit of the mid-game. Full iron barding on horse and rider. The terror of open-field engagements against unformed infantry. Expensive in iron, expensive in population, and countered hard by terrain and pike formations — but worth it when conditions align.

**Role:** Heavy cavalry, shock weapon
**Strength:** Open-field devastation against infantry not in pike formation; extreme morale impact on enemy units struck in the charge; pursuit capability against routing enemies
**Weakness:** Completely negated by Pike Square formation; poor in forest and rough terrain; iron costs are substantial and ongoing
**Countered by:** Pike Squares, rough terrain, Crossbowmen sustained fire
**Requirement:** Stable (warhorses), Armory, iron (heavy)
**Special mechanic — The Charge:** A Knight unit that has not engaged in the current engagement gains a charge bonus on first contact. After the charge is spent, they fight as heavy cavalry without the bonus until they disengage and reform.

---

##### Longbowmen
Range and accuracy that changes siege dynamics. A trained Longbowman unit at elevation can suppress fortified positions and break up assault formations before they reach the walls. Among the most tactically versatile ranged units in the game.

**Role:** Elite ranged suppression, siege fire support
**Strength:** Greater range than Crossbowmen; effective at elevation; sustained fire during sieges; can break charge formations before impact
**Weakness:** Weather-sensitive (strong wind penalties); requires space to operate effectively; weak in melee
**Countered by:** Cavalry closing fast; melee infantry overwhelming their screen
**Requirement:** Training Grounds (upgraded), Archery Range (new Military building, Level 3)
**Unlock note:** Longbowmen replace Bowmen as the primary ranged unit at Level 3. Bowmen may still be recruited at lower cost if needed.

---

##### Shield Wall
A unit built entirely around defense. A Shield Wall unit that holds a position and is not flanked is among the most durable infantry formations in the game. It does not advance. It absorbs.

**Role:** Defensive anchor, morale anchor
**Strength:** Exceptional damage absorption from frontal assault; morale aura — adjacent units do not rout as long as the Shield Wall holds; can hold a position indefinitely against infantry assault
**Weakness:** No offensive capability; loses all effectiveness if flanked; cannot pursue; will eventually fall to sustained Crossbow or Longbow fire
**Countered by:** Flanking maneuvers, ranged sustained fire
**Requirement:** Armory, Barracks (upgraded)
**Formation bonus:** Two or more Shield Wall units adjacent create a wall that provides a morale anchor to the entire formation behind them — armies fighting from behind a Shield Wall have higher baseline morale throughout the engagement

---

##### Siege Ballista
Heavy siege weapon. Fixed position, cannot move while deployed. Tears down walls efficiently. Effective against densely packed infantry. Has a long deployment time that must be protected during setup.

**Role:** Siege, anti-formation weapon
**Strength:** Most efficient wall and gate destruction of Level 3; high single-hit damage against infantry clusters; psychological effect on defenders watching a ballista aim at their position
**Weakness:** Fixed when deployed; long setup time; requires engineer crew (tied to Siege Engineer unit or specialist crew); vulnerable during setup
**Countered by:** Cavalry charges during setup; Longbowmen fire during setup; sally-out infantry assault
**Requirement:** Engineering Camp (new Military building, Level 3), wood + iron

---

#### Faith-Influenced Level 3 Units

These units require Temple + Shrine investment in addition to military prerequisites. They are not superior in every context — they are specialized, and their power is situational and faith-consistent.

---

##### Old Light — Flame Warden (Light Doctrine)
Support infantry that carry consecrated fire in controlled forms — lanterns, braziers, sacred torches. Their presence restores morale to adjacent units after battle. They are not primarily fighters — they are sustainers.

**Role:** Post-combat morale restoration, sustained campaign support
**Mechanics:** Units adjacent to a Flame Warden begin recovering morale faster after each engagement. Armies with Flame Wardens can sustain longer campaigns without returning to settlement for morale recovery.
**Doctrine requirement:** Old Light, Light doctrine active, Temple built
**Faith intensity threshold:** Moderate

---

##### Old Light — Inquisitor (Dark Doctrine)
The Old Light's dark doctrine produces this unit: disciplined suppressors of competing faith expressions. Inquisitors do not fight armies — they fight ideological environments. In conquered territory, they disrupt enemy faith intensity. In their own territory, they identify and suppress heterodox belief.

**Role:** Faith suppression, psychological warfare
**Mechanics:** Deployed in captured territory, Inquisitors gradually reduce the enemy faith intensity of the local population. This is slow and methodical — not instant. Enemy dynasties will notice their faith intensity dropping in occupied regions and must choose to retake the territory or accept the ideological erosion.
**Secondary mechanic:** Inquisitors also detect covert operations more effectively — their training in identifying hidden beliefs translates to identifying hidden agents.
**Doctrine requirement:** Old Light, Dark doctrine active, Temple built
**Faith intensity threshold:** Moderate

---

##### Blood Dominion — Blood Rider (Dark Doctrine)
Cavalry that grows stronger as allied units die nearby. They feed on the sacrifice of their comrades — mechanically, not just thematically. A Blood Rider unit engaged in a battle where friendly casualties are occurring gains escalating attack power. They are terrifying in attritional battles where losses are ongoing.

**Role:** Escalating damage cavalry, morale disruptor
**Mechanics:** Each friendly unit destroyed within range of a Blood Rider unit provides a stacking attack bonus for the duration of the engagement. This bonus resets between engagements.
**Design tension:** Blood Riders are strongest when the army is losing — this creates a gameplay decision about whether to sacrifice weakening units deliberately to power Blood Riders, at the cost of those units and their population.
**Doctrine requirement:** Blood Dominion, Dark doctrine active, Temple built
**Faith intensity threshold:** Moderate

---

##### Blood Dominion — Covenant Priest (Light Doctrine)
A ritualist who performs animal sacrifice during or immediately before battle to invoke a short-term surge in morale and attack. Not a combatant. Must be protected. But a Covenant Priest who successfully completes a pre-battle ritual can change the outcome of an engagement that would otherwise be lost.

**Role:** Pre-battle ritual support, morale spike
**Mechanics:** At the beginning of an engagement, the Covenant Priest may perform a ritual (consuming one unit of livestock from the Ranch) to provide a morale and attack bonus to all adjacent allied units for a short duration. The ritual takes time — time that the enemy may disrupt with ranged fire or a fast advance.
**Doctrine requirement:** Blood Dominion, Light doctrine active, Temple built
**Faith intensity threshold:** Moderate

---

##### The Order — Iron Legionary (Light Doctrine)
Heavy infantry with disciplined shield formation capability. The Iron Legionary can form a testudo — an interlocking shield formation that is nearly impenetrable from the front and highly resistant from the sides. In this formation, the unit cannot attack. It moves slowly. But it cannot be broken by anything short of siege weapons or extreme flanking.

**Role:** Near-impenetrable advance formation, breakthrough unit
**Mechanics:** Two modes — standard combat mode and testudo formation. In testudo, the unit has dramatically increased defense in all directions and is immune to ranged fire. Movement speed is reduced by two-thirds. Cannot attack in testudo. Transitioning between modes takes several seconds.
**Design use:** Iron Legionaries in testudo can advance through ranged fire that would destroy any other unit, reach a fortified position, and then transition back to combat mode for the assault.
**Doctrine requirement:** The Order, Light doctrine active, Temple built
**Faith intensity threshold:** Moderate

---

##### The Order — Warden-Enforcer (Dark Doctrine)
Military police with a dark doctrine mandate. Warden-Enforcers suppress desertion and panic within their own army and inflict fear penalties on enemy routing units — preventing routing enemies from rallying. Their presence makes a defeated army more defeated and their own army more obedient.

**Role:** Internal discipline, rout suppression
**Mechanics:** Allied units within range of a Warden-Enforcer cannot rout regardless of morale — they hold position at all costs, including death. Enemy routing units near a Warden-Enforcer take additional morale damage and are less likely to rally. Warden-Enforcers can also execute deserters, producing a minor resource return and a morale impact on remaining troops (positive for discipline, negative for morale — a tradeoff).
**Doctrine requirement:** The Order, Dark doctrine active, Temple built
**Faith intensity threshold:** Moderate

---

##### The Wild — Thornrider (Light Doctrine)
Light cavalry on elk or large stags rather than horses. Dramatically faster than horse cavalry in forested and rough terrain. Virtually invisible in dense forest while stationary. A Thornrider unit that attacks from forest concealment has a significant first-strike advantage.

**Role:** Forest cavalry, ambush strike
**Strength:** Fast in forests; invisible when stationary in forest; significant first-strike bonus from concealment
**Weakness:** Poor armor; poor on open plains against formed cavalry; cannot pursue into non-forest terrain efficiently
**Countered by:** Watchtower networks that reveal forest movement; open-terrain cavalry
**Doctrine requirement:** The Wild, Light doctrine active, Temple built, forest territory nearby
**Faith intensity threshold:** Moderate

---

##### The Wild — Bear Rider (Dark Doctrine)
Heavy animal cavalry on bears. Not fast — bears are not horses — but the intimidation mechanic is real and mechanically expressed. Enemy units with low morale will break before the Bear Riders engage them. Enemy units with moderate morale must make a morale check on contact. Only high-morale units resist.

**Role:** Intimidation cavalry, morale crusher
**Strength:** Intimidation aura that affects low-morale enemies before combat begins; significantly more durable than horse cavalry in close terrain; effective against routing enemies
**Weakness:** Slower than horse cavalry; countered by high-morale units with pike formations; bears require specialized feeding (consumes food at higher rate than horse cavalry)
**Special:** The Wild's dark doctrine mechanic — at high faith intensity, Bear Riders can be enhanced with ritual preparation that increases their intimidation aura range and duration
**Doctrine requirement:** The Wild, Dark doctrine active, Temple built, animal husbandry buildings
**Faith intensity threshold:** Moderate-high

---

### Level 4 Units — Irreversible Divergence (Added 2026-03-18, Fifth Session)

Level 4 is the point of no return. Every unit at this level is faith-specific. A dynasty that has not committed to a faith with serious investment cannot field these units. They are rare, expensive, individually powerful. Losing one is felt in the army's morale. They carry names and weight.

These units should be thought of as sub-hero units — not as numerous as standard forces, never fielded in large numbers, but capable of changing the course of an engagement through their presence.

---

##### Old Light — Sunblade Champion (Light Doctrine)
The apex expression of Old Light light doctrine military power. An individually consecrated warrior who heals on kill, restores adjacent unit morale, and radiates a passive inspiration to nearby troops. Not the most damaging unit — the most sustaining.

**Role:** Battlefield sustainer, morale anchor, elite fighter
**Mechanics:** On each kill, restores a small portion of morale to adjacent allied units. Passive aura reduces morale loss for all units within a moderate radius. Can perform a "Lay on Hands" ability once per engagement that fully restores morale of one adjacent unit.
**Grand Sanctuary requirement:** Yes
**Conviction interaction:** Moral conviction amplifies the healing and morale effects. A Cruel-conviction dynasty fielding a Sunblade Champion receives reduced effects as the population perceives the contradiction.

---

##### Old Light — Purge Templar (Dark Doctrine)
Inquisitor cavalry. Elite riders who target enemy bloodline members and faith practitioners specifically. Dramatically effective against enemy commanders with religious roles. Their presence forces an enemy to protect their faith leadership.

**Role:** Counter-commander cavalry, faith practitioner hunter
**Mechanics:** Bonus damage against enemy Bloodline Members, faith units, and enemy commanders. Can identify and target faith-aligned enemies in battle. When a Purge Templar kills an enemy Bloodline Member, surrounding enemy units suffer a severe morale penalty.
**Grand Sanctuary requirement:** Yes
**Conviction interaction:** Dark conviction amplifies their ruthlessness. A dynasty with Moral conviction suffers a recruitment cost penalty for Purge Templars — the population does not produce these willingly.

---

##### Blood Dominion — Bloodbound (Dark Doctrine)
A unit that cannot retreat. A unit that fights to the death. As it takes wounds, it grows stronger in attack. As it takes casualties within its formation, the survivors become more dangerous. It is the most frightening unit to face in a narrow engagement.

**Role:** Suicidal assault, last-resort siege breaker
**Mechanics:** Cannot receive a retreat order. Cannot rout. As the unit's HP drops, attack power increases proportionally — a Bloodbound at 25% health hits harder than it did at full health. When other Blood Dominion units rout nearby, Bloodbound absorbs their conviction and gains a temporary surge.
**Design note:** The Bloodbound cannot be saved once committed. This is intentional. The player chooses to send them knowing they will not return. The question is whether the damage they inflict justifies the population cost.
**Grand Sanctuary requirement:** Yes
**Conviction interaction:** Cruel conviction is essentially required — a Moral conviction dynasty cannot produce Bloodbound recruits because the population will not volunteer for suicide missions.

---

##### Blood Dominion — Covenant Scion (Light Doctrine)
A consecrated warrior assigned permanently to a specific Bloodline Member as their guardian. Not a free unit — they are assigned to a person and fight harder as that person's survival is threatened.

**Role:** Bloodline Member bodyguard, escalating defender
**Mechanics:** Assigned to one Bloodline Member. Provides a defensive bonus to that member at all times. As the protected member's health drops, the Scion's attack and defense increase proportionally. A Covenant Scion fighting to protect a wounded Bloodline Member is among the most dangerous individual combatants in the game.
**Special:** If the assigned Bloodline Member is killed, the Covenant Scion does not die — they enter a state called Blood Grief, gaining maximum possible attack for a limited duration before their morale collapses.
**Grand Sanctuary requirement:** Yes

---

##### The Order — Iron Sentinel (Light Doctrine)
Tower-equivalent infantry. They plant themselves in one location and hold it indefinitely. They do not advance, do not pursue, do not respond to orders that would move them from their position. They simply hold. In the right position, an Iron Sentinel unit can change the tactical geometry of an entire battlefield.

**Role:** Fixed defensive position, chokepoint holder
**Mechanics:** When the "Root" command is given, the Iron Sentinel becomes stationary and gains dramatically increased defense in all directions. They hold this position until destroyed or ordered to disengage. In Root state, they cannot be moved by morale damage — they hold regardless of what happens around them. One Iron Sentinel unit in a narrow pass renders that pass impassable without siege weapons.
**Weakness:** Once the enemy understands their position, they route around rather than through. Effective only in terrain that channels enemies through the Sentinel's position.
**Grand Sanctuary requirement:** Yes

---

##### The Order — Dominion Arbiter (Dark Doctrine)
Eliminates the morale mechanic for routed enemies. When enemy units rout, they typically recover morale over time and may rally. The Dominion Arbiter pursues routing enemies and executes them before they can rally. They stop the cycle of rout-and-recover that makes extended battles possible.

**Role:** Rout suppression, pursuit execution
**Mechanics:** Automatically pursues routing enemy units and eliminates them before rally can occur. Does not engage in standard battle — only pursues routing targets. Exceptionally fast. Cannot be stopped from pursuit without losing the target.
**Design effect:** The Dominion Arbiter changes the risk calculus of battle. Armies facing an Order dynasty with Arbiter units cannot afford to let their line bend — any unit that routes will be eliminated, not just temporarily weakened.
**Grand Sanctuary requirement:** Yes

---

##### The Wild — Forest Sovereign (Light Doctrine)
A Bloodline Member equivalent unit of the faith. The Forest Sovereign does not fight personally — they call a territorial aura in forested terrain that transforms every Wild unit in range into something more than they were. This unit is among the strongest force multipliers in the game in the right terrain.

**Role:** Faith force multiplier, terrain-locked aura unit
**Mechanics:** Creates a territorial aura in forested terrain within range. All Wild units in the aura gain: increased speed, forest stealth, and enhanced attack. The aura moves with the Forest Sovereign. Loses all power outside of forested terrain — in open ground, this unit has no special mechanics.
**Weakness:** Completely terrain-dependent. An enemy who forces the Wild army to fight in open terrain neutralizes this unit.
**Grand Sanctuary requirement:** Yes

---

##### The Wild — Bonebreaker (Dark Doctrine)
A Bear Rider evolved into something that primarily attacks structures rather than infantry. A single Bonebreaker can destroy a Lumber Camp in a charge, disable a Quarry, and demolish a Stable. It is the Wild's answer to siege weapons — not a weapon of war but of economic violence.

**Role:** Structure destruction, economic sabotage
**Mechanics:** Optimized for structure damage. Against infantry, average. Against resource buildings, devastating — four to five times faster than a Siege Ballista at destroying undefended economic infrastructure. Can also target Watchtowers and Shrines.
**Special — Born of Sacrifice eligible:** A Bonebreaker that has destroyed three or more structures during its service life becomes eligible for a Born of Sacrifice cycle that produces an extraordinary successor.
**Grand Sanctuary requirement:** Yes

---

### Level 5 Units — Apex Forces (Added 2026-03-18, Fifth Session)

One unit type per faith at the apex. These exist in small numbers — one or two per dynasty, maximum. They are the legacy of a civilization's total commitment to its covenant. They carry names. They are remembered after the match.

---

#### The Unbroken — Old Light Apex
A consecrated battalion that cannot be broken by morale damage. Only death removes them from the field. They fight with the morale bonus of a dynasty at full Moral conviction regardless of circumstances. Even in a losing battle, The Unbroken hold. This is not AI behavior — it is a mechanical state: morale damage does not apply to them.

**Population cost:** Very high — only a dynasty with large, loyal population can field them
**Faith cost:** Requires Grand Sanctuary and sustained maximum faith intensity for one full Level progression
**Conviction requirement:** Moral conviction only — Cruel conviction dynasties cannot produce The Unbroken
**Battlefield effect:** Any allied unit adjacent to The Unbroken is immune to rout for the duration of the engagement

---

#### The Sacrificed — Blood Dominion Apex
The ultimate expression of the Born of Sacrifice system applied to the faith. A unit created through the actual sacrifice of a Bloodline Member — named for that member, carrying their spirit (mechanically: inheriting all accumulated experience of that member). The most individually powerful unit in the game.

**Creation:** Requires Grand Sanctuary, maximum faith intensity (dark or light), and the deliberate sacrifice of one Bloodline Member. The sacrifice is a player choice, not a mechanic that occurs automatically.
**Power:** The Sacrificed unit retains the combat experience and specialization of the sacrificed Bloodline Member in addition to its own apex combat capabilities.
**Identity:** Named for the sacrificed member. The name persists through the match. If The Sacrificed is destroyed, the dynasty records the loss of both the unit and the original member.
**Conviction effect:** Creating The Sacrificed shifts conviction significantly toward Cruel — the population understands what happened, even if they do not witness it directly. There is no moral-conviction path to The Sacrificed.

---

#### The Mandate — The Order Apex
A perfectly drilled, disciplined force with no morale system. They feel no fear, no hesitation, no retreat. They execute orders and die in formation. The Mandate does not rout. The Mandate does not panic. The Mandate does not disobey.

**Mechanical property:** Complete morale immunity — not "resistant," not "immune to rout," but genuinely morale-free. No morale damage, no morale bonus. They are at the same functional state at the beginning and the end of a losing battle.
**Special:** The Mandate can be ordered to do things no other unit can be ordered to do — hold a position against impossible odds, advance into certain casualties, defend a building that will certainly fall. Other units would break. The Mandate executes.
**Creation requirement:** Grand Sanctuary, maximum Order faith intensity, full Level 5 progression
**Limitation:** If every member of the formation is killed, the formation is gone. They do not retreat to rebuild. There is no survivors contingency. The Mandate is a single-use weapon of certainty.

---

#### The First Wild — The Wild Apex
Not quite an army. A force that brings the Age Before with it when it walks. The First Wild is a unit formation built around a core of apex Wild warriors accompanied by a pack — bears, wolves, stags — that responds to the formation's faith aura rather than direct control. When The First Wild approaches, the terrain responds. Forests deepen around them. Water rises in streams. Animals in the region move toward them.

**Mechanical properties:**
- The pack element is semi-controlled: it responds to broad commands (attack, retreat, scatter, consolidate) but not precise orders
- Forested terrain within range of The First Wild grows — unforested terrain near them begins generating light forest cover over time (mechanically: revealing the forest mechanics in terrain that previously didn't have them)
- All Wild units in range gain maximum possible faction bonuses
- Enemy units approaching The First Wild through forest are revealed to them (perfect vision in forest for The First Wild's range)
**Weakness:** In open terrain, The First Wild is extremely powerful but not supernatural — the terrain bonus is lost, the pack responds less cohesively
**Creation requirement:** Grand Sanctuary, sustained maximum Wild faith intensity, Level 5 progression, significant wild territory under control

## Elite Units

Elite warriors and commanders may arise through battlefield achievements and dynastic training systems. Exceptional commanders and war heroes can elevate the prestige of the dynasty and establish lesser houses.

Bloodline commanders can influence battlefield morale (future design idea).

## Born of Sacrifice Units

Born of Sacrifice is a late-game elite army-forging mechanic. Players may sacrifice multiple armies to create a powerful elite force. Example: five standard armies sacrificed to produce one elite force.

**Elite Army Benefits:**
- Siege capability
- Enhanced armor
- Extreme morale

**Naming:** Units can be named by the player or assigned names through a random generator.

These units are rare, extremely powerful, and often become legendary figures within a match. The mechanic creates identity-rich, memorable battlefield units tied to the ideological and dynastic character of the civilization.

## Army Composition Rules

### Level 1 Typical Composition
- Militia providing numbers
- Swordsmen providing mobility and scouting
- Spearmen holding defensive formations
- Hunters or Bowmen providing ranged support

### General Army Requirements
Armies are composed of units drawn from the population and trained through military infrastructure. Armies require:
- Food supply
- Gold payment
- Leadership (bloodline commanders or appointed generals)
- Morale support

Leadership provides battlefield advantages.

### Recruitment System
Army recruitment is not chosen as a single doctrine but controlled through adjustable sliders. Dynasties can mix recruitment approaches including family obligations, paid soldiers, and faith volunteers.

Including daughters in the army may produce a negative attack buffer but a positive healing and sustainment buffer. This creates a meaningful strategic tradeoff.

### Military Buildings
- **Barracks** — Trains infantry
- **Training Grounds** — Improves soldier training
- **Armory** — Provides equipment upgrades
- **Fortified Keep** — Major defensive stronghold
- **Watchtower** — Provides early warning of enemy approach

### Player Manual Unit Expansion (Added 2026-03-15)

## Troop Promotion System

Troops can be promoted after multiple successful battles. This creates a battlefield experience progression mechanic.

## Champion Emergence

Great champions can emerge from battle and continue as great generals or be granted land and form a lesser house under the banner.

## Born of Sacrifice — Specific Creation Concept

Sacrificing five armies with the right faith knowledge level creates an advanced-level army with heavy or siege-adjacent capability.

## Sustainment and Support Dynamics

Combat must include sustainment and support dynamics. A reckless war machine that lets soldiers die with little support burns through manpower quickly and can drive morale low. Morale, loyalty, and sustainment are core factors, not superficial bonuses. Dynasties that neglect care for wounded, logistics, and support experience internal and frontline consequences.

## Bloodline Commander Presence

When a bloodline member leads an army, the army gains dynastic weight — not merely a combat buff. Their presence affects morale, legitimacy, reputation, succession security, and the political meaning of the war itself.
