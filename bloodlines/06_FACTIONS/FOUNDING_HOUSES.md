# Bloodlines — Founding Houses

The great houses that founded the playable factions. Each house has a bloodline, a faith alignment, a homeland, and defining traits that shape their faction identity across generations.

### Content Added 2026-03-15

## Founding House List — Historical Record

The canonical project memory records 9 founding Houses with a late-game Trueborn re-emergence lore component.

## Canonical Founding House List (SETTLED 2026-03-15)

1. **Trueborn** — The oldest lineage, special neutral city role
2. **Highborne** — Noble heritage, elevated bloodline
3. **Ironmark** — Martial strength, forged identity
4. **Goldgrave** — Wealth and buried power
5. **Stonehelm** — Fortification and endurance
6. **Westland** — Frontier expansion, western territories
7. **Hartvale** — Heart of the valley, pastoral strength
8. **Whitehall** — Governance and institutional authority
9. **Oldcrest** — Ancient lineage, traditional power

This list was selected from Variant 1 (Session 1) and confirmed as canonical on 2026-03-15. All nine suffixes are unique: -born, -borne, -mark, -grave, -helm, -land, -vale, -hall, -crest.

## Design Principles for House Naming

1. Two syllables or three syllables maximum
2. Distinct first letter shapes for UI readability
3. No repeated suffix patterns

These principles reference classic RTS faction naming (GDI, NOD, China, USA, GLA) where factions have clear visual and linguistic distinction for quick recognition.

## House Visual Identity

All characters share a common ancestry and physical heritage. The visual difference between houses is expressed through distinct hair colors and styles. This creates recognizable identity without dividing the world into separate ethnic groups. Dynastic cultural ancestry is reflected through hair color differences among bloodlines.

## House Role in Gameplay

The ruling noble house is the central strategic identity of the player. The player does not merely command a color-coded faction. The player guides a dynastic civilization. Noble houses represent blood, authority, continuity, specialization, political leverage, and legitimacy. Houses are structural gameplay entities that interact with the population, faith, politics, diplomacy, and inheritance.

Not every match will necessarily include every house as a playable dynasty. Unused houses may appear as AI kingdoms or minor factions.

## Trueborn House Special Role

The Trueborn house is the oldest lineage and plays a special role within the world through the neutral Trueborn city. The Trueborn city serves as a stabilizing power and can raise coalitions against emerging hegemons in the late game.

### Full House Strategic Profiles (Added 2026-03-18, Fifth Session)

The following profiles expand each house with strategic identity, mechanical strengths and weaknesses, cultural flavor, and their relationship to the Age of Reclamation's political moment. These profiles are grounded in the canonical hair color assignments, house names, and design framework.

---

## House Trueborn — Silver Hair

**The Mediators. The Survivors. The Question.**

Trueborn does not start with military strength or economic dominance. They start with something no other house has: the weight of institutional legitimacy. The Trueborn name is the most politically loaded in the known world. The Trueborn City bears their name. The Compact of the Crossroads was built on their absence. Whether they are extinct or not is the question that shapes the world's politics.

A player choosing Trueborn is choosing to play a dynasty whose name carries extraordinary gravity — and whose every action will be measured against the legend that name carries.

**Strategic Strengths:**
- Unique relationship with the Trueborn City: higher baseline trust, access to city resources and intelligence earlier than other dynasties
- Population stability bonus: populations under Trueborn governance are inherently more stable — the name carries legitimacy that reduces the governance investment required for loyalty
- Diplomatic access: Trueborn can initiate and join the Trueborn City's coalition as a member rather than just a potential target
- All four faiths have a slightly more open initial relationship with a Trueborn dynasty — the name is old enough to predate the doctrinal conflicts

**Strategic Weaknesses:**
- No inherent military bonus — the dynasty must build military capability the same as anyone else, from nothing
- Expectations: any cruel action by Trueborn carries disproportionate diplomatic and population consequence. The world expects more from this name.
- The city will investigate their legitimacy claim at some point. If the investigation is inconclusive, the city's trust bonus remains. If it resolves against them, the bonus is lost permanently.

**The Question:** Does a Trueborn player represent the genuine return of the bloodline — or the best current approximation of it? The game does not answer this. The player's actions answer it.

**Flavor:** Silver-haired rulers who carry the memory of a better world. Playing Trueborn is playing the burden of an ideal.

## House Highborne — Dark Gold Hair with Highlights

**The Aristocrats. The Old Money. The Believers in Better Blood.**

Highborne was wealthy before the Frost and never forgot it. Their dark gold hair with lighter highlights marks them immediately — it reads as wealth, as selective heritage, as the visual signature of a family that has been choosing its marriages very carefully for a very long time. They believe that bloodline purity, elevated governance, and the quality of birth produce better rulers. And they have enough evidence for this belief to make it dangerous.

**Strategic Strengths:**
- Starts with a gold reserve — old family accounts, preserved assets from the Age Before that were tracked and reclaimed during the Reclamation
- Bloodline Members develop faster: better tutors, better academies, better starting conditions produce more capable individuals more quickly
- Academy produces graduates with higher baseline capability
- Trade relationships are more favorable at start — old Highborne names carry weight in merchant circles

**Strategic Weaknesses:**
- Population loyalty requires constant investment. The common people do not love Highborne naturally — they respect them at best, resent them at worst.
- Military recruitment is culturally expensive. The Highborne aristocratic class does not view soldiering as an honorable path — it views soldiering as something you send other people's sons to do.
- Marriage politics are complicated. A Highborne marriage to a dynasty considered beneath their station is culturally costly within their own bloodline hierarchy, even if it's diplomatically valuable.

**Flavor:** Playing Highborne is playing with a head start on prestige and a permanent debt in popular support.

## House Ironmark — Black Hair

**The Soldiers. The Forged. The House That Remembers Every War.**

Ironmark has been at war in every generation since the Great Frost. Not always as the aggressor. Not always as the winner. But always at war. Black hair because they were forged in iron and smoke — literally, in the great mining communities that the Ironmark bloodline anchored during the Age of Survival. They know how to fight, they know how to lose, and they know how to come back. These are not the same knowledge.

**Strategic Strengths:**
- Military buildings cost less and complete faster — Ironmark communities have built more barracks and training grounds than any other dynasty in history
- Troops have higher baseline morale — Ironmark soldiers grow up knowing what war is before they enter it
- Born of Sacrifice recycling produces higher-quality successor units — the institutional military memory in an Ironmark community runs deeper than anywhere else
- Iron production is naturally higher — the Ironmark bloodline's historical connection to mining gives them a production bonus

**Strategic Weaknesses:**
- Agricultural development is slow — land that could be farmland is usually cleared for training grounds or roads to mining sites
- Faith investment is expensive — military cultures develop deep skepticism of religious commitment that they cannot see and touch
- Population growth is naturally lower — the martial lifestyle culture produces fewer children and loses more adults to military service

**Flavor:** The house that wins wars and struggles to build kingdoms. Eventually, every Ironmark player faces the moment when the war is over and they have to figure out what to govern.

## House Goldgrave — Bright Gold Hair

**The Merchants. The Bankers. The Dynasty That Discovered Gold Is Just a Promise.**

Bright gold hair because they advertise. Goldgrave understood something in the Age Before that other dynasties are still learning: money is not wealth. Money is a story that people agree to believe. A Goldgrave ruler knows this the way other rulers know how to command armies — it is the foundation of their power, not an abstraction.

**Strategic Strengths:**
- Natural currency dominance victory path affinity — economic infrastructure costs less to build, trade routes generate more gold, and the world begins the match with slightly more familiarity with Goldgrave currency than other dynasties' gold
- Markets generate higher income
- Diplomatic ransom negotiations are naturally more favorable — Goldgrave negotiators are experienced
- Can purchase influence with other dynasties through economic means that would require military leverage for other houses

**Strategic Weaknesses:**
- Military is expensive to maintain — Goldgrave hires rather than breeds soldiers, and hired soldiers are less loyal and more expensive than trained professionals
- Conviction defaults toward Neutral by persistent economic pragmatism — the merchant mentality resists both deep moral commitment and deep cruelty, which means Goldgrave can struggle to access the most powerful conviction milestone mechanics at either end
- Other dynasties begin the match with heightened suspicion of Goldgrave's motives — they have seen what Goldgrave can do with enough economic leverage, and they would rather prevent it than deal with it

**Flavor:** Every war Goldgrave fights is a war they should have been able to buy their way out of.

## House Stonehelm — Brown Hair

**The Defenders. The Mountain Lords. The Dynasty That Has Never Lost a Position It Chose to Hold.**

Stonehelm has never taken territory it could not hold. This is not a platitude — it is the governing principle of how the house operates. They do not advance a line they cannot reinforce. They do not build a position they cannot supply. Brown hair because they come from the earth and they intend to stay.

**Strategic Strengths:**
- Fortification buildings are cheaper and structurally stronger — Stonehelm constructions are built to outlast the people who built them
- Population behind walls are nearly immune to morale damage — being defended is part of their cultural identity
- Stone production is doubled
- Watchtower networks reveal enemy movement at greater range and earlier
- Territorial loyalty is higher in Stonehelm-controlled regions — their reputation for stable governance makes populations more willing to accept Stonehelm rule

**Strategic Weaknesses:**
- Offensive military operations are significantly more expensive and slower
- Cavalry is culturally underinvested — Stonehelm doctrine prioritizes what holds over what moves
- The match's reward for patience is structural — a Stonehelm player who rushes will be weaker than a Stonehelm player who waits

**Flavor:** Stonehelm is the house that other houses eventually run into when they have overextended. They are always still there.

## House Westland — Vivid Red Hair

**The Aggressors. The Frontiersmen. The House That Arrives First.**

Westland is always at the edge. They push forward before others are ready. Vivid red hair because you will see them coming. In the Age of Survival, the Westland communities were the ones who moved the farthest — who established the most remote settlements, who pushed into the most exposed territory, who lost the most people to cold and attack and terrible luck, and who kept going anyway. This is encoded in their culture now.

**Strategic Strengths:**
- First-mover territorial bonuses — territory claimed before other dynasties is claimed faster and with reduced infrastructure cost
- Infantry moves faster — Westland doctrine prioritizes mobile warfare
- Morale bonus in enemy territory — Westland soldiers fight better when they are somewhere they should not be
- Caravan escort missions level units faster — the frontier culture of constant movement produces experienced troops organically

**Strategic Weaknesses:**
- Consolidation is culturally difficult — Westland territory that is not constantly reinforced tends to develop loyalty problems
- Administrative governance is the weakest Westland system — the dynasty produces fighters and explorers, not administrators and planners
- The cultural impulse toward aggression can produce mechanical pressure toward overextension that the design makes painful

**Flavor:** Westland wins the first hour and fights to survive the fourth.

## House Hartvale — Orange Hair

**The Cultivators. The Harvest Lords. The Dynasty That Will Not Let the World Starve Again.**

Orange hair because they are the color of autumn harvest — abundance before the cold returns. Hartvale remembers the Frost in a specific way: as hunger. Their covenant with the land is not religious in its first form — it is experiential. Their ancestors were the people who kept food growing in impossible conditions, who kept others fed, who understand better than anyone what a population costs to lose.

**Strategic Strengths:**
- Food production is the highest of any house — across all farm types, at all levels
- Population growth rate is naturally the highest — Hartvale communities are healthy, fed, and stable
- Ranch produces a passive food reserve in addition to its standard output
- Population loyalty is high in agricultural territories — they are known for taking care of their people in a way that is practical and visible
- Trueborn City trust bonus for sustained humanitarian governance

**Strategic Weaknesses:**
- Military is culturally reluctant — conscription carries a loyalty penalty with Hartvale populations that other houses don't face
- Gold income is lower — agrarian communities generate less trade-based wealth
- Iron production is average — mining is not the Hartvale cultural tradition

**Flavor:** Hartvale is the house with the most population by hour three — and the least army to defend it. Playing Hartvale is learning to convert surplus into military strength fast enough to not get eaten.

---

## House Whitehall — Neutral Brown Hair

**The Diplomats. The Witnesses. The House That Sits at Every Table.**

Neutral brown hair because they are not meant to be noticed — they are meant to be believed. Whitehall has survived across the Age of Survival and the Age of Reclamation not by being the strongest or the richest but by being the most indispensable. They have people everywhere. They are present in every negotiation. They know things about other dynasties that those dynasties would prefer they did not know. In most cases, they have chosen not to use what they know — which is why they know it.

**Strategic Strengths:**
- Can initiate diplomacy with all dynasties from match start — no trust-building period required
- Alliance agreements last longer and cost less to maintain
- Espionage networks are naturally more effective and take less investment to maintain
- Marriage politics favor Whitehall — every house will marry into them, because every house understands the value of the information Whitehall carries
- Trueborn City trust bonus from the start — Whitehall has maintained useful relationships with the city's council for generations

**Strategic Weaknesses:**
- Military standing is below average — no one fears them, which means the threat of military force carries less weight in their diplomacy than it should
- Conviction defaults toward Neutral — Whitehall is constitutionally resistant to extremes, which means the most powerful conviction milestone mechanics at either end require deliberate effort to access
- The political assassination of a Whitehall Bloodline Member causes disproportionate diplomatic consequences — everyone loses something when a Whitehall member dies, because everyone had a relationship with them

**Flavor:** Whitehall is the house that controls every room it is not in.

## House Oldcrest — Black, Gray, and Pepper Hair

**The Rememberers. The Ancient Ones. The House That Knows.**

Oldcrest is old. Old enough that some of their lore predates the Great Frost with genuine continuity — not legend, but preserved record. Old enough that they have seen every style of ruler and every style of failure. Pepper hair because time has touched them, and they wear it with authority. An Oldcrest elder with pure white hair carries something in their posture that other dynasties' young leaders cannot replicate — the actual weight of having seen things.

**Strategic Strengths:**
- All four ancient faiths cost less to build toward — Oldcrest records of pre-Frost religious practice give them a genuine head start on every covenant
- Bloodline Members start with more accumulated experience — their tutors are older, their references are deeper
- Memory mechanic unique to Oldcrest: they accumulate a passive "Remembered History" intelligence resource that can be spent to predict an opponent's likely next action — an intelligence system that has no equivalent elsewhere
- Conviction changes are slower — Oldcrest has seen enough of both moral rule and cruel rule to have genuine perspective on both, making them harder to push to extremes

**Strategic Weaknesses:**
- Bloodline Members age and die faster — the older the starting roster, the shorter their remaining service time
- Population growth is the slowest of all nine houses — old blood, cultural conservatism, careful reproduction
- Military innovation is slow — they teach old doctrine, which is excellent doctrine, but the newest tactical developments take time to reach Oldcrest doctrine

**Flavor:** Oldcrest knows what every other house is about to do. The question is whether knowing is enough.

### Inter-House Political Relationships at Match Start

The following are proposed natural tension and alliance tendencies at match start, grounded in the house profiles above. These are tendencies, not locks — player action can alter all of them.

| Relationship | Houses | Default Tension |
|---|---|---|
| Economic rivalry | Goldgrave ↔ Whitehall | Both want control of information and money flow; different methods, same target |
| Military respect | Ironmark ↔ Stonehelm | Two military cultures with completely different doctrines; mutual recognition, mutual wariness |
| Prestige competition | Highborne ↔ Trueborn | Both believe in bloodline quality; Highborne resents the moral gravity of the Trueborn name |
| Agricultural partnership natural | Hartvale ↔ Stonehelm | Stonehelm wants food security; Hartvale wants military protection |
| Cultural friction | Oldcrest ↔ Westland | Oldcrest's conservatism versus Westland's frontier impulse |
| Diplomatic default friendship | Whitehall ↔ Everyone | Whitehall maintains something with every house by design |
| Ideological tension | Trueborn ↔ Blood Dominion faiths | The Trueborn name carries Old Light resonance; Blood Dominion dynasties know it |

The house count conflict has been resolved. Nine founding houses is the canonical answer. The Player Manual's 8-house variant was a drafting variant and does not supersede settled canon.

The Player Manual's Hartborne and Westgrave do not replace Hartvale and Westland from the canonical list. They are archived as historical variants alongside prior naming sessions.

2. **Highborne** — Darker gold with highlights
3. **Ironmark** — Black
4. **Goldgrave** — Bright gold

Hartborne appears in the Player Manual but not in the settled 9-house list (Hartvale occupies a similar position). Westgrave appears in the Player Manual but not in the settled list (Westland occupies a similar position). These differences were explicitly resolved on 2026-03-18: nine houses confirmed, Player Manual 8-house variant archived.

### Lesser House Naming System and Character Customization (Added 2026-03-18)

## Player Name Customization

Players may customize first names of characters. Lineage tracking is maintained regardless of name changes.

## Lesser House Naming System (LOCKED)

Final enforced rule: lesser house names MUST include the parent bloodline root as a visible prefix or suffix.

This decision was made after rejecting earlier naming approaches that lacked clarity and realism.

Rules:
* Root must be immediately visible in the name
* Names must be readable instantly
* Avoid abstract or overly complex constructions
* Avoid fantasy-style compound nonsense
* Maintain clarity over linguistic subtlety

Examples by house:

**Ironmark:** Ironvale, Ironford, Blackiron
**Goldgrave:** Goldreach, Goldhaven, Deepgold
**Hartvale:** Hartwood, Greenhart
**Whitehall:** Whiteford, Whitestone

This naming convention ensures lesser houses are immediately recognizable as extensions of their parent bloodline across UI, diplomacy, and map.
