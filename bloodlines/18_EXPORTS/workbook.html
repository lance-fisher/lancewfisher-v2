Project Identity and Core Philosophy

Game Title: Bloodlines Locked

Title History: Evolved from an early title direction of "Crown and Conquest" into the locked title "Bloodlines." The name reflects the design's shift toward hereditary power, dynastic identity, and generational consequence.

Genre: Massively large-scale medieval real-time strategy game. Locked

Design Inspiration: Command and Conquer style RTS with strong emphasis on base building, offense and defense, resource gathering, strategic freedom, and broad viability across multiple playstyles. The project explicitly values the artistic and structural lesson of C&C Generals: the game does not need to be visually extravagant if balance, playability, and experimentation value are strong.

What Bloodlines Is Not: Not Age of Empires building style. Not Total War combat style.

Creative Intent: Built to stand the test of time. Deep, balanced systems that allow repeated return and experimentation. This is a serious long-form game design, not a lightweight concept sketch.

Core Design Philosophy

Priorities: Clarity in presentation. Balance across viable playstyles. Replayability through emergent narrative. Long-term depth that does not peak early.

The game now centers not just on kingdoms and armies, but on hereditary power, dynastic identity, ideological divergence, moral trajectory, family continuity, and the social character of a civilization.

The Four Foundational Pillars Locked

  1. Territory — physical space, resources, loyalty required to hold it
  2. Population — living resource that feeds economy, military, faith, and politics
  3. Faith — spiritual covenant that empowers and constrains
  4. Dynasty — ruling bloodline with members, marriages, succession, and legacy

No single pillar can be neglected without consequences across the other three.

The Four Ancient Faiths

The four covenants predate the founding houses. Faith is a major strategic axis, not decorative. Selected at end of Stage 1 when the dynasty encounters tribes, ruins, and sacred sites. The dynasty aligns with an existing covenant; it does not create one.

The Old Light (Covenant of the First Flame)

Protection, mercy, divine guardianship, defense of innocents, enlightenment, unity, societal cohesion, disciplined governance, defensive stability. Followers seek to preserve life and maintain order through compassionate governance.

The Blood Dominion (The Red Covenant)

Conquest, sacrifice, domination, strength as divine favor. Darker tone, moral cost and conviction consequences, strong military benefits. Power is the truest measure of divine approval.

The Order (Covenant of the Sacred Mandate)

Law, doctrine, institutional authority, structured governance under divine mandate. Rigid hierarchy, administrative control. Governance itself is a sacred act; laws are divine instructions.

The Wild (Covenant of the Thorned Elder Roots)

Nature mysticism, ancient spirits, balance of the natural world. Adaptive survival, environmental strength. The oldest covenant; power from harmony with the earth, forests, and waters.

Faith Mechanics: Three Components Locked

  1. Covenant — which religion the dynasty follows
  2. Faith Intensity — degree of active practice (costly, population-scaled, maintenance-dependent)
  3. Faith Alignment — light vs dark spectrum. Same covenant can be interpreted differently. Large differences within the same covenant cause schisms.

Faith Actions (consume resources, may consume captives or population): Rituals, offerings, temple construction, priesthood expansion, religious festivals, sacrificial rites.

Faith Buildings: Shrine (small, +intensity), Temple (major, enables rituals), Grand Sanctuary (late-game, faith victory). Locked

Spiritual Manifestations Proposed: At extreme intensity, angels, demons, cursed armies, healing, resurrection, plague removal, nature forces. World-level pressure from extreme dark behavior.

The Conviction System

Core Concept Locked: Conviction is a behavioral morality spectrum shaped permanently by actions. Not chosen in a menu. Produced by what the player actually does.

Faith is chosen. Conviction is lived. Faith determines doctrine and worldview. Conviction determines what kind of civilization the player has become through action. These two systems influence each other but remain separate.

Actions that shape conviction: Warfare practices, treatment of prisoners, enslavement, protection of civilians, pillage, sacrifice, governance decisions.

Conviction permanently shapes: Available abilities, faith alignment interactions, kingdom development paths, late-game powers, how other dynasties perceive the civilization.

Conviction directions: Conquest, prosperity through trade, faith authority, territorial governance, dynastic prestige.

Design tension: High conviction enables extraordinary actions (including Born of Sacrifice). Low conviction leads to desertion, unrest, and collapse from within.

What Is NOT Yet Defined Open

Specific triggers and thresholds. Whether conviction is visible to the player. How it is measured or scored. Whether it is one axis or many. Scope (per-dynasty, per-territory, per-army). Exact interaction formulas with other systems.

Population and Resource Economy

Population Model Locked

Population is a unified pool, single realm-wide value. Not just a cap but a living strategic resource: labor, military, civic, and ideological body. Can be converted to workers, soldiers, specialists, or religious participants. Every conversion has a cost; every soldier is a laborer lost.

Growth: Depends on food and water. Housing determines max capacity, replacing traditional RTS power grid mechanics. Locked

Population determines: Workforce, tax revenue, recruitment capacity, territorial loyalty.

Decline causes: Famine, warfare, plagues, religious conflict.

Loyalty: Low loyalty reduces tax income, increases sabotage/rebellion. Reckless warfare lowers morale, causes mutinies. Population is a political body, not just a labor pool.

Five Primary Resources Locked

ResourceRoleSource
GoldCurrency, trade, recruitment, diplomacy, ransomTaxation, markets, trade networks
FoodPopulation growth, army sustenanceFarms, ranches
WaterPopulation survival, agricultural productionWells, territory water sources
WoodConstruction, fuel, siege equipment, shipsLumber camps (renewable, location-dependent)
StoneWalls, towers, major structuresQuarries (finite)

Secondary Resource: Influence Proposed

Design philosophy: Grounded and legible. Strong core simplicity with deep interaction. Resource nodes create natural conflict points.

Water design: Essential but not a trivial hard-counter. Fish-diet vs plains-hunting tradeoffs. Coastal civilizations may have food through fishing but lower military strength.

Currency Dominance Proposed: Introduce own currency. World transitions to dynasty's standard. Economic victory when world economy depends on it.

Territory and Loyalty

Core Rule Locked: Territory requires both military control and population loyalty. A region held only by force is unstable and may revolt. This is a defining design departure from most RTS games.

Each territory contains: Resources, population, infrastructure, loyalty level.

Low loyalty consequences: Decreased tax revenue, difficult recruitment, sabotage risk, rebellions.

Control factors: Military presence, local loyalty, infrastructure, faith influence.

Loyalty maintenance: Governance structures, faith influence, economic prosperity, dynasty reputation.

Voluntary Integration Proposed: Late-game, smaller territories join willingly due to prosperity. Governance attraction rather than conquest.

The Dynastic System and Bloodline Members

The dynastic system is the heart of Bloodlines and one of the four foundational pillars. It is the source of the game's title and its emotional core. Players shepherd a family through triumph and tragedy.

Bloodline Members Locked

Active cap: 20 members. Beyond 20, members become dormant (mechanical state, not permanent removal).

Birth: Born into dynasty over time. Paths chosen at birth, trained from childhood.

Training paths: Military command, governance, religious leadership, diplomacy, covert operations, economic stewardship.

Active roles: Commanders, governors, diplomats, ideological leaders.

Starting leader options: Father, first oldest son, second oldest son, brother of the king, firstborn son of the king.

Dynastic Interactions Established

Captured bloodline members: Enslaved as workers, executed, ransomed, or married into the capturing dynasty.

Marriage diplomacy: Cross-dynasty marriages create new branches. Children may declare loyalty to either bloodline or form an entirely new dynasty.

War heroes & lesser houses: Exceptional commanders earn titles and lands, creating lesser houses. Lesser houses are optional AI-autonomous or player-controlled.

Recruitment: Adjustable sliders (not hard doctrines). Mix family obligations, paid soldiers, faith volunteers. Locked

Sons/Daughters tradeoff Proposed: Including daughters produces negative attack buffer but positive healing/sustainment buffer.

Covert operations: Assassination and covert warfare exist as strategic tools.

Family Tree UI

Not decorative. A critical gameplay element. Player must always know: who leads, who the heirs are, specializations, heritage, population reputation.

Born of Sacrifice

This is the system that gives Bloodlines its moral weight. Extraordinary power at extraordinary cost. Locked

Mechanic: Sacrifice multiple armies to create a powerful elite force. Example: five standard armies produce one elite force.

Elite army benefits: Siege capability, enhanced armor, extreme morale.

Naming: Player-named or random generator. These units become legendary figures within a match.

Design intent: Not just a stat upgrade. Identity-rich, memorable battlefield units tied to the civilization's ideological and dynastic character. Sacrifice is not free. It costs people. It may cost your own children. Players who rely heavily on sacrifice field terrifying armies but rule hollowed-out populations.

What Is NOT Yet Defined Open

Specific sacrifice ratios, faith-specific variations, cooldowns/limits, diplomatic consequences of sacrifice, whether bloodline members can be sacrificed, relationship to conviction scoring.

UI/UX and Technical Direction

Technical Architecture Direction Established

A deterministic RTS architecture bootstrap prompt was generated: clean sim/presentation separation, deterministic fixed-tick loop (20 TPS), seeded RNG, command buffer pipeline, snapshot system, per-tick hash for desync detection.

Non-negotiables: No Math.random in sim. No Date.now in sim. No floats in sim state. Stable iteration order. Seeded RNG consumption must not depend on object key ordering.

UI/UX and Audio/Visual: Empty. No design work yet.
