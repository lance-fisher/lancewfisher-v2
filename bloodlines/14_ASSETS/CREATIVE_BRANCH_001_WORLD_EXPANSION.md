## SECTION A — Unit Progression (Levels 1-5)

The design confirms Level 1 units are locked. Levels 2-5 are an open gap. The following is a proposed full military progression built to be consistent with:
- Warcraft 3 unit count scale (lower count, high individual value)
- The Great Frost medieval setting
- Iron as the primary mid-late military resource
- Cavalry as a major military dimension
- Faith doctrine paths producing distinct unit options
- Population scarcity forcing meaningful unit composition choices

---

### Level 1 Units — LOCKED (Reference)

| Unit | Role |
|------|------|
| Militia | Emergency defense, expendable |
| Swordsmen | Frontline + reconnaissance |
| Spearmen | Defensive infantry, strong vs cavalry |
| Hunters | Light ranged, ambush |
| Bowmen | Ranged support (Offensive or Defensive spec) |

---

### Level 2 Units — Iron Age Transition

Level 2 represents the transition from survival to consolidation. Iron begins flowing. The army starts developing specialization. Cavalry enters the field.

| Unit | Role | Key Notes |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Men-at-Arms** | Heavy infantry upgrade from Swordsmen | Full iron armor, slower but dramatically more durable; forms the backbone of mid-game armies |
| **Pike Square** | Anti-cavalry formation | Upgrade from Spearmen; devastating to cavalry charges; slow, immobile, requires positioning |
| **Scout Rider** | Light cavalry, harassment and flanking | First cavalry unit; fast, light; best used for raiding supply lines and forcing repositioning; dies quickly to pike formations |
| **Crossbowmen** | Mid-range suppression | Replaces Hunters at range; slower rate of fire than Bowmen, significantly more armor piercing; counters Men-at-Arms |
| **Siege Engineer** | Construction and demolition | Not a combat unit; tears down walls, builds field fortifications; requires Armory + Training Grounds |

*Faith-Neutral. All available regardless of covenant.*

---

### Level 3 Units — Ideological Expansion Era

Level 3 is where faith begins shaping the military. Every dynasty still has access to base unit types, but faith investment starts unlocking specialized forces. Iron demand peaks.

**Base Units (All Dynasties):**

| Unit | Role | Key Notes |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Knight** | Heavy cavalry | Full iron barding; the terror of open-field engagements; expensive in iron, expensive in population; negated by Pike Squares and terrain |
| **Longbowmen** | Elite ranged | Range and accuracy that changes siege dynamics; can suppress fortified positions |
| **Shield Wall** | Defensive anchor | Absorbs incoming damage while the line holds; loses effectiveness when flanked; morale anchor for adjacent units |
| **Siege Ballista** | Siege weapon | Tears down walls; effective against clustered infantry; requires Engineering Camp (new military building) |

**Faith-Influenced Units (require Shrine + Temple investment):**

| Unit | Faith | Doctrine | Role |
|------|-------|----------|------|
| **Flame Warden** | Old Light | Light | Support infantry with healing aura; restores morale to adjacent units after battle |
| **Inquisitor** | Old Light | Dark | Suppresses enemy faith intensity in captured territory; disrupts enemy faith morale mechanics |
| **Blood Rider** | Blood Dominion | Dark | Cavalry that grows stronger as allied units are killed nearby; feeds on the sacrifice around them |
| **Covenant Priest** | Blood Dominion | Light | Battlefield ritualist; performs animal sacrifice during combat to invoke a short-term morale and attack surge |
| **Iron Legionary** | The Order | Light | Heavy infantry with shield discipline; can form a testudo-style formation that is nearly impenetrable but immobile |
| **Warden-Enforcer** | The Order | Dark | Military police unit; suppresses desertion and panic; inflicts fear penalties on enemy routing units |
| **Thornrider** | The Wild | Light | Light cavalry on stags or elk; extremely fast, poor armor; excels in forests and uneven terrain; invisible in dense forest |
| **Bear Rider** | The Wild | Dark | Heavy animal cavalry; bear mounts; intimidation mechanic that causes nearby low-morale enemy units to break without engaging |

---

### Level 4 Units — Irreversible Divergence

Level 4 is the point of no return. Military composition now reflects total civilizational commitment. Every unit at this level is faith-specific. Dynasties that have spread faith investment thinly do not reach these units.

These units are rare, expensive, and individually powerful. Losing one is felt.

| Unit | Faith | Doctrine | Role |
|------|-------|----------|------|
| **Sunblade Champion** | Old Light | Light | Elite paladin-equivalent; heals on kill; restores adjacent unit morale; inspires nearby troops passively |
| **Purge Templar** | Old Light | Dark | Inquisitor cavalry; charges enemy faith practitioners specifically; dramatically effective against enemy bloodline members with faith roles |
| **Bloodbound** | Blood Dominion | Dark | Unit that cannot retreat; fights to the death; grows in attack power as it takes wounds; terrifying in siege scenarios |
| **Covenant Scion** | Blood Dominion | Light | Consecrated warrior assigned to a Bloodline Member; bodyguard who fights harder as the protected member's health drops |
| **Iron Sentinel** | The Order | Light | Tower-equivalent infantry; plants itself in one location and holds indefinitely; loses all effectiveness if forced to move |
| **Dominion Arbiter** | The Order | Dark | Executes routing enemy units on contact; eliminates the morale penalty to the enemy from routing (enemies never escape — enemies stop routing because death ends the fear) |
| **Forest Sovereign** | The Wild | Light | Calls a temporary territorial aura in forest terrain; all Wild units in range gain speed, stealth, and attack bonus |
| **Bonebreaker** | The Wild | Dark | Directs a bear or boar in targeted structure destruction; Born of Sacrifice-eligible animal unit |

---

### Level 5 Units — Apex

One unit per faith. These are the legendary forces of a civilization that has committed completely. Only one or two can exist at a time. They are household names at match end.

| Unit | Faith | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **The Unbroken** | Old Light | A consecrated battalion that cannot be broken by morale damage; only death removes them; fights with the morale bonus of a dynasty at full moral conviction regardless of circumstances |
| **The Sacrificed** | Blood Dominion | A unit created through an actual bloodline member sacrifice; carries the name of the sacrificed member; permanently stronger than any other unit type; the ultimate Born of Sacrifice expression |
| **The Mandate** | The Order | A perfectly drilled and disciplined force with no morale system — they feel no fear, no hesitation, no retreat; they execute orders and die in formation |
| **The First Wild** | The Wild | A primal force — half army, half natural catastrophe; brings a pack of bears, wolves, and great stags; the landscape itself responds to their presence; forests grow around them, water rises near them |

## SECTION B — Great House Strategic Identities

The nine houses have locked names and hair colors. Their strategic personalities have never been specified. The following profiles are proposed based on the thematic logic of each name.

---

### House Trueborn — Silver Hair
**Strategic Identity:** The Mediators. The Survivors.

Trueborn does not start with military strength or economic dominance. They start with something no other house has: institutional legitimacy. They are believed to be extinct. Their return to the world — however the player triggers it — carries enormous narrative weight.

**Strengths:**
- Unique starting bonus: higher than normal Trueborn city trust (they share the name)
- Population loyalty bonuses: populations under Trueborn governance are inherently more stable
- Diplomatic access: Trueborn can initiate the Trueborn coalition as a member, not just a target
- Faith neutrality bonus: all four faiths are slightly more willing to cooperate under a Trueborn banner

**Weaknesses:**
- No inherent military bonus at start
- Economic development is average
- High expectations from other players and AI dynasties — any cruel action is perceived as a betrayal of legacy

**Flavor:** Silver-haired rulers who carry the memory of a better world. Playing Trueborn is playing the burden of an ideal.

---

### House Highborne — Dark Gold with Highlights
**Strategic Identity:** The Aristocrats. The Old Money.

Highborne was wealthy before the Frost and never forgot it. They believe in bloodline purity, elevated governance, and the idea that the better-born are better suited to rule.

**Strengths:**
- Starts with a gold bonus (old reserves)
- Bloodline Members gain experience faster (selective breeding, excellent tutors)
- Academy is unlocked one tier earlier
- Trade partners offer better rates (old family relationships)

**Weaknesses:**
- Population loyalty requires constant investment — the common people do not love Highborne naturally
- Military recruitment is harder among their own population (the aristocratic class sees soldiering as beneath them)
- Reputation damage for marrying "below station" (cross-dynasty marriages with lower-prestige houses)

**Flavor:** Playing Highborne is playing with a head start on prestige and a permanent debt in popular support.

---

### House Ironmark — Black Hair
**Strategic Identity:** The Soldiers. The Forged.

Ironmark has been at war in every generation since the Great Frost. They know how to fight, they know how to lose, and they know how to come back. Black hair because they were forged in iron and smoke.

**Strengths:**
- Military buildings cost less and build faster
- Troops have higher baseline morale
- Born of Sacrifice recycling produces higher-quality successor units (institutional military memory is deeper)
- Natural iron production bonus (mining tradition)

**Weaknesses:**
- Agricultural development is slow (land cleared for war, not farming)
- Faith investment is more expensive (military culture is skeptical of religious commitment)
- Population tends toward lower growth (martial lifestyle culture)

**Flavor:** The house that wins wars and struggles to build kingdoms.

---

### House Goldgrave — Bright Gold Hair
**Strategic Identity:** The Merchants. The Bankers.

Goldgrave discovered that gold is just a promise — and they have been in the business of making promises ever since. Bright gold hair because they advertise.

**Strengths:**
- Natural currency dominance victory path affinity (economic infrastructure is cheaper)
- Market generates more gold per cycle
- Diplomatic ransom options are more favorable
- Can purchase influence with other dynasties more cheaply
- Currency dominance threshold is lower (world economy already partially on their standard)

**Weaknesses:**
- Military production is expensive (they hire rather than train)
- Conviction shifts toward Neutral by default — the merchant mentality resists both moral commitment and cruelty
- Other dynasties are suspicious of their motives (espionage risk is higher against Goldgrave specifically)

**Flavor:** Every war Goldgrave fights is a war they should have been able to buy their way out of.

---

### House Stonehelm — Brown Hair
**Strategic Identity:** The Defenders. The Mountain Lords.

Stonehelm has never taken a territory it could not hold. It has never advanced a line it could not reinforce. Brown hair because they come from the earth and they intend to stay.

**Strengths:**
- Fortification buildings are dramatically cheaper and stronger
- Population behind walls are nearly immune to morale damage
- Stone production is doubled
- Watchtower network reveals enemy movement unusually early
- Territorial loyalty is higher in Stonehelm-held regions (stable governance reputation)

**Weaknesses:**
- Offensive military operations are slower and more expensive
- Cavalry is less effective (mountain warfare culture prioritizes infantry)
- Expansion impulse is low — winning through Stonehelm requires patience the match rewards late

**Flavor:** Stonehelm is the house that other houses eventually run into when they have overextended. They are always still there.

---

### House Westland — Vivid Red Hair
**Strategic Identity:** The Aggressors. The Frontiersmen.

Westland is always at the edge. They push forward before others are ready. Vivid red hair because you will see them coming.

**Strengths:**
- First-mover bonus: territory claimed before other dynasties is claimed faster
- Swordsmen are faster (Westland field doctrine emphasizes mobile infantry)
- Morale bonus when in enemy territory (they fight better when they are somewhere they should not be)
- Caravan escort duty levels units faster (frontier culture)

**Weaknesses:**
- Overextension risk is higher (cultural impulsiveness translates to design pressure toward aggressive decisions)
- Consolidation is slow — Westland territory that is not reinforced is not held
- Population loyalty requires military presence — governance administration is Westland's weakest system

**Flavor:** Westland wins the first hour and fights to survive the fourth.

---

### House Hartvale — Orange Hair
**Strategic Identity:** The Cultivators. The Harvest Lords.

Hartvale remembers that the world starved during the Great Frost. They will not let it happen again. Orange hair because they are the color of autumn harvest — abundance before the cold returns.

**Strengths:**
- Food production is 30% higher across all farm types
- Population growth rate is the highest of all nine houses
- Ranch also produces a small food reserve passively (superior animal husbandry)
- Population loyalty is high in farming territories (they are known for taking care of their people)
- Trueborn city reputation bonus for humanitarian governance

**Weaknesses:**
- Military expansion is culturally reluctant — conscription penalties
- Gold income is lower (agrarian culture, less trade focus)
- Iron production is average (mining is not a Hartvale strength)

**Flavor:** Hartvale is the house with the most population by hour three — and the least army to defend it.

---

### House Whitehall — Neutral Brown Hair
**Strategic Identity:** The Diplomats. The Witnesses.

Whitehall sits at every table. Neutral brown hair because they are not meant to be noticed — they are meant to be believed. They have survived by being useful to everyone and threatening to no one.

**Strengths:**
- Can initiate diplomacy with all dynasties from match start (no trust buildup required)
- Alliance agreements last longer and cost less to maintain
- Espionage is more effective (they have people everywhere)
- Marriage politics are more favorable (every house will marry into Whitehall)
- The Trueborn city trusts Whitehall above most other houses by default

**Weaknesses:**
- Military standing is below average (no one fears them)
- Conviction defaults strongly toward Neutral — Whitehall cannot easily become either Moral or Cruel without active effort
- Political assassination of Whitehall members causes unusually large diplomatic penalties (everyone loses something when a Whitehall member dies)

**Flavor:** Whitehall is the house that controls every room it is not in.

---

### House Oldcrest — Black, Gray, Pepper Hair
**Strategic Identity:** The Rememberers. The Ancient Ones.

Oldcrest is old. Old enough that some of their lore predates the Great Frost. Old enough that they have seen every house rise and fall. Pepper hair because time has touched them — and they wear it with authority.

**Strengths:**
- All four ancient faiths cost less to build toward (their records of pre-Frost religious practice give them a head start on every covenant)
- Bloodline members start older and more experienced (not young heirs — a starting roster with actual competence)
- Memory mechanic: Oldcrest accumulates a "Remembered History" resource passively — this resource can be spent to predict an enemy's likely next action (intelligence system)
- Conviction swings are dampened (Oldcrest has seen cruelty and seen mercy — they are harder to push to extremes)

**Weaknesses:**
- Bloodline members age and die faster (older roster means shorter service life)
- Population growth is the slowest of all nine houses (old blood, fewer children)
- Military innovation is slower — they teach old doctrine

**Flavor:** Oldcrest knows what every other house is about to do. The question is whether knowing is enough.

## SECTION C — Territory and Terrain Types

The Great Frost left the world with distinct geographic identities. These proposed terrain types are grounded in the post-Frost world and the era of Reclamation.

---

| Terrain | Resource Affinity | Strategic Value | Faith Affinity | Hazard |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|----------------|--------|
| **Reclaimed Plains** | Food (strong), Water (moderate) | Farming heartland; fastest population growth | Hartvale advantage; Old Light fits (agricultural faith) | Drought vulnerability |
| **Ancient Forest** | Wood (very strong), Food (hunting, moderate) | Dense cover; Wild faith mechanics activate | Wild covenant primary territory | Harder to build non-wood structures |
| **Stone Highlands** | Stone (very strong), Iron (moderate) | Defensive high ground; fortifications cheaper | Stonehelm advantage | Cavalry penalty; slow movement |
| **Iron Ridges** | Iron (very strong), Stone (moderate) | Mid-late game contested territory; iron wars start here | Ironmark advantage | Exposed; no natural water |
| **River Valleys** | Water (very strong), Food (strong), Gold (trade) | Most stable starting territory; hardest to disrupt | Old Light fits | Flooding vulnerability; no iron |
| **Coastal Zones** | Gold (trade, very strong), Food (fishing) | Naval access; trade routes; currency dominance path | Goldgrave advantage | Exposed to naval raids |
| **Frost Ruins** | Special: Lore discovery, occasional resource caches | Unique events and discoveries; pre-Frost civilization artifacts | All faiths (ancient sites) | Unstable — structural collapses, hostile remnants |

## SECTION D — Conviction Action Weights

A proposed framework for how player actions shift conviction. Actions are tracked by pattern, not isolated incident. These weights are cumulative — repeated actions compound.

**Scale:** Actions produce conviction points (CP). Threshold bands determine Moral / Neutral / Cruel status. Specific thresholds TBD in data layer.

### Actions That Shift Toward Moral

| Action | CP Shift | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Release prisoners without ransom | +2 | Minor; generous but economically negligible |
| Honor a peace agreement when breaking it would be advantageous | +5 | Significant; sacrifice of opportunity |
| Protect civilian zones during conflict (no pillage order) | +3 per territory | Moderate; consistent behavior required |
| Veteran retirement with land grant | +2 per cycle | Minor; cumulative across many recycling cycles |
| Rebuild a destroyed territory (yours or conquered) | +4 | Visible investment in human welfare |
| Provide food aid to a starving allied or neutral population | +6 | High; genuine sacrifice of resources |
| Allow conquered population to maintain their faith | +3 | Tolerance under power |
| Heal wounded prisoners before ransom | +1 | Small; basic decency |
| Execute a traitor through public trial | +2 | Justice performed visibly |

### Actions That Shift Toward Cruel

| Action | CP Shift | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Execute prisoners of war | -4 | Significant; eliminates ransom and diplomatic value |
| Pillage a territory | -3 per territory | Moderate; cumulative if done repeatedly |
| Discard veterans without recognition | -1 per cycle | Minor individually; compounds through Born of Sacrifice cycles |
| Enslave conquered population | -6 | Major; clear act of dehumanization |
| Burn farmland during war | -5 | Devastating to civilian population; visible moral crime |
| Force faith conversion at sword point | -5 | Violates the faith-conviction separation mechanic; also destabilizes faith intensity |
| Execute a bloodline member of a defeated dynasty | -8 | Very significant; eliminates diplomatic options permanently |
| Sacrifice unbeliever population (requires extreme threshold unlock) | -15 | Catastrophic single-act shift; most powerful cruel action in the game |
| Conduct a raid specifically targeting water infrastructure | -7 | Attacks survival; universally condemned |
| Imprison an innocent (false accusation of treason) | -4 | Injustice against the dynasty's own people |

### Neutral Zone Behavior (No CP shift either direction)

| Action | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Ransom prisoners at market rate | Standard exchange; no moral weight |
| Conscript troops through family obligation | Expected; not cruel |
| Enforce taxation at standard rates | Governance; neutral |
| Execute a guilty traitor without trial | Edge — could shift slightly cruel if pattern repeats |

## SECTION E — Political Events System (Proposed)

Events fire based on game state triggers — they are not scripted in sequence. The following are proposed named events organized by trigger category.

### Faith Events

| Event | Trigger | Effect |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **The Schism** | Two dynasties share same faith but opposite doctrine paths | Faith splits — both dynasties take -faith intensity; world populations begin to choose sides |
| **The Calling** | Any dynasty reaches 80%+ faith intensity | Surrounding neutral populations feel drawn toward that faith; minor defections begin |
| **The Heresy** | Light doctrine dynasty commits extreme cruel action | Internal faith fracture; a population segment forms an authentic faith resistance movement |
| **Holy War Declaration** | One faith reaches 60%+ global population share | The dominant faith's Bloodline leaders can declare holy war — all followers of that faith receive a morale bonus; all opposing dynasties receive a morale penalty |
| **The Covenant Test** | Level 4 progression reached | The faith demands a grand act of commitment — a sacrifice, a construction, a war — specific to the doctrine path; failure costs faith intensity; success unlocks apex mechanics |

### Dynastic Events

| Event | Trigger | Effect |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **Succession Crisis** | Head of bloodline dies with no mature heir | Bloodline enters instability; population loyalty drops; other dynasties may attempt marriage offers or aggression |
| **The Champion** | A non-bloodline unit survives five major battles | Unit becomes eligible for Champion designation; can be granted land and title (forms lesser house) or retired with honor |
| **Blood Betrayal** | A bloodline member is captured and refuses to be ransomed | Captured member's home territory suffers loyalty collapse; other members' morale drops |
| **The Prodigy** | Random rare event on Bloodline Member birth | A child is born with exceptional aptitude in one area; they develop faster and reach higher limits in their specialization |
| **The Elder's Wisdom** | Any bloodline member survives to old age (Oldcrest has higher frequency) | Old member offers one-time advice event — player may gain intelligence on an opponent or a production bonus |

### Economic Events

| Event | Trigger | Effect |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **The Iron Drought** | Iron production disrupted for 3+ consecutive cycles | Army modernization halts; existing iron units cannot be replenished; iron-holding territories become contested immediately |
| **Trade Collapse** | Currency dominance dynasty takes heavy military losses | World faith in the dominant currency wavers; other dynasties gain opportunity to reintroduce gold standard |
| **The Abundant Harvest** | Multiple consecutive cycles without food disruption | Population growth spike; recruitment window opens at reduced conscription cost |
| **Famine** | Food production drops below population minimum | Population declines; faith intensity drops; conviction shifts slightly cruel if no action taken |
| **Market War** | Two dynasties attempt simultaneous currency dominance | Forced conflict event — one must fail |

### Military Events

| Event | Trigger | Effect |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **The Rout** | An army is destroyed with 90%+ casualties in a single battle | Survivors carry Rout status — reduced morale for several cycles; a Bloodline Member must address them to restore morale |
| **The Stand** | An outnumbered force holds a position against two or more times their number | Surviving units gain Veteran status immediately; morale boost dynasty-wide; Conviction shifts slightly Moral |
| **The Desertion** | Conviction crosses into Cruel while army is in extended campaign | A percentage of active troops desert during the night; percentage scales with how Cruel the conviction is |
| **The Siege Breaks** | An enemy siege is repelled with full structure intact | Defending units gain experience bonus; population loyalty in that territory rises significantly |

### World Events

| Event | Trigger | Effect |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **The Frost Echo** | Late game, any dynasty occupies Tundra territory in winter cycle | A miniature climate event; Frost Ruins become accessible with fresh resource caches; exposed armies take attrition damage |
| **The Old Light Returns** | Trueborn dynasty re-emerges | Global faith intensity rises across all covenants; the world recognizes the moment has changed |
| **Trueborn Summons** | Any dynasty approaches military hegemony | Trueborn city sends formal notice to all players; coalition window opens |
| **The Great Reckoning** | One dynasty holds 70%+ of map territory | All remaining dynasties receive a global coalition bonus until the hegemon's territory falls below 60%; Trueborn mobilizes |
