## SECTION A: What Faith Actually Is in Bloodlines

The player who chooses a covenant at the start of a match is not selecting a faction bonus. They are committing their civilization to an answer. A specific, non-negotiable answer to the question that every civilization in this world has been forced to answer by the memory of The Great Frost: why does power exist, and what is it for?

Each of the four covenants is a complete theology — a total account of the world's nature, the cause of its suffering, the mechanism of its redemption or endurance, and the obligations it places on those who hold power within it. None of them are naive. None of them are soft. All four emerged from the wreckage of a world that nearly ended, and all four carry in their doctrine the memory of that near-ending. They differ on what it meant, what caused it, and what the living owe the dead and each other. These are not abstract questions. In this world, the answers you build your civilization around determine what your armies are willing to do, who your population believes they are, what kinds of leaders your bloodline produces, and whether your dynasty can survive being tested.

What separates light doctrine from dark doctrine within any given covenant is not goodness versus evil. That framing is too simple for what these faiths actually are. What separates them is where the doctrine places the cost. Light doctrine places cost on the dynasty — the ruler pays, leads through sacrifice, governs through obligation. Dark doctrine places cost on the governed, the conquered, the weak, the apostate. The theology is often identical. The same framework that makes Blood Dominion's light doctrine a system of shared obligation held together by mutual bloodshed can be read, by a different temperament with the same scriptures, as a justification for unlimited extraction from anyone too weak to object. The faith contains both readings. The civilization the player builds determines which reading wins.

## THE OLD LIGHT — Covenant of the First Flame

### What This Faith Is

The Old Light does not hold that the divine is watching from a distance. It holds that the divine is the act of witnessing itself — that the sacred is the capacity to see clearly, to hold what has been seen in memory, and to be changed by it. The flame is not a symbol of warmth. It is a symbol of inescapable illumination. You cannot be in the presence of fire and hide. Everything becomes visible: the wound, the rot, the beauty, the face of the person dying in front of you. The Great Frost, in Old Light theology, was not wrath. It was the consequence of collective forgetting — a civilization that had stopped looking at itself, that had allowed comfortable ignorance to calcify into organized blindness, until the world had no choice but to extinguish a light that had already gone dark on its own. The Frost did not punish. It completed.

This faith demands two things that pull against each other in ways the dynasty must resolve through its choices. First: it demands that the living hold the dead in active memory — not as sentiment but as obligation. The names of the fallen are not ceremonial. They are a form of debt, and a form of witness, and the reckoning they represent is real. To follow Old Light and let your people die unnamed, unremembered, used as raw material with no acknowledgment, is not merely political failure. It is theological betrayal. Second: it demands that what the flame illuminates be faced. The light is not a mercy that looks away. When it finds corruption — in a population, a bloodline, a doctrine, a civilization — it does not grieve and move on. Something that has been seen by the light has been called to account, and the account must be settled. Light doctrine settles accounts through mercy and witness. Dark doctrine settles them through fire.

### Light Doctrine — The Remembrance Compact

**The doctrine's logic:**

A dynasty walking the Remembrance Compact has accepted that the light's obligation runs in both directions. They are witnessed by the same divine attention they invoke when they light the flame for their dead. This produces dynasties of unusual moral seriousness, and unusual moral vulnerability. They cannot commit atrocities anonymously. They cannot let their populations die in uncounted anonymity and still claim the flame. Every death that passes through their civilization enters the record and will be there when judgment comes. This is not a comfortable doctrine to hold power under, which is precisely why dynasties that hold it tend to hold it ferociously — because the alternative is the dark path, and the dark path begins as relief from accountability and ends as an inquisition.

The Remembrance Compact demands that the ruler bleed alongside their people — not symbolically, but structurally. The faith's rituals are designed so that the most powerful effects require the dynastic member's personal participation in an act of witness, of formal acknowledgment of cost. The covenant cannot be maintained by gold alone. It requires the dynasty to keep looking at what it has done.

**Rituals:**

**The Naming of the Fallen**
- Building required: Wayshrine (Tier 1) or higher
- Who participates: One Bloodline Member as officiant; open to all units and population in the territory
- What is consumed: The ritual requires the physical burning of a record — the names of the dead inscribed on materials that will combust fully in the flame. 25 Timber (the pyre), 15 Provisions (funeral sustenance distributed to the attending population), 10 Rare Materials (inscription materials and the vessel for the ash). The ash is then interred at the base of the Wayshrine.
- Duration: 2 cycles. Cannot be interrupted without consequence.
- What happens to participants: The officiant Bloodline Member carries a Witness Mark for 10 cycles — a narrative status denoting that they have formally entered the dead into the record. Units who attended the Naming gain Compact Bond, a persistent morale floor that prevents their resolve from dropping below 40% for 15 cycles.
- Mechanical effect: All Bloodline Members who died in the preceding 10 cycles are entered into the Compact Record. Each entered member generates +3 Conviction stability per cycle for 20 cycles. Enemy propaganda targeting this population is halved in effectiveness — a population whose dead are named and remembered has an anchor identity that resists replacement. Faith intensity: +5%.
- Interruption: If the Wayshrine is attacked mid-rite, the pyre is scattered. The names are not entered. The population in the territory experiences a Grief Without Resolution event — faith intensity drops 4% and morale of all units in the territory drops 15% for 10 cycles. This is the worst outcome because the dead have been dishonored at the moment they were offered remembrance.

**The Vigil of Reckoning**
- Building required: Hall of Remembrance (Tier 2) or higher
- Who participates: A dedicated Flame Keeper plus a minimum of 5% of the territory's population, who stand vigil in shifts. At least one Bloodline Member must attend for one of the 5 cycles.
- What is consumed: 45 Timber, 35 Provisions (the vigil-keepers must be fed), 25 Rare Materials. The Flame Keeper who leads the vigil does not sleep for its duration. At the vigil's end they are physically depleted, entering a rest state that reduces their administrative output for 5 cycles.
- Duration: 5 continuous cycles. The flame cannot be allowed to go out.
- What happens to participants: The Flame Keeper emerges from the vigil with the Witness Deepened trait for 20 cycles — all faith intensity generation from this building is increased 20%. The Bloodline Member who attended gains the Compact Bound trait permanently for this match, meaning they are immune to faith corruption mechanics for the remainder of the game.
- Mechanical effect: All injured units in home territory recover morale and health at +40% rate for 10 cycles following the vigil's completion. Grief events from war deaths have their Conviction cost reduced 50% — the population has been given a formal structure for loss rather than being left to metabolize it silently. One Flame Warden unit is generated at no material cost upon completion.
- Interruption: If interrupted before cycle 3: no effect, resources lost, the attempted vigil becomes a failure that triggers a minor morale collapse in the participating population (they gathered in grief and were denied the completion of it). If interrupted between cycles 3 and 5: partial effect activates — healing bonuses apply for 5 cycles only, no Flame Warden generated.

**The Recitation of Witness**
- Building required: The Eternal Cathedral (Tier 3)
- Who participates: Senior Flame Keeper as primary officiant; the entire Cathedral clergy; one Bloodline Member must be present for the final cycle
- What is consumed: 60 Provisions, 45 Rare Materials (the illuminated scrolls — each one records an enemy faction's military actions against Old Light forces in detail), 30 Gold, 15 Faith Currency. The scrolls are read aloud in their entirety and then sealed in the Cathedral vaults. This is not performance. The reading is theological: the light has witnessed everything done here and will remember it.
- Duration: 1 cycle, but requires 3 cycles of preparation during which the scribes compile the scrolls.
- What happens to participants: The Bloodline Member present for the final cycle gains The Recorded Name — their deeds from this match are specifically entered into the Cathedral record. If this Member dies in a subsequent battle, the Cathedral generates a passive manifestation-level morale effect for 3 cycles.
- Mechanical effect: All enemy factions bordering Old Light territory are subject to The Witnessing for 15 cycles. Enemy Bloodline Members who die fighting Old Light during this period are recorded, creating Diplomatic Weight tokens usable to propose non-aggression agreements at doubled acceptance probability. All Old Light units gain +7% combat morale for 15 cycles. Faith intensity: +10%.
- Interruption: The Recitation cannot be interrupted once begun — it is entirely internal. However, if the Cathedral is destroyed within 5 cycles of completion, all effects end and faith intensity drops 15%.

**The Rite of Ash Absorption**
- Building required: Hall of Remembrance (Tier 2) or higher
- Who participates: Flame Keeper officiant; the survivors of the defeated units; any Bloodline Member who chose to lead the defeated force
- What is consumed: 20 Timber, 25 Provisions, 15 Rare Materials. The weapons of units that were destroyed in the defeat are burned. The smoke is witness. The Bloodline Member who led the force and survived must formally speak the names of those lost under their command. If they were not present for the battle, the rite is unavailable.
- Duration: 1 cycle. Requires a minimum of 2 cycles without active combat in the ritual territory.
- What happens to participants: The Bloodline Member who speaks the names carries the Weight of Command for 10 cycles — they cannot avoid Conviction accountability for the deaths under them, but this accountability is formal and acknowledged rather than unresolved. This prevents the worst of the Conviction debt from festering.
- Mechanical effect: Absorbs 40% of the incoming Conviction penalty from military defeat, distributing the damage across a 20-cycle decay period rather than hitting immediately. The population experiences the loss as tragedy rather than catastrophic humiliation. Surviving units from the defeated force who witnessed the rite gain +20% combat resolve for 5 cycles — they have been given meaning for what they survived. Faith intensity: +4%.

**Passive doctrine effects:**

- Latent (0-20%): No doctrine effects. The faith exists as social infrastructure only.
- Active (20-40%): Background grief recovery activates in home territory. Conviction losses from natural disaster, plague, and famine events are reduced 15% because the faith gives the population a cognitive framework for loss. The dead are being held by something. The living can continue.
- Devout (40-60%): Injured units in home territory recover 25% faster. Bloodline Members who survive battles in which other Members died gain the Witness trait permanently — +10% to all Conviction-affecting decisions for 20 cycles per surviving battle. The weight of what they saw becomes moral authority rather than psychological damage.
- Fervent (60-80%): All Remembrance rites cost 20% fewer resources. Enemy faith corruption and propaganda mechanics targeting Old Light populations operate at half conversion rate. The Compact's population has an identity anchor too specific to be replaced generically. A passive presence radiates from faith buildings: enemy units within 3 hexes suffer -7% morale — not from threat, but from the weight of being observed by something that remembers.
- Apex (80-100%): The Eternal Cathedral, if built, passively generates diplomatic memory records of all military actions taken against Old Light. These are visible as in-game intelligence and presentable to neutral factions as evidence. Additionally, all Old Light units gain the Remembered passive: when they die in combat, adjacent living units receive +12% combat morale for 3 cycles as the fallen enter the record in real time.
- Sustained Apex (10+ cycles): Dynasty of Witness activates. All Bloodline Members gain once-per-lifetime access to The Record — the full weight of the dynasty's documented history bearing on a single Conviction check or diplomatic action, overriding external pressure. Cannot be faked. Cannot be purchased.

**Spiritual manifestation — The Choir of the Witnessed (Fervent+):**

The Choir manifests when Old Light reaches Fervent intensity and at least one major engagement — 20 or more units, 10 or more dead — has occurred in home territory within the past 15 cycles. What arrives is not an army. It does not look like an angel and does not behave like a weapon. It appears as a slow upwelling of pale golden light from the Hall of Remembrance or the Eternal Cathedral — not fire, not lightning, but a warm diffuse illumination that seems to have no single source, accompanied by the sound of many voices speaking low and overlapping, distinct words unrecoverable, the register unmistakable: this is the sound of people being listed. They are being named. All of them.

Mechanically, the Choir freezes the current combat morale of every unit in the territory — own forces and enemy alike — at its present value. Routing units stop routing. Frenzied units stop overextending. Both sides fight at exactly the resolve they carried when the Choir appeared. This is the Remembrance Compact's vision of what the divine does with a battle: it holds it. It witnesses it. It does not intervene to make one side win. In a battle turning through morale collapse, this rescues a routing Old Light army from dissolution. Against an enemy winning through psychological pressure rather than attrition, it is infuriating.

Duration: 8 cycles. Ends if the Hall of Remembrance or Eternal Cathedral is destroyed, or faith intensity drops below Fervent. Cannot be attacked directly. Enemy dark doctrine rituals at Fervent or above can disrupt it over 3 cycles of sustained counter-ritual effort — the Choir is patient but not invulnerable.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Flame Warden (Level 3, Light Doctrine)**
The Flame Warden carries the torch of the Hall of Remembrance not as weapon but as standard — a live flame maintained through combat by deliberate effort. The Warden has chosen the hardest thing the doctrine asks: to hold the light steady while the battle moves around it.
- Vigil Aura: All units within 4 hexes gain +10% morale stability — a floor effect, not a ceiling boost. Morale cannot drop below 40% within this radius while the Warden stands.
- Last Rites: When any allied unit dies within 2 hexes, the Flame Warden may spend one action to perform a battlefield naming. This prevents the death from radiating the panic/grief propagation that normally ripples through nearby units when allies fall. The death is absorbed into the record rather than broadcasting as terror.
- Torch of Witness: Once per battle, the Flame Warden designates an enemy unit as Witnessed. That unit loses all stealth, ambush bonus, and flanking advantage for the remainder of the battle — they have been seen. They cannot become unseen.

**Sunblade Champion (Level 4, Light Doctrine)**
The Sunblade Champion is a warrior who has undergone the Vigil of Reckoning themselves and emerged from it. They fought before the ceremony. They fight differently after it.
- Purification Strike: Attacks are specifically effective against spiritually corrupted units — dark doctrine faith units at high intensity, entities manifested from dark doctrine rituals, units under active curse effects. +30% damage against these targets.
- Bearer of Remembrance: The Champion extends Vigil Aura at half effectiveness even without a Flame Warden present, serving as an anchor point for the doctrine's morale-stabilizing presence.
- The Unquenched: Once per engagement, when reduced to critical health, the Champion's torch gutters and holds — recovering 20% maximum health. Cannot trigger below 5% health. The name is stated on the unit card. Its meaning is not inspirational. It is theological: what the compact holds, it holds.

**Apex unit — The Unbroken (Light Doctrine expression):**
The Light Doctrine Unbroken is ash-grey. Lit from within. It carries both sword and torch, and both look as though they have been in use since longer ago than anyone can measure. It does not enter the battlefield with triumph. It enters the way a witness enters a room: with attention.

Stats profile: High durability, moderate offense. The Unbroken regenerates 5% health per cycle during combat while standing in Old Light territory. Its morale cannot break. It is immune to fear, curse, and corruption effects. The unit or formation that kills The Unbroken is marked Witnessed for the remainder of the match — visible to all factions, carrying -10% morale when facing any Old Light force they subsequently meet. The record remembers who extinguished the flame.

Unique ability — Keeper of the Record: Once per match, The Unbroken invokes the full weight of what the dynasty's flame has witnessed. Area of effect: 6 hexes. Allied units within radius gain the Remembered trait for 10 cycles. Enemy units within radius suffer -20% morale for 10 cycles — not from the warrior but from the presence of everything they have done being held, documented, and permanent.

The Choir of the Witnessed is active for the first 5 cycles of The Unbroken's presence. They arrive together. The feeling is not of a weapon being deployed. It is of the faith itself coming to see what its followers built.

### Dark Doctrine — The Purging Flame

**The doctrine's logic:**

The Purging Flame path has arrived at a conclusion that follows logically from the same theology the Remembrance Compact holds — and that is what makes it genuinely dangerous rather than simply cruel. If the light sees everything, and if what it sees has moral weight, and if moral weight demands response, then the faith that only witnesses without acting is not mercy but cowardice at scale. The Purging Flame dynasty has decided that the light's nature is not simply to record but to judge, and that judgment without consequence is not judgment at all but observation. Heresy is not error. It is contagion. Apostasy is not disagreement. It is the seed of the Frost planted in a warm civilization, and every generation that mercy permits to grow is a generation closer to the cold returning.

This doctrine produces dynasties of profound internal coherence. There is no ambiguity in a Purging Flame court — you hold to the light as the Inquisitor defines it, or you are the problem being solved. This is not cynicism. It is completely sincere. A Purging Flame dynasty believes it is merciful in the most important sense: it is protecting the many clean from contamination by the few corrupt. The horror of this doctrine is not that it is hypocritical. It is that it is entirely earnest, and that earnestness makes it capable of industrialized atrocity with a clean institutional conscience.

**Rituals:**

**The Inquisitor's Examination**
- Building required: Hall of Remembrance (Tier 2) or higher, with active Inquisitor unit present
- Who participates: The Inquisitor as primary examiner, assisted by hall clergy. The targeted settlement's population undergoes formal inquiry — questioning, observation of devotional practice, review of social relationships.
- What is consumed: 25 Gold (inquisitorial apparatus), 20 Rare Materials (instruments of examination and documentation), 25 Labor (the tribunal process is intensive and occupies significant human hours). The Inquisitor who conducts the examination cannot be redeployed for its duration.
- Duration: 3 cycles.
- What happens to participants: The Inquisitor carries Authority of Finding for 10 cycles following the examination, making subsequent examinations in this territory 20% faster. The targeted settlement's population does not recover full happiness until the finding is resolved — the examination itself is a form of controlled pressure applied to the social body.
- Mechanical effect: The settlement is classified Clean or Marked. Clean generates +5% faith intensity and temporary loyalty bonus — the population feels affirmed and surveilled simultaneously, which reinforces doctrinal compliance. Marked generates a mandatory choice: perform the Purging Rite (see below), exile the heretics (population reduction, the exiled scatter into neighboring territories carrying faith disruption pressure), or delay (the Marked status corrupts adjacent settlements at -3% faith intensity per cycle until resolved). Note on randomness: at extreme dark doctrine weight, the Examination will always return Marked. The instrument has become too eager. This is a mechanic and a warning.
- Faith intensity change: +5% if followed by Purging Rite within 5 cycles; +2% if Clean; -4% if delayed beyond 5 cycles

**The Purging Rite**
- Building required: Hall of Remembrance (Tier 2), must follow an Examination with a Marked result
- Who participates: Inquisitor as officiant; Purge Templar units if available as enforcers; the condemned population as material
- What is consumed: 15 Timber (the pyres — plural, at scale), 10 Rare Materials (restraints, official seals of the finding), 35 Labor (the rite is physically intensive and requires significant personnel). The population of the purged settlement is reduced. The reduction is not symbolic.
- Duration: 1 cycle.
- What happens to participants: Inquisitor unit gains +20% combat morale for 15 cycles — they have been affirmed in their function. Purge Templar units present gain the Cleansed Blade trait for 10 cycles, increasing their damage against heresy-designated targets by 15%.
- Mechanical effect: The Marked designation is eliminated. Faith intensity +8%. Population of the purged settlement suffers -30% happiness for 20 cycles but does not rebel unless dynasty Conviction is critically low — the remaining population has internalized the doctrine's account of what happened. Adjacent enemy populations with line of sight to the purge suffer -15% voluntary conversion likelihood toward Old Light. The purge is visible. It is meant to be visible.
- Interruption: Cannot be interrupted once begun.

**The Writ of Unclean Declaration**
- Building required: The Eternal Cathedral (Tier 3)
- Who participates: Senior clergy of the Cathedral conduct the formal theological declaration; a Bloodline Member must ratify it; it is distributed through all available diplomatic and trade channels without additional cost
- What is consumed: 45 Gold, 35 Rare Materials, 25 Faith Currency. The declaration is a permanent document. It cannot be retracted within the match.
- Duration: 2 cycles to draft and ratify; effect is permanent once issued.
- What happens to participants: The Bloodline Member who ratifies the Writ carries the Declarant status — they become the named theological authority for the designation and face double the Conviction consequences if the dynasty later violates the spirit of the declaration through accommodation or mercy to the declared population.
- Mechanical effect: Formally designates an entire enemy dynasty as Unclean — a theological classification with material consequences. Old Light units fighting the declared dynasty gain +22% combat morale for 20 cycles. Neutral factions receive -20% likelihood of trade agreements with the declared dynasty. Old Light populations in the declared dynasty's territory become immune to faith conversion pressure from that dynasty's faith for the declaration's duration. The Writ cannot be cancelled. The light has spoken.
- Faith intensity change: +12%

**The Cathedral Flame**
- Building required: The Eternal Cathedral (Tier 3)
- Who participates: Full Cathedral clergy; an Inquisitor unit must be stationed within 2 hexes to anchor the ritual's enforcement dimension; a Bloodline Member must attend the lighting ceremony
- What is consumed: 55 Timber (the fuel — the Flame is large, maintained at scale), 45 Gold, 35 Rare Materials, 25 Provisions (the clergy tending the Flame must be fed continuously). Once established, the Flame requires 10 Gold and 5 Timber per cycle to maintain.
- Duration: Established in 3 cycles, maintained for up to 25 cycles while upkeep is paid.
- What happens to participants: The Bloodline Member who attends the lighting carries the Flame's Authority for the duration — immune to diplomatic blackmail and intimidation mechanics. The Cathedral clergy enter a sustained heightened devotional state that increases their faith intensity output by 20%.
- Mechanical effect: The Cathedral Flame is a visible, permanent fixture — it can be seen from anywhere on the map. Enemy units within 5 hexes of the Cathedral suffer -12% morale (the weight of judgment made architectural). Inquisitor and Purge Templar units stationed within 3 hexes gain +18% base combat stats. Faith intensity generation across all territories with Shrines or higher increases 15%. If the Cathedral is attacked and destroyed while the Flame burns, The Flame's Grievance activates: all enemy units in the territory suffer -28% morale for 12 cycles. The destruction of a burning Cathedral is not politically neutral. It is desecration, and the game treats it as desecration.
- Faith intensity change: +6% on establishment, +3% passive per cycle for duration

**Passive doctrine effects:**

- Latent (0-20%): No doctrine effects.
- Active (20-40%): Inquisitor units become available from faith buildings. Civilian populations in home territory generate faith intensity at 10% higher rate — the doctrine creates social pressure that functions as enforcement. Visible compliance becomes normative.
- Devout (40-60%): Mixed-faith populations (Old Light + any other faith) experience conversion toward Old Light at +25% accelerated rate. Doctrinal pressure has reached a social tipping point where conformity reinforces itself. Heresy detection events trigger more frequently, generating more Examination opportunities.
- Fervent (60-80%): Purging Rites cost 30% fewer resources. All Old Light units gain Flame-Touched — immunity to enemy demoralizing unit abilities and propaganda effects. A routing enemy force that has faced Purge Templar units within the past 5 cycles takes 50% longer to reorganize. The scattering is not military disorganization. It is fear.
- Apex (80-100%): The Vigilance activates as a passive territorial effect across all home territory. Any enemy unit entering home territory and remaining for more than 2 cycles is automatically detected regardless of stealth capability. The faith network has become a comprehensive surveillance infrastructure. Non-Old Light populations in home territory convert at double rate, and conversion mechanics shift from persuasion to suppression. The light no longer invites. It illuminates and waits.
- Sustained Apex (10+ cycles): Unclean Sight activates dynasty-wide. The dynasty sees the faith intensity of all units on the battlefield regardless of fog of war. Dark doctrine units of enemy faiths appear as distinct tactical markers. The inquisitorial theology at full expression: the light sees everything, everywhere, simultaneously.

**Spiritual manifestation — The Consuming Witness (Fervent+):**

Where the Remembrance Compact summons a choir, the Purging Flame summons silence. The Consuming Witness arrives as a pillar of white-gold fire rising from the highest faith building in the territory — visible from anywhere on the map, without sound except for a single sustained tone like a bell struck and never allowed to die. It does not move. It does not speak. It burns. The core is blinding. Around it, heat distortion bends visible space. At 50 meters from the building it stands over, the air smells of hot metal.

Mechanically, this is an area denial and attrition entity. Any non-Old Light unit within 4 hexes of the building suffers -5% health per cycle from proximity — described in-game as "the weight of judgment" rather than conventional damage. Old Light units in the same radius gain complete immunity to morale loss from any source for the duration. Inquisitor and Purge Templar units within the radius gain +25% all combat stats. Enemy dark doctrine manifestations — Hunger, Blood spirits, Wild entities — suffer double the proximity attrition when within range. The light is specifically hostile to things that exist within darkness, not as a metaphysical rule but as the faith's earned encounter with its opposite.

Duration: 10 cycles. Ends when the faith building it manifests from is destroyed or faith intensity drops below Fervent. Cannot be targeted directly. Counter-ritual by a Fervent-or-higher enemy faith takes 4 cycles of sustained effort to dispel.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Inquisitor (Level 3, Dark Doctrine)**
The Inquisitor is not primarily a combat unit. They are a specialist in identifying and eliminating spiritual contamination who has learned to operate in environments where contamination fights back.
- Heresy Sight: Identifies enemy faith units that are currently buffed by active rituals. These units carry a distinct visible marker, giving intelligence about which enemy units are spiritually enhanced.
- Interrogation: Once per battle, the Inquisitor selects an enemy unit for concentrated targeting — that unit suffers -22% to all stats for 5 cycles from the psychological weight of individual examination. Against dark doctrine faith units, the penalty is -35%.
- Flame Judgement: Attacks deal +18% damage to units designated Unclean via the Writ of Unclean Declaration, or any unit belonging to a dynasty currently under an active Examination result.

**Purge Templar (Level 4, Dark Doctrine)**
The Purge Templar is the Inquisition made fully martial. This is not a warrior who happens to follow a faith. This is the enforcement arm of a sacred institution, and they conduct themselves accordingly.
- Sacred Fire: The Templar can apply fire to a targeted area of terrain — 3 hex radius, units within suffer -12% movement and -6% health per cycle, fire lasts 3 cycles. Faith buildings in burning terrain suffer accelerated structural damage.
- Unyielding Sentence: Each enemy unit the Templar kills generates +2% faith intensity immediately. This is the only unit in the game that directly generates faith intensity through combat kills. At high faith intensity and in sustained engagements, this compounds.
- Purge Formation: When two or more Purge Templars act in the same cycle, they activate Purge Formation — a coordinated advance providing +22% damage to all units in the formation against any target, immune to flanking from the direction the formation faces. Enemy units in sight of a Purge Formation who are not actively engaged suffer -12% morale from proximity alone.

**Apex unit — The Unbroken (Dark Doctrine expression):**
Where the Remembrance Compact Unbroken is ash-grey and lit from within, the Purging Flame Unbroken is white and lit from outside — the flame so intense it seems to consume the figure wearing it rather than emanate from them. The pyre-staff. The sword made of compressed fire. The face in unit portraits and art carries not serenity but absolute certainty. This is the face of someone who knows they are right. Who has always known.

Stats profile: Same structural durability as the Light Doctrine version — near-impossible to break, morale that cannot be shattered — but with different offense. The Purging Flame Unbroken deals 27% more base damage. It has the Inquisitor's Heresy Sight as a permanent passive. It generates faith intensity on kills at 5% per kill rather than 2%. It does not feel like the faith's witness. It feels like the faith's verdict.

Unique ability — The Final Reckoning: Once per match, The Unbroken condemns a single territory. All non-Old Light units in the territory immediately suffer -22% health and -32% morale. All Old Light units gain +22% all stats. The terrain in the Condemned territory loses all defensive bonuses for 12 cycles — no forest cover, no hill advantage, no fortification. The light burns through cover equally. It makes no distinction between the rock and the heretic sheltering behind it.

The Consuming Witness is active for the first 5 cycles of The Unbroken's presence. The combination does not feel like a faith arriving to witness. It feels like a faith arriving to collect what is owed.

### Faith Spread — Old Light

Old Light spreads through loss. It is most compelling to populations that have experienced significant collective grief — war dead, famine, plague, mass displacement — who have not been given a framework for what they lost. Old Light offers that framework at the cost of nothing except sustained remembrance. The faith enters through the stories that travel with refugees and traders: that somewhere there is a civilization that names its dead, that holds them in the record, that does not let them dissolve into anonymity. This is not a small offer in a world that nearly ended.

Light doctrine Old Light is uniquely resistant to displacement once rooted because it attaches itself to local history. To convert away from Old Light is, literally, to abandon the names of your dead. The faith has made itself synonymous with collective memory, which means removing it feels like a second death for every ancestor it has claimed.

Dark doctrine Old Light spreads differently: it spreads through fear of the Inquisitor. Populations under occupation by a Purging Flame dynasty experience conversion not through attraction but through the systematic elimination of alternatives. The threat is not abstract. The pyres are visible.

### Conflict and Compatibility

**Old Light vs. Blood Dominion:** Theological first-principles incompatibility. Old Light honors the dead by remembering them. Blood Dominion honors them by using them as fuel. These frameworks cannot reconcile at the doctrinal level. In mixed populations, constant low-grade social tension even without political pressure. In war, atrocity rates between these two factions are elevated because each side interprets the other's ritual practice as active sacrilege, and sacrilege is a reason to escalate beyond what military necessity requires.

**Old Light vs. The Order:** The most navigable cross-covenant relationship. The Record and the Law are structurally compatible — both are permanent, both bind the present to the past, both hold people accountable to something larger than immediate interest. A light-doctrine Old Light dynasty and a light-doctrine Order dynasty can achieve stable coexistence with modest diplomatic investment. The fracture point emerges when an Order dark doctrine dynasty encounters an Old Light light doctrine dynasty: the Order says the Law is absolute and admits no exception; the Remembrance Compact says that exceptions for mercy and acknowledgment of cost are the whole point. This is not academic disagreement when applied to prisoners, apostates, and conquered populations.

**Old Light vs. The Wild:** The deepest theological difference in the game. Old Light holds that the dead persist in the record and that this persistence carries moral weight on the living. The Wild holds that death returns things to the cycle and that grief is a human conceit the forest does not share. Old Light finds this almost incomprehensible — a spiritual tradition that forgets its dead is not a faith, it is animal metabolism. Wild populations resist Old Light conversion because the Wild's theological frame has no slot for what Old Light is offering. However: both faiths make sacred claims about specific places. Common diplomatic language can sometimes be built around territory that has been made sacred by what it witnessed and what it nurtured — an imperfect but functional overlap in a game that requires finding language somewhere.

## THE BLOOD DOMINION — The Red Covenant

### What This Faith Is

The Blood Dominion knows something that other faiths spend generations trying to avoid knowing: the bond between people, between a dynasty and its population, between the living and the dead, is not maintained by sentiment. It is maintained by cost. Things that cost nothing mean nothing. The covenant between a ruler and those they rule, between a soldier and the cause they die for, between a civilization and its own survival, is not sealed by rhetoric or law or memory. It is sealed by blood. The shedding of it, the sharing of it, the willingness to be cut open in service of something larger than the individual body — this is the mechanism through which the sacred bond activates. Not metaphorically. The blood is the covenant. The covenant is the blood.

The theology comes from a specific reading of the world's near-ending. The Great Frost did not arrive because civilization forgot something, as Old Light holds. It arrived because civilization had stopped paying. The bonds between people had become cheap — maintained by habit and convenience rather than genuine cost — and cheap bonds do not hold when the cold comes. The Frost was the consequence of a world full of people who had promised things they had not paid for. The Blood Dominion's resolution: pay. Pay first, pay visibly, pay in a currency that cannot be counterfeited. And if the world demands payment before it will hold together, the question the dynasty must answer is: whose blood? Who pays the covenant's cost? The light path and the dark path give different answers. The theology is identical.

### Light Doctrine — The Covenant Bond

**The doctrine's logic:**

A dynasty walking the Covenant Bond path has accepted that the ruler bleeds first. The most sacred act within this doctrine is the ruler's willingness to pay before demanding payment, to demonstrate through visible personal cost that the covenant is real and mutual. This produces dynasties of unusual unit loyalty because the population understands, in visceral terms, that their sacrifice is shared rather than extracted. When a Covenant Priest opens their own palm before a battle and bleeds into the ground of the territory they are defending, the soldiers watching are not receiving inspiration. They are receiving a theological statement about what the covenant obligates the strong to do. They believe it because they have watched it happen.

The vulnerability of this doctrine is directly proportional to its strength. A Covenant Bond dynasty that takes heavy losses has not merely lost soldiers. The covenant is strained — the shared sacrifice structure has absorbed more than it was designed to absorb in one period, and if the losses are not formally acknowledged and entered into the covenant record through the proper rites, the bond between the survivors and the ruling dynasty begins to attenuate. The covenant promise was mutual. The survivors paid their part. If the dynasty does not pay its part in return, the bond weakens. This makes Blood Dominion light doctrine strong in sustained defensive war but vulnerable to prolonged attrition — the covenant maintenance cost scales with losses in a way that cannot be indefinitely deferred.

### Dark Doctrine — The Blood Rite

**The doctrine's logic:**

The Blood Rite path has absorbed the Covenant Bond's theology fully. It agrees that the covenant must be paid, that cost is the mechanism through which the bond becomes real, that sacrifice is not cruelty but stewardship. It has drawn a different conclusion about who pays. The weak exist within the covenant as material. The strong exist within it as architects. Asking the weak to opt out of their contribution is like asking the earth to decline to accept the grain that's sown in it — the question is categorically wrong. The covenant is not a negotiation between equals. It is a hierarchy of purpose, and the Blood Rite priest who stands before the altar and performs the Tithe is not committing violence. They are completing the covenant's structure.

This doctrine does not hide from itself. Its rituals are performed publicly. Its priests do not dissemble about what they are doing. What makes the Blood Rite path genuinely disturbing is not that it is secretly ashamed of itself — it is that it is completely at peace with itself, and that peace is indistinguishable from certainty, and certainty at scale looks identical to institutions that have made atrocity orderly and gave it a title and a calendar.

### Faith Spread — Blood Dominion

Blood Dominion spreads through visibility. It does not require trade routes or cultural prestige. It requires that what it does be seen. A neighboring population with line of sight to a Blood Convocation does not experience abstract conversion pressure. They experience a specific encounter with a civilization that has something their own does not: a willingness to pay costs that makes it capable of things theirs cannot match. Some convert because they respect the power. Some because they fear becoming its material. Some because the theology genuinely answers something: that suffering has purpose, that obligation runs both ways between the strong and the structure they are sustained by.

Blood Dominion also spreads most aggressively in the immediate aftermath of conquest. Occupied territories experience maximum conversion pressure within the first 10 cycles — before the population has developed conceptual language to resist it. The covenant's physical presence in the territory is felt before the theology can be articulated, and by the time the theology can be articulated, the population has already been changed by witnessing the rituals.

### Conflict and Compatibility

**Blood Dominion vs. Old Light:** Old Light honors the dead by holding them in memory. Blood Dominion honors them by making the living use them as fuel. These are not reconcilable frameworks. Mixed populations experience constant pressure. In war, atrocity rates between these factions are elevated because each side interprets the other's ritual practice as active desecration of the dead and is therefore willing to commit acts beyond what military necessity requires.

**Blood Dominion vs. The Order:** The most functional cross-covenant relationship Blood Dominion has. The Order understands that structure requires cost, that hierarchy has obligations, that law is not sentiment. Two dark doctrine dynasties — Blood Rite and Order dark path — can cooperate because each recognizes in the other a theology that does not flinch from what power costs. The friction emerges from their competing accounts of what governs the covenant's relationship with ordinary people: Blood Dominion says sacrifice, The Order says compliance with law. These produce different populations in different states of subjugation, and eventually the two frameworks make claims the other cannot accommodate.

**Blood Dominion vs. The Wild:** Surprising structural compatibility at the theological level — both faiths accept the premise that the strong consume the weak as a basic fact of existence. Dark doctrine Blood Dominion and dark doctrine Wild can cooperate with minimal friction around this shared premise. The incompatibility emerges in practice: Blood Dominion wants to sacrifice nature as material (livestock, land, the labor of people living in it), and the Wild's dark doctrine holds that nature defines the terms of all consumption. What the Wild's dark path considers natural predation, Blood Dominion's dark path considers covenant sustenance, and eventually these descriptions make competing claims about the same piece of ground.

## THE ORDER — Covenant of the Sacred Mandate

### What This Faith Is

The Order does not hold that the divine speaks through fire, or through blood, or through the forest's indifferent growth. It holds that the divine speaks through structure. Through the fact that law exists, that hierarchy can be made to function, that human beings are capable of building institutions that outlast any individual and that constrain even those with the power to ignore them. In a world that nearly ended from chaos — from the collapse of every system that had organized people's lives — The Order offers the most specific answer to why The Great Frost came: because there was no law that held. Civilization failed not because it forgot the dead or refused to pay, but because it had no mechanism capable of making anyone accountable to anything larger than their immediate appetite. The Frost was the outcome of a world without binding law. The Mandate's answer is permanent, comprehensive, enforced law — not as convenience but as theology. Governance is not administration. It is the closest act to the sacred that living people can perform.

The Order asks its followers to believe something difficult: that the law matters more than the person. That an institution built correctly is more valuable than any individual within it, including the individual who built it. The light doctrine says this means the mandate exists to protect the governed — the law binds the powerful more than the weak, because the powerful have the most capacity to do harm unchecked. The dark doctrine says this means the mandate exists to enforce the hierarchy — the law defines who is governed and who governs, and compliance is the only expression of faith the institution requires. Both paths use the same texts. Both paths have enforced them at scale. The civilizations they produce are recognizably related and mutually incomprehensible.

### Light Doctrine — The Iron Compact

**The doctrine's logic:**

A dynasty walking the Iron Compact path has accepted that the law they invoke is the law they live under. The Mandate cannot be claimed as protection for the population while being used as a tool of extraction for the dynasty — the faith has no mechanism for this kind of hypocrisy that does not produce measurable structural consequences. The Iron Compact produces dynasties with unusual institutional stability because their populations experience governance as something other than organized exploitation. The law is the same for everyone, including the ruling bloodline, and the faith's rituals are specifically designed to enforce this through public ceremony that is genuinely binding rather than ceremonially hollow.

The vulnerability of this doctrine is bureaucratic scale. The Iron Compact operates through formal process — trials, declarations, oaths with legal weight, institutions that must be maintained. These require resources, personnel, and time that a crisis may not allow. An Iron Compact dynasty under rapid military pressure will find its governance machinery straining because the doctrine does not permit the summary shortcuts that crisis seems to demand. The dark path's appeal in moments of emergency is that it offers efficiency — one person deciding, no process required. The Iron Compact's answer is that efficiency purchased at the cost of the Mandate is not efficiency but dissolution of the thing that makes the dynasty worth defending.

**Rituals:**

**The Proclamation of Mandate**
- Building required: Lawpost (Tier 1) or higher

- What is consumed: 20 Gold (for the official proclamation's material production), 15 Rare Materials (the formal documentation — these are legal instruments, not decorative objects), 20 Labor (for the process of drafting, reviewing, and formally witnessing). The Proclamation is a legal act. Its materials are the machinery of law.
- Duration: 1 cycle for the public ceremony, but requires 2 cycles of preparation.
- What happens to participants: The Bloodline Member who formally declares the Proclamation carries the Mandate Seal for 15 cycles — a status that makes all governance actions they take within this period 18% more effective. Population representatives who witness it return to their settlements carrying the Mandate Acknowledged status, which reduces their settlement's governance grievance accumulation by 20% for 10 cycles.
- Mechanical effect: All population settlements in the territory receive formal acknowledgment that the dynasty's authority derives from the Mandate rather than from force alone. Loyalty stability in all settlements increases for 12 cycles. Faith intensity +5%.

**The Trial of Account**
- Building required: Hall of Mandate (Tier 2) or higher

### Dark Doctrine — The Iron Fist

**The doctrine's logic:**

The Iron Fist path takes the Iron Compact's theology and removes its condition. The Iron Compact holds that law binds the powerful as much as the weak, because the powerful have the most capacity for harm. The Iron Fist holds that this is sentimentality dressed as jurisprudence. The Mandate exists because some people are capable of enforcing order and some people require it to be enforced on them, and the law's function is not to balance these groups but to institutionalize their relationship. The dynasty is the Mandate. The Mandate is the dynasty. Compliance is the only legitimate expression of faith available to those who are not in the dynasty, because they are not in the position of interpreting the law — they are the population the law governs.

This doctrine produces the most administratively efficient civilizations in the game. The Iron Fist's institutional machinery does not slow down for consultation or formal process — it decides and enforces. Its population compliance rates are extremely high because non-compliance has been made formally illegal and the enforcement apparatus is comprehensive. What it produces in exchange for this efficiency is a civilization whose stability is entirely top-down — the population is compliant, not loyal, and the moment the dynasty demonstrates any weakness, the entire compliance architecture becomes available for capture by whoever can project authority over it.

### Faith Spread — The Order

The Order spreads through adjacent populations observing institutional stability over time. It does not spread through conquest primarily — it spreads through the contrast between the dynasty's governance coherence and the disorder elsewhere. Populations experiencing factional conflict, governance failure, or arbitrary rule are most susceptible to Order conversion because the faith offers the most concrete answer to disorder: law, hierarchy, enforcement. Populations in neighboring territories who have watched an Order dynasty for 10 cycles and found it consistently more stable than their own governance begin converting not out of idealism but out of pragmatic calculation.

Dark doctrine Order spreads through a different mechanism: it spreads through the demonstration that non-compliance has visible, predictable costs. A Formal Suppression conducted in line of sight of neighboring territories generates conversion pressure in those territories not because the observers find the doctrine attractive but because the alternative to converting has been made observable.

### Conflict and Compatibility

**The Order vs. Old Light:** Structurally compatible until tested by specific circumstances. The Record and the Law are not incompatible as foundations for a civilization. The fracture emerges around the question of whether accountability processes are genuine or procedural — Old Light demands emotional and moral acknowledgment of cost, The Order demands formal procedural compliance. These produce very different governance cultures when mercy, exceptions, and the treatment of conquered populations become active questions.

**The Order vs. Blood Dominion:** The most functional dark-doctrine cross-covenant pairing. Both accept that power requires cost, that hierarchy has obligations, that law and sacrifice are mechanisms for maintaining what would otherwise dissolve. Cooperation is achievable between two dark-doctrine dynasties of these faiths because neither will try to moralize the other out of their methods. The conflict emerges when their frameworks make competing claims about the same territory: Blood Dominion wants to sacrifice it, The Order wants to govern it. These are not reconcilable at scale.

**The Order vs. The Wild:** The deepest structural incompatibility in the game, more fundamental even than Old Light vs. The Wild. The Order holds that civilization and its laws are the highest achievement available to human beings. The Wild holds that civilization is a temporary arrangement that the world tolerates conditionally and will eventually end. The Order's entire theology is a refutation of the Wild's, and vice versa. Two dynasties of these faiths cannot coexist at high faith intensity without one actively working to eliminate the other's cultural presence.

## THE WILD — Covenant of the Thorned Elder Roots

### What This Faith Is

The Wild does not hold that the divine is memory, or covenant, or law. It holds that the divine is the fact of the world — the indifferent, total, prior-to-everything fact of existence before human beings arrived and began naming things and building structures and deciding that their arrangements were important. The Great Frost was not punishment, not consequence, not abandonment. It was the world asserting what it always was: larger than what had been built inside it, indifferent to the buildings, patient in the way that things are patient when they do not die. The civilizations that fell to the Frost were not being punished. They were being reminded. The Wild's theology is built on this reminder, and it is the most ancient faith in the game precisely because it requires the least from the world to remain true. The forest does not need to believe in The Wild for The Wild's theology to be correct.

The Wild makes an offer to its followers that no other faith makes: it does not promise that your civilization will survive. It does not promise that what you build will last. It promises that if you understand your relationship to the world correctly — if you acknowledge what you are and what the world is, if you take what the world provides without pretending you own it, if you contribute to the living system you exist within rather than simply extracting from it — then you will be part of something larger than your dynasty, and that something will outlast you even when you do not. The light doctrine builds toward a kind of sacred participation in the world's life. The dark doctrine concludes that participation is for the weak, that natural law is predation, and that winter is not cruel because cruelty requires intention and winter has none.

### Light Doctrine — The Living Compact

**The doctrine's logic:**

A dynasty walking the Living Compact path has accepted that the world is a partner rather than a resource. Its territories are managed the way a person manages a relationship — you take, and you give back, and the accounting is not purely financial. The faith's rituals are structured as exchanges: you release something into the natural world, and the world responds. You take something from what it provides, and you acknowledge the taking formally. You mark territory not with walls but with living things — planted groves, maintained waterways, designated resting territories for animals. These acts are not conservation in the modern ecological sense. They are covenant-keeping in the oldest sense: the dynasty has made an agreement with the ground it stands on, and the ground is aware of the agreement.

This doctrine's military and strategic vulnerability is its geography-dependence. The Living Compact's strongest bonuses are generated within territories the dynasty has genuinely maintained over time — territories with active Grove Markers, Spirit Lodges functioning at full capacity, Thornrider units that have been stationed in the territory long enough to know its patterns. A Living Compact dynasty that has been in a territory for 20 cycles fights very differently in that territory than a dynasty that arrived 5 cycles ago. This is intentional. The faith rewards depth of presence over breadth of conquest.

Additionally, Wild faith spread through adjacent territories accelerates: the health and productivity of the dynasty's territory is visible to neighboring populations as evidence of what the faith produces.
- Sustained Apex (10+ cycles): The Breathing Land activates. Home territory generates 1% faith intensity per cycle passively without any investment — the covenant has reached a state of self-sustenance. Population in home territory does not experience resource scarcity penalties during seasonal events because the covenant has formally aligned the dynasty's resource cycle with the territory's production cycle.

**Spiritual manifestation — The Waking Green (Fervent+):**

The Waking Green manifests when The Wild reaches Fervent intensity on the light doctrine path and at least two Grove Markers have been actively maintained in the territory for 15 or more cycles. What arrives is ecological, not supernatural in any conventional sense. Vegetation in the territory appears to accelerate — vines move visibly along defensive structures, roots appear along paths where there were no roots, animal presence intensifies. This is not dramatic. It is steady and comprehensive, the way a forest demonstrates its indifference to what happens at the scale of a battle.

Mechanically, the Waking Green activates all terrain bonuses in the territory at 150% normal value. Forest cover grants +30% rather than +20% defensive bonus. Elevated terrain increases movement cost for non-Wild units by an additional 15%. Any enemy unit that enters dense natural terrain in the territory suffers a 3-hex vision reduction that lasts until they exit the terrain. Wild units — Thornriders, Bear Riders, Forest Sovereigns — gain complete immunity to terrain movement penalties for the duration.

Duration: 12 cycles. Ends if both anchor Grove Markers are destroyed or faith intensity drops below Fervent. The manifestation cannot be directly attacked. Enemy forces can end it by destroying the Grove Markers, which requires finding them first.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Thornrider (Level 3, Light Doctrine)**
The Thornrider is the living expression of the Living Compact's covenant with motion — a mounted unit that exists in continuous relationship with the territory it moves through, not as a traveler but as a recognized presence.
- Territory Reading: In any territory where the dynasty has an active Grove Marker, the Thornrider gains +15% all stats. This is not a bonus granted by the building. It is the building's presence indicating that the Thornrider belongs there.
- The Quiet Approach: Thornriders in natural terrain do not trigger enemy detection until they are within 2 hexes of the detecting unit, regardless of the detecting unit's normal detection radius. They move with the patterns of the territory rather than against them.
- Grove Bonding: When a Thornrider is stationed in a territory for 3 consecutive cycles, they establish a passive bond with that territory. If attacked in their bonded territory, they gain a one-time +20% combat effectiveness surge — the territory's awareness of the attack is shared with the rider.

**Forest Sovereign (Level 4, Light Doctrine)**
The Forest Sovereign has spent enough time in the covenant relationship that the relationship has changed them. Their connection to territory is not a tactical bonus. It is something else.
- Living Armor: The Forest Sovereign's defense increases by 1% per cycle they spend in natural terrain, up to a maximum of 20%. This accumulation resets if they leave natural terrain for 3 cycles. The covenant of sustained presence produces sustained return.
- Sovereign Sight: Within any territory designated as the dynasty's Living Compact territory, the Forest Sovereign can see all units regardless of fog of war. The territory knows what passes through it and shares what it knows.
- Grove Call: Once per battle, the Forest Sovereign calls the territory's cooperation. For 3 cycles, all terrain bonuses in the battle's location are calculated at 200% normal value. Enemy units in terrain penalty states during this period suffer double the standard penalty. The territory has been formally requested to assist, and it has agreed.

**Apex unit — The First Wild (Light Doctrine expression):**
The Light Doctrine First Wild appears as something between a rider and a grove — the figure is mounted, armored, but the armor has become partially incorporated with natural material, branches and living growth wound through the metal, the mount an animal so old that the distinction between wild and tamed has ceased to be meaningful. This unit looks like what happens to a person when they have honored the covenant long enough that the covenant has begun to honor them back.

Stats profile: Moderate offense, high mobility, very high terrain advantage multipliers. The First Wild does not fight best in open field. It fights best where the dynasty has been. In a territory with 20+ cycles of Grove presence, The First Wild's combat effectiveness is 45% higher than its base stats — the living land amplifies it. Out of such territory, it is a capable apex unit. In its home ground, it is nearly unbeatable.

Unique ability — The Grove's Memory: Once per match, The First Wild invokes the full memory of the dynasty's covenant relationship with all territory it has ever maintained a Grove in. For 10 cycles, all allied units in any Living Compact territory gain the Breathing Land's full defensive benefits regardless of whether they are in natural terrain. The territory everywhere the dynasty has tended is active simultaneously. The Waking Green manifests in all of these territories simultaneously for the duration.

### Dark Doctrine — The Consuming Wild

**The doctrine's logic:**

The Consuming Wild has heard the Living Compact's theology and decided that half of it is sentiment. The world is indifferent. The Wild's theology is built on that truth. The Consuming Wild path takes that truth to its complete form: if the world is indifferent, and if indifference is the law, and if winter kills the weak and strengthens the herd, then mercy is not a virtue — it is a refusal to participate honestly in what the world is. The forest does not preserve the lame deer. The flood does not spare the poorly positioned. The strong consume the weak because that is the mechanism through which the living world maintains itself, and a civilization that replicates this mechanism is not cruel. It is accurate.

This doctrine produces dynasties of genuine military ferocity — the Consuming Wild is the most aggressive faith path in the game in its territory control mechanics, because the faith frames expansion and predation as theologically correct rather than as moral compromises. What it produces in exchange for this ferocity is a civilization with no mechanism for the kind of internal solidarity that other faiths generate. The Consuming Wild population is not bonded by covenant, or law, or shared memory. It is bonded by strength. The moment the dynasty shows weakness, the binding unravels.

**Rituals:**

**The Culling**
- Building required: Grove Marker (Tier 1) or higher, Bear Rider unit present
- Who participates: Bear Rider oversees; the designated population — the dynasty's weakest settlement, determined by lowest productivity and loyalty metrics, is selected — contributes its least productive members as the ritual material; no ceremony beyond the act itself
- What is consumed: The designated individuals. The settlement's population decreases. 15 Rare Materials (the markers placed to formally designate the cull zone), 20 Gold (for the Bear Rider unit's deployment and logistics). The dynasty's food production improves because the population consuming resources has been reduced.
- Duration: 2 cycles.
- What happens to participants: The Bear Rider unit carries the Culling Mark for 10 cycles — all combat effectiveness +18%. The remaining population in the culled settlement enters a Survival Hierarchy state: loyalty is replaced by a dominance compliance metric that measures whether the population believes the dynasty is the strongest available authority. It will hold as long as the dynasty is winning.
- Mechanical effect: Food production improves 8% in the territory as resource pressure decreases. Faith intensity +7%. All military units in the territory gain +12% combat morale for 10 cycles. The world has been shown that this dynasty understands what the world is.

**The Pack Rite**
- Building required: Spirit Lodge (Tier 2) or higher
- Who participates: Senior Consuming Wild priest; all combat units currently stationed in the territory; the prey — captive enemy soldiers or designated local population as material; Bear Rider or Bonebreaker unit leads the rite
- What is consumed: 35 Provisions, 25 Rare Materials (sacred predator markings applied to participating units), 20 Gold. The prey is consumed within the rite — not symbolically.
- Duration: 2 cycles.
- What happens to participants: All participating combat units gain the Pack-Marked status for 12 cycles — +15% damage, +10% movement speed, and the ability to coordinate attacks without movement penalty (the pack moves as one organism). The priest who conducts the rite gains the Apex status for 8 cycles — their faith intensity generation doubles.
- Mechanical effect: Faith intensity +10%. All Pack-Marked units function as a coordinated formation even when geographically dispersed — they share a morale pool during Pack-Marked duration rather than individual morale meters. This makes them resistant to morale manipulation (you cannot break half the pack's morale without breaking all of it) but vulnerable to a single concentrated morale collapse (if the pack breaks, all Pack-Marked units rout simultaneously).

**The Territory Claim**
- Building required: Spirit Lodge (Tier 2) or higher
- Who participates: Bonebreaker unit leads; the dynasty's military force physically occupies the territory; population of the occupied territory is not consulted
- What is consumed: 40 Gold, 30 Rare Materials (Consuming Wild territory markers — these are predator markers, not living materials), 25 Labor. The Bonebreaker unit is engaged in the territory for the duration.
- Duration: 3 cycles.
- What happens to participants: The Bonebreaker unit carries the Apex Claim for the territory permanently — +15% combat effectiveness in this specific territory for the remainder of the match. Any enemy unit entering the territory triggers an automatic detection event immediately (the markers have saturated the territory's awareness with a different signal than the Living Compact's gentle detection).
- Mechanical effect: The territory is formally claimed under the Consuming Wild's authority. Enemy populations in the territory immediately begin faith conversion toward the Wild at +20% rate — not through attraction but through the understanding that the alternative is being the next subject of the Culling. Faith intensity +9%.

**The Apex Feast**
- Building required: The First Grove (Tier 3)
- Who participates: Full Consuming Wild clergy; all apex predator units (Bear Riders, Bonebreakers) present in the territory; a mass of captives serves as the ritual's material; a Bloodline Member must preside
- What is consumed: 75 Provisions, 55 Rare Materials, 45 Gold, 35 Faith Currency. And the captives. The Apex Feast is the Consuming Wild's most definitive ritual statement.
- Duration: 4 cycles, during which the territory generates Horror events visible to all neighboring factions.
- What happens to participants: All Bonebreaker and Bear Rider units in the territory gain the Feast-Blessed status for 25 cycles — +20% all combat stats, immunity to morale reduction from any source, health regeneration 5% per cycle during combat. The Bloodline Member who presides carries the Apex Mark permanently for this match.
- Mechanical effect: The territory's faith intensity reaches maximum possible value for 15 cycles. All Wild units across the entire dynasty gain +10% combat effectiveness for 15 cycles — the Feast's spiritual impact radiates outward. Horror events generated reach 16 hexes, affecting all neighbors simultaneously. Faith intensity +20%.

**Passive doctrine effects:**

- Latent (0-20%): No doctrine effects.
- Active (20-40%): Combat units gain +5% damage when fighting in natural terrain. The Culling option becomes available. Bear Rider units are available.
- Devout (40-60%): Enemy populations within 2 hexes of Consuming Wild territory begin experiencing conversion pressure not through attraction but through proximity to something that has demonstrated it can consume them. The conversion rate is slow but persistent. Capturing enemy units generates partial faith intensity as a passive — the covenant is fed by predatory success.
- Fervent (60-80%): Pack Rite costs 25% fewer resources. All combat units gain +8% movement speed in natural terrain. Routing enemy units within Consuming Wild territory are automatically hunted by any available Wild unit without command input — the hunting instinct has been mechanically formalized.
- Apex (80-100%): Territory claimed under the Consuming Wild generates passive predation pressure on all adjacent unclaimed territory — enemy resource production within 3 hexes of Consuming Wild territory decreases 5% per cycle as the covenant's presence diminishes what the adjacent land is willing to provide for those not part of the compact. All Bonebreaker units gain their full combat package permanently.
- Sustained Apex (10+ cycles): The Feeding Ground activates. Every military engagement victory in home territory generates an automatic faith intensity bonus of 2% — the land has registered the kill and fed on it. The distinction between war and ritual has been collapsed.

**Spiritual manifestation — The Cold (Fervent+):**

The Cold does not announce itself. It arrives as a temperature drop in the affected territory — perceptible, consistent, and deepening over the first 3 cycles following manifestation. There is no visual entity. There is no sound. There is winter, in a localized and immediate form, and the territory has gone still the way territory goes still before predation.

Mechanically, The Cold imposes a cold-weather penalty on all non-Wild units in the affected territory — -8% movement speed, -5% morale, and +10% resource consumption for food (the units are cold, and cold bodies require more to maintain combat effectiveness). Wild units are immune to all Cold effects. The Cold persists as a territorial condition for 15 cycles and generates a passive secondary effect: enemy units that spend 5 or more cycles within The Cold without being engaged in combat suffer a stack of Winter-Touched, reducing their maximum health ceiling 5% per stack. The Wild did not create this. It invited it. The Frost was always there.

Duration: 15 cycles. Unlike most manifestations, The Cold cannot be ended by destroying a building — the cold does not live in a building. It can only be countered by a sustained Old Light Great Blessing or a Blood Dominion Grand Rite of sufficient scale, which produce enough warmth and violence respectively to displace it. Counter-ritual effort from either faith takes 5 cycles to complete.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Bonebreaker (Level 3, Dark Doctrine)**
The Bonebreaker is the Consuming Wild's predator specialist — a rider trained specifically to isolate, pursue, and eliminate the weakest element of any opposing force.
- Predator Sight: At the start of any engagement, the Bonebreaker automatically identifies the enemy unit with the lowest current health percentage in the area. This unit is marked as prey — the Bonebreaker deals +25% damage to it specifically.
- Pursuit Mastery: When an enemy unit routs, the Bonebreaker gains +30% movement speed for 3 cycles and prioritizes pursuit automatically. Routed units cannot escape the Bonebreaker through movement alone.
- The Culling Blow: Once per battle, the Bonebreaker performs a targeted kill attack on a unit below 20% health, bypassing normal combat resolution. The targeted unit is eliminated without a combat roll. This can be used on any unit below the threshold, including Bloodline Members if they are present on the field and meet the condition.

**Bear Rider (Level 4, Dark Doctrine)**
The Bear Rider is the Consuming Wild's apex mounted unit — a rider bonded to a bear through the Consuming Wild's animal covenant, which is different from the Living Compact's gentle grove-bonding in every way that matters.
- Pack Alpha: All Bonebreaker units within 5 hexes of the Bear Rider gain +15% combat effectiveness. They are the pack. The Bear Rider is what they orient toward.
- Crushing Advance: The Bear Rider's charge attack ignores the target's defensive terrain bonus entirely — mountains, forest, fortifications. The bear does not recognize human arrangements as obstacles.
- Winter Coat: The Bear Rider is immune to all cold, weather, and environmental effects, including those generated by enemy faith manifestations. They are already what The Cold is trying to make everything else.

**Apex unit — The First Wild (Dark Doctrine expression):**
The Dark Doctrine First Wild does not have the living material wound through its armor. The armor is stripped and functional, and the mount is not an animal that has been in relationship with a dynasty for generations — it is something older, something that agreed to carry a rider not through covenant but through recognition of equivalent predatory status. This figure and this mount share the same understanding of what the world is, and both have acted on it for long enough that acting on it has become their nature.

Stats profile: Significantly higher base damage than the light doctrine version, exchanging terrain-specific bonuses for raw offensive capability. The Dark Doctrine First Wild fights at full effectiveness regardless of territory type — it does not need to be where the dynasty has been. It is the apex predator. The territory it stands on becomes its territory by the act of standing on it.

Unique ability — The Purge: Once per match, The First Wild initiates a predation sweep across a territory. All enemy units below 40% health in the territory are immediately killed without combat resolution — the sweep takes them before the battle formally closes on them. This generates faith intensity equal to 2% per unit eliminated, and activates The Cold manifestation in the territory immediately regardless of faith intensity threshold. The First Wild did not bring the Frost. The Frost has always been here. The Wild simply stopped keeping it back.

## Cross-Covenant Mechanics

### Faith Alliance

When two dynasties of the same faith form a formal Faith Alliance, the shared covenant activates effects beyond what either dynasty produces individually. These effects are calibrated to whether the allied dynasties share the same doctrine path or hold different paths within the same faith.

**Same-covenant, same-doctrine alignment:**
Both allied dynasties gain a 15% faith intensity bonus that does not require ritual or building investment — the presence of a fellow practitioner at the same depth of doctrine is itself spiritually reinforcing. All rituals conducted by either dynasty within 8 hexes of the border region between their territories cost 10% fewer resources. Spiritual manifestations of the same type can propagate across the border: if one dynasty's manifestation is active and the allied dynasty reaches Fervent, the manifestation expands into the allied territory for 3 cycles at 50% effect rather than requiring a separate manifestation trigger.

**Same-covenant, different-doctrine alignment:**
The Faith Alliance provides only the baseline diplomatic bonuses between allied dynasties. No spiritual reinforcement activates — a Remembrance Compact dynasty and a Purging Flame dynasty reading the same texts and reaching different conclusions are not spiritually unified, even if they are politically cooperative. Tension events are generated periodically (every 15 cycles) as the doctrine difference creates friction around specific policy decisions. If the tension events are left unresolved, the alliance weakens without formal dissolution — it degrades rather than breaks cleanly, which creates governance uncertainty.

**Holy War multiplier:**
Any Faith Alliance active during a jointly declared Holy War against a third party receives an additional +10% combat morale bonus for both allied armies in every engagement against the target dynasty, stacking with all existing faith bonuses. The shared enemy clarifies what the shared faith is for.

### Holy War

Holy War is a formal faith-state declaration, not simply a political conflict. When a dynasty declares Holy War, the following mechanics activate beyond the standard war state:

**Faith intensity generation:**
Every military engagement that occurs after a Holy War declaration generates passive faith intensity at 2% per cycle for the declaring dynasty, regardless of outcome. The act of fighting in the name of the covenant sustains it.

**Conviction interaction:**
Atrocities committed during Holy War generate 30% less Conviction penalty for the declaring dynasty — the faith framework absorbs some of the moral cost of warfare by declaring it sanctioned. This is not a removal of accountability. It is a delay. The suppressed Conviction debt accumulates and releases in a single event if the Holy War ends in a loss or a drawn outcome — the faith's promise of divine sanction was not honored, and the population's reckoning with what was done in its name arrives all at once.

**Unit behavior:**
All faith units of the declaring dynasty gain +15% combat morale during Holy War for the duration. Non-faith units gain +5%. Units of the targeted dynasty that share the declaring dynasty's covenant (same faith, fighting each other in a schismatic Holy War) suffer -15% morale — they are fighting people who hold the same texts and the same gods, and the theology cannot easily explain why.

**Population conversion:**
Territories conquered during an active Holy War experience 40% accelerated faith conversion toward the conquering dynasty's faith. The population has been told the conquest was sanctioned. Some believe it.

**Termination:**
Holy War ends with either the military conclusion of the conflict or a formal religious truce brokered by a neutral dynasty at Devout or higher faith intensity. A religious truce carries its own diplomatic weight — the broker gains significant neutral faction goodwill.

### Faith Conversion

When a dynasty conquers a territory, the local population's faith does not change overnight. Conversion is a mechanics model, not an instant switch. The method available to the conquering dynasty depends on its doctrine path.

**Light doctrine conversion:**
Available to all four faiths' light paths. Light doctrine conversion operates through presence, performance, and demonstrated benefit. Faith buildings generate passive conversion pressure proportional to their tier and the dynasty's faith intensity level. Rituals conducted at high visibility generate conversion events that offer the local population the choice to participate. Conversion through light doctrine is slow — typically 15-25 cycles to shift majority faith affiliation in a reluctant population — but it is stable. Populations converted through light doctrine methods are less likely to revert upon conquest by a different dynasty because the conversion produced genuine cultural change rather than surface compliance.

Specific methods by faith:
- Old Light light doctrine: Names the local dead in Old Light rites — the population's grief and memory are incorporated into the covenant. Highly effective in populations that have experienced recent losses.
- Blood Dominion light doctrine: Offers the Covenant Bond to local leaders — shared sacrifice rituals that invite participation rather than imposing it. Effective among military populations who respond to the offer of belonging through cost.
- Order light doctrine: Establishes legal infrastructure that benefits the local population — dispute resolution, property protection, formal rights. Effective in populations experiencing disorder or arbitrary rule.
- Wild light doctrine: Maintains and improves local natural resources — the living compact produces visible territory health that the local population can observe. Effective in agricultural and pastoral populations with strong land connection.

**Dark doctrine conversion:**
Available to all four faiths' dark paths. Dark doctrine conversion operates through suppression of the existing faith and systematic replacement. The timeline is faster — typically 8-12 cycles to achieve nominal compliance — but the conversion is brittle. A nominally converted population under dark doctrine is one crisis away from reversion if the suppressing dynasty shows weakness. Additionally, forcibly converted populations generate ongoing Conviction pressure on the ruling dynasty at 1% per cycle per converted settlement, representing the sustained moral cost of maintained suppression.

Specific methods by faith:
- Old Light dark doctrine: Inquisitor's Examination followed by Purging Rite. Eliminates alternative faith practice, generates compliance through the demonstrated cost of heterodoxy. Produces clean metrics with simmering resentment beneath.
- Blood Dominion dark doctrine: Tithe and Convocation conducted publicly in the converted territory. The local population understands that the covenant's demands apply to them now. Compliance follows from the demonstration.
- Order dark doctrine: Mandate Assertion followed by Formal Suppression. The law is declared, the non-compliant are processed, the remainder is governed. The cleanest institutional mechanism in the game.
- Wild dark doctrine: The Culling conducted in the new territory, followed by Territory Claim markers. The population's weakest members are removed and the remainder understands what the faith is. Fastest compliance generation in the game; shortest stability duration.

### Faith Intensity Decay — The Exact Model

Faith is not a resource that holds at whatever level it reaches. It requires continuous maintenance. These are the specific decay mechanics:

**Base decay rate by tier:**
- Latent (0-20%): No decay. The faith exists as ambient cultural presence and is self-sustaining at minimal intensity.
- Active (20-40%): 0.5% decay per cycle if no faith building is actively maintained. Maintaining a Tier 1 building at minimum upkeep prevents all decay at this tier.
- Devout (40-60%): 1% decay per cycle against base. Requires a Tier 1 building at full upkeep OR a Tier 2 building at minimum upkeep to prevent decay. At minimum upkeep, intensity stabilizes but cannot grow.
- Fervent (60-80%): 1.5% decay per cycle against base. Requires a Tier 2 building at full upkeep OR a Tier 3 building at minimum upkeep to prevent decay. Additionally, requires at least one ritual to have been completed within the past 10 cycles — without recent ritual activity, decay rate doubles to 3% per cycle.
- Apex (80-100%): 2% decay per cycle against base. Requires a Tier 3 building at full upkeep and at least one major ritual completed within the past 8 cycles. Additionally requires a Bloodline Member in an active faith role — if no Member is serving as a faith leader (light doctrine) or covenant officiant (dark doctrine), decay rate triples to 6% per cycle.

**Minimum maintenance investment by tier:**
- Tier 1 building minimum upkeep: 5 Gold per cycle, 3 Provisions per cycle
- Tier 2 building minimum upkeep: 12 Gold per cycle, 8 Provisions per cycle, 4 Rare Materials per cycle
- Tier 3 building minimum upkeep: 22 Gold per cycle, 15 Provisions per cycle, 8 Rare Materials per cycle

**Accelerated decay triggers:**
- Faith building destroyed: -15% faith intensity immediately plus doubled decay rate for 8 cycles in the affected territory
- Bloodline Member faith leader killed or removed from role: -8% intensity immediately plus +1% additional decay per cycle for 10 cycles
- Covenant ritual disrupted mid-execution: -3% to -8% faith intensity depending on ritual tier; see individual ritual entries
- Enemy dark doctrine manifestation active in territory: +50% decay rate for the duration of the manifestation
- Dynasty Conviction drops to Cruel while holding light doctrine path: +1% additional decay per cycle per Cruel tier increment below neutral — the faith and the conviction are misaligned, and the population begins to notice
- Dynasty Conviction rises to Moral while holding dark doctrine path: +1% additional decay per cycle per Moral tier increment above neutral — same misalignment in the opposite direction
- Territory under active military occupation by enemy force: Decay rate doubles for every cycle of continuous occupation
- Faith building upkeep defaulted for 3+ cycles: Faith intensity drops to the ceiling of the next lower tier and stabilizes there, requiring a major ritual to recover
