# PROPOSED / CREATIVE BRANCH 002 — NOT YET CANON
# Design Content — 2026-03-18, Creative Branch 002

---

This document designs the complete faith doctrine mechanics for all four covenants: Old Light, Blood Dominion, The Order, and The Wild. Both doctrine paths — Light and Dark — are fully designed for each covenant, including rituals, passive effects, spiritual manifestations, unit abilities, apex unit mechanics, faith spread behavior, and cross-covenant interactions. Cross-covenant mechanics follow the four covenant entries.

## Old Light

### Core Identity

Old Light is a faith of preservation and witness. It does not believe the divine instructs the living so much as it watches them — a vast, patient attention that has seen everything that has ever been done and remembers it all. Flame is the primary symbol not because it destroys but because it illuminates: fire shows what is hidden, burns away shadow, and lets the dead be seen one final time before they pass into the record. To follow Old Light is to be someone who understands that what is done cannot be undone, only remembered or forgotten, honored or betrayed. The faith's central promise is continuity: your dynasty will not be erased. The dead will be remembered. The living will carry forward what was given to them.

The tension at the heart of Old Light is that its theology of remembrance contains within it a theology of accountability. If the light sees everything, then nothing is hidden. If nothing is hidden, then the wicked cannot escape into anonymity. The dark doctrine does not invent cruelty — it inherits it from the same source the light doctrine inherits mercy. Both ask: what does the light do when it finds something that should not be allowed to persist? The light doctrine answers: illuminate it, heal it, bring it forward. The dark doctrine answers: burn it out.

### Light Doctrine Path — The Remembrance Path

**What this doctrine believes:**

A dynasty walking the Remembrance Path holds that the divine presence in the world is fundamentally an act of witnessing. The sacred flame does not consume — it reveals. When an Old Light priest lights a candle at the bedside of the dying, the meaning of the gesture is not comfort in a vague sense but a specific theological claim: this person is being seen. Their life is entering the record. They will not be forgotten. This doctrine produces dynasties that tend to be unusually loyal to their populations because they believe their people are held in permanent divine attention. Abandoning them, exploiting them past reckoning, or leaving their sacrifices unacknowledged is not merely political foolishness — it is a form of sacrilege, an act of willful forgetting in the presence of a god whose nature is to remember.

The relationship between this doctrine and its enemies is shaped by the same principle. An Old Light dynasty on the Remembrance Path does not despise its enemies — it acknowledges them. Enemy dead may be given remembrance rites, which to other factions can look like weakness but functions as a form of moral authority: we honor even those who came against us, because they too are witnessed. This posture creates genuine diplomatic weight. It also creates genuine vulnerability. A dynasty that commits atrocities cannot hide from its own theology.

**Ritual mechanics:**

**The Kindling of Names**
- Building required: Wayshrine (Tier 1) or higher
- Resource cost: 20 Timber (fuel for the flame), 10 Provisions (offerings of sustenance), 5 Rare Materials (inscribed memorial plates)
- Time to complete: 2 cycles
- Mechanical effect: All Bloodline Members who died in the previous 10 cycles are formally entered into the remembrance record. Each entered member generates a passive +3 Conviction stability for the ruling dynasty for the next 15 cycles, stacking per member. Living Bloodline Members who witness the rite receive a temporary morale aura that spreads to units within their command radius for 5 cycles.
- Faith intensity change: +4% per rite performed, maximum once per 5 cycles
- Interruption: If the Wayshrine is attacked during the rite, the rite fails and the faith intensity change is reversed to -2% (the light was disrupted, an act of desecration)

**Vigil of the Long Flame**
- Building required: Hall of Remembrance (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 40 Timber, 30 Provisions, 20 Rare Materials, 15 Labor (for the vigil-keepers)
- Time to complete: 5 cycles (a continuous vigil, not a single event)
- Mechanical effect: For the duration of the vigil and 10 cycles afterward, all injured units recovering in home territory heal at 40% increased rate. Population grief events (triggered by war deaths) have their conviction cost halved — the population has been given a formal space to grieve rather than carrying loss privately. If completed without interruption, one new Flame Warden unit is generated at no material cost.
- Faith intensity change: +8% on completion
- Interruption: If interrupted before cycle 3, the vigil produces no effect and costs resources. If interrupted between cycles 3 and 5, partial effect: half the healing bonus applies for 5 cycles only. Enemy raiding parties specifically targeting the Hall of Remembrance can interrupt the vigil.

**The Recitation of the Fallen**
- Building required: The Eternal Cathedral (Tier 3)
- Resource cost: 60 Provisions, 40 Rare Materials, 30 Gold (for the illuminated scrolls), 10 Faith Currency (canonical name TBD — represents accumulated devotion of the population)
- Time to complete: 1 cycle
- Mechanical effect: All enemy factions bordering Old Light territory are subject to a diplomatic effect called "The Witnessing" for 10 cycles. Enemy Bloodline Members who die fighting Old Light forces during this period are recorded by the Cathedral scribes. This has no immediate mechanical effect but creates a Diplomatic Leverage token that can be used to propose non-aggression agreements at doubled acceptance probability — the implication being that Old Light will remember what its enemies did, and also what they refrained from doing. Additionally, all Old Light units gain +5% combat morale for 10 cycles, not because they are enraged but because they understand they are part of something being recorded.
- Faith intensity change: +10% on completion
- Interruption: Cannot be interrupted once begun — the Recitation is a purely internal, sacred act. However, if The Eternal Cathedral is destroyed within 5 cycles of the rite, all effects are negated and faith intensity suffers -15%.

**The Rite of Ash Return**
- Building required: Hall of Remembrance (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 15 Rare Materials, 25 Provisions (funeral provisions), 10 Timber
- Time to complete: 1 cycle
- Mechanical effect: This rite is performed immediately following a military defeat in which significant unit losses occurred. It formally acknowledges what was lost. Mechanically: prevents the Conviction penalty associated with military defeat from reaching its full value — the rite absorbs 40% of the incoming Conviction damage, distributing it across a longer decay period rather than hitting immediately. The population experiences the loss as tragedy rather than humiliation. Additionally, surviving units from the defeated force who were present for the rite receive +15% combat resolve for the next 5 cycles.
- Faith intensity change: +3% (the faith is being exercised in adversity, which strengthens it)
- Interruption: If the territory is under active siege, the rite cannot be performed. It requires a minimum of 2 cycles without combat in the ritual territory.

**Passive doctrine effects:**

- At Active (20-40%): Population in home territory generates slow background grief recovery. Conviction losses from natural disasters, famine events, and plague events are reduced by 15% because the faith gives the population a framework for loss rather than leaving it raw.
- At Devout (40-60%): Injured units in home territory heal 25% faster. Bloodline Members who survive battles in which other Members died gain a persistent Witness trait that gives them +10% to all Conviction-affecting decisions for 20 cycles.
- At Fervent (60-80%): All remembrance rites cost 20% fewer resources. Enemy propaganda and faith-corruption mechanics targeting Old Light populations have their conversion rate halved — the Remembrance Path population has a stable identity anchor that resists replacement. A passive aura around faith buildings causes enemy units within 3 hexes to suffer -5% morale (the weight of being watched).
- At Apex (80-100%): The Eternal Cathedral, if built, passively generates diplomatic memory records of all military actions taken against Old Light by all factions. These records are visible as in-game logs and can be presented to neutral factions as evidence in diplomatic negotiations — a unique information-asymmetry mechanic. Additionally, all Old Light units gain the passive trait "Remembered" which means if they die in combat, their name enters the cathedral record and adjacent living units receive +10% combat morale for 3 cycles.
- Sustained Apex for 10+ cycles: A dynasty-wide trait "Dynasty of Witness" activates. All Bloodline Members gain access to a once-per-lifetime ability to call on "The Record" — invoking the full history of their dynasty's deeds to sway a single Conviction check or diplomatic action with overwhelming historical weight. This cannot be faked or purchased.

**Spiritual manifestation (Fervent+ eligible):**

**The Choir of the Witnessed**

When Old Light reaches Fervent intensity and at least one major battle (defined as a battle involving 20+ units and resulting in 10+ deaths) has occurred in home territory within the past 15 cycles, the Choir manifests. Visually, it appears as a slow bloom of golden light above the Hall of Remembrance or The Eternal Cathedral — not fire, not lightning, but a warm, diffuse illumination that seems to come from everywhere and nowhere, accompanied by a sound like many voices speaking quietly over each other, words indistinct but clearly meaningful.

Mechanically, the Choir is not a combat entity. It is a presence. All units in the same territory as the Choir — own and enemy alike — have their combat morale frozen at its current value. Routing units stop routing. Zealous overextension stops. Both sides fight at exactly the resolve they entered the battle with. This is the Remembrance Path's vision of what the divine does: it holds things steady, it witnesses the struggle, it does not intervene to make one side win. The effect is profound in battles that are turning due to morale collapse — it rescues a routing Old Light army from dissolution. It is also maddening for an enemy that was winning through psychological pressure.

The Choir lasts for 8 cycles after manifestation. It ends if The Eternal Cathedral or Hall of Remembrance is destroyed, or if the faith intensity drops below Fervent. It cannot be directly attacked. Enemy dark doctrine rituals of sufficient intensity can disrupt it.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Flame Warden (Level 3, Light Doctrine)**
The Flame Warden carries the sacred torch of the Hall of Remembrance — not as a weapon primarily but as a standard. Unique abilities:
- Vigil Aura: While the Flame Warden is alive and in formation, all units within 4 hexes gain +10% morale stability. This is specifically a stability effect, not a bonus — it prevents morale from dropping below a floor rather than raising the ceiling.
- Last Rites: When any allied unit dies within 2 hexes of the Flame Warden, the Flame Warden can spend one action to perform a battlefield rite. This generates no faith intensity but prevents the death from triggering the panic/grief propagation that typically ripples through nearby units when allies fall. The death is absorbed rather than radiated.
- Torch of Witness: Once per battle, the Flame Warden can designate an enemy unit as Witnessed. That unit cannot benefit from stealth, ambush bonuses, or flanking bonuses for the remainder of the battle — they have been seen.

**Sunblade Champion (Level 4, Light Doctrine)**
The Sunblade Champion is a warrior-priest, not a pure combatant. Unique abilities:
- Purification Strike: The Sunblade Champion's attacks carry a faith-charged quality that is effective against spiritually corrupted units (dark doctrine units at high faith intensity, demon manifestations, units under curse effects). Against these targets, the Sunblade Champion deals 30% additional damage.
- Bearer of Remembrance: The Sunblade Champion passively extends the morale-stability radius of the Flame Warden and functions as a secondary anchor point for it even without a Flame Warden present, at half effectiveness.
- The Unquenched: If the Sunblade Champion is reduced to critical health but not killed, once per engagement they may spend their next action to recover 20% of maximum health. This ability is called "The Unquenched" as a named property on the unit card, and its visual expression is their sword-flame guttering then steadying. It cannot trigger if the unit is below 5% health — the flame cannot sustain itself on that little.

**Apex expression — The Unbroken (Light Doctrine path):**

The Unbroken is the ultimate expression of what the Remembrance Path believes: that the light endures. Not triumphant, not conquering — enduring. The unit manifests when the Covenant Test is completed along the Light Doctrine path. Visually, The Unbroken appears as a single figure in ash-grey armor that seems to be lit from within, carrying both a sword and a torch. The sword has never been sheathed — the implication being this warrior has been fighting since before anyone can remember, and the flame they carry has never gone out.

Stats profile: The Unbroken is a high-durability, moderate-offense unit. It is not the single most powerful combat entity on the field but it is nearly impossible to remove. Its health regenerates at 5% per cycle during combat as long as it is standing inside Old Light territory. Its morale never breaks — it literally cannot route. It is immune to fear, corruption, and curse effects. Enemy units that deal the killing blow to The Unbroken suffer a permanent trait called "Witnessed" for the remainder of the match, visible to all factions, marking them as the force that extinguished the immortal flame. This trait functions as a stigma in diplomatic contexts and carries -10% morale for those units against any Old Light force they subsequently face.

Unique ability — Keeper of the Record: Once per match, The Unbroken can invoke the full weight of Old Light history, generating an area effect around it equal to 6 hexes in which all allied units gain the Remembered trait for 10 cycles. All enemy units in the same radius suffer -20% morale for 10 cycles — not from fear of the warrior but from the oppressive awareness of everything they have done being observed, recorded, and never forgotten.

The Unbroken under a Light Doctrine path manifests with the Choir of the Witnessed active for its first 5 cycles on the field — the two spiritual phenomena are linked. It feels less like a weapon being deployed and more like the faith itself arriving to see what its followers have built.

### Dark Doctrine Path — The Purging Flame Path

**What this doctrine believes:**

A dynasty on the Purging Flame Path has arrived at a specific theological conclusion: the light does not merely illuminate. It judges. And judgment without consequence is not judgment at all — it is observation. If the divine sees everything, and if the divine is not indifferent, then the divine must have a position on what it sees. The Purging Flame doctrine says that position is not neutral. Heresy is not just error. Apostasy is not just disagreement. They are corruption, and corruption spreads, and the flame that illuminates the face of the corrupt must then make a choice: show mercy and allow the corruption to continue, or purge it so that what remains is clean.

This doctrine produces dynasties that are internally coherent to a degree that can become totalizing. There is no room for ambiguity in a Purging Flame court — you hold to the light or you are the problem that needs resolving. The faith becomes an instrument of social control that the dynasty believes, genuinely, is an act of mercy at scale: better that the few who are corrupt be removed than that the many who are clean become contaminated. The darkness of this doctrine is not that it is insincere. It is that it is completely sincere, and that sincerity is what makes it capable of atrocity.

**Ritual mechanics:**

**The Inquisitor's Finding**
- Building required: Hall of Remembrance (Tier 2) or higher, with an active Inquisitor unit present
- Resource cost: 20 Gold (for the inquisitorial apparatus), 15 Rare Materials (instruments of examination), 20 Labor (for the tribunal process)
- Time to complete: 3 cycles
- Mechanical effect: Initiates a formal inquisitorial examination of a specific population settlement. At completion, the settlement is classified as either Clean (no heresy found) or Marked (heresy found). A Clean settlement generates +5% faith intensity and a temporary loyalty bonus from the population who feel affirmed. A Marked settlement generates a choice: the dynasty may perform a Purging Rite (see below), exile the heretics (population reduction, they flee to neighboring territories potentially spreading faith disruption), or delay action (the Marked status persists and corrupts adjacent settlements at -3% faith intensity per cycle until resolved). Note: the outcome of the Finding has a random component influenced by how dark the current doctrine weight is. At extreme dark doctrine intensity, the Inquisitor's Finding will always find heresy — the instrument has become too eager.
- Faith intensity change: +5% if a Purging Rite follows within 5 cycles, +2% if the Finding returns Clean, -3% if the dynasty chooses to delay
- Interruption: If the Inquisitor unit is killed before the Finding completes, the process fails and generates a Conviction penalty (a failed inquisition suggests weakness or interference).

**The Purging Rite**
- Building required: Hall of Remembrance (Tier 2), must follow an Inquisitor's Finding with a Marked result
- Resource cost: 10 Timber (for the pyre), 5 Rare Materials, 30 Labor (the rite is intensive and requires significant personnel)
- Time to complete: 1 cycle
- Mechanical effect: Eliminates the Marked heresy from the targeted settlement. Generates +8% faith intensity. Population in the purged settlement suffers -25% happiness for 15 cycles but does not rebel unless Conviction of the ruling dynasty is already critically low — the purge is accepted as necessary by the remaining population if the dynasty is governing with sufficient Conviction weight. Adjacent enemy faction populations who have line of sight to the purge suffer -10% likelihood of voluntary faith conversion to Old Light (the purge is visible and frightening). However, Old Light Inquisitor units gain +15% combat morale for 10 cycles after a Purging Rite — they have been affirmed in their purpose.
- Faith intensity change: +8%
- Interruption: Cannot be interrupted once begun.

**The Writ of Unclean Declaration**
- Building required: The Eternal Cathedral (Tier 3)
- Resource cost: 40 Gold, 30 Rare Materials, 20 Faith Currency
- Time to complete: 2 cycles
- Mechanical effect: Formally declares an entire enemy dynasty as Unclean — a theological designation, not merely a political one. This declaration is broadcast via trade networks and diplomatic channels (mechanically, it costs no diplomatic actions to circulate). Effects: Old Light units fighting the declared dynasty gain +20% combat morale for 15 cycles. Neutral factions receive -15% likelihood of forming trade agreements with the declared dynasty (the stigma is real and has material consequence). Most significantly, any Old Light population in the declared dynasty's territory suffers zero faith-conversion pressure from that dynasty's own faith for the duration of the declaration — the Unclean designation provides a spiritual immunity of sorts, a wall of categorical rejection.
- Faith intensity change: +12% on completion
- Interruption: If The Eternal Cathedral is attacked during the rite, the Writ fails. The Cathedral itself takes additional symbolic damage and the faith intensity penalty is -5% rather than the neutral result of mere failure.

**The Cathedral Flame**
- Building required: The Eternal Cathedral (Tier 3)
- Resource cost: 50 Timber, 40 Gold, 30 Rare Materials, 20 Provisions (the flame must be fed)
- Time to complete: 3 cycles to establish, then maintained for up to 20 cycles
- Mechanical effect: Lights a Great Flame within the Cathedral that serves as a permanent beacon and spiritual weapon. Mechanically: all enemy units approaching within 5 hexes of the Cathedral suffer a passive -10% morale penalty (the flame is visible, it watches them, it judges them). Old Light Inquisitor and Purge Templar units based within 3 hexes of the Cathedral gain +15% base combat stats while the Flame burns. The Flame accelerates faith intensity generation by 15% for the duration. If the Cathedral is attacked and destroyed while the Flame burns, the destruction triggers a backlash event called "The Flame's Grievance" — all enemy units in the territory suffer a severe -25% morale penalty for 10 cycles, as if they have personally committed an act of terrible desecration.
- Faith intensity change: +5% on establishment, then passive +3% per cycle for the duration
- Interruption: The enemy can attempt to extinguish the Cathedral Flame by raiding the Cathedral territory. If they succeed, the faith intensity gain stops and the dynasty suffers -10% faith intensity as the extinguishing registers as a humiliation.

**Passive doctrine effects:**

- At Active (20-40%): Inquisitor units are available from faith buildings. Civilian populations in home territory generate faith intensity at 10% higher rate because the doctrine path creates social pressure that functions as enforcement — people practice their faith visibly.
- At Devout (40-60%): Populations with mixed faith composition (Old Light + another faith) experience accelerated conversion toward Old Light at +20% rate. The doctrinal pressure has reached a tipping point where social conformity begins working in the dynasty's favor. Heresy detection events trigger more frequently in settlements, generating more Inquisitor's Finding opportunities.
- At Fervent (60-80%): Purging Rites cost 30% fewer resources. All Old Light units gain a passive trait "Flame-Touched" that gives them immunity to enemy demoralizing unit abilities and propaganda effects. An enemy force that routs from a battle involving Purge Templar units does not simply flee — it scatters, making reorganization take 50% longer.
- At Apex (80-100%): A passive territory effect called "The Vigilance" applies to all home territory: any enemy unit that enters home territory and remains for more than 2 cycles is automatically detected even if it has stealth capabilities. The faith network has become a comprehensive observation web. Additionally, non-Old Light populations in home territory convert at doubled rate, and conversion is no longer fully voluntary — social pressure mechanics are replaced by active suppression mechanics.
- Sustained Apex for 10+ cycles: A dynasty-wide trait "Unclean Sight" activates. The dynasty can permanently see the faith intensity of all units on the battlefield, regardless of fog of war. Dark doctrine units of enemy faiths are visible as distinct tactical markers. This is the ultimate expression of the inquisitorial theology: the light sees everything.

**Spiritual manifestation (Fervent+ eligible):**

**The Consuming Witness**

Where the Remembrance Path's manifestation is the Choir — many voices, gentle, holding — the Purging Flame Path summons The Consuming Witness: a pillar of intense white fire that rises from the highest faith building in the territory, visible from anywhere on the map. It does not move. It burns. Visually it is blinding at the center with a golden-white core, and it generates visible heat distortion around it. There is no sound — unlike the Choir — except for a deep, continuous tone like a single bell struck and never allowed to fade.

Mechanically, The Consuming Witness is an area denial and attrition entity. Any non-Old Light unit within 4 hexes of the building it manifests at suffers -5% health per cycle from proximity. This is described in-game as "the weight of judgment" rather than as damage in a conventional sense. Old Light units within the same radius gain complete immunity to morale loss from any source for the duration. Units of the Inquisitor or Purge Templar class within the radius gain +25% all combat stats. The Consuming Witness lasts for 10 cycles or until the faith building it manifests at is destroyed. It cannot be targeted directly. Enemy dark doctrine manifestations (demons, nature entities) suffer double the proximity attrition when within range — the light is specifically harmful to things that exist within darkness.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Inquisitor (Level 3, Dark Doctrine)**
The Inquisitor is a specialist in identifying and eliminating spiritual contamination, and that function extends into combat in specific ways. Unique abilities:
- Heresy Sight: The Inquisitor can identify enemy faith units that are buffed by active rituals. These units glow with a distinct marker visible only to the controlling player, giving tactical information about which enemy units are currently benefiting from ritual effects.
- Interrogation: Once per battle, the Inquisitor can single out an enemy unit and direct concentrated attention at it. That unit suffers -20% to all stats for 5 cycles due to the psychological weight of being individually targeted. If the unit is a faith unit of a dark doctrine path, the penalty is -30%.
- Flame Judgement: The Inquisitor's attacks deal +15% damage to any unit designated as Unclean (from the Writ of Unclean Declaration) or any unit that belongs to an enemy dynasty currently under an Inquisitor's Finding result.

**Purge Templar (Level 4, Dark Doctrine)**
The Purge Templar is the militant wing of the inquisitorial apparatus made fully martial. Unique abilities:
- Sacred Fire: The Purge Templar can apply a burning effect to a targeted area of terrain (3 hexes radius). Units in burning terrain suffer -10% movement and -5% health per cycle. The fire lasts 3 cycles. Faith buildings in burning terrain suffer accelerated damage.
- Unyielding Sentence: When the Purge Templar kills an enemy unit, it generates +2% faith intensity immediately. This is the only unit in the game that generates faith intensity directly through combat kills. At high intensity this compounds rapidly.
- Purge Formation: When two or more Purge Templars act in the same cycle, they may form a Purge Formation — a coordinated advance that provides +20% damage to all units in the formation against any target and that cannot be flanked from the same side the formation faces. Enemy units that witness a Purge Formation and are not actively engaged suffer a -10% morale penalty from the psychological impact alone.

**Apex expression — The Unbroken (Dark Doctrine path):**

The Unbroken manifests differently when reached through the Purging Flame path. The figure is not ash-grey — it is white, blazing, lit not from within but from outside, the flame so intense it seems to consume the figure wearing it. The torch has become a pyre-staff. The sword is translucent, as if made of compressed flame. The expression on the Unbroken's face — the rare close view of it in portraits and unit art — is not serene. It is certain.

Stats profile: The same durability architecture as the Light Doctrine version — hard to kill, morale that does not break — but with different offensive profiling. The Dark Doctrine Unbroken deals 25% more base damage than its Light Doctrine equivalent. It has the Inquisitor's Heresy Sight as a permanent passive. It generates faith intensity on kills at 5% rather than 2%.

Unique ability — The Final Reckoning: Once per match, The Unbroken may declare a single territory as Condemned. All non-Old Light units in the territory immediately suffer -20% health and -30% morale. All Old Light units in the territory gain +20% all stats. The Condemned territory has its terrain features stripped of any defensive bonuses for 10 cycles — no forest cover, no hill advantage, no fortification bonus. The light burns everything that might provide cover equally. Mechanically this is distinct from the Light Doctrine version in that it is actively destructive rather than morale-stabilizing.

The Consuming Witness manifestation is active for the first 5 cycles of The Unbroken's presence. The combination of the manifestation and the unit creates an area in which non-Old Light forces suffer severe attrition and morale penalties simultaneously. The Dark Doctrine Unbroken does not feel like the faith arriving to witness what was built. It feels like the faith arriving to collect.

### Faith Spread Mechanics for Old Light

Old Light spreads primarily through cultural prestige and proximity rather than active missionary campaigns. The faith is rooted in a theology of remembrance, which makes it most compelling to populations that have experienced significant loss — populations that have been through wars, famines, plagues, or population upheaval find Old Light's framework of grief and continuity immediately relevant to their experience. As a result, Old Light's most effective spread mechanic is conquest's aftermath: territories that Old Light has passed through, even if contested, tend to retain some residual faith intensity because the dead were honored there. The faith leaves traces. Long trade route proximity to Old Light civilization also generates slow conversion pressure — not through missionary activity but through cultural osmosis, the stories traveling along with goods. Old Light is uniquely resistant to being displaced once rooted in a population because it ties itself to local history and local dead: to convert away from Old Light is to abandon the memory of everyone you have ever lost.

### Faith Conflict with Other Covenants

**Old Light vs. Blood Dominion:** Deep theological incompatibility at the level of first principles. Old Light remembers the dead. Blood Dominion believes the dead are most honored through sacrifice in their name, which Old Light reads as desecration. Populations with significant representation of both faiths experience constant low-grade social tension even without external pressure. When both dynasties are at war, atrocity events are more frequent — both sides believe the other is committing sacrilege, and sacrilege justifies escalation.

**Old Light vs. The Order:** Surprisingly navigable relationship. Both faiths believe in a persistent, structured reality — the Record and the Law are not incompatible as concepts. An Old Light dynasty and an Order dynasty can achieve stable faith coexistence and even faith alliance more easily than any other cross-covenant pairing. The tension that does emerge is around accountability: Old Light believes the Law must be applied with mercy and remembrance, while The Order's dark doctrine believes the Law is absolute and exempts no one. When a dark Order dynasty and a light Old Light dynasty are allied, friction eventually surfaces around how prisoners, apostates, and conquered populations are treated.

**Old Light vs. The Wild:** The Wild's indifference to the dead — its belief that death returns things to the cycle, that grief is a human construct that the forest does not share — is the point of maximum theological difference with Old Light. Old Light finds the Wild's acceptance of death without remembrance almost incomprehensible, a form of spiritual illiteracy. Wild populations in Old Light territory convert very slowly. However, there is a specific point of contact: both faiths assign deep meaning to place. Old Light treats specific locations as sacred because of what happened there. The Wild treats specific locations as sacred because of what lives there. Diplomacy between the two faiths occasionally finds language around "this ground has been consecrated by what it has witnessed and nurtured" that satisfies both frameworks, though the underlying theology remains irreconcilable.

## Blood Dominion

### Core Identity

Blood Dominion is a faith of covenant, cost, and communal identity forged through shared sacrifice. It does not ask for compliance. It asks for surrender — not the surrender of defeat but the surrender of offering, the deliberate giving of something that matters so that the covenant can persist. The faith understands that things which cost nothing mean nothing, and that the bond between a dynasty and its people, between the living and the dead, between a bloodline and its god, is made real only when it has been paid for in blood. This is not a metaphor in Blood Dominion theology. The blood is the covenant. The shedding and the sharing of it, the willingness to be wounded in behalf of something larger, is the mechanism through which the sacred bond activates.

What separates Blood Dominion's light doctrine from its dark doctrine is not the cost they require but what the cost is for. The Covenant Bond path believes that sacrifice builds shared identity — you bleed together and become more than you were separately, bound by something no external force can easily sever. The Blood Rite path believes that sacrifice establishes hierarchy — you bleed those below you to consecrate your own power, and this is not cruelty but order, the natural expression of a faith that knows the strong exist to pour the weak into the ground as fuel for something greater. Both paths use the same theology. Both paths mean it.

### Light Doctrine Path — The Covenant Bond Path

**What this doctrine believes:**

A dynasty walking the Covenant Bond Path understands sacrifice as mutual. The ruler bleeds first, or does not ask others to bleed. This is not a decorative principle — it is built into the ritual structure in ways that create genuine mechanical consequences. The Covenant Bond path produces dynasties that have unusual unit loyalty because their populations understand, in visceral terms, that their suffering is shared rather than extracted. When a Blood Dominion Covenant Priest stands before a battle and opens their own palm and says "I bleed with you," the soldiers who see it are not receiving an inspirational gesture. They are witnessing a theological act that obligates the covenant in both directions.

This doctrine's relationship to its enemies is shaped by its understanding of what makes the covenant powerful and what makes it vulnerable. The covenant is strongest when the people who constitute it are intact, when deaths have been acknowledged and absorbed properly, when the blood that has been shed has been formally entered into the covenant record. A Blood Dominion dynasty that loses heavily in battle is not weakened only militarily — the covenant is strained. The Covenant Bond path's primary tactical imperative is to keep losses within what the communal sacrifice structure can absorb. Enormous casualties without proper covenant rites afterward leave the surviving population fractured, the bond between them weakened, the faith intensity decaying. This makes Blood Dominion light doctrine both very strong defensively (the bond provides genuine morale durability) and genuinely vulnerable to war of attrition (the cost of maintenance scales with losses).

**Ritual mechanics:**

**The Shared Cup**
- Building required: Blood-Altar (Tier 1) or higher
- Resource cost: 20 Provisions (for the feast that surrounds the rite), 10 Rare Materials (the ritual vessel), 5 Gold (the Altar's upkeep during the rite)
- Time to complete: 1 cycle
- Mechanical effect: One Bloodline Member participates in the rite alongside representatives of 3 population settlements. The participating Member takes a formal Wound — not a health reduction but a narrative status that lasts 5 cycles. During those 5 cycles, the 3 settlements represented in the rite generate faith intensity at +15% rate and their population happiness is +10%. The wound is a form of payment: the Member's investment creates the population's return. The Wound status prevents the Member from leading cavalry charges or initiating combat during its duration.
- Faith intensity change: +5% per rite, maximum once per 3 cycles
- Interruption: If the Blood-Altar is attacked during the rite, the vessel is desecrated. The Shared Cup fails, and the Wound status activates without the population benefit — the Member paid the cost with no return, an omen of bad faith.

**The Bloodline Renewal Ceremony**
- Building required: Covenant Hall (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 40 Provisions, 25 Gold, 20 Rare Materials, 15 Labor (for the ceremony structure)
- Time to complete: 3 cycles
- Mechanical effect: A major covenant ceremony that formally renews the bond between the ruling bloodline and all active population settlements. At completion, all population settlements reset their loyalty drift to neutral — grievances accumulated from taxation, war, or misrule are formally acknowledged and absorbed by the ceremony rather than allowed to compound. This does not mean the grievances are resolved (underlying conditions remain) but that the population has been given a formal mechanism for expressing them and having them witnessed by the dynasty. Additionally, all Bloodline Members present during the ceremony gain +10 to their base Conviction modifier for 20 cycles.
- Faith intensity change: +10% on completion
- Interruption: If interrupted before cycle 2, no effect and resources are lost. If interrupted between cycles 2 and 3, partial effect: loyalty drift reset applies to only the settlements closest to the Covenant Hall.

**The Binding of the Fallen**
- Building required: Covenant Hall (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 30 Provisions, 20 Rare Materials, 15 Rare Materials (memorial bindings — a specific rare material use)
- Time to complete: 2 cycles
- Mechanical effect: Formally enters all units who died in the past 10 cycles into the covenant — not just Bloodline Members, but ordinary soldiers. Each entered soldier generates a persistent effect on surviving units from the same formation: +8% morale for 15 cycles. This stacks across multiple lost comrades. A formation that has lost heavily and been through the Binding Ceremony can emerge with very high morale because the deaths have been given meaning within the covenant framework. Additionally, the surviving members of fully decimated units (units reduced below 20% strength) who undergo the Binding do not disband — they persist as "Covenant Remnants" with special status, at half strength but full morale, and cost no upkeep for 10 cycles.
- Faith intensity change: +6%
- Interruption: Enemy raiding parties can disrupt the Covenant Hall during this rite. If disrupted, the deaths remain unbound — they weigh on the surviving units as pure loss rather than covenant contribution.

**The Covenant Scar**
- Building required: The Wound (Tier 3)
- Resource cost: 50 Gold, 40 Rare Materials, 30 Provisions, 20 Faith Currency
- Time to complete: 1 cycle
- Mechanical effect: The most significant light doctrine ritual available. A Bloodline Member undergoes a formal scarring — a permanent, visible marking that signals their covenant status. The scarred Member gains a permanent passive trait that makes them immune to Conviction pressure from all external sources for the remainder of the match (they cannot be intimidated, diplomatically destabilized, or faith-corrupted). All units led by the scarred Member gain +15% combat morale as a persistent aura. Most significantly, the Covenant Scar establishes a Spiritual Bond between the scarred Member and up to 5 units they designate — those units gain the Scarred Bond trait, meaning if the scarred Member dies, those units receive a +30% combat morale surge and full health restoration rather than the grief penalty they would otherwise experience. The death of someone fully covenant-bonded becomes fuel rather than loss.
- Faith intensity change: +15% on completion
- Interruption: Cannot be interrupted. The Covenant Scar is a purely personal act.

**Passive doctrine effects:**

- At Active (20-40%): Units that have fought together for more than 5 cycles begin accumulating a Bond Score between them. Units with high Bond Scores gain small but persistent mutual buffs when deployed together (+5% morale, +3% combat effectiveness). This is the faith's macro-level expression of covenant: it rewards relationships that persist over time.
- At Devout (40-60%): All Bloodline Members who share a covenant rite (any ritual involving multiple Members) gain a permanent +5 relationship score with each other. Bloodline Member betrayals and defections are 30% less likely at this doctrine intensity — the covenant bond is spiritually reinforced, making personal ruptures genuinely costly to the Member who initiates them.
- At Fervent (60-80%): Units routing from battle have a 25% chance per cycle of rallying without a commander intervention — the covenant bond holds them. The Covenant Hall generates passive faith intensity even without active rituals (+1% per cycle) as the building itself becomes a site of informal covenant activity. Shared Cup rites cost 20% fewer resources.
- At Apex (80-100%): The dynasty's entire military force gains the Covenant Army trait — a persistent state in which any unit that witnesses another unit die in battle receives +5% combat stats for 3 cycles rather than the usual morale penalty. At full Apex, the deaths of allies fuel the living. Every loss is a covenant payment that strengthens the survivors. This fundamentally inverts the usual attrition dynamic: Blood Dominion at Apex fights best when it has already taken losses.
- Sustained Apex for 10+ cycles: The Wound building, if constructed, emits a passive spiritual resonance across all home territory. Populations in home territory do not experience morale penalties from death events — births, deaths, and military losses are metabolized through the covenant framework. Population growth rate increases 20% as the covenant actively supports renewal.

**Spiritual manifestation (Fervent+ eligible):**

**The Covenant Host**

The Covenant Host manifests when Blood Dominion reaches Fervent intensity and a Bloodline Member with the Wound status (from The Shared Cup) is currently active on the field. It appears as a formation of figures alongside any cavalry or infantry force the controlling dynasty deploys — not separate spirits but overlapping presences, the visual impression being that every living soldier is slightly doubled, as if their fallen comrades are still marching with them. The aesthetic is not ghostly in a cold sense but warm, close, with the quality of remembered presences rather than haunting ones.

Mechanically, the Covenant Host gives every unit currently on the field a persistent +1% health restoration per cycle for the manifestation's duration. This is small per unit but across an entire army over time it is significant — a force that has been fighting for 10 cycles under the Covenant Host will have meaningfully more health than a force of equivalent size that has fought for the same duration without it. The Host also adds a special morale mechanic: when any unit is reduced below 20% health in the presence of the Host, they gain a one-time surge of +25% combat effectiveness rather than entering the critical health penalty state. The presence of the fallen is most intensely felt when the living are closest to joining them.

The Covenant Host lasts for 12 cycles. It ends if the Covenant Hall is destroyed or if faith intensity drops below Fervent. Enemy dark doctrine manifestations can disrupt it, but the disruption requires sustained effort because the Host is diffuse — it is not a single entity to be targeted.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Covenant Priest (Level 3, Light Doctrine)**
The Covenant Priest is a ritual specialist and battlefield chaplain whose primary function is maintaining the covenant under combat conditions. Unique abilities:
- Bond Mending: Once per battle, the Covenant Priest can perform a brief battlefield rite targeting one allied unit. That unit's morale is restored to full and any negative status effects from grief or fear are cleared.
- Shared Wound: The Covenant Priest may voluntarily absorb a percentage of damage dealt to an adjacent allied unit, splitting it between the Priest and the adjacent unit. The Priest takes 40% of what would have been the other unit's full damage. This is the light doctrine expression of shared sacrifice at the individual level.
- Covenant Witness: Any unit that dies within 3 hexes of the Covenant Priest contributes to a Covenant Stack. At 5 deaths, the Covenant Priest can activate a Covenant Surge — all allied units within 4 hexes gain +20% combat effectiveness for 3 cycles. The dead are paying forward.

**Covenant Scion (Level 4, Light Doctrine)**
The Covenant Scion is a warrior who has undergone the Covenant Scar rite in addition to full martial training. Unique abilities:
- Scarred Will: The Covenant Scion is immune to morale breaks and cannot route. When reduced below 30% health, instead of entering critical penalty state, the Scion enters Scarred Will — combat stats increase by 15% for 5 cycles. The near-death experience triggers rather than diminishes them.
- Bonding Aura: All units led by the Covenant Scion accumulate Bond Score twice as fast. Units that have fought under the Scion for 5+ cycles gain a permanent trait called "Scion-Bonded" that persists even if they are reassigned to a different commander.
- The Price Paid: Once per battle, the Covenant Scion can perform a total sacrifice — spending all remaining health above 1 point as a ritual offering. The health spent converts to a morale and health restoration surge for all allied units within 5 hexes, proportional to the health spent. The Scion survives at 1 health point. This is a tactical sacrifice mechanic that rewards precise timing.

**Apex expression — The Sacrificed (Light Doctrine path):**

The Sacrificed is not a victorious warrior. It is a warrior who has paid the full covenant price and persisted. Its appearance on the field carries the weight of total cost — this is a figure who has given everything and is still standing, not because they were spared but because the covenant held them. Visually, The Sacrificed appears as a figure covered in healed scars, armor that has been repaired so many times it is more repair than original material, moving with the particular deliberateness of someone who has been to the edge of what is survivable and knows exactly how close that edge is.

Stats profile: The Sacrificed has very high health relative to other apex units but starts each engagement at 60% of maximum health — the cost of its nature has already been paid. What makes it powerful is what it does with that reduced starting position. At or below 60% health, The Sacrificed deals 30% more damage. At or below 40% health, it deals 50% more damage. At or below 20% health, it is nearly immune to morale effects and deals 70% more damage. The Sacrificed gets stronger as it is hurt, systematically.

Unique ability — The Final Covenant: Once per match, The Sacrificed can invoke the full weight of the covenant — every sacrifice ever made by the dynasty, every wound taken, every rite performed. This creates a 10-cycle window in which all allied units within 6 hexes cannot be reduced below 1 health point. They can be immobilized, stunned, or captured, but they cannot die. The Sacrificed itself, if killed during this window, transfers its health pool equally across all allied units in the radius, distributing its remaining vitality to the covenant before it goes.

The Sacrificed under the Light Doctrine path feels like the faith's ultimate proof of concept: a being that demonstrates by existing that the covenant's promise is real, that what is given is not lost.

### Dark Doctrine Path — The Blood Rite Path

**What this doctrine believes:**

The Blood Rite path inherits the same theology as the Covenant Bond path and draws a different conclusion from it. If sacrifice creates the covenant, and if the covenant requires ongoing sustenance through sacrifice, then the question of who provides the sacrifice is not a moral question but a practical one. The Blood Rite dynasty has decided, with full theological justification, that the people best positioned to provide sacrifice are the ones with the least ability to withhold it. The weak are not less than the strong — they are, in Blood Dominion dark doctrine, the most valuable contribution the covenant can receive, because their sacrifice demands nothing in return. They do not need to be asked. They do not need to consent. The covenant is fed, the dynasty is strengthened, and the strong continue to do what strength is for.

This doctrine is not secretly ashamed of itself. That is what makes it genuinely disturbing. The priests of the Blood Rite path perform their rites in public. They believe sincerely that what they are doing is correct, that the sacrifice of the few for the covenant of the many is not cruelty but stewardship. The horror responses this triggers in neighboring populations — especially Old Light and Order factions — are interpreted within the Blood Rite framework as spiritual weakness, an inability to face what the covenant actually costs.

**Ritual mechanics:**

**The Tithe of the Weak**
- Building required: Blood-Altar (Tier 1) or higher, with a Blood Rider unit present
- Resource cost: 30 Labor (the tithers), 10 Gold (the apparatus), 10 Rare Materials
- Time to complete: 2 cycles
- Mechanical effect: A specific number of the lowest-loyalty population in the designated settlement are formally sacrificed in the Tithe. Population number decreases by a small fixed amount (varies with intensity — at base, 50 individuals in game abstraction terms). In return: faith intensity in the settlement increases by 10%, the Blood-Altar generates a Blood Reserve token, and all military units housed in the settlement gain +15% combat morale for 10 cycles. If the settlement was Low Loyalty prior to the Tithe, the remaining population's loyalty paradoxically increases — the doctrine teaches that the sacrifice was honor, and the survivors understand themselves as having been spared for being stronger.
- Faith intensity change: +8%
- Interruption: Neighboring factions with high Old Light or Order faith intensity generate a Horror event when the Tithe occurs in line-of-sight territory. This triggers -10% trade willingness from those factions immediately.

**The Blood Convocation**
- Building required: Covenant Hall (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 50 Provisions, 30 Rare Materials, 20 Gold, 20 Labor
- Time to complete: 4 cycles
- Mechanical effect: A mass sacrifice rite performed at the Covenant Hall involving captive populations, enslaved enemy soldiers, or volunteered civilians. The Convocation generates Blood Reserve tokens equal to the scale of participation, and each token can be spent to enhance one unit permanently — adding a single stat point to their base profile. Maximum 5 units can be enhanced per Convocation. The enhancement is permanent and persists even if faith intensity later drops. Additionally, the Convocation triggers a region-wide morale effect: all Blood Dominion units in the territory gain +20% combat morale for 15 cycles. The rite is visible to neighboring factions and triggers Horror responses up to 8 hexes away.
- Faith intensity change: +12%
- Interruption: If disrupted by enemy raid, the Convocation fails. All captive sacrificial population escapes. A "Convocation Disrupted" event generates -15% faith intensity and a domestic Conviction penalty, as the failed rite is interpreted as the covenant being insufficient.

**The Rite of Dominion**
- Building required: Covenant Hall (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 40 Gold, 30 Rare Materials, 10 Faith Currency
- Time to complete: 2 cycles
- Mechanical effect: A dynasty-wide declaration that formally establishes the Blood Rite doctrine's hierarchy: the covenant flows from the strong to the strong through the sacrifice of the weak. Mechanically, all Bloodline Members gain +10% to combat statistics for 20 cycles. All enemy diplomatic overtures received during this period are penalized: enemy factions receive -20% to the effectiveness of any diplomatic action taken against the Blood Rite dynasty because the dynasty's theological framework renders it genuinely indifferent to external moral arguments. Additionally, Blood Rider units gain the Dominion Mark — a visual distinction in unit portraits and a passive +5% damage bonus for 20 cycles.
- Faith intensity change: +7%
- Interruption: No. The Rite of Dominion is internal.

**The Wound's Hunger**
- Building required: The Wound (Tier 3)
- Resource cost: 60 Gold, 50 Rare Materials, 40 Provisions, 30 Faith Currency
- Time to complete: 3 cycles
- Mechanical effect: The most powerful and most costly dark doctrine ritual. The Wound building becomes an active site of mass sacrifice for the duration of the rite and for 10 cycles afterward. Any captive enemies brought to The Wound during this period can be sacrificed, with each sacrifice generating: +5% health for all Blood Dominion units currently on the field (distributed equally), +3% faith intensity, and one Blood Reserve token. The rate of sacrifice is limited by how many captives are available. If more than 20 captives are sacrificed in a single activation, the rite escalates into a Grand Feeding — The Wound enters a 5-cycle state in which all Blood Dominion units deal +25% damage and the Bloodbound units gain their full apex combat package temporarily.
- Faith intensity change: +15% at base, +5% additional per 5 captives sacrificed above the threshold
- Interruption: The Wound can be assaulted. If damaged during the Hunger, the rite does not fail but produces half effects. If The Wound is destroyed during the Hunger, a catastrophic faith backlash triggers: -20% faith intensity and a region-wide panic event.

**Passive doctrine effects:**

- At Active (20-40%): Blood Reserve tokens begin accumulating passively from normal military casualties (enemy kills, not own losses). Each kill generates a fractional token, so active combat maintains the covenant's fuel supply. Blood Rider units are available.
- At Devout (40-60%): Enemy captives generate Blood Reserve tokens at twice the rate when brought to faith buildings. Low-loyalty populations in home territory experience 20% lower probability of rebellion because the Blood Rite doctrine suppresses resistance — the population understands what non-compliance leads to. This is mechanically distinct from normal loyalty: it is compliance through fear rather than through satisfaction.
- At Fervent (60-80%): All Blood Dominion units gain a passive regeneration of 2% health per cycle during combat. They are sustained by the covenant's accumulation of sacrifice. Blood Convocation rites cost 30% fewer resources. Horror events generated by Blood Rite rituals now affect enemy populations up to 12 hexes away, not just 8.
- At Apex (80-100%): The Blood Rite doctrine has reached a state of total saturation. Human sacrifice costs no Faith Currency — it has been normalized to the point where the bureaucracy of sanctified killing runs without additional spiritual overhead. Enemy populations in adjacent territories experience faith conversion pressure toward Blood Dominion not through cultural attraction but through fear, at a rate of -3% of their native faith per cycle. The covenant is consuming outward.
- Sustained Apex for 10+ cycles: A dynasty-wide trait "The Hungry Covenant" activates. Every battle victory generates a bonus Blood Reserve token regardless of casualties. The covenant feeds on victory itself, not just on sacrifice.

**Spiritual manifestation (Fervent+ eligible):**

**The Hunger**

The Hunger is not a being. It is a presence that arrives when Blood Dominion reaches Fervent intensity on the dark doctrine path and at least one Blood Convocation has been completed in the past 20 cycles. It manifests as a visible darkening of the sky above The Wound, not darkness as in night but darkness as in a deep red-brown atmospheric shift, as if the blood poured into the ground has reached up and stained the air. There is a sound: low, rhythmic, like a very slow pulse heard from inside.

Mechanically, The Hunger creates a zone of effect centered on The Wound that extends 8 hexes in all directions. Within this zone, enemy units suffer -3% maximum health per cycle — not damage, but reduction in their health ceiling. This represents the covenant's presence actively diminishing what is against it. This effect is cumulative: a unit that spends 10 cycles within The Hunger's radius has lost 30% of its maximum health ceiling, permanently for the duration of the manifestation. Units that die within the zone generate Blood Reserve tokens at double rate. Blood Dominion units within the zone regenerate health 5% faster per cycle on top of the passive regeneration from Fervent intensity.

The Hunger lasts 15 cycles. Unlike the Choir or the Covenant Host, The Hunger does not end when the faith building is destroyed — it lasts its full duration once manifested. Destroying The Wound during the manifestation causes the Hunger to localize to the rubble of the building, which means the 8-hex radius moves with the rubble location (it cannot be moved). It can be ended early only by a specific Order faith ritual or by a mass Old Light Recitation of the Fallen directed at the territory.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Blood Rider (Level 3, Dark Doctrine)**
The Blood Rider is a mounted combatant consecrated to the covenant through personal ritual scarring. They are faster than Covenant Priests and oriented toward combat rather than ceremony. Unique abilities:
- Blood Price: Each enemy unit killed by the Blood Rider generates a charge. At 3 charges, the Blood Rider can activate a Covenant Surge — dealing their next attack at 150% normal damage and generating a Blood Reserve token.
- Marked for the Covenant: Once per battle, the Blood Rider may mark an enemy unit as a sacrifice target. All Blood Dominion units deal +15% damage to the marked target. If the marked unit is killed by any Blood Dominion unit, the killing unit gains full health restoration.
- Red Charge: In a charge action, the Blood Rider deals bonus damage proportional to the distance covered — the longer the approach, the greater the impact. At maximum charge distance, the bonus is +40% damage.

**Bloodbound (Level 4, Dark Doctrine)**
The Bloodbound has undergone the full covenant sacrifice rite, giving a significant portion of their own blood at The Wound in exchange for supernatural covenant reinforcement. They are physically enhanced. Unique abilities:
- Covenant Flesh: The Bloodbound's physical stats (health, damage) are permanently 15% higher than equivalent Level 4 units of other doctrine paths because their covenant sacrifice has been answered with a physical return.
- Unquenchable Thirst: The Bloodbound heals 5% health for every enemy unit it kills. This regeneration is unconditional and applies even during combat actions.
- Blood Debt: Once per battle, the Bloodbound can invoke the full weight of the covenant's unpaid obligations — summoning a fraction of The Hunger effect into their immediate location (3 hexes) for 3 cycles. Enemy units in this radius suffer the health ceiling reduction effect of The Hunger but at 50% rate.

**Apex expression — The Sacrificed (Dark Doctrine path):**

The Sacrificed under the Dark Doctrine path manifests not as a figure who paid the covenant price themselves but as the covenant's instrument — a warrior who has fed the covenant so thoroughly that the covenant has remade them. Visually, the Dark Doctrine Sacrificed appears with deep red-black armor that seems to absorb light, with scarring that is not the healed silver of the light doctrine version but raised and still-flushed, as if always freshly made. There is something wrong in the way it moves — too fluid, as if it no longer has the hesitations of a body that fears being hurt.

Stats profile: The Dark Doctrine Sacrificed has higher base damage than its Light Doctrine equivalent, dealing 25% more at full health. Its regeneration is faster, at 8% health per cycle rather than the passive 5% base. However, unlike the Light Doctrine version, it does not get stronger as it is hurt — it fights at peak performance when healthy, and at normal performance when wounded. It does not share the covenant's vulnerability to attrition that the light doctrine version does.

Unique ability — The Harvest: Once per match, The Sacrificed can initiate a Harvest state that lasts 5 cycles. During the Harvest, any unit killed within 6 hexes of The Sacrificed — allied or enemy — is absorbed into the covenant, generating +3% health for The Sacrificed and +1% health for all Blood Dominion units on the field per kill. There is no cap. In a large battle, the Harvest can make The Sacrificed nearly unkillable through sustained slaughter because the death around it feeds it.

The Hunger manifestation is active for the first 8 cycles of the Dark Doctrine Sacrificed's presence, not just 5 — the unit's arrival intensifies the spiritual atmosphere rather than simply accompanying it.

### Faith Spread Mechanics for Blood Dominion

Blood Dominion spreads most effectively through conquest and through the visceral visibility of its rituals. It does not require trade routes or cultural prestige in the way Old Light does. It spreads because it is seen. Populations in neighboring territories who have witnessed a Blood Convocation or a Tithe of the Weak through line of sight begin experiencing faith conversion pressure not from attraction but from a specific psychological response: the realization that the Blood Dominion dynasty has something its enemies do not, a willingness to pay costs that makes them genuinely more capable. Some populations convert out of respect for that power, some out of fear, and some out of genuine theological attraction to a faith that promises to make them part of the strong rather than the sacrificed. Blood Dominion also spreads aggressively along military occupation lines: conquered territories experience extremely high conversion pressure within the first 10 cycles of occupation, as the covenant's presence is physically felt by the local population before they have the conceptual language to resist it.

### Faith Conflict with Other Covenants

**Blood Dominion vs. Old Light:** Described above from the Old Light side. From Blood Dominion's perspective, Old Light's theology of remembrance is a form of weakness costumed as virtue — holding onto the dead rather than using them. The two faiths' populations do not coexist peacefully at scale.

**Blood Dominion vs. The Order:** The Order is the one faith that Blood Dominion has genuine respect for, specifically because The Order understands cost. The Order's dark doctrine (the Law is absolute and merciless) maps onto the Blood Rite path's understanding of the covenant more naturally than any other cross-covenant relationship. Two dark doctrine dynasties — Blood Rite and The Order's dark path — can achieve stable cooperation because each recognizes in the other a theology that does not flinch from what power costs. The tension is that Blood Dominion operates through sacrifice and The Order operates through law, and these frameworks eventually produce conflicting claims about what governs the covenant relationship with ordinary people.

**Blood Dominion vs. The Wild:** Surprising compatibility at the theological surface level — both faiths accept that death is part of the natural order and that strength is real. But the compatibility is shallow. The Wild does not believe in covenant at all, and Blood Dominion is nothing but covenant. The Wild's dark doctrine says the strong inherit the earth because that is natural law, not because there is a covenant to feed. Blood Dominion finds the Wild's lack of ritual discipline almost offensive. The Wild finds Blood Dominion's elaborate sacrifice architecture an unnecessary complication of what should be a simple relationship: you are strong enough to survive, or you are not.

## The Order

### Core Identity

The Order is the faith of law as a sacred act, not merely a civic one. It holds that the divine presence in the world is not personal, not remembering, not hungry for blood — it is structural. It is the framework that makes civilization possible. The Order's theology begins from a single claim: chaos is the natural state of the world, and the divine gift to humanity is the capacity to impose order on that chaos through law. Law is not an expression of human preference. Law is the closest thing to the divine that human beings can construct. Following the Law, maintaining it, enforcing it, extending it to new populations — these are sacred acts because they are participating in the divine project of pushing back the chaos that would otherwise consume everything.

What follows from this is a theology that produces dynasties of enormous competence and genuine stability. Order populations tend to be better fed, better organized, better defended than equivalent populations under other faiths, not because The Order is kinder but because it is more organized. It applies the same precision to agriculture and trade that it applies to military discipline. This makes Order dynasties the most administratively powerful in the game in several specific domains: taxation efficiency, population management, military cohesion. The dark doctrine does not corrupt this capacity — it clarifies it. The light doctrine says the Law protects. The dark doctrine says the Law does not exist to protect — it exists to function. What is protected by a functioning Law is a consequence, not a purpose.

### Light Doctrine Path — The Mandate of Protection

**What this doctrine believes:**

A dynasty following the Mandate of Protection understands law as a trust. The Order gave the dynasty authority, and authority is not ownership — it is stewardship. You hold the law in trust for the people it protects. This produces a specific tension that the best Mandate of Protection dynasties live with consciously: the law is absolute, but the law's purpose is the people it governs. When a law produces an outcome that fails the people, the dynasty following the Mandate of Protection faces a genuine dilemma. They cannot simply override the law — that is the path to chaos, and chaos is the thing the faith exists to prevent. But they cannot ignore that the people are being failed. The resolution the doctrine provides is reform through process: the law can be amended, elaborated, clarified — but only through legitimate channels that themselves follow established procedure. The Mandate of Protection's light doctrine produces dynasties that are unusually good at governance and unusually slow to adapt in crises, because adaptation requires process and process takes time.

This doctrine's relationship to its military is complex. The Iron Legionary is the light doctrine's finest unit, and the Legionary fights better when fighting for a law that protects, for a mandate that is genuine rather than performed. Mandate of Protection dynasties experience unusual unit coherence because their soldiers believe in what they are defending. They are not fighting for the dynasty's enrichment. They are fighting because the structure they protect is worth defending.

**Ritual mechanics:**

**The Reading of the Mandate**
- Building required: Lawpost (Tier 1) or higher
- Resource cost: 20 Rare Materials (for the written Mandate copies distributed to all settlements), 15 Gold, 10 Labor (for the readers who distribute and explain the Mandate)
- Time to complete: 1 cycle
- Mechanical effect: The Mandate is formally read aloud in all active settlements within the dynasty's territory. Each settlement receives a Law Clarity token that lasts 15 cycles. Law Clarity settlements generate 10% more tax revenue, experience 20% lower civil unrest, and generate faith intensity at 5% higher rate. The tokens do not stack — each Reading refreshes the existing Law Clarity rather than adding to it.
- Faith intensity change: +3% per Reading, maximum once per 5 cycles
- Interruption: If a Lawpost is destroyed during the Reading, the affected settlement does not receive its Law Clarity token. The Reading itself does not fail — it continues in all other settlements.

**The Trial of the Mandate**
- Building required: Hall of Mandate (Tier 2) or higher, with an Iron Legionary or higher-tier unit present
- Resource cost: 30 Gold, 20 Rare Materials, 20 Labor
- Time to complete: 3 cycles
- Mechanical effect: A formal legal proceeding adjudicating a specific domestic dispute, policy question, or territorial claim. The player designates the subject of the Trial. At completion, the Trial generates a Mandate Ruling — a persistent status effect applied to the relevant territory, population, or unit. Rulings can grant: +10% trade efficiency for a trade route for 20 cycles, +15% unit cohesion for a garrison force for 20 cycles, -20% resource extraction cost from a specific settlement for 20 cycles, or +10% diplomatic reception from a specific neighboring faction for 20 cycles. Only one Ruling can be active per category at a time. A new Trial replaces the old Ruling.
- Faith intensity change: +6%
- Interruption: If the Hall of Mandate is raided during the Trial, the proceeding fails. This is treated as a severe desecration — the interruption of a lawful process is, to The Order, one of the most serious crimes that can be committed. The dynasty receives +5% faith intensity from the outrage of the affront, but the Trial must be restarted.

**The Commission of Iron**
- Building required: Hall of Mandate (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 40 Gold, 30 Rare Materials, 20 Provisions (equipment and provisioning), 15 Labor
- Time to complete: 2 cycles
- Mechanical effect: Formally deploys an Iron Commission — a combined legal-military force — to a designated territory. The Commission establishes a legal presence that passively enforces Order doctrine. Mechanically: the designated territory converts faith intensity toward The Order at +3% per cycle for 10 cycles. Population compliance (the Order's version of loyalty) increases by 15% in the designated territory. Any non-Order faith buildings in the designated territory experience a 20% reduction in their faith intensity generation while the Commission is present. The Commission is represented by a unit attachment to the territory, not a separate entity, but can be disrupted by military action.
- Faith intensity change: +5%
- Interruption: If military combat occurs in the designated territory before the Commission's 10 cycles expire, the Commission is disrupted and must be restarted.

**The Iron Decree**
- Building required: The Iron Spire (Tier 3)
- Resource cost: 60 Gold, 50 Rare Materials, 30 Faith Currency, 20 Labor
- Time to complete: 2 cycles
- Mechanical effect: A decree issued from The Iron Spire carries the full weight of the law's ultimate authority. The decree designates a specific action, territory, or condition as Mandated — the law has spoken, and its word is final. Mechanically: a Mandated territory cannot be voluntarily ceded or traded for 30 cycles regardless of diplomatic circumstances. A Mandated military action (such as the defense of a specific border) causes all units executing that action to gain +20% all stats for the duration of the action. A Mandated law (a specific prohibition or requirement on the dynasty's own behavior) generates +15% faith intensity and a permanent +5 diplomatic reception with neutral factions when issued and upheld — neutral factions interpret the decree as evidence of genuine rule of law rather than mere power.
- Faith intensity change: +12%
- Interruption: No. The Iron Decree cannot be interrupted once issued.

**Passive doctrine effects:**

- At Active (20-40%): All resource extraction operations in home territory generate 5% additional resources due to the organizational efficiency that Order doctrine imposes on the population. Tax revenue increases by 8%.
- At Devout (40-60%): Unit cohesion (the mechanic governing whether units follow complex orders rather than defaulting to simple advance-and-engage behavior) increases by 15% for all Order military forces. Orders with 3 or more steps that would normally require commander oversight can now be executed autonomously by units at Devout intensity.
- At Fervent (60-80%): Trial of the Mandate produces two Mandate Rulings per proceeding rather than one. All Commission of Iron deployments cost 25% fewer resources. The Iron Spire, if built, generates a passive +2% faith intensity per cycle from the sheer institutional weight of its presence.
- At Apex (80-100%): A dynasty-wide trait "Perfect Order" activates. All population settlements in home territory generate zero civil unrest regardless of underlying conditions — the law has been so thoroughly internalized that the ordinary friction of governance has been smoothed away. This is not because the people are content (they may not be) but because dissent has no institutional channel through which to manifest except through the law itself, which is Order-governed. Additionally, all Order units gain +10% base combat stats permanently.
- Sustained Apex for 10+ cycles: The dynastic trait "The Living Mandate" activates. The dynasty's Bloodline Members become personally identified with the law — their decisions are experienced by the population as legal acts rather than personal ones. Conviction changes from Bloodline Member behavior are dampened by 30% in both directions (extreme mercy and extreme cruelty both produce smaller Conviction swings) because the population interprets Member actions through the filter of the Mandate rather than through personal moral judgment.

**Spiritual manifestation (Fervent+ eligible):**

**The Vanguard of the Mandate**

The Vanguard manifests when The Order reaches Fervent intensity on the light doctrine path and at least one Iron Decree has been issued in the past 25 cycles. It appears as a formation of armored figures overlaying the dynasty's own military forces — not separate from them but perfectly aligned with them, moving when they move, stopping when they stop, the impression being less of a supernatural army and more of the law's intention made briefly visible. The visual quality is precise and cold, everything at right angles, no wasted motion.

Mechanically, the Vanguard is a force multiplier. Every Order unit currently deployed gains +15% base combat stats and immunity to rout for the Vanguard's duration. More significantly, the Vanguard imposes a tactical discipline effect on the entire battlefield: units acting out of formation (flanking runs, individual charges, skirmishing) have their bonuses for those tactics reduced by 20%, affecting enemy forces more than Order forces since Order units are specifically trained for formation combat. The Vanguard thus makes the battle more formal, more structured — it imposes the law's preference for order onto the mechanics of combat itself.

The Vanguard lasts for 10 cycles. It ends if The Iron Spire is destroyed or if faith intensity drops below Fervent. It cannot be attacked. Enemy disruption requires sustained ritual opposition.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Iron Legionary (Level 3, Light Doctrine)**
The Iron Legionary is the backbone of the Mandate of Protection's military structure. Unique abilities:
- Formation Discipline: When two or more Iron Legionaries are in formation (adjacent hexes), each gains +10% to all combat stats. This bonus scales: 3 in formation gives +15%, 4 gives +20%, maximum at 5+ gives +25%. The Iron Legionary is fundamentally a unit that requires others to function at peak.
- Law Shield: The Iron Legionary can designate an adjacent hex as a Protected Zone for 2 cycles. Any allied unit retreating through the Protected Zone is not considered to be routing — it is executing a lawful withdrawal — and does not suffer the combat penalties usually associated with retreat.
- Mandate's Will: Once per battle, the Iron Legionary can issue a Mandate's Will call to all friendly units within 4 hexes, granting them immunity to the next morale-break event they would otherwise suffer.

**Iron Sentinel (Level 4, Light Doctrine)**
The Iron Sentinel is a veteran of the Mandate's enforcement, experienced in both law and war. Unique abilities:
- Inviolable Post: The Iron Sentinel can designate a specific hex as its Post for the duration of a battle. While at the Post, the Sentinel has +30% defense and cannot be moved by knockback, displacement, or morale-driven repositioning effects. Units adjacent to the Sentinel at its Post gain +15% defense.
- Mandate of Witness: The Iron Sentinel passively extends Law Clarity tokens to combat areas — any territory in which an Iron Sentinel fights for 3+ cycles gains a partial Law Clarity effect (half the resource and unrest bonuses of the full token) for 5 cycles after the battle.
- Counter-Law: When an enemy unit uses a special ability in the Iron Sentinel's adjacency, the Sentinel may spend a reaction to reduce the ability's effect by 40%. This represents the law's capacity to nullify extra-legal power through institutional resistance.

**Apex expression — The Mandate (Light Doctrine path):**

The Mandate is the embodied law — not a warrior who follows the law but a being in which the law and the warrior are no longer distinguishable. Visually, The Mandate appears as a figure in armor so perfectly constructed that no individual piece is visible as a piece — it is a seamless surface, mirror-bright, with a visual effect that varies: in direct sunlight it reflects everything around it. In darkness it seems lit from within. It carries a halberd that is both weapon and standard, the Order sigil at its head.

Stats profile: The Mandate has the highest defense of any apex unit in the game. It is functionally impervious to standard unit attacks when in formation with two or more Iron Legionary or Iron Sentinel units. Its offense is moderate — it is not designed to destroy, it is designed to hold. It cannot route. It cannot be corrupted, converted, or affected by any spiritual status effect.

Unique ability — The Last Law: Once per match, The Mandate may declare the Law for a territory. All combat in the designated territory for the next 10 cycles is subject to the Law's structure: units that break formation lose all formation bonuses regardless of faction. Units that attack from ambush or stealth have those bonuses reduced by 50%. Units that rout cannot return to the battle regardless of morale recovery mechanics. The Law describes how combat must be conducted, and while the Law is active, all factions must fight within its framework. Order units are built for this framework and are unaffected. Enemy factions that rely on flexible, opportunistic tactics suffer most.

The Mandate under the Light Doctrine path arrives without the spiritual phenomenon that usually accompanies apex units — no manifestation reinforces it. The implication is that The Mandate does not require the divine to supplement it. The Mandate is what the divine intended.

### Dark Doctrine Path — The Dominion Through Fear Path

**What this doctrine believes:**

The Dominion Through Fear path has arrived at the same starting point as the Mandate of Protection — the law is sacred, chaos must be prevented — and has made a specific decision about what keeps people within the law. The answer is not protection. The answer is fear. Fear of the law's consequence is the mechanism that keeps chaos at bay, and consequences that are feared must be consequences that are real and visible and disproportionate enough to deter. The dark doctrine does not believe in second chances. It does not believe in reform. It believes in clarity: the law has been declared, it has been made available, and if you violate it, the consequence is total. This is mercy, in the dark doctrine's framework — it removes ambiguity. You know exactly what will happen. Choose accordingly.

This doctrine produces dynasties of extraordinary political stability because the population genuinely does not know where the line is, which means they stay well back from it. The dark doctrine's genius is that the actual cost of enforcement is low once the reputation is established. Dynasties do not need to execute constantly. They need to have executed visibly and memorably enough that the possibility is always present. The cruelty is not in the volume of its exercise. It is in the certainty.

**Ritual mechanics:**

**The Public Judgement**
- Building required: Lawpost (Tier 1) or higher, with a Warden-Enforcer unit present
- Resource cost: 20 Gold, 10 Labor, 10 Rare Materials
- Time to complete: 1 cycle
- Mechanical effect: A public enforcement action — an execution, a severe corporal punishment, a highly visible application of law's consequence — performed in the designated settlement. Immediate effect: population compliance in the settlement increases by 20% for 20 cycles. Any low-loyalty population in the settlement that was approaching unrest has their trajectory reversed. Faith intensity increases because the doctrine has been exercised. Side effect: the Public Judgement generates a visible event marker accessible to all factions. Neighboring populations in line-of-sight territory experience -5% happiness but +10% compliance — they are not happy about what they witnessed, but they comply more readily. Old Light and Wild populations in sight experience -15% voluntary faith conversion toward Order.
- Faith intensity change: +5%
- Interruption: Cannot be interrupted once begun.

**The Writ of Absolute Law**
- Building required: Hall of Mandate (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 40 Gold, 30 Rare Materials, 20 Faith Currency
- Time to complete: 2 cycles
- Mechanical effect: Issues a Writ that classifies a specific behavior or category of behavior as absolutely prohibited, with consequence described publicly. The Writ generates compliance in home territory equivalent to 25% across all settlements for 15 cycles — no civil unrest events can trigger in that period regardless of underlying conditions. Additionally, any population that receives the Writ and has existing Order faith intensity begins generating at +10% rate because the Writ is theologically significant: it is the law at its most explicit. If violated — if a Writ offense event occurs — the dynasty must perform a Public Judgement within 3 cycles or suffer -15% faith intensity as the Writ is perceived as empty.
- Faith intensity change: +8%
- Interruption: No.

**The Fear Proclamation**
- Building required: Hall of Mandate (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 50 Gold, 30 Rare Materials, 25 Labor
- Time to complete: 3 cycles
- Mechanical effect: A mass communication of the dynasty's capacity for enforcement — the details of major punishments, the extent of the law's reach, the specifics of the Warden-Enforcer's authority — distributed to all territories within diplomatic reach. Effects: enemy factions receive -15% to aggressive diplomatic actions (raids, demands, declarations of war) against the dynasty for 20 cycles. Non-aligned populations bordering the dynasty have their faith conversion to Order accelerated by 15% — the Fear Proclamation works as a perverse form of missionary activity. The fear of the law is its own advertisement. Any neutral faction that is currently in a military conflict with a non-Order power receives a +15% probability of making a peace agreement with the Order dynasty during the Proclamation's active period, preferring Order's enforcement to their enemy's chaos.
- Faith intensity change: +7%
- Interruption: If the Hall of Mandate is disrupted during the Proclamation, the Proclamation fails. This is interpreted as weakness — the dynasty claimed an authority it could not defend — and generates -10% faith intensity.

**The Iron Decree of Dominion**
- Building required: The Iron Spire (Tier 3)
- Resource cost: 70 Gold, 55 Rare Materials, 35 Faith Currency, 25 Labor
- Time to complete: 3 cycles
- Mechanical effect: A declaration that extends the law's absolute authority over a conquered or newly controlled territory. The designated territory is placed under direct Dominion governance: all population compliance mechanics are replaced by a Fear Compliance mechanic that starts at 80% regardless of prior history. The territory generates 20% more resources than normal because the population is working under conditions of total law enforcement. However, the Fear Compliance mechanic decays at -5% per cycle without active maintenance (a Warden-Enforcer present, or a Public Judgement performed every 10 cycles). If Fear Compliance drops below 30%, a Dominion Collapse event occurs: the population rises in open revolt and all Order faith buildings in the territory are destroyed immediately.
- Faith intensity change: +15% on completion
- Interruption: If The Iron Spire is attacked during the rite, the Decree fails. The failed Decree generates a significant weakness signal — -20% fear compliance in all territories for 10 cycles, because the dynasty attempted to assert ultimate authority and could not protect the instrument of that assertion.

**Passive doctrine effects:**

- At Active (20-40%): Fear Compliance is the primary loyalty mechanic in home territory rather than the conventional satisfaction-based loyalty. Fear Compliance is harder to move upward but does not naturally decay from ordinary bad governance the way satisfaction does. Warden-Enforcer units are available.
- At Devout (40-60%): All resource extraction operations generate 15% additional output. Fear Compliance decays 30% more slowly. Enemy propaganda and conversion attempts against Order populations have -25% effectiveness — a population governed by fear has been effectively removed from the persuasion marketplace.
- At Fervent (60-80%): The Warden-Enforcer units gain a passive ability called Absolute Authority — any enemy unit that retreats from a battle in which Warden-Enforcers participated cannot regroup for 3 additional cycles, as if they are fleeing the law rather than merely an army. All Public Judgement rites cost 30% fewer resources. The dynasty gains a permanent +10% diplomatic leverage in all negotiations — other factions respect, on some level, the clarity of what The Order will do.
- At Apex (80-100%): Population compliance across all home territory is effectively locked — it cannot drop below 40% through any normal mechanic while Apex is maintained. The dynasty has achieved a state of total behavioral control. Simultaneously, the administrative efficiency of this state generates +25% to all resource production across home territory. The population is working at maximum productive output because no energy is being wasted on ambiguity.
- Sustained Apex for 10+ cycles: The dynastic trait "The Law Made Absolute" activates. The dynasty is immune to all cross-covenant conversion attempts. No other faith can achieve meaningful penetration of Order territory while this trait is active. The law has sealed the domain.

**Spiritual manifestation (Fervent+ eligible):**

**The Arbiter**

The Arbiter manifests when The Order's dark doctrine reaches Fervent intensity and at least two Public Judgements have been performed in the past 20 cycles. It appears as a single figure — taller than a human, in armor that is entirely featureless (no joints, no visible seams, no heraldry), carrying a scale on one side and a blade on the other. It stands motionless above the Hall of Mandate or The Iron Spire, visible from the surrounding hexes as a silhouette. It does not move. It observes.

Mechanically, The Arbiter's presence is a passive enforcement effect. Any unit within 6 hexes that suffers a morale break immediately has a second check applied: they must roll against their discipline score or be permanently eliminated from the battle (not merely routing but dissolving as a unit, the survivors scattering beyond recovery range). This mechanic makes rout states near The Arbiter potentially terminal rather than recoverable. Order units are immune to this effect — the law they follow protects them from the law's judgment. Enemy units in the vicinity are subject to it. The probability of the secondary rout-to-elimination check is proportional to how far the unit has deviated from expected behavior in the battle — routing quickly (before taking significant losses) results in higher probability of elimination than routing after sustained combat. The Arbiter judges the nature of the failure, not just its occurrence.

The Arbiter lasts 10 cycles. It cannot be attacked. It ends when faith intensity drops below Fervent or when the faith building it stands above is destroyed. In that case, the Arbiter completes one final judgment on any enemy unit currently in rout state within range before departing.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Warden-Enforcer (Level 3, Dark Doctrine)**
The Warden-Enforcer is the law's active instrument — not a warrior in the conventional sense but a specialist in making resistance impossible. Unique abilities:

- Capture: The Warden-Enforcer can attempt to capture a fleeing enemy unit rather than killing it. - The Mark of Sentence: Once per battle, the Warden-Enforcer can designate an enemy unit as Sentenced. Sentenced units cannot receive morale boosts or healing from any source for 5 cycles. The sentence has been passed; the outcome is determined.

**Dominion Arbiter (Level 4, Dark Doctrine)**
The Dominion Arbiter is the judicial tier of the Warden-Enforcer system — higher authority, broader reach, capacity for mass enforcement. Unique abilities:
- Absolute Jurisdiction: Within 5 hexes of the Dominion Arbiter, all non-Order units suffer -20% to special ability effectiveness. The Arbiter's authority diminishes the power of anything extra-legal.
- Mass Sentence: The Dominion Arbiter can apply the Sentenced status to all enemy units within 3 hexes simultaneously, once per battle.
- Law's Finality: When the Dominion Arbiter kills an enemy unit, the kill cannot be denied by any revival or revival-equivalent mechanic. If the target would otherwise be recovered through a faith manifestation or unit ability that prevents death, Law's Finality overrides it. The law's execution is final.

**Apex expression — The Mandate (Dark Doctrine path):**

The Dark Doctrine Mandate manifests without the mirror-bright armor of the light doctrine version. It is darker, the armor absorbing light rather than reflecting it, the same featureless seamlessness but heavier, the visual impression being that it has accumulated everything the law has passed through it. It carries the same halberd but the standard at its head has changed: the Order sigil is present but reversed, inverted — a symbol not of protection but of judgment.

Stats profile: The Dark Doctrine Mandate has higher offense than its light doctrine equivalent. Where the light doctrine version is designed to hold, the dark doctrine version is designed to terminate. Its defense is slightly lower. Its damage is 30% higher. It shares the inability to be corrupted or spiritually affected, but it also has a specific addition: it permanently generates the Enforcement Presence aura of the Warden-Enforcer at +25% intensity, meaning any non-Order unit within 5 hexes of The Mandate suffers -30% morale continuously.

Unique ability — The Final Sentence: Once per match, The Mandate may issue a Final Sentence to an entire territory. For 10 cycles, all non-Order units in the territory have their maximum health reduced by 25% and cannot receive healing from any source. All Order units in the territory gain +25% all stats. The territory itself generates no faith intensity for any non-Order covenant during the sentence period — the law has spoken and is awaiting execution.

The Arbiter manifestation accompanies The Dark Doctrine Mandate for the first 8 cycles of its presence. The combination creates a territory state in which rout is potentially fatal, all non-Order units are debuffed, and The Mandate is personally terminating anything the combined system identifies as a threat to Order.

### Faith Spread Mechanics for The Order

The Order spreads through administrative demonstration more than any other mechanism. Populations that live under Order governance — even Order governance through conquest — experience measurably better administration than equivalent populations under other faiths. Roads work. Taxes are predictable. Markets are regulated. The Order's spread mechanic reflects this: every 10 cycles a population spends under Order control, they accumulate +5% conversion pressure toward the faith regardless of the method of initial acquisition. The Order is genuinely better at running things, and populations notice. The counterweight is that Order populations convert away from the faith unusually quickly if Order governance visibly fails — the faith's promise is functionality, and a failing Order dynasty is a failing advertisement. The dark doctrine's Fear Compliance mechanic supplements this: fear prevents apostasy but cannot generate genuine faith intensity above a ceiling — a purely fear-governed population caps at Active intensity and cannot progress further without genuine theological buy-in from at least a portion of the population.

### Faith Conflict with Other Covenants

**The Order vs. Old Light:** Described from the Old Light side above. From The Order's perspective, Old Light's theology is philosophically nearby but practically inefficient — remembrance as a governance mechanism produces populations that are emotionally coherent but administratively diffuse. The Order respects Old Light more than it respects Blood Dominion or The Wild. It disagrees with it about what the divine actually wants from civilization.

**The Order vs. Blood Dominion:** Mutual respect between dark doctrines, as noted above. Light Order and light Blood Dominion have genuine friction: The Order's protection-through-structure theology conflicts with Blood Dominion's sacrifice-as-renewal theology because The Order views sacrifice as a cost that weakens the structure it is meant to sustain.

**The Order vs. The Wild:** Maximum incompatibility. The Wild holds that the natural state of the world is the valid state. The Order holds that the natural state of the world is chaos that must be overcome. These positions are not merely different — they are structurally opposed. Wild populations in Order territory resist conversion almost completely. Order populations in Wild territory destabilize without the structural support they depend on. Any border between an Order dynasty and a Wild dynasty is the game's most active faith conflict zone by default.

## The Wild

### Core Identity

The Wild is not a faith in the way the other covenants are faiths. It does not have a theology in the sense of a systematic account of a divine being's nature and preferences. It has a relationship — a direct, unmediated, ongoing relationship with the living world that it calls the First Grove, the original state of all things before the other faiths built their walls and their altars and their spires and claimed they could explain what the world meant. The Wild says the world does not mean. It lives. The oak does not grow to teach a lesson. The wolf does not hunt to demonstrate a principle. They live because life insists on living, and the sacred thing about that is the insistence itself, the endless pressing forward of a world that does not need to be justified.

Following the Wild means learning to read what the living world is doing rather than imposing a framework on it. A Wild dynasty tends to have a particular quality in its culture: an attentiveness, a responsiveness, an ability to work with what is rather than forcing what should be. This makes Wild dynasties excellent at terrain exploitation, excellent at adapting to military conditions, genuinely terrible at formal governance and administrative efficiency. The Wild does not have The Order's institutional coherence. It has something harder to measure: situational intelligence applied to a living environment by people who have trained themselves to pay attention to it.

The dark doctrine does not represent a corruption of this attentiveness. It represents its honest extension. The living world is also predatory. It is also indifferent to suffering. The forest floor is covered in the bones of things that did not survive. The dark doctrine says: this is not a lesson about cruelty. It is a description of reality. Pretending that harmony with nature means harmony with everything is a lie the light doctrine tells itself. The strong inherit the earth because that is what strength is for, and the weak are nutrients.

### Light Doctrine Path — The Harmony Path

**What this doctrine believes:**

A dynasty following the Harmony Path understands its civilization as a thing that grows within the living world rather than on top of it. This is not a passive stance. The Harmony Path is full of active ritual and discipline — but the discipline is oriented toward understanding what the living world is doing and finding the position within it that both the dynasty and the world can sustain. Wild dynasties on the Harmony Path tend to be positioned in terrain that they know more deeply than their enemies do. They fight better in forests, hills, and river systems not because of some vague affinity but because they have rituals that specifically build that knowledge, scouts that have trained in specific terrain for years, animal partners that move through terrain in ways human formations cannot.

The Harmony Path's central tension is between the world's regenerative capacity and the dynasty's needs. The light doctrine says that if you take only what can be regrown, if you time your harvests with the living world's own rhythms, if you make yourself part of the pattern rather than an exception to it, you will never exhaust what sustains you. This is true over long timescales. It is genuinely difficult over the short ones. Wild dynasties on the Harmony Path tend to be strong in the mid-game and extraordinary in the late game — their resource base compounds over time in ways that other factions' bases do not, because they have not depleted what feeds them.

**Ritual mechanics:**

**The Grove Marking**
- Building required: Grove Marker (Tier 1) or higher
- Resource cost: 15 Timber (not consumed — these become the marker structure), 10 Provisions (for the feast accompanying the marking), 5 Rare Materials (pigments and carvings)
- Time to complete: 1 cycle
- Mechanical effect: Formally marks a territory as sacred to The Wild. The marked territory generates +10% of all natural resources (forest, river, game) that occur in it for 20 cycles. Animal units in the marked territory gain +5% all stats. Non-Wild units in the marked territory suffer a -5% movement penalty as the terrain seems subtly more resistant to them — roots shift, ground is uneven in ways that were not visible, water is deeper at fords. The Grove Marker itself becomes a territory-value multiplier.
- Faith intensity change: +4%
- Interruption: If the Grove Marker is attacked during the marking, the territory registers the desecration and generates a Wild Wrath event — all Wild units in the territory receive +15% combat stats for 5 cycles in response.

**The Animal Communion**
- Building required: Spirit Lodge (Tier 2) or higher, in a territory with native wildlife (not agricultural zones)
- Resource cost: 30 Provisions (offerings to draw the animals), 20 Rare Materials (bond tokens — specific items used to establish the communion bond), 10 Labor (for the Wild priests who conduct the rite)
- Time to complete: 2 cycles
- Mechanical effect: Establishes a formal communion bond between a designated Wild unit and the territory's native animals. The bonded unit gains a persistent companion animal effect — the specific animal varies by terrain type (forest: wolf, river delta: river eagle, hills: mountain cat, plains: stag). The companion animal grants the unit: +10% movement, +10% scouting radius, immunity to ambush from the relevant terrain type. The companion animal is not a separate unit — it is a property of the bonded unit. If the bonded unit dies, the Animal Communion must be performed again to restore the bond. Maximum 3 units can be bonded simultaneously per Spirit Lodge.
- Faith intensity change: +7%
- Interruption: If the Spirit Lodge is attacked during the Communion, the communion fails. The animal that was approaching the lodge retreats and cannot be called again for 5 cycles.

**The Renewal Rite**
- Building required: Spirit Lodge (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 40 Provisions, 25 Timber, 20 Labor
- Time to complete: 3 cycles
- Mechanical effect: A regenerative ritual performed over a territory that has been damaged by warfare (deforestation, agricultural burning, siege damage). At completion, all resource-producing improvements in the territory that were destroyed by combat are restored at 50% of original value without the usual rebuilding cost. Additionally, the territory's base resource generation is increased by 10% for 30 cycles — the Renewal Rite does not just restore what was lost but encourages the land to grow beyond its prior state. If a Renewal Rite is performed in a territory with no prior damage, the resource increase is 20% for 30 cycles rather than 10%.
- Faith intensity change: +8%
- Interruption: Active combat in the territory prevents the Renewal Rite from being performed. The land cannot be renewed while it is being fought over.

**The First Pact**
- Building required: The First Grove (Tier 3)
- Resource cost: 60 Timber, 50 Provisions, 30 Rare Materials, 20 Faith Currency
- Time to complete: 4 cycles
- Mechanical effect: The most powerful light doctrine ritual of The Wild. The First Pact formally renews the original covenant between the dynasty and the living world. At completion: all Wild faith intensity generation is increased by 20% for 40 cycles. All animal-bonded units gain a permanent stat increase (+5% all stats, permanent). The territory housing The First Grove becomes inviolable from a resource depletion standpoint — it cannot be logged, hunted out, or agricultural-stripped for the duration of the Pact. If the territory is invaded during the Pact's active period, the invading force enters an environment that is actively hostile to them — all terrain advantages double for the defending force.
- Faith intensity change: +18%
- Interruption: If The First Grove is attacked during the rite, the Pact fails. The First Grove's attempt to establish contact with the living world's deepest layer has been interrupted. This is the most severe desecration possible for a Wild dynasty and generates: -15% faith intensity, a Wild Wrath event across all Wild territories, and a permanent -10% resource generation in The First Grove's territory for 20 cycles, representing the land's grief at the failed connection.

**Passive doctrine effects:**

- At Active (20-40%): Wild units in territories with Grove Markers are invisible to enemy scouting within forest and dense terrain. They are not stealthed in a mechanical sense — they simply do not register on the enemy's minimap when in appropriate terrain. This reflects the Harmony Path's attentiveness to the living world operating as a form of camouflage.
- At Devout (40-60%): All animal cavalry units (bears, wolves, river mounts) gain +15% all stats. Animal companions bonded through Animal Communion generate their benefits at 25% higher rate. Forest and hill terrain in home territory generates 15% more resources as the Wild dynasty's stewardship compounds.
- At Fervent (60-80%): Wild units never trigger the panic propagation that normally spreads when allied units die nearby — the living world's indifference to individual death is spiritually integrated, so deaths are absorbed without the emotional cascade. Animal Communion rites cost 30% fewer resources. Wild faith buildings in home territory are difficult to find without specific scouting capabilities — they do not appear on enemy maps unless the enemy has active scouts in the territory.
- At Apex (80-100%): A dynasty-wide trait "Living World" activates. Every territory under Wild control that has been held for 10+ cycles generates its own sustained Renewal Rite effect without resource cost — the land is sustaining itself. Additionally, enemy military forces moving through Wild territories suffer -10% health per 5 cycles of sustained presence, representing the environment actively working against them without any Wild military engagement.
- Sustained Apex for 10+ cycles: The dynastic trait "First Children" activates. Wild units gain the permanent ability to perceive enemy movement in adjacent territories without scouting infrastructure — the animal network communicates across the terrain. This is a strategic information advantage that becomes more valuable the larger the territory under Wild control becomes.

**Spiritual manifestation (Fervent+ eligible):**

**The Living World**

The Living World manifests when The Wild reaches Fervent intensity on the Harmony Path and at least three territories have active Grove Markers. Its visual expression is not a specific entity — it is a diffuse greening of the territories under Wild control, a visible acceleration of growth that would normally take seasons compressed into cycles. Trees grow visibly. Grass moves against the wind. The quality of light in Wild territories shifts, becoming more dappled, more alive.

Mechanically, The Living World is a territory-modifier manifestation rather than a unit-specific effect. For its duration, all Wild territories generate double natural resources. All non-Wild units in Wild territories suffer -10% health per 5 cycles from environmental attrition — the land is not attacking them, but they are not welcome in it and the land knows it. All Wild units in home territory gain +15% all stats from the harmonized presence of the living world at full expression. Animal cavalry units gain +25% instead. The most tactically significant effect: all terrain bonuses for Wild units in home territory double. A forest that normally gives +15% defense to all units gives Wild units +30% defense while The Living World is manifested.

The Living World lasts 15 cycles. It does not end when a faith building is destroyed — it is not localized to a building. It ends only if faith intensity drops below Fervent. Enemy dark ritual attacks can suppress it temporarily (reducing it to 50% effectiveness for 3 cycles) but cannot end it early.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Thornrider (Level 3, Light Doctrine)**
The Thornrider is a cavalry unit who has undergone the Animal Communion rite specifically with a forest predator. Unique abilities:
- Terrain Surge: In forest or densely vegetated terrain, the Thornrider's charge deals 30% additional damage and has a 20% chance to cause the target to lose their next action (disoriented by the sudden emergence from dense cover).
- Wild Communion: The Thornrider can communicate with animals in the territory, generating intelligence on enemy unit positions within 4 hexes that bypasses conventional stealth. Once per battle, this can reveal hidden or stealthed units.
- Bramble Path: After moving, the Thornrider leaves a 2-hex trail that functions as difficult terrain for enemy units for 3 cycles. Wild units pass through it at normal speed.

**Forest Sovereign (Level 4, Light Doctrine)**
The Forest Sovereign is a veteran rider and nature-bonded champion who embodies the Harmony Path's vision of a warrior fully integrated into the living world. Unique abilities:
- Living Armor: The Forest Sovereign's connection to the living world gives them a natural regeneration of 5% health per cycle even during active combat. This regeneration stops only if they are in terrain with no natural elements (bare stone, heavily urbanized areas, desert).
- First Path: The Forest Sovereign can designate a movement path of up to 6 hexes at the start of a cycle. Any unit following the same path that cycle gains +10% movement. The Forest Sovereign has found the living world's preferred routes.
- Sovereign's Claim: When the Forest Sovereign enters a territory for the first time in a battle, that territory registers their presence and all Wild units in the territory gain +10% combat stats for 5 cycles. The land recognizes its champion.

**Apex expression — The First Wild (Light Doctrine path):**

The First Wild is not an individual. It is the living world's decision to send something of itself into the battle — not an animal, not a human, but a convergence of the two and the terrain itself. Visually, The First Wild appears as a figure that does not maintain a fixed form: it is partially mounted (something under and around it that is part of what it is), partially foliage (organic material woven through its form rather than worn over it), with eyes that glow with the particular green-gold of late afternoon light through a forest canopy.

Stats profile: The First Wild has the highest movement speed of any apex unit. It is not the most durable (lower defense than The Mandate, lower health ceiling than The Sacrificed) but it is almost impossible to catch and deals enormous damage in terrain-assisted circumstances. In forest terrain, its combat stats increase by 40%. In any terrain with a Grove Marker, by 30%. On bare stone or in heavily structured terrain, it fights at base stats, which are still high but not apex-tier.

Unique ability — The World Answers: Once per match, The First Wild can call the living world's full attention to a single battle. For 5 cycles, all terrain in the battle area becomes Wild-favored regardless of its actual type — even a stone fortress gains forest-equivalent terrain bonuses for Wild units. The environment itself participates in the fight. Enemy units suffer -3% health per cycle for the duration simply from the sheer biological pressure of an ecosystem paying attention to them.

The First Wild under the Harmony Path manifests in the presence of The Living World — the two effects stack, making Wild territories under both The First Wild and The Living World simultaneously effectively impenetrable to conventional military assault. The First Wild feels less like a unit deployed and more like a question the living world has finally decided to answer directly.

### Dark Doctrine Path — The Indifferent Wild Path

**What this doctrine believes:**

A dynasty following the Indifferent Wild path has absorbed the same attentiveness the Harmony Path cultivates and arrived at a different conclusion about what the living world is saying. The living world does not protect. It produces. It burns. It floods. It starves. It grows without caring which things it grows over. The insistence of life that the Harmony Path celebrates is also, honestly, the indifference of life. The tree that grows through a wall does not dislike the wall. It does not care about the wall. It simply grows, and the wall is in the way, and after enough cycles there is no wall. The Indifferent Wild path says: this is the truth, and a dynasty that has the courage to follow it will be the tree, and their enemies will be the wall.

This doctrine produces dynasties that are genuinely frightening not because they are cruel in a personal sense but because they are systematic. They do not pursue weakness out of malice. They pursue it because weakness is what the living world eliminates. The Bear Rider does not hate the infantry it rides through. The Bonebreaker does not despise the settlement it consumes. They are doing what the living world does: assessing what can sustain itself and what cannot, and acting accordingly.

**Ritual mechanics:**

**The Culling**
- Building required: Grove Marker (Tier 1) or higher, with a Bear Rider unit present
- Resource cost: 20 Provisions (for the feast afterward), 10 Rare Materials, 10 Labor
- Time to complete: 1 cycle
- Mechanical effect: A formal declaration that a designated weak population (low-loyalty, low-faith, low-productivity settlements) is subject to natural law's elimination. Mechanically: the targeted settlement's lowest-productivity portion of population is consumed — population count decreases by a fixed amount. In return: the remaining population's productivity increases by 20%, their food and resource generation efficiency increases (fewer mouths, same land), and the territory generates a Culling token. Culling tokens can be spent to accelerate the growth of a different settlement by providing the excess — the consumed population's resources are redistributed. This is the doctrine's vision of sustainable predation: the weak are fuel for the strong, and the ecosystem benefits.
- Faith intensity change: +6%
- Interruption: Cannot be interrupted. The Culling is a swift act.

**The Feral Consecration**
- Building required: Spirit Lodge (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 35 Provisions, 25 Rare Materials, 15 Labor
- Time to complete: 2 cycles
- Mechanical effect: Consecrates an animal companion through the Indifferent Wild framework rather than the Harmony Path's communion framework. The difference is significant: where Animal Communion creates a bond of understanding, the Feral Consecration creates a bond of shared predatory nature. The consecrated animal companion grants the bonded unit: +15% combat stats (higher than Animal Communion's +10%), +20% damage against routing or fleeing enemy units specifically (the predator's instinct to pursue), and the ability to detect wounded or low-health enemy units at +5 hexes range (the scent of weakness). The bonded unit permanently gains the trait "Feral Bond" — visible in their unit card and in diplomatic contexts, marking them as following the dark doctrine path of The Wild.
- Faith intensity change: +8%
- Interruption: If the Spirit Lodge is destroyed during the Consecration, the animal consecration fails catastrophically. The animal, now partially consecrated, becomes hostile to everyone in the territory for 3 cycles — a genuine combat hazard.

**The Territorial Claim**
- Building required: Spirit Lodge (Tier 2) or higher
- Resource cost: 40 Timber, 30 Provisions, 20 Rare Materials
- Time to complete: 3 cycles
- Mechanical effect: Performs a ritual that formally claims a territory through the Indifferent Wild's natural law framework — not as legal ownership but as predatory dominion. Any non-Wild unit that enters the claimed territory for the next 20 cycles experiences -10% all stats (the territory recognizes them as prey, not as part of the ecosystem). All Wild units in the claimed territory gain +15% all stats. Most significantly: any hostile act performed against the dynasty in the claimed territory during this period generates no negative Conviction events — the doctrine explicitly validates predatory defense of claimed territory as natural law. There is no moral weight to killing in your claimed territory.
- Faith intensity change: +9%
- Interruption: If combat occurs in the territory before the rite completes, the Territorial Claim accelerates — the interrupted rite completes immediately but at 60% effectiveness.

**The Great Consumption**
- Building required: The First Grove (Tier 3)
- Resource cost: 70 Timber, 60 Provisions, 40 Rare Materials, 25 Faith Currency
- Time to complete: 5 cycles
- Mechanical effect: The ultimate expression of the Indifferent Wild path. The First Grove is consecrated as the center of a zone of absolute natural predation for 30 cycles. In this zone (12 hexes from The First Grove): all non-Wild population settlements experience resource depletion at -5% per cycle, as if the land is being consumed from beneath them. Wild territories in the zone generate +25% all natural resources, as if the consumption is being redistributed. Enemy military units in the zone suffer -5% health per cycle. Wild cavalry units (Bear Riders, Bonebreakers) in the zone gain +30% all stats. This is the doctrine's vision of ecosystem equilibrium: Wild populations thrive because what they take from the weak feeds them.
- Faith intensity change: +20%
- Interruption: The ritual can be interrupted by a sustained assault on The First Grove across all 5 cycles. If interrupted in cycles 1-3, no effect. If interrupted in cycles 4-5, half the resource and stat effects apply but the population depletion effect does not.

**Passive doctrine effects:**

- At Active (20-40%): Bear Rider units are available. All Wild units deal +10% bonus damage to enemy units that are currently under any negative status effect (wounded, fleeing, corrupted, diseased). The doctrine rewards attacking weakness.
- At Devout (40-60%): Enemy populations in territories bordering Wild control experience faith conversion pressure through fear at +10% rate. Natural disaster events (floods, droughts, famines) in Wild territories generate faith intensity rather than consuming it — the doctrine interprets these events as the living world's natural pruning rather than punishment.
- At Fervent (60-80%): All Wild units gain +15% damage against enemies at below 50% health. Culling rites cost 30% fewer resources. Animal cavalry units gain an additional unique ability: the Pursuit instinct — if an enemy unit routes, the nearest animal cavalry unit may immediately move up to 3 hexes toward the routing unit as a free action, potentially catching and eliminating it before it escapes.
- At Apex (80-100%): A dynasty-wide trait "Natural Law" activates. All combat wins in Wild territories generate a Culling token in addition to normal spoils — victory is resource, which is what natural law says it should be. Enemy populations in adjacent territories experience -5% native faith generation per cycle from proximity to the doctrine's environmental effects. The living world is consuming outward.
- Sustained Apex for 10+ cycles: The dynastic trait "The World Has No Mercy" activates. All Wild units become permanently immune to morale breaks from death events — the doctrine has fully integrated death as function rather than loss. No unit in the Wild military will ever rout from grief at a fallen comrade.

**Spiritual manifestation (Fervent+ eligible):**

**The Consumed World**

Where the Harmony Path's Living World manifests as growth and presence, the Indifferent Wild's manifestation is The Consumed World — a visible expression of what the living world looks like when it stops producing and starts reducing. The visual is striking: the landscape in Wild territories that has been longest under their control begins showing signs of consumption. Trees are larger and darker. Animal shapes — not the warm, communal shapes of the Harmony path's communion — move in the shadows at the edge of visibility, predatory and purposeful. There is no sound that can be identified as sound. Only an ambient awareness of being watched by things that have made a decision about you.

Mechanically, The Consumed World is the inverse of The Living World. Rather than generating resources in Wild territory, it depletes them from enemy territory. All enemy territories within 8 hexes of active Wild territories experience -8% resource generation per cycle for the duration. Wild territories within the same radius generate +15% resources (the consumption flows inward). Enemy units in Wild territories suffer -2% health per cycle — a sustained attrition that does not spike but never stops. Bear Riders, Bonebreakers, and all animal cavalry in Wild territories gain +20% all stats. Most significantly: any enemy unit reduced to below 20% health in a Wild territory is flagged for Pursuit — every animal cavalry unit on the field can immediately initiate their free-action Pursuit move toward the flagged target.

The Consumed World lasts 12 cycles. It can be suppressed but not ended early by enemy action unless all Wild faith buildings in the adjacent territories are destroyed simultaneously — a nearly impossible military coordination requirement.

**Doctrine-specific unit abilities:**

**Bear Rider (Level 3, Dark Doctrine)**
The Bear Rider is the signature unit of the Wild's dark doctrine, a mounted combatant whose weapon is the bear as much as it is the rider. Unique abilities:
- Rending Charge: The Bear Rider's charge attack deals damage in two components: rider damage and bear damage, resolved separately. Total damage is 25% higher than equivalent cavalry charges. Against infantry in tight formations, bear damage is doubled — the bear tears through formation geometry in a way that a horse cannot.
- Pursuit: When an enemy unit routs, the Bear Rider may immediately move up to 4 hexes toward the routing unit as a free action. If the Bear Rider reaches the routing unit, it eliminates it rather than merely damaging it.
- Apex Predator: The Bear Rider deals +20% damage to any unit with a lower current health percentage than the Bear Rider. The weaker the target, the more dangerous the Bear Rider becomes against it.

**Bonebreaker (Level 4, Dark Doctrine)**
The Bonebreaker is what the Bear Rider becomes when the doctrine is fully internalized — a rider and bear pair who have lost the distinction between themselves and the natural law they represent. Unique abilities:
- Consumption Drive: Each enemy unit killed by the Bonebreaker increases its combat stats by +3% for the remainder of the battle, stacking up to a maximum of +30%. The more it kills, the stronger it gets.
- Unnatural Terror: Enemy units within 3 hexes of the Bonebreaker suffer -15% morale continuously. Units that witness the Bonebreaker kill an allied unit make an immediate morale check or begin routing.
- Territorial Wrath: In a territory with a Grove Marker or higher Wild faith building, the Bonebreaker's all stats increase by an additional +25% on top of any other terrain bonuses. The dark doctrine's version of terrain-attuned combat: in the Wild's claimed territory, the Bonebreaker is expressing the land itself.

**Apex expression — The First Wild (Dark Doctrine path):**

The Dark Doctrine First Wild appears as something the light doctrine version looks like from a distance but reveals itself to be something fundamentally different up close. Where the Harmony Path's First Wild has the quality of life at full expression, the Indifferent Wild's First Wild has the quality of life at full consumption. The foliage woven through its form is not growing — it is consuming. The mounted element under it is not a companion animal but something that has been partially absorbed into the rider, the boundary between them dissolved. Its eyes are amber, predatory, with no recognition in them for anything outside the living world's own hierarchy.

Stats profile: The Dark Doctrine First Wild has the same movement speed as the light doctrine version. It deals 40% more damage. It has lower health than the light doctrine version but compensates with a regeneration mechanic: it heals 3% per enemy unit killed. Against routing or fleeing enemies, it deals 60% more damage. In forest terrain, its combat stats increase by 50% rather than 40%.

Unique ability — The World Has No Mercy: Once per match, The First Wild can invoke the Indifferent Wild's ultimate expression, triggering a 7-cycle state in which all animal cavalry units on the field gain the First Wild's movement speed and deal damage as if they were the First Wild themselves. The entire Wild cavalry force becomes, briefly, apex predators. During this state, the Pursuit free-action mechanic applies to every Wild unit on the field, not just cavalry. There is nowhere to flee.

The Consumed World manifestation accompanies The Dark Doctrine First Wild for its full presence on the field rather than the first 8 cycles — the unit's continued existence sustains the manifestation. The combination creates an environment in which resources are actively flowing from enemy territories to Wild territories, enemy units are in sustained attrition, and the First Wild is hunting the weakest elements of the enemy force specifically.

## Cross-Covenant Mechanics

### Faith Alliance mechanics

A Faith Alliance activates when two dynasties of the same covenant form a military alliance and both have active faith intensity at Devout or higher. The faith bond creates specific bonuses beyond those of standard military alliances.

**Same covenant, same doctrine path:**
When two dynasties share both covenant and doctrine path, the bonuses are amplified. This represents the most natural alignment possible — not merely the same god but the same understanding of what that god asks for.
- Combined faith intensity bonus: Each dynasty's faith intensity generation increases by 15%, representing the spiritual resonance of a shared faith practiced by allied populations.
- Shared ritual access: Each dynasty can perform the other's rituals at their own faith buildings at 75% of normal resource cost, as if they share an institutional relationship.
- Unified spiritual manifestation: If both dynasties are at Fervent or above simultaneously, their manifestations can merge — two Choirs of the Witnessed, or two Consumed Worlds, within proximity of each other amplify to 150% of single-manifestation effectiveness in their overlapping territory.
- Combat cohesion: Allied units of the same covenant and doctrine path fighting in the same territory gain +10% all stats from the presence of fellow believers.

**Same covenant, different doctrine path:**
This is the more interesting and more common case. Two dynasties of the same covenant but different doctrine paths share a theological root but disagree about what it requires. This produces a faith alliance that is productive but occasionally tense.
- Combined faith intensity bonus: 8% (lower than same-doctrine, reflecting the partial alignment).
- Diplomatic friction threshold: If one doctrine path performs a rite that the other doctrine path finds theologically contrary (an Old Light dark doctrine Purging Rite witnessed by a light doctrine Old Light ally, for example), a Doctrinal Tension event triggers. This does not break the alliance but imposes -20% diplomatic reception on all actions between the two dynasties for 10 cycles. If 3 Doctrinal Tension events occur within 30 cycles, a Schism Pressure event triggers.
- Cross-doctrine population effect: Allied populations experience cross-doctrine pressure. A light doctrine population bordering a dark doctrine allied dynasty will experience gradual conversion pressure toward the dark path. This is not inevitable but requires active maintenance to prevent.

**Faith Alliance schism:**
If a Faith Alliance breaks — regardless of whether it was same-doctrine or different-doctrine — a Schism event occurs with consequences proportional to the alliance's duration and the faith intensity of both parties.
- Brief alliance (under 20 cycles): Minor schism. -15% faith intensity for both parties, -20% diplomatic reception between them for 30 cycles.
- Established alliance (20-60 cycles): Moderate schism. -25% faith intensity for both parties. A Schism Declaration is generated, visible to all factions, publicly identifying the breach. Neutral factions reduce their diplomatic willingness with both parties by 10%.
- Long alliance (60+ cycles): Major schism. -35% faith intensity for both parties. A formal Heresy Declaration can be issued by either party against the other — using the same mechanics as the Writ of Unclean Declaration or equivalent for other covenants. Both populations experience cross-faith conversion pressure toward different covenants as the shared identity fractures.

### Holy War mechanics

Beyond the political event that initiates a Holy War, the state of Holy War creates specific mechanical conditions that differ from conventional warfare.

**Declaration conditions:**
A Holy War can be declared when: the declaring dynasty is at Fervent or Apex intensity, the target dynasty follows a different covenant, and either a faith aggression threshold has been crossed (conquest of faith buildings, ritual disruption, faith-conversion above 30% of declared dynasty's home territory) or the dominant global faith threshold has been reached (one faith constitutes 50%+ of all population).

**What changes in Holy War state:**

Faith intensity gain during combat: Any military action conducted in a Holy War state generates faith intensity for the declaring dynasty at a rate of +1% per major battle won, rather than the passive generation from buildings alone. The war itself becomes a faith act. The declared-upon dynasty generates +2% faith intensity from defensive victories — being the target of a Holy War creates a rally effect.

Unit behavior: All units belonging to the declaring dynasty gain the Holy War trait for the duration of the war. This trait gives +15% combat morale across all forces and makes units immune to conversion-via-conquest (enemy faith buildings in captured territory have no conversion effect on Holy War-designated forces). Units belonging to the declared-upon dynasty gain +10% combat morale from the Rally of the Faithful — being attacked for their faith strengthens it.

Population effects: In territories that change hands during a Holy War, faith conversion is accelerated by 50% in both directions — victories convert faster, but any reversal of those gains also converts back faster. Holy War territories are highly volatile faith-wise compared to conventional conquest.

Spiritual manifestation eligibility: During a Holy War, both parties' spiritual manifestations (if eligible by intensity) have their duration extended by 30%. The divine presence intensifies under conditions of religious conflict.

Holy War conclusion: A Holy War ends through military victory (one side is militarily unable to continue), negotiated settlement (rare — both parties must agree through the diplomatic system), or exhaustion (both dynasties' faith intensity drops below Active simultaneously, suggesting the population no longer sustains the war's religious meaning). At conclusion, the victor gains +20% faith intensity and a Mandate of Victory status that provides diplomatic weight for 30 cycles. The defeated dynasty suffers -25% faith intensity and a Period of Doubt in which their covenant's passive mechanics operate at 75% for 20 cycles.

### Faith conversion of conquered populations

When a dynasty conquers a territory, they inherit a population with existing faith composition. Converting that population is one of the game's primary faith mechanics.

**Methods of conversion:**

Natural pressure conversion: Simply holding a territory with active faith buildings creates passive conversion pressure. The rate is 2% per cycle at base, modified by the difference in faith intensity between the conquering dynasty and the conquered territory's prior faith. A high-intensity faith dynasty converts low-intensity populations much faster than it converts high-intensity ones.

Active missionary conversion: Requires a Tier 2 faith building in the territory (Covenant Hall, Hall of Remembrance, Hall of Mandate, or Spirit Lodge). Generates 5% conversion per cycle but costs 20 Provisions and 10 Gold per cycle to maintain. A missionary mission must be actively managed — it does not run automatically.

Conversion through ritual: Performing any faith ritual in the conquered territory generates conversion pressure at double the natural rate for 5 cycles per ritual. Multiple rituals stack.

Forced conversion: Available only to dark doctrine paths with specific buildings and faith intensity thresholds. The Dark Order's Public Judgement can be used as a coercion mechanism, generating 10% conversion per application but also generating Horror events visible to neighboring factions. Dark Blood Dominion's Tithe of the Weak converts remaining population faster than natural pressure but reduces population count. The mechanics are effective but visible.

**Costs, timelines, and risks:**

Timeline for full conversion: A population at 80% of its original faith converting under natural pressure alone at base rate would take approximately 40 cycles to reach 50% new faith composition, and 80 cycles to reach full conversion. Active missionaries halve these numbers. Forced conversion compresses the timeline by approximately 75% but accelerates resistance risk.

Resistance risk: Populations that retain more than 30% of their original faith after 20 cycles of occupation begin generating Conversion Resistance events. These events reduce conversion rate, increase unrest, and can trigger small revolts in high-faith settlements. The strongest conversion resistance comes from populations at Devout or above in their original faith — these populations have a faith anchor that is difficult to dislodge.

Conversion rollback: If the conquering dynasty loses faith intensity below Active during the conversion process, all progress reverts at 50% of the rate it was gained. Conversions that are not fully complete are fragile. Only populations that have reached full conversion (80%+ new faith) resist rollback effectively.

Faith building compatibility: Some faiths convert more easily to specific neighboring faiths. Old Light and The Order's populations intermix with some tension but without extreme resistance. Blood Dominion populations resist Old Light conversion specifically (the theological distance is maximal). Wild populations resist Order conversion specifically. These compatibility differences modify conversion rate by 25% in either direction.

### Faith intensity decay — full model

Faith intensity is not a static resource. It is a living condition that requires active maintenance to sustain and naturally trends toward the lowest sustainable level without input.

**Base decay rate:**
Without any maintenance activity (no rituals, no active buildings, no Bloodline Member devotion), faith intensity decays at the following rates per cycle:

- Latent (0-20%): No decay. Latent faith is the floor state and is stable without input.
- Active (20-40%): -1% per cycle. At base, an Active faith dynasty that performs no maintenance will return to Latent in 20 cycles.
- Devout (40-60%): -2% per cycle. Without maintenance, return to Active in 10 cycles.
- Fervent (60-80%): -3% per cycle. Without maintenance, return to Devout in roughly 7 cycles.
- Apex (80-100%): -4% per cycle. The highest tier is the hardest to maintain. Without input, return to Fervent in 5 cycles.

**Minimum maintenance to hold steady at each tier:**

Each tier has a specific maintenance budget — the minimum faith intensity generation per cycle required to offset decay.

- Active: 1% per cycle generation. This requires at minimum one active Tier 1 faith building in a settled territory. A single Wayshrine, Blood-Altar, Lawpost, or Grove Marker in an occupied territory provides approximately 1-2% per cycle. Low-maintenance tier.
- Devout: 2% per cycle. Requires either multiple Tier 1 buildings or one Tier 2 building. An active Hall of Remembrance, Covenant Hall, Hall of Mandate, or Spirit Lodge provides 2-4% per cycle under normal conditions. Achievable without active ritual performance.
- Fervent: 3% per cycle. Requires Tier 2 buildings plus active ritual performance (at least one ritual every 5 cycles) or one Tier 3 building. At this intensity, maintenance begins to require genuine investment of resources and attention.
- Apex: 4% per cycle. Requires active Tier 3 building plus regular ritual performance plus population faith composition above 60% in the majority of home territories. Holding Apex is the most resource-intensive steady state in the faith system. A dynasty can hold Apex but it costs them.

**Accelerated decay triggers:**

Enemy faith buildings in territory: -1% per cycle per active enemy faith building within home territory. Multiple enemy buildings stack. A territory with 3 enemy faith buildings decays at +3% above base rate.

Military defeat in home territory: A significant military defeat (loss of 10+ units in home territory in a single cycle) triggers an immediate -5% faith intensity event representing the population's spiritual crisis. This cannot be mitigated by ritual in advance but can be partially offset by performing the Rite of Ash Return (Old Light) or equivalent consolation rites within 3 cycles.

Bloodline Member death or apostasy: Each Member death generates -3% faith intensity. Apostasy — a Member formally renouncing the covenant — generates -8% and triggers a population event. If the apostate Member joins another faith, they carry -15% faith intensity for the original dynasty and begin generating conversion pressure for their new faith.

Enemy territory occupation: If more than 40% of home territories are under enemy occupation, faith intensity decays at double the base rate. The faith is being systematically displaced.

Doctrine path misalignment (advanced rule): If the dynasty's accumulated conviction pattern and their active doctrine path are significantly misaligned — i.e., a dynasty pursuing the Dark Doctrine mechanically while having consistently Light Conviction choices — their faith intensity generation from building passive effects is reduced by 20%. The living world, the covenant, the law, and the light all respond to what is genuinely practiced, not merely to what buildings are present.

**Recovery from below minimum:**

If faith intensity drops below the threshold for a tier and maintenance is restored, recovery follows the same rate as natural generation — there is no penalty for having dropped. The faith does not remember the gap. It simply resumes from where it is. This design prevents the system from punishing dynasties that suffer temporary disruption too severely while still making sustained neglect have real consequences.
