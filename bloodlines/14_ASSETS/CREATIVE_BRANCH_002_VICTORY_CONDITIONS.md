## VICTORY PATH 1: MILITARY CONQUEST

### Core Philosophy

Military Conquest is not a victory of annihilation — it is a victory of dominion. The dynasty that wins through conquest has not simply destroyed its enemies; it has built a civilization so formidable, so disciplined, and so culturally coherent that resistance against it collapsed under its own futility. This is the path of the dynasty that turned soldiers into a civilization and war into an institution. It demands that the player build an empire that can be held, not merely taken. Every castle garrisoned, every road fortified, every conquered lord who kneels and serves rather than escapes and rebels — these are the true marks of conquest victory. The Military Conquest winner did not win because their armies were invincible. They won because their state made conquest permanent.

### Prerequisites

Before Military Conquest becomes a viable primary path, the following conditions must be functional:

**Infrastructure prerequisites:**
- At least three fully garrisoned fortifications (Castle-tier or above) in different territorial zones
- An active war council building (unlocks military doctrine trees)
- Conscription infrastructure capable of sustaining armies across at least two active fronts simultaneously
- Functional road network connecting all core territories (reduces suppression cost)
- At least one siege weapons manufactory

**Resource prerequisites:**
- Sustained iron and timber production sufficient to replace 30% of total army strength per campaign season without running a deficit
- Food surplus at 15% above army consumption requirements (starvation triggers mutiny chains)
- At least one treasury reserve sufficient to pay army wages for 20 match-minutes without income (campaign fund)

**Conviction prerequisites:**
- Moral and Neutral conviction both support Conquest. Moral conquest players receive loyalty bonuses in occupied territories but move slower diplomatically. Neutral conquest players have the clearest path to the raw mechanics. Cruel conquest players can pursue conquest but face accelerated rebellion and defection rates — the path is harder but not blocked.
- Conviction must not be at Cruel extreme (below -80 on a -100 to +100 axis) without a counterbalancing Fervent or Apex faith presence in the core territory, or army loyalty thresholds become impossible to sustain.

**Diplomatic prerequisites:**
- At least two kingdoms at Hostile or War state (conquest requires targets)
- No more than four kingdoms at Allied state (alliance web limits conquest target range without triggering coalition formation prematurely)

**Faith prerequisites:**
- No minimum faith intensity required. However, Faith at Apex tier in the opposing dynasty creates suppression cost multipliers for occupied territories. Conquest players benefit from having at least Devout faith presence to counter-suppress occupied religious populations.

### The Victory Path — Stage by Stage

**Early Game (0 to 30 minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 0 to 60 minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

The early conquest player is building the war machine's foundation before anyone else has identified them as a military threat. The correct early-game posture is controlled aggression — take the first minor tribes and frontier territories, but do not yet push against the nine founding houses directly. The goal is to accumulate occupied territory and test the garrison system without triggering early coalition formation.

The player is building:
- Barracks chains in every core territory
- Iron processing and timber cutting operations oriented toward weapons rather than trade
- Border fortifications that double as launching platforms for later campaigns
- A treasury reserve (do not overspend on prestige or faith infrastructure in this phase)

The first occupation threshold to cross is 15% of the total map territory garrisoned and holding stability above 50%. This is the foundation line. Below it, the military path is viable but fragile. Above it, the player has demonstrated they can hold what they take.

Opponents in this phase typically ignore early conquest expansion unless it directly threatens their borders. The tell is visible: military players have low faith investment, visible barracks density, and growing army counts on the map interface. Aware opponents begin shoring up their own defenses. Most do not recognize the pattern until mid-game.

**Mid Game (30 to 90 minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 60 to 180 minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

Mid-game is the hot war phase. The conquest player is pushing against at least two of the nine founding houses simultaneously, or sequentially with minimal rest. The strategic goal is to deny opponents recovery time between campaigns. A kingdom that falls, regroups, and re-challenges is twice as expensive as one that is systematically dismantled.

The player is building:
- Siege engine stockpiles targeted at castle-tier fortifications
- Occupation administration infrastructure (governors, garrison chains, tax extraction systems)
- Suppression units for newly occupied territories with high resistance populations
- Intelligence networks to identify which enemy commanders are loyalty risks (targets for bribery or assassination before major battles)

The mid-game thresholds to cross are:
- 30% of map territory under direct control (the point at which The Great Reckoning begins to register this player as a potential hegemon)
- At least three of the nine founding houses reduced to Hostile or below (vassalized, destroyed, or exiled)
- Army loyalty ratings above 70% across all active fronts (below this, mid-campaign mutiny becomes statistically likely)

Opponent response in mid-game is where the path gets expensive. Neighboring kingdoms begin forming defensive coalitions. The conquest player will typically face the first Hostile coalition at or near the 35% territory mark. This coalition must be either disrupted diplomatically (peel off members through separate peace treaties or bribery) or defeated militarily before it consolidates.

The worst mid-game mistake is overextension: taking territory faster than garrison infrastructure can follow. Ungarrisoned occupied territories generate a suppression debt that compounds. By late game, a conquest player who overextended in mid-game finds their army fighting their own empire.

**Late Game (90+ minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 180+ minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

Late game for Military Conquest is the final push to the victory threshold while managing The Great Reckoning coalition. At 70% territory control, The Great Reckoning fires. Every surviving dynasty receives the coalition bonus. Trueborn mobilizes. The conquest player must now hold what they have, finish off or vassalize the remaining holdouts, and sustain stability across the empire long enough to cross the final threshold and hold it.

The player is doing:
- Suppressing rebellion in overextended territories
- Buying off or assassinating coalition leaders
- Using the economic surplus accumulated mid-game to fund the final campaigns
- Timing the final push to cross the victory threshold in a single coordinated campaign rather than grinding slowly across it (slow crossing gives opponents more time to react)

The decisive late-game question is garrison math: can the conquest player maintain 65% stability across all occupied territories while the Reckoning coalition is actively raiding their borders? This is the test the path was always building toward.

### The Victory Threshold

All five of the following conditions must be met simultaneously and sustained for 10 continuous match-minutes (scaled for match length — 10 minutes in a 2-3 hour match, 20 minutes in a 4-6 hour match, 30 minutes in an 8-10+ hour match):

1. **Territory control:** 75% of total map territory under direct dynastic control (not vassal control — the player's own administration)
2. **Stability floor:** Average stability across all held territories at or above 60%
3. **Active army count:** At least one functional field army (minimum 500 strength units) in existence (cannot win through pure administration — requires military presence)
4. **No successful active rebellion:** No rebellion with 100+ units active within dynastic territory during the sustained window
5. **Surrender or vassalization of at least 6 of the 9 founding houses:** Dead, exiled, vassalized, or formally surrendered dynasties count. Allied but independent dynasties do not count.

The victory is declared by the game when all five conditions are met simultaneously and the timer expires. There is no player declaration step. The game announces "The [Dynasty Name] holds dominion over the known world" with a cinematic sequence showing the final territories folding under the dynasty's banner.

There is no final battle requirement, but in practice the sustained stability window forces a final military confrontation with whoever is holding out, because active enemies will raid and destabilize territories during the window.

### Visibility to Other Players

**Publicly visible at all times:**
- Total territory percentage (visible to all players on the map)
- Army strength counts (visible when armies are within vision range)
- Barracks and fortification counts (visible once a player has a spy network or adjacent territory)
- Whether The Great Reckoning has been triggered (global event notification)

**Visible at specific thresholds:**
- At 40% territory: the game broadcasts a "Rising Hegemon" warning to all players, identifying the dynasty by name
- At 60% territory: the game broadcasts "The [Dynasty] marches toward dominion — their conquest has no end"
- At 70%: Reckoning triggers, full coalition visibility activates

**Hidden until crossed:**
- The player's specific garrison stability numbers (opponents cannot see internal suppression rates without a spy network in those territories)
- Army loyalty ratings (hidden without intelligence infiltration)
- Which of the five victory conditions have been met (opponents see territory count but not the full checklist)

**Unambiguous path tell:** A conquest player who has moved through 40% of the map in mid-game with visible military infrastructure and no meaningful faith or economic investment is unambiguously on this path. The tell is the shape of their expansion — continuous adjacent territory rather than strategic trade post placement.

### Counter-Play

**Optimal counter-strategy:**

The conquest path has a fundamental vulnerability: stability. A conquest player's territory is only as strong as their garrison and suppression infrastructure. The optimal counter-strategy is not to defeat their armies directly — it is to make their empire expensive to hold.

Step one is attrition behind their lines. Raiding parties, sympathizer uprisings, and assassination of occupation governors all drive suppression costs up without requiring a decisive field battle. A conquest player managing four active rebellions cannot simultaneously push their front lines forward.

Step two is disrupting their supply chain. Military conquest is extraordinarily resource-intensive. Food, iron, timber, and coin all flow toward the front. Interdicting trade routes, raiding resource nodes, and burning granaries forces the conquest player to divert campaign resources to logistics instead of expansion.

Step three is economic war. A conquest player who neglects economic development (common — military infrastructure crowds out trade investment) can be isolated financially. Raising trade route tariffs, blocking currency exchange access, and forming economic leagues that exclude the conquest player creates a siege-without-walls dynamic.

**Point of no return:**

If the conquest player crosses 60% territory control with stability above 65% across their empire and no active major rebellion in progress, it is extremely difficult to stop them through conventional means. At that point, the only viable counter is The Great Reckoning coalition acting as a unified force — not fragmented reactive defense.

The genuine point of no return is when the victory threshold timer begins. Once the 10-minute window starts, stopping the conquest player requires either triggering a major rebellion within their territory (hard at that point — suppression infrastructure is mature) or fielding a coalition army large enough to seize and hold territory, dropping the conquest player below 75%.

**Natural coalition formations:**

Faith Divine Right and Territorial Governance players both have reason to oppose conquest — it eliminates their player base and their path's prerequisites. Economic Dominance players oppose conquest because conquered economies stop functioning as free-trade networks. Dynastic Prestige players oppose conquest because conquest often involves killing the bloodline members on whom Prestige players depend for their own win condition.

In other words: a well-advanced conquest player triggers opposition from all four other path players simultaneously. This is by design. The Reckoning is the game's structural guarantee that conquest has to be earned.

**Fatal mistakes for the conquest player:**

- Skipping garrison infrastructure to push faster. This creates a stability debt that compounds.
- Destroying rather than vassalizing. Dead kingdoms cannot be taxed, conscripted, or stabilized. They become ghost territories that fuel endless rebellion.
- Neglecting faith suppression in conquered territories. A Fervent enemy faith population in an occupied territory generates rebellion faster than any other single variable.
- Triggering the Reckoning before army fatigue has recovered. Launching the final push on exhausted armies invites coalition counter-attacks that the player cannot repel.

### Victory Path Interactions with Other Paths

**Military Conquest and Territorial Governance:**
These paths are structurally opposed in method but share a territory threshold. A Territorial Governance player who is attracting willing integration is building toward the same map percentage as a conquest player. They compete for the same territories but through opposite means. A conquest player can absorb a Territorial Governance player's willingly integrated territories, but those absorbed populations retain their cultural loyalty to the previous dynasty — generating ongoing suppression requirements that a purely military occupier is not built to handle. Pursuing both simultaneously is extremely difficult: the brutal efficiency of conquest poisons the goodwill that Territorial Governance requires.

**Military Conquest and Dynastic Prestige:**
These paths have significant synergy in the early-to-mid game. Military victory generates Prestige points through battlefield honors, major battle victories, and the capture of rival bloodline members. A dynasty pursuing Military Conquest with a secondary Prestige accumulation is viable — the Prestige serves as a safety valve in the event the military threshold becomes unreachable. The tension arises when Prestige requirements demand preserving rival bloodline members (for marriage and treaty prestige) while conquest logic demands their elimination.

**Military Conquest and Faith Divine Right:**
These paths are compatible early but conflict severely at the Ideological Expansion stage. Faith Divine Right requires maintaining specific faith thresholds in home territories. Conquest requires sustained resource focus on military infrastructure, crowding out faith investment. A dynasty trying to hold Fervent or Apex faith intensity while running four campaign fronts will find both objectives degraded. The paths can coexist at Active faith intensity, but Devout and above becomes incompatible with full military expansion unless the player accepts slower conquest pacing.

**Military Conquest and Economic Dominance:**
These paths are almost entirely incompatible. Economic Dominance requires a trust-based trade network, which in turn requires at least Neutral conviction and demonstrated reliability as a trade partner. Military conquest players tend toward actions that destroy trust (occupation, resource seizure, route interdiction). A conquest player can benefit economically from conquered trade hubs, but they cannot build the currency standard trust network while actively raiding their way across the map. The only viable overlap is taking the Trueborn City intact and immediately using its trade infrastructure — but this is a single inflection point, not a sustained dual-path.

### Conviction Constraints

**Moral conviction (positive axis):**
Moral conquest players receive occupied population loyalty bonuses (+15% starting stability in newly occupied territories) and access to the "Liberation" narrative frame — conquered lords who accept Moral rule generate less rebellion than those under neutral or cruel occupation. The downside is that Moral conviction players face higher costs for siege warfare (the faith population disapproves of deliberate destruction) and cannot use the most efficient suppression tools (mass conscription of conquered populations, starvation sieges).

**Neutral conviction (0 to mild positive or negative):**
The optimal conviction band for Military Conquest. Neutral players have access to the full toolkit — they can use harsh suppression when necessary and offer generous terms when advantageous. No penalties on either side. The path is designed around Neutral as the base case.

**Cruel conviction (negative axis):**
Cruel conquest is harder but not impossible. Cruelty accelerates early expansion (enemies surrender faster to avoid atrocities) but dramatically increases rebellion rates in occupied territories. A Cruel conquest player faces a suppression math problem: they take territory cheaply but hold it expensively. The viable Cruel conquest strategy is to minimize the number of territories held at any given time by conquering, extracting, and moving on — but this conflicts with the stability threshold in the victory conditions. Cruel players must compensate with heavy garrison investment and faith suppression infrastructure. The path is playable but punishing.

**Optimal conviction posture:** Neutral, drifting slightly positive (Moral-leaning Neutral) gives the best combination of flexibility and occupied population compliance. Pure Moral is viable but slower. Pure Cruel is viable but requires compensating infrastructure investment.

### House Affinity

**Ironmark:** The house of forged discipline and military doctrine. Ironmark's founding culture orients around professional soldiery, fortification mastery, and the systematic reduction of enemy positions. Their unique units and building trees are built for sustained occupation and long-campaign sustainability. Ironmark is the canonical Military Conquest house.

**Stonehelm:** The mountain lords whose geographic position makes defense and expansion naturally complementary. Stonehelm has mechanical bonuses to garrison efficiency and fortification construction cost. Their terrain advantages in mountain passes make their core territories nearly impregnable, giving them the stable rear base that conquest demands.

**Westland:** The frontier dynasty whose entire history is one of expansion into ungoverned territory. Westland has early-game expansion bonuses — cheaper road construction, faster border fortification, and minor tribe integration bonuses (relevant for the early conquest phase against non-house territories). They are not the best mid-to-late game conquest house, but they accelerate the foundational phase faster than any other.

### The Loss Condition

Military Conquest fails through internal collapse rather than external defeat. The specific failure modes are:

**Stability cascade:** If three or more territories drop below 30% stability simultaneously, a cascade effect triggers that spreads to adjacent territories. A conquest player who has overextended and triggered multiple rebellions simultaneously can watch their empire dissolve in 15-20 match-minutes. This is the most common failure mode.

**Army loyalty failure:** If army loyalty drops below 40% on the primary field army (typically through sustained resource shortage or excessive campaign duration without rest), a mutiny event triggers. A mutinied army dissolves, defects partially to a rival dynasty, or turns on occupation administrators. Recovering from a mid-campaign mutiny is extremely difficult.

**Reckoning coalition decisive victory:** If the Reckoning coalition, acting in coordination, successfully seizes enough territory to push the conquest player below 60% control while simultaneously triggering rebellions in the remaining held territory, the conquest player cannot recover the territory count without stabilizing first. The window for this counter-offensive is narrow but real.

**Gradual degradation:** A conquest player who wins every battle but never crosses the stability threshold looks like they are winning until they are not. A player at 74% territory with 58% average stability is perpetually one rebellion cascade away from losing 10 points of territory. Gradual degradation is the failure mode that conquest players least recognize until it is too late.

## VICTORY PATH 2: ECONOMIC DOMINANCE (CURRENCY STANDARD)

### Core Philosophy

Economic Dominance is the victory of a dynasty that understood, before anyone else did, that power flows through commerce. The dynasty that wins through the currency standard did not merely accumulate wealth — it became the world's financial architecture. Every trade route that settled in their coin, every kingdom that borrowed from their banks, every merchant that priced their goods in their currency instead of gold was a vote for this dynasty's supremacy — a vote cast freely, driven by rational self-interest, not conquest or coercion. The currency standard victory is the victory of trust compounded into hegemony. It says: this civilization built something the world needed, and the world chose it. The strategic depth of this path lies in the tension between that trust and the ruthless leverage that trust eventually enables. A dynasty that achieves currency dominance can purchase enemy armies, strangle credit to rivals, and collapse kingdoms without fielding a single soldier. But only if they kept their hands clean long enough to become indispensable.

### Prerequisites

Before Economic Dominance becomes a viable primary path:

**Infrastructure prerequisites:**
- Grand Market (capital-tier building unlocking currency issuance)
- Banking House (enables currency lending and dynasty reserve management)
- At least three active external trade routes (with separate non-vassal kingdoms)
- Mint (converts raw gold/silver into dynasty currency at volume)
- Trade Post network (at least five posts in foreign territories — requires diplomatic access)
- Commodity Exchange (enables price-setting for at least three trade goods)

**Resource prerequisites:**
- Gold surplus above 20% of annual consumption (currency issuance requires a reserve backing)
- At least three luxury commodities in domestic production (spices, silk, fine metals, rare stone — these are the anchor goods that make the currency standard attractive)
- No active food deficit (economic trust networks collapse instantly if the issuing dynasty is seen as unstable)

**Conviction prerequisites:**
- Moral or Neutral conviction required. Cruel conviction above -40 on the axis makes it impossible to reach the Trade Network Trust threshold necessary for currency adoption. Other dynasties will not accept the currency of a known tyrant as a standard because they cannot trust the issuing power to honor financial obligations.
- The conviction requirement is the tightest constraint on this path — players who drift Cruel while pursuing Economic Dominance will find trade partners quietly withdrawing, even without a declared reason.

**Diplomatic prerequisites:**
- At least five dynasties at Cordial or Allied state (trade network trust requires sustained positive diplomatic relations)
- No active war declarations against trade partner dynasties (war kills trade routes instantly)
- Formal trade agreements with at least three dynasties

**Faith prerequisites:**
- Faith intensity at Active or above within home territory (Latent faith is associated with cultural weakness, which undermines currency trust)
- No Apex-intensity hostile faith in major trade partner territories (zealot economies reject foreign currency standards on religious grounds)

### The Victory Path — Stage by Stage

**Early Game (0 to 30 minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 0 to 60 minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

The economic player's early game is invisible to opponents. They appear to be a prosperous, commercially-minded dynasty with no particular military ambition. This invisibility is the path's greatest early-game advantage. While conquest players are building armies and prestige players are managing bloodlines, the economic player is constructing the infrastructure that will make every other dynasty financially dependent.

The player is building:
- Trade post networks in as many foreign territories as diplomatic access allows
- Commodity production specialization (pick two or three anchor goods and produce them at scale)
- The Banking House and Grand Market as capital investments
- Diplomatic relationships — the early economic player should be the most agreeable dynasty at the table, offering favorable terms, avoiding military entanglements, and signing as many trade agreements as possible

The first meaningful threshold is establishing currency issuance. This requires the Grand Market, the Mint, and a gold reserve above 20%. Once the dynasty coin is issued, it begins circulating in trade networks. Circulation volume determines the path's progression.

The early mistake to avoid is military neglect. Economic players require a credible defensive military — not offensive, but sufficient to make conquest of their trade hub territories expensive. An economic player with no military is the most attractive target on the map.

**Mid Game (30 to 90 minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 60 to 180 minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

Mid-game is where the path either succeeds or stalls. Currency adoption has a network effect — once enough dynasties are trading in the dynasty's coin, it becomes easier to convince the remaining holdouts because they are losing access to trading partners who have already converted.

The player is building:
- Currency Exchange infrastructure in every city where trade posts are established
- Financial leverage instruments (the Credit system — dynasty offers loans to struggling kingdoms, denominated in dynasty currency, creating debt-based dependency)
- Intelligence networks to monitor which trade partners are considering alternative currencies
- Economic defensive tools — the ability to withhold currency access, raise tariffs, and trigger economic isolation against kingdoms that threaten the trade network

The mid-game thresholds to cross:
- 50% of active dynasties using the currency standard for at least 50% of their trade transactions (the "majority adoption" mark — this is when opponents start noticing the threat)
- Three dynasties with active debt obligations denominated in dynasty currency (leverage threshold — these dynasties now have direct financial incentive to not oppose the economic player)
- Trueborn City trade integration — the Trueborn City as world knowledge conduit is also the world's primary neutral trade hub. Integrating with Trueborn City's trade infrastructure (not conquering it — integrating diplomatically) is the single highest-value mid-game economic action. It accelerates currency adoption by 25-35%.

Opponent response in mid-game typically begins at the 50% adoption mark. Other players start recognizing the pattern. Military players may begin targeting trade post networks (easily destroyed, hard to rebuild under military pressure). Alternative currency movements may emerge — one or two dynasties attempt to establish competing standards. The economic player must now defend their network while expanding it.

**Late Game (90+ minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 180+ minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

Late game for Economic Dominance is the weaponization phase. The currency standard is not just an economic win condition — it is a weapon. At 70%+ adoption with active debt leverage on multiple dynasties, the economic player can:

- Purchase loyalty from enemy army units (units whose commanders are in debt to the dynasty currency system have measurable loyalty defection rates)
- Withhold currency access from specific dynasties (equivalent to an economic blockade — trade stops, treasury growth stops, army payment becomes difficult)
- Force economic dependency terms on minor kingdoms that cannot sustain themselves without access to the trade network
- Trigger the Resolution Battle by pushing any of the above hard enough that the target dynasty formally contests the currency standard

The late game is fundamentally about timing the Resolution Battle correctly. The economic player wants to trigger it when their military assets (purchased loyalty units, allied kingdoms under debt, their own defensive force) are at maximum concentration and the opposing coalition is fragmented.

### The Victory Threshold

All four of the following conditions must be met simultaneously and sustained for 15 continuous match-minutes (scaled: 15 min for 2-3 hour match, 30 min for 4-6 hour match, 45 min for 8-10+ hour match):

1. **Adoption rate:** 70% or more of all active (non-eliminated) dynasties using the dynasty's currency as their primary trade standard (defined as 60%+ of their transactions denominated in dynasty coin)
2. **Reserve backing:** Dynasty gold reserve above 30% of total issued currency value (the standard must be credible — over-issued currencies collapse)
3. **Trade network integrity:** At least 8 active external trade routes in operation simultaneously
4. **No contested state:** No active Resolution Battle in progress (the win cannot be claimed while the standard is being militarily disputed)

Alternatively, the victory can be achieved through the Resolution Battle path: if the economic player triggers a Resolution Battle, wins it decisively (capturing the opposing coalition's primary treasury or eliminating their military capacity to contest), and then meets the adoption rate and reserve thresholds, the victory is immediate without the sustained timer.

The victory is declared by the player through a formal "Claim the Standard" action in the Grand Market interface. The game validates the conditions and, if met, broadcasts the declaration globally. A 5-minute opposition window opens during which any dynasty may attempt to trigger a Resolution Battle. If no battle is triggered within the window, the win is confirmed.

### Visibility to Other Players

**Publicly visible at all times:**
- That a dynasty has issued its own currency (visible in trade interface when any dynasty enters a trade agreement with them)
- The number of trade posts a dynasty has placed in foreign territory (visible to the host territory's controlling dynasty)
- General prosperity indicators (wealthy territories are visibly different from poor ones)

**Visible at specific thresholds:**
- At 40% currency adoption: the game broadcasts a market bulletin "The [Dynasty] coin is becoming the language of commerce"
- At 60% adoption: global notification "The [Dynasty] currency standard dominates the known trade networks"
- At debt leverage threshold (3+ dynasties in debt): not publicly announced, but detectable by spy network activity against the economic player's banking infrastructure

**Hidden until crossed:**
- Exact adoption percentages (opponents see directional signals, not precise numbers, without banking spy infiltration)
- Which specific dynasties are under debt obligation (debt agreements are private)
- Reserve backing levels (internal financial data — hidden without banking espionage)

**Unambiguous path tell:** An economic player who has avoided all wars, maintains Cordial or Allied state with the majority of the table, and has visibly established trade post networks in multiple foreign territories is unambiguously on this path by mid-game. The absence of military investment and the presence of extensive diplomatic engagement are the clearest tells.

### Counter-Play

**Optimal counter-strategy:**

The currency standard's Achilles heel is trust. It requires the issuing dynasty to maintain consistent, positive, reliable behavior over a sustained period. The optimal counter-strategy is trust destruction — not through military force, but through narrative and economic subversion.

Establishing a competing currency is the highest-priority counter. Even a weaker competing standard fragments the network effect. A dynasty that issues its own coin and convinces three or four kingdoms to adopt it instead has directly reduced the dominant currency's adoption rate and prevented the threshold from being reached.

Targeting trade post infrastructure is the second lever. Trade posts are relatively low-strength buildings. A military dynasty with even a modest raiding force can systematically destroy trade posts in foreign territories, which interrupts the currency circulation and forces the economic player to continuously rebuild rather than accumulate.

Triggering diplomatic crises between the economic player and their major trade partners is the third lever. A fabricated insult, a manufactured border incident, a whisper campaign suggesting the currency is backed by debt rather than real reserves — these create diplomatic fractures that push trade partners from Cordial toward Neutral or Hostile, disrupting the relationship conditions required for currency adoption.

**Point of no return:**

At 65% adoption with three or more dynasties in active debt, the standard is extremely difficult to dislodge. Competing currencies at that point face a chicken-and-egg problem: they need adopters to be viable, but adopters won't leave a dominant standard for an uncertain alternative. The practical point of no return is when the Resolution Battle becomes the only counter option — and even then, the economic player often has the financial resources to outbid military opponents for loyalty.

The genuine last window is around 55% adoption. A coalition that destroys the economic player's Trueborn City integration and triggers simultaneous military raids on six or more trade posts at once can cause a visible adoption collapse that shakes partner confidence and starts a momentum reversal.

**Natural coalition formations:**

Military Conquest players oppose Economic Dominance because purchased army loyalty is an existential threat to the conquest path. A conquest player whose army starts receiving currency-standard loyalty payments from a rival dynasty is looking at defection and mutiny. Faith Divine Right players oppose Economic Dominance because Moral/Neutral conviction economic players represent a secular power center that competes with religious authority. Dynastic Prestige players have the weakest opposition motivation — prestige players and economic players can often coexist or even cooperate.

**Fatal mistakes for the economic player:**

- Neglecting military defense entirely. Trade posts without military protection are a free resource node for every raider on the map.
- Accepting any Cruel conviction drift. Even a single high-profile cruel action can cascade into trade partner defection.
- Over-issuing currency without reserve backing. If the reserve backing condition drops during the sustained window, the timer resets and partner confidence drops immediately.
- Entering a war during the accumulation phase. Wars kill trade routes, and dead trade routes during the adoption ramp-up phase can push the path back by 30-40 match-minutes.

### Victory Path Interactions with Other Paths

**Economic Dominance and Military Conquest:**
Structurally incompatible as described above. The one viable hybrid is the "imperial merchant" variant: conquer the Trueborn City and major trade hubs militarily, then use that captured trade infrastructure to accelerate currency adoption from a position of military dominance. This is extremely difficult to execute because the conviction damage from conquest makes trust-building expensive, but it is theoretically viable for a Neutral conviction player.

**Economic Dominance and Faith Divine Right:**
Mild incompatibility. Economic players at Moral conviction have access to "faith-trade" synergies — a dynasty with both widespread faith presence and dominant currency integration can leverage religious networks as distribution channels for currency adoption. This is one of the few cross-path synergies that produces genuine mechanical benefit. However, faith investment and economic infrastructure investment compete for the same resources, making the dual path slow.

**Economic Dominance and Territorial Governance:**
Strong synergy. Territorial Governance works by making your territory so prosperous that neighbors want to join. Economic Dominance works by making your currency standard so stable that neighbors want to adopt it. Both paths reward the same player behaviors: investment in domestic prosperity, positive diplomatic posture, and restraint from destructive military action. A player on Territorial Governance who is simultaneously accumulating currency adoption is arguably pursuing both paths in parallel. The limits emerge at the late game: Territorial Governance requires map territory thresholds that pull the player toward administrative expansion, while Economic Dominance is indifferent to territorial size as long as trade networks are intact.

**Economic Dominance and Dynastic Prestige:**
Complementary in mid-game. Prestigious dynasties make attractive financial partners. Economically dominant dynasties have the resources to fund expensive prestige activities (grand marriages, magnificent feasts, mercenary competitions). The paths do not directly conflict, and a secondary economic development strategy is a viable support layer for a primary Prestige player.

### Conviction Constraints

**Moral conviction (positive axis):**
Moral conviction is the natural home of the economic path. Moral players receive the Trade Network Trust modifier (+20% adoption rate acceleration), access to the "Faith-Trade Integration" synergy, and preferential treatment in diplomatic negotiation. The Moral economic player is the canonical version of this path.

**Neutral conviction:**
Viable and effective. Neutral conviction removes the Moral bonuses but also removes the risk of Cruel drift. A Neutral economic player has the flexibility to take occasionally harsh actions (blocking trade access to a hostile kingdom) without triggering the conviction penalties that would undermine trust.

**Cruel conviction (negative axis):**
Increasingly restrictive as conviction moves negative. Mild Cruel (-20 to -40) reduces adoption rate by 15% and makes debt agreements more expensive to negotiate. Moderate Cruel (-40 to -60) caps adoption rate at 55% maximum (insufficient to win). Severe Cruel (below -60) makes currency adoption mathematically impossible — no dynasty trusts a currency backed by a tyrant. At this point the path is blocked.

**Optimal conviction posture:** Moral, with careful management to avoid Moral extremism (extreme Moral prevents the credit leverage tools that make the path powerful in late game — a purely pacifist economic player cannot threaten withholding currency access, which is the primary late-game weapon).

### House Affinity

**Goldgrave:** The house whose name is literally their domain. Goldgrave's founding culture is merchant aristocracy — wealth as status, commerce as statecraft. Their unique buildings include the earliest access to the Grand Market, preferential Mint construction rates, and a unique Credit Line building that accelerates debt-leverage accumulation. Goldgrave is the canonical Economic Dominance house.

**Trueborn:** The Trueborn are the world's neutral arbiters and the keepers of the world knowledge conduit. Their natural diplomatic standing and trade hub position gives them unmatched early access to multi-dynasty trade networks. A Trueborn player pursuing Economic Dominance starts with the Trueborn City infrastructure already partially in their favor, making Trueborn City integration substantially cheaper than for any other house.

**Hartvale:** The agricultural breadbasket. Hartvale's commodity surplus provides the food and resource stability that an economic player desperately needs as a foundation. Hartvale's food production bonuses translate directly into trade goods surplus, which gives them a lower-cost path to the anchor commodity requirements. They are not the best late-game economic house, but they have the most stable foundation phase.

### The Loss Condition

Economic Dominance fails through trust collapse and isolation:

**Adoption reversal:** If three or more dynasties simultaneously withdraw from the currency standard (triggered by a successful competing currency launch, a major diplomatic breach, or a military defeat that makes the issuing dynasty look vulnerable), the adoption percentage can drop sharply below the threshold. Recovery from adoption reversal is extremely difficult because the perception of weakness accelerates further withdrawal.

**Reserve collapse:** If the gold reserve backing drops below 15% of issued currency value (typically through warfare draining the treasury), the currency begins losing value in trade markets. Trade partners reduce usage, adoption percentages drop, and the window for recovery is short. A prolonged treasury drain that is not repaired triggers a debt crisis event that can cascade into full adoption reversal.

**Resolution Battle loss:** If a Resolution Battle is triggered and the economic player loses militarily, the currency standard is formally contested, partners withdraw until the political situation stabilizes, and the adoption rate drops to a floor percentage. Recovery is possible but requires 40-60 match-minutes of rebuilding.

**Slow path failure:** A dynasty that reaches 50% adoption but cannot push past it for 30 match-minutes will typically see competing currencies established and adoption begin drifting backward. The network effect works both ways — a stalled standard loses credibility, and the slow bleed of trade partners to alternatives is the most demoralizing failure mode.

## VICTORY PATH 3: FAITH DIVINE RIGHT

### Core Philosophy

Faith Divine Right is the victory of a dynasty that believed, and made the world believe with them. Not through fear, not through armies, not through coin — but through the lived experience of a civilization under divine mandate. The dynasty that wins through Faith Divine Right has turned the abstract machinery of faith into the most concrete force in the game: the willingness of millions of people across a fractured world to accept one dynasty's claim to rule as legitimate, sacred, and ordained. This is not a religious victory in the narrow sense of converting enemies. It is a civilizational victory: this dynasty became the spiritual center of the world, and the world's resistance collapsed not because it was defeated, but because the idea of resisting a divinely blessed dynasty felt, to enough people, like fighting the sky. The Faith Divine Right winner has not just built a great civilization — they have convinced the world that their civilization was the point.

### Prerequisites

Before Faith Divine Right becomes a viable primary path:

**Infrastructure prerequisites:**
- Grand Temple or Cathedral (the capital-tier faith building for the dynasty's chosen faith)
- Faith Propagation Network (at least five faith outposts in non-home territories)
- Holy Order (military-faith hybrid unit tier unlocked — the enforcement arm of divine mandate)
- Pilgrimage Route Network (infrastructure connecting holy sites across multiple territories — drives faith intensity in transit populations)
- Sacred Library (unlocks advanced theological doctrine trees)

**Resource prerequisites:**
- Faith Tithes infrastructure generating at least 20% of total dynasty income from religious sources
- No active famine or poverty crisis in home territory (divine mandate is undermined by a starving homeland)
- Sufficient military strength to defend against coalition during the declaration window (minimum 40% of the largest opposing army's strength in defensive units)

**Conviction prerequisites:**
- Moral conviction strongly preferred. The Faith Divine Right path is designed around Moral conviction as the primary band. Neutral conviction players can pursue the path but face higher faith intensity thresholds. Cruel conviction players are mechanically blocked from the declaration: the formal Divine Right declaration requires a Faith Authority approval step that checks conviction history, and Cruel players cannot receive this approval without sustained redemptive action.

**Faith prerequisites:**
- Home territory faith intensity at Devout or above (Level 3 of 5) before declaration is permitted
- At least 3 of the 4 ancient faiths must have been formally engaged (either adopted, studied, or formally positioned against in doctrine)
- Dynasty faith intensity at 70% coverage of home territory (70% of home territory population at the dynasty's faith at Active or above)

**Diplomatic prerequisites:**
- At least two dynasties at Cordial or Allied state who share or are compatible with the dynasty's faith
- No active blasphemy charge from a rival faith authority (these can be contested through theological debate buildings but must be resolved before declaration)

### The Victory Path — Stage by Stage

**Early Game (0 to 30 minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 0 to 60 minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

The faith player's early game is rapid domestic faith construction and careful theological positioning. Unlike military players who must expand outward, faith players build inward first — constructing the spiritual infrastructure in their home territories before they can credibly propagate outward.

The player is building:
- Temples, shrines, and faith schools in every home territory district
- Pilgrimage route connections between home holy sites
- The Sacred Library for theological doctrine research
- An early faith outpost network in neighboring neutral territories (minor tribes are the easiest first converts — they have low faith resistance and generate faith network nodes cheaply)

The first meaningful threshold is reaching Devout intensity (Level 3) in the home capital territory. This unlocks the Holy Order and marks the player as a credible faith power rather than a merely observant dynasty. Below Devout, faith is decoration. At Devout, it is a political force.

Opponents in this phase typically dismiss the faith player as non-threatening. This is the faith path's early-game gift: it generates no visible military threat and no visible economic competition. Other players are building armies and trade networks. The faith player is filling in the spiritual map.

**Mid Game (30 to 90 minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 60 to 180 minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

Mid-game is the Ideological Expansion phase. The faith player is now actively propagating their faith into foreign territories and working toward the conditions for the formal Divine Right declaration.

The player is building:
- Faith propagation missions in enemy and neutral territories (each mission takes time and requires a faith envoy unit)
- Holy Order armies for defending faith territories and supporting propagation
- Theological debate infrastructure for countering rival faith authorities
- The prophetic announcement calendar — the pre-declaration requirement checklist must be met, and each requirement is publicly visible, so the player must time their announcement to hit as many requirements simultaneously as possible

The mid-game thresholds to cross:
- Fervent faith intensity (Level 4) in the home capital territory — unlocks the Divine Right Declaration mechanism
- Faith coverage at 50% across all dynasty-controlled territories at Active or above
- At least two foreign territories (outside home zone) with Active or above faith presence from the dynasty's faith
- Formal engagement with all four ancient faiths (each engagement records in the dynasty history and satisfies the theological breadth prerequisite)

The opposition response in mid-game is religious and political. Rival faith dynasties (those whose path depends on different faith states) begin countering with their own faith propagation to prevent the dynasty from reaching Fervent intensity in foreign territories. Military players may attempt to destroy faith infrastructure as a secondary objective in otherwise unrelated border conflicts.

**Late Game (90+ minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 180+ minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

The formal Divine Right Declaration is the defining late-game event for this path. Once declared:

- A spread clock activates (visible to all players) — typically a 20 match-minute window during a 2-3 hour game, scaled proportionally
- All milestones required for final victory are publicly displayed
- An opposition coalition window opens immediately
- The dynasty must reach all remaining thresholds while surviving the coalition
- The declaration cannot be withdrawn — the dynasty either wins or falls

The late game is primarily defensive. The faith player has been building toward this moment for the entire match. Now they must hold on while the world attacks. The Holy Order, diplomatic allies, and faith network provide the defensive layer. The spread clock determines how long they must hold.

### The Victory Threshold

The formal Divine Right Victory requires ALL of the following to be true at the moment the spread window expires:

1. **Home faith intensity:** Apex (Level 5) in the dynasty's capital territory
2. **Territorial faith coverage:** 60% of all controlled territories at Devout (Level 3) or above
3. **Foreign faith presence:** At least 40% of all non-dynasty territories have at least one Active (Level 2) faith presence from the dynasty's faith
4. **Surviving the declaration window:** The dynasty must still exist as a functioning polity when the window expires (not conquered, not in rebellion cascade)
5. **Faith authority recognition:** At least 4 of the 9 founding houses (living or represented) must have formally recognized the Divine Right declaration (recognition can be coerced, purchased, or earned through genuine faith spread)
6. **No active defilement:** No major holy site of the dynasty's faith may be under enemy occupation or desecration during the final 5 minutes of the window

The victory is declared by the game when the spread window expires and all conditions are met. The cinematic sequence shows the dynasty's faith symbols spreading across the world map. If conditions are not all met when the window expires, the declaration fails, the dynasty suffers a major prestige and faith authority collapse, and the path requires a full rebuild cycle before another declaration can be attempted (minimum 30 match-minutes before a second attempt is permitted).

### Visibility to Other Players

**Publicly visible at all times:**
- Faith intensity level in each territory (visible on map as faith overlay)
- Temple and cathedral construction (visible to any dynasty with adjacent territory or trade posts)
- When a dynasty reaches Fervent intensity (Level 4), a global notification fires: "[Dynasty] reaches Fervent devotion — the fires of faith grow higher"

**Visible at specific thresholds:**
- The formal Divine Right Declaration is a global event — all players are notified immediately with the full milestone checklist displayed
- All milestones on the declaration checklist are publicly visible during the spread window
- Opposition coalition window is announced publicly — all players know they have X minutes to join the opposition

**Hidden until crossed:**
- Theological doctrine research directions (opponents cannot see which doctrine upgrades are being pursued without spy network access to the Sacred Library)
- Faith mission progress in foreign territories (hidden until the mission reaches Active intensity in that territory)
- Which founding houses have privately agreed to recognize the declaration before the formal announcement

**Unambiguous path tell:** A dynasty with heavy temple construction, no serious military buildup, and active faith missions in multiple foreign territories is on this path by mid-game. The tell becomes undeniable when they reach Fervent intensity — the global notification is the confirmation.

### Counter-Play

**Optimal counter-strategy:**

Faith Divine Right has two counter levers: doctrinal and military. The doctrinal counter is establishing or reinforcing a competing faith authority before the declaring dynasty reaches Devout intensity. Once a rival faith authority has deep roots in territories the faith player wants to convert, propagation becomes a slow war of attrition rather than a clean expansion. The player who builds an impenetrable competing faith wall in three to four territories effectively caps the declaring dynasty's foreign faith coverage.

The military counter is faith infrastructure destruction. Temples, cathedrals, pilgrimage routes, and sacred libraries are buildings. Buildings burn. A targeted military raid on a faith player's sacred infrastructure during the mid-game propagation phase can set their timeline back by 30-40 match-minutes. The key targets are the pilgrimage route nodes — destroying a pilgrimage route drops faith intensity in all territories connected to it.

During the formal declaration window, the opposition coalition's goal is to capture or desecrate holy sites and to eliminate the faith player's military capacity to defend them. Holy Order units can be defeated. The faith player is not invincible militarily. The coalition must act quickly — the window is finite.

**Point of no return:**

Once the formal declaration window is active and the faith player has recognition from 4 of the 9 founding houses, stopping the victory becomes extremely difficult because winning requires taking down the recognition count, which means reconverting or intimidating dynasties that have already committed publicly to the declaration's legitimacy. The practical point of no return is when the spread window has less than 5 minutes remaining and all six conditions are already met.

The last realistic window to stop the path entirely (preventing the declaration from being attempted) is before the dynasty reaches Fervent intensity. After Fervent, the declaration mechanism is unlocked. Preventing an attempt is still possible through diplomatic pressure on the Faith Authority recognition prerequisite, but it requires active coordination.

**Natural coalition formations:**

Economic Dominance players recognize Faith Divine Right as a direct threat because a theocratic world order disrupts secular trade networks. Military Conquest players oppose it because Divine Right legitimacy would delegitimize conquest as a path to authority. Rival faith dynasties oppose it on purely theological grounds. Territorial Governance players oppose it because voluntary integration becomes much harder when the dominant cultural message is religious obligation rather than prosperity.

The Divine Right declaration is the most universally opposed victory path, which is by design — it requires not just building a faith infrastructure but surviving the world's response to that infrastructure.

**Fatal mistakes for the faith player:**

- Declaring too early. The declaration window is finite and cannot be recovered if conditions are not met by expiration. Declaring before all conditions are in reach is a path-ending error.
- Neglecting military defense. A faith player with Apex intensity and no army is a target. Holy Orders are strong faith-territory defenders but cannot replace a real field army during coalition warfare.
- Allowing a major holy site to fall to a rival during the propagation phase. Desecrated holy sites generate faith intensity collapse in connected territories, and recovering from a desecration requires a full reconstruction cycle.
- Converting too many territories too quickly. Faith propagation that outpaces the support infrastructure creates shallow Active intensity coverage that can be reversed. Depth of faith (Devout and above) in key territories is more valuable than wide shallow coverage.

### Victory Path Interactions with Other Paths

**Faith Divine Right and Military Conquest:**
Minimal synergy, significant conflict. The Holy Order provides military force, but faith resources and military resources compete. The path combinations that semi-work are Moral conquest with faith support (using faith legitimacy to ease occupation) or using military expansion to clear territories before faith propagation follows. Neither is a true dual-path — they are a primary path with a support element from the other.

**Faith Divine Right and Economic Dominance:**
Partial synergy as described in the Economic section. A faith-backed currency standard (Moral conviction economic player with Devout+ faith) has a trust multiplier. But deep faith investment and deep economic investment require the same resource pools and the same time investment. The dual path is theoretically possible but practically requires a very long match (6+ hours) to accumulate the infrastructure of both.

**Faith Divine Right and Territorial Governance:**
Moderate synergy. Faith makes territories culturally cohesive, and cohesive territories are more likely to voluntarily integrate with the dynasty. A Territorial Governance player with strong faith integration in their own territory can use faith as an attraction mechanism — foreign populations with shared faith are more likely to accept integration. This is the most natural cross-path pairing for the Faith player who wants a secondary accumulation mechanism.

**Faith Divine Right and Dynastic Prestige:**
Strong thematic synergy, moderate mechanical synergy. Prestigious bloodlines with divine mandate make natural narrative partners. A dynasty pursuing both paths uses religious authority to reinforce dynastic legitimacy and dynastic marriages to spread faith through bloodline connections. The mechanical benefit is real but not transformative — Prestige infrastructure and Faith infrastructure do not share resource requirements, so they do not crowd each other out the way military and faith do.

### Conviction Constraints

**Moral conviction (positive axis):**
The canonical conviction band for this path. Moral players receive Faith Authority approval bonuses, accelerated propagation rates (+20%), and preferential recognition from other Moral dynasties during the declaration window. The most important Moral bonus is in the resistance calculation: Moral dynasty faith propagation into foreign territories faces lower resistance from foreign populations.

**Neutral conviction:**
Viable with higher infrastructure investment. A Neutral player can reach Apex faith intensity and complete the declaration, but requires approximately 15-20% more faith infrastructure investment to reach the same intensity levels as a Moral player. The path is harder but not blocked.

**Cruel conviction (negative axis):**
Blocked at -40 or below. The Faith Authority recognition step explicitly checks conviction history. A dynasty with Cruel conviction cannot receive the theological seal of approval required for the formal declaration. The path of "cruel theocrat" is specifically excluded — the design intent is that Divine Right authority requires some degree of demonstrated moral legitimacy. A Cruel player who wants to convert to faith victory must first undertake a sustained redemption arc (actions that move conviction from Cruel toward Neutral), which typically requires 20-30 match-minutes of deliberate play.

**Optimal conviction posture:** Deep Moral, with care not to trigger Moral extremism (extreme Moral prevents the Holy Order from using suppression tactics in occupied territories, weakening the defensive capacity during the declaration window).

### House Affinity

**Whitehall:** The house of sacred law and theological governance. Whitehall's founding culture treats faith as jurisprudence — every law has divine backing, every court decision has theological justification. Their buildings include early access to the Sacred Library and a unique Theological Court that accelerates Faith Authority recognition processes. Whitehall is the canonical Faith Divine Right house.

**Oldcrest:** The dynasty of ancient lineage and ancestral memory. Oldcrest's deep roots in the world's oldest traditions give them a natural faith credibility that newer houses must earn over time. Oldcrest has reduced propagation resistance in territories with ancestral faith ties, and their pilgrimage route construction is cheaper than any other house. They are the strongest house for the early and mid-game faith expansion phase.

**Highborne:** The aristocratic faith tradition. Highborne's cultural emphasis on sacred bloodlines (reinforced by their position in the dynastic prestige space) makes them natural allies for Divine Right — their theological framework already includes the concept of divinely-appointed leadership. Highborne has unique access to the Sacred Marriage doctrine, which allows faith spread through dynastic alliances rather than just missions.

### The Loss Condition

Faith Divine Right has two failure modes:

**Declaration failure:** The most decisive failure. The declaration is made, the window expires, and one or more conditions are not met. The dynasty suffers a catastrophic faith authority collapse — intensity drops by two levels in all territories, the Faith Authority cannot approve a second declaration for 30 match-minutes, and founding houses that had tentatively extended recognition withdraw it. This is often fatal to the path because rebuilding from a failed declaration while surviving the post-declaration coalition is nearly impossible.

**Gradual suppression:** The slower failure mode. A coalition of faith opponents progressively destroys faith infrastructure, captures holy sites, and establishes competing faith authorities in territories the faith player needs. This does not produce a single decisive failure moment — it produces a slow ceiling, where faith intensity stops growing and the declaration threshold becomes permanently out of reach. A dynasty in this state is often the last to recognize they have lost, because there is no explicit failure event. They just never cross the threshold.

## VICTORY PATH 4: TERRITORIAL GOVERNANCE (VOLUNTARY INTEGRATION)

### Core Philosophy

Territorial Governance is the victory of a dynasty that understood that the strongest borders are the ones people choose to live inside. Every kingdom in history that grew through conquest discovered the same truth eventually: conquered land is expensive, angry, and temporary. The dynasty that wins through voluntary integration built something different — a civilization so organized, so prosperous, and so just that neighboring populations requested to join it. Not submission, not surrender, not the silence of occupation. The knock on the door. The petition to be included. Territorial Governance is the slowest and, in some ways, the most demanding victory path, because it requires the player to hold themselves to a higher standard than any external enemy could impose. There are no shortcuts. You cannot pressure people into choosing you. You build the civilization they want to live in, and then you wait for them to notice. And then you do it again, and again, until the map turns your color not because you painted it, but because it wanted to be.

### Prerequisites

Before Territorial Governance becomes a viable primary path:

**Infrastructure prerequisites:**
- Administrative Hall (capital-tier building for regional governance)
- Civic Network (infrastructure in each territory providing public goods — roads, water, granaries)
- Integration Bureau (specialized building managing voluntary integration petitions from foreign populations)
- Cultural Broadcast infrastructure (libraries, schools, performance halls — spreading the dynasty's cultural output into adjacent territories)
- A Prosperity Index of 75 or above in home territories (the index measures the composite of food security, safety, taxation fairness, and public goods access)

**Resource prerequisites:**
- Sustained food surplus at 20% above population consumption in all home territories
- Treasury positive in all home territories simultaneously
- Cultural output sufficient to generate Attraction Aura in at least two adjacent foreign territories

**Conviction prerequisites:**
- Moral conviction required. Not preferred — required. Voluntary integration is mechanically blocked at Neutral-Cruel boundary (-20 or below on the axis). Populations do not petition to join a dynasty they perceive as neutral or indifferent to their welfare. They petition to join dynasties that are visibly, demonstrably, and consistently Moral. This is the tightest conviction constraint in any victory path.
- Moral conviction must be sustained above +40 continuously from the time the first integration petition is accepted. A single major Cruel action during this period resets the Attraction Aura in all adjacent territories, losing months of integration goodwill.

**Diplomatic prerequisites:**
- No wars declared for at least 20 continuous match-minutes before the first integration petition can be accepted
- At least 3 dynasties at Cordial or Allied state (the reputation of the integrating dynasty is partially derived from how well-regarded they are by their peers)
- No active territorial disputes with the population's current governing dynasty (integration petitions from disputed territories require the current governor to either release the territory or be at war with the integrating dynasty)

**Faith prerequisites:**
- No minimum, but shared faith between the integrating dynasty and the petitioning population significantly accelerates integration success rates. A foreign population whose faith matches the dynasty's faith integrates at 1.5x the base rate. Faith mismatches do not block integration, but they slow it.

### The Victory Path — Stage by Stage

**Early Game (0 to 30 minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 0 to 60 minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

The territorial governance player's early game is domestic perfection. Every resource, every building choice, every policy decision is oriented around maximizing the Prosperity Index in home territories. This is the foundation everything else rests on: a prosperous home territory generates Attraction Aura, which is the passive signal that foreign populations pick up as the trigger for integration petitions.

The player is building:
- Civic infrastructure in every home district
- Cultural output buildings (libraries, academies, performance halls)
- Governance buildings (justice courts, administrative halls, public councils)
- Defensive military sufficient to protect the prosperity investment (Governance players need credible defense — an undefended prosperous territory is the most attractive target on the map)

The first meaningful threshold is reaching Prosperity Index 75 in the capital territory and three adjacent territories simultaneously. At this threshold, Attraction Aura becomes strong enough to register in adjacent foreign territories as a visible pull signal. Minor tribes and border settlements begin generating low-level integration interest.

The early game mistake to avoid is military neglect combined with visible prosperity. A dynasty with a 90 Prosperity Index and no army is advertising itself as a conquest target. The prosperity that makes integration work also makes the territory desirable to conquest players who do not care about the voluntary mechanism.

**Mid Game (30 to 90 minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 60 to 180 minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

Mid-game is the cascade phase. The first integrations attract attention from neighboring populations, which generates additional integration interest. The player is now managing two processes simultaneously: maintaining the prosperity infrastructure that drives integration and processing the administrative requirements of newly integrated territories.

The player is building:
- Integration administration infrastructure for each newly integrated territory
- Cultural integration programs (converting integrated population loyalty from their original dynasty to the integrating dynasty)
- Extended Civic Networks reaching into newly integrated zones
- Diplomatic infrastructure to manage the original governing dynasty's response to the integration (they almost never let territory integrate willingly without protest)

The mid-game thresholds to cross:
- 25% of map territory integrated through voluntary petition (the "recognized model" mark — other dynasties start sending diplomatic missions to understand how the governance dynasty's territory is so stable)
- Integration retention rate above 80% (integrated territories that later rebel or petition to leave count as negative integration events — a dynasty with 30% territory but 20% retention rate is not on a winning path)
- At least one founding house territory with Active integration interest (when major houses start expressing integration interest, the path enters its most politically volatile phase)

The opposition in mid-game is primarily diplomatic and economic. Conquest players who see territories voluntarily integrating away from them will use military force to block integration (occupying the petitioning territory before the integration completes, which resets the petition). Economic players may attempt to outbid the governance dynasty's prosperity by flooding adjacent territories with trade subsidies to suppress the Attraction Aura differential.

**Late Game (90+ minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 180+ minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

Late game for Territorial Governance is the final integration wave — pushing from 50-60% territory to the victory threshold while defending the existing integrated territory from conquest players who now recognize the threat.

The player is doing:
- Executing the largest and most complex integration petitions (major founding house territory — these are high-value, high-resistance, and very slow)
- Managing suppression of integration opponents (conquest players who are systematically raiding integrated territories to destabilize them)
- Using the Integration Bureau's late-game diplomacy tools (formal Accession Treaties with willing dynasties — the most efficient late-game integration mechanism, converting an entire dynasty's territory at once through negotiated terms)
- Defending integration against The Great Reckoning if another dynasty has crossed 70% through conquest (the Reckoning coalition creates military pressure that the governance player must survive)

### The Victory Threshold

All four of the following conditions must be met simultaneously and sustained for 20 continuous match-minutes (scaled: 20 min for 2-3 hour match, 35 min for 4-6 hour match, 50 min for 8-10+ hour match):

1. **Voluntary integration coverage:** 65% of total map territory integrated through voluntary petition or formal Accession Treaty (conquered territory does not count — it must be petition-based or treaty-based)
2. **Integration retention rate:** 85% or above across all integrated territory (no more than 15% of integrated territory in active rebellion, integration reversal, or petition-to-leave status)
3. **Prosperity Index:** 70 or above across 75% of all integrated territories (the prosperity must be real and sustained — not just the home core)
4. **Conviction maintenance:** Moral conviction at +40 or above continuously throughout the sustained window

The victory is declared by the player through a formal "Proclamation of Union" in the Administrative Hall. The game validates conditions and, if met, opens a 3-minute challenge window during which any dynasty may attempt to trigger a military intervention. If no successful intervention occurs, the win is confirmed. A successful intervention requires the challenger to seize and hold at least 10% of the integrating dynasty's territory within the 3-minute window.

### Visibility to Other Players

**Publicly visible at all times:**
- Prosperity Index of each territory (visible to any dynasty with trade posts or adjacent territory)
- Which territories have submitted integration petitions (visible as a map indicator to all players)
- The Attraction Aura intensity around a governance dynasty's territories (visible as a soft glow indicator on the map — obvious to any player who is watching)

**Visible at specific thresholds:**
- At 40% voluntary integration: global notification "The [Dynasty] model of governance has become a beacon — populations across the known world look toward their example"
- At 55%: global notification "The [Dynasty] has achieved what no dynasty has before — a union built on choice rather than conquest"

**Hidden until crossed:**
- The exact Prosperity Index numbers in home territories (visible directionally but not precisely without trade post access)
- Integration petition processing timelines (how long until a petition converts to formal integration)
- Which specific founding house territories have active integration interest (high-value intelligence — hidden without spy network access to the Integration Bureau)

**Unambiguous path tell:** A governance player is the most visible path player on the map from mid-game onward. Territories with Prosperity Index above 75 have a visible map indicator. Integration petitions are publicly visible events. The path is essentially impossible to conceal past the point where three or more territories have been integrated. The only question is timing: the threat may not be recognized as decisive until very late.

### Counter-Play

**Optimal counter-strategy:**

Territorial Governance has a structural vulnerability: the prosperity requirement is expensive to maintain across a large integrated territory, and it requires sustained Moral conviction. The optimal counter-strategy targets both.

Economic raiding is the highest-leverage counter. Raiding the resource nodes in integrated territories — granaries, farms, trade routes — drops the Prosperity Index faster than the governance player can rebuild. A governance player who is simultaneously managing integration petitions and rebuilding raided infrastructure will fall below the Prosperity Index threshold, triggering integration reversals in the most recently integrated (and therefore most fragile) territories.

Cultural counter-propagation is the second lever. Flooding adjacent territories with cultural output from a competing dynasty — a rival library network, a competing faith presence, prestige events that redirect cultural attention — suppresses the Attraction Aura differential that drives integration petitions. If two dynasties have similar Prosperity Indexes in territories adjacent to a contested population, the integration petition goes nowhere because neither dynasty has a clear advantage.

Military occupation of petitioning territories (before the petition completes) is the most direct counter. This is expensive diplomatically and converts the governing dynasty from Hostile to War with the governance player, but it is mechanically clean: an occupied territory cannot petition for integration. Conquest players who are losing territories to voluntary integration may prefer to destroy the territory rather than lose it.

**Point of no return:**

At 55% voluntary integration with retention above 85%, the path is nearly unstoppable. The network effect of well-governed integrated territory generates more integration interest from adjacent populations than a fragmented coalition can disrupt through raiding. The practical point of no return is at 50% integration — below this, raiding and cultural counter-propagation can meaningfully slow the path. Above it, the territory mass creates its own momentum.

The last genuine window to stop the path is between 30% and 45% territory. During this phase, the governance player is still building administrative infrastructure for newly integrated territories, and their Prosperity Index is most vulnerable to disruption because they are spreading resources across a larger area than their infrastructure was originally designed for.

**Natural coalition formations:**

Military Conquest players oppose Territorial Governance directly and immediately because they are competing for the same territory through opposite mechanisms. Economic Dominance players have moderate opposition motivation (voluntary integration creates politically stable territories that are less likely to adopt foreign currency standards because they are prospering under their own governance). Faith players have mixed motivation — if the governance dynasty's faith aligns with theirs, the governance victory actually spreads their faith. If not, they have a competitive reason to oppose it.

The governance path generates the least coordinated opposition of any victory path because it threatens each opponent's path differently and in ways that are not easily communicated as a shared threat. The "benevolent hegemon" narrative is genuinely hard to build a coalition against.

**Fatal mistakes for the governance player:**

- Letting Conviction drift even briefly. A single -20 conviction event during the sustained victory window resets the whole timer. Governance players must be extraordinarily disciplined about avoiding any action that generates Cruel conviction penalties.
- Neglecting military defense. Prosperity without security is an invitation. The most common governance player defeat is being conquered by a mid-game military player who correctly identified the prospered territory as the most valuable target on the map.
- Accepting too many integration petitions at once. Each new integration requires administrative bandwidth. A governance player who accepts 8 petitions simultaneously will have insufficient administrative capacity to maintain Prosperity Index across all of them, triggering retention failures in the newest territories.
- Ignoring the Integration Bureau's resistance signals. Every petition has a resistance probability. High-resistance petitions (from territories with strong cultural identity or recent conflict with the governance dynasty) should be delayed until resistance has been lowered through cultural investment. Forcing a high-resistance integration produces a fragile integration that is one raid away from reversal.

### Victory Path Interactions with Other Paths

**Territorial Governance and Military Conquest:**
Nearly incompatible. The conviction requirement for Territorial Governance (+40 Moral) and the conviction flexibility required for Military Conquest (including Cruel options) are structurally opposed. A player who is conquering militarily cannot simultaneously maintain the Moral conviction required for voluntary integration. The only viable overlap is the "liberator" narrative: conquering a territory and then rapidly transitioning to Moral governance to generate integration loyalty. This is extremely difficult to execute because conquest conviction damage is significant and not quickly recovered.

**Territorial Governance and Economic Dominance:**
Strong synergy as described in the Economic section. The shared behavioral profile — Moral conviction, diplomatic restraint, prosperity investment — means these paths reinforce each other at the infrastructure level. A governance player can credibly add currency standard adoption as a secondary accumulation strategy. The limit is that full investment in both paths simultaneously requires a very long match to accumulate infrastructure for both.

**Territorial Governance and Faith Divine Right:**
Moderate synergy. Shared faith between governance dynasty and petitioning populations is a significant multiplier on integration rates. A governance player with Devout faith integration in their territories can target faith-compatible populations as priority integration targets, accelerating the path through the mid-game phase. The tension is that Faith investment competes with Civic investment for the same resource pools.

**Territorial Governance and Dynastic Prestige:**
Weak mechanical synergy, strong thematic synergy. Prestigious dynasties attract integration interest because prestige is visible and aspirational — populations want to join celebrated dynasties. But Prestige infrastructure investment and Civic infrastructure investment compete directly, and a player who tries to do both will find both paths underfunded.

### Conviction Constraints

**Moral conviction (positive axis):**
Required for the path. The specific thresholds are: Moral +40 for basic integration petition eligibility, Moral +60 for accelerated Attraction Aura range, Moral +80 for the Accession Treaty mechanism (the most efficient late-game integration tool). Moral extremism (above +90) creates its own complications: extreme Moral dynasties sometimes refuse integration petitions from populations they consider morally inferior, which is a mechanical blocker the player must manage.

**Neutral conviction (0 to +20):**
Partially available. A Neutral player can receive integration petitions but at 50% of the normal rate. The Attraction Aura is weaker, and the Accession Treaty mechanism is locked. Neutral players can technically reach the victory threshold but it takes substantially longer than Moral players.

**Cruel conviction (below -20):**
Blocked. Populations do not petition to join dynasties they fear. At -20 or below, no new integration petitions are generated. Existing integrated territories begin generating retention failures as populations process the conviction signal from the governing dynasty.

**Optimal conviction posture:** High Moral (+60 to +80), carefully managed to avoid extremism. The governance player is the most conviction-constrained player in the game — they have the least room for error in this dimension and the most severe consequences for transgression.

### House Affinity

**Hartvale:** The agricultural prosperity dynasty. Hartvale's food production superiority translates directly into Prosperity Index leadership — food security is one of the highest-weighted components of the index, and Hartvale reaches food surplus thresholds faster than any other house. Their unique buildings include the Public Granary (high-value civic infrastructure) and the Harvest Festival (a periodic event that generates massive Attraction Aura surges). Hartvale is the canonical Territorial Governance house.

**Trueborn:** The neutral arbiters. Trueborn's inherent diplomatic credibility means that integration petitions directed toward a Trueborn governance player carry a legitimacy modifier — populations trust the Trueborn to govern fairly because the Trueborn's cultural identity is built around fair dealing. Trueborn receive a 20% bonus to integration petition conversion rates.

**Highborne:** The cultural nobility. Highborne's cultural output infrastructure — already substantial given their dynastic prestige orientation — is directly reusable as Territorial Governance Attraction Aura infrastructure. A Highborne player on the governance path uses their cultural production for double duty: generating prestige events that build secondary Prestige accumulation while simultaneously broadcasting governance appeal to adjacent populations.

### The Loss Condition

Territorial Governance fails through prosperity collapse and integration reversal:

**Retention cascade:** If more than 20% of integrated territories simultaneously drop below the Prosperity Index threshold (triggered by sustained raiding or resource shock), an integration reversal cascade begins. Integrated populations begin submitting "petition to leave" requests. Once three or more territories are in active reversal, the cascade effect pulls additional territories into reversal as the narrative of governance failure spreads. Recovering from a full reversal cascade requires rebuilding Prosperity Index in every affected territory simultaneously — a near-impossible task under continued military pressure.

**Conviction failure:** A single major Cruel action during the sustained victory window resets the 20-minute timer entirely. A series of smaller Cruel actions that push conviction below +40 resets all integration petition eligibility. If conviction drops below -20, integrated territories begin petitioning to leave. Conviction failure is the most sudden and irreversible failure mode on this path.

**Military overwhelm:** A dedicated conquest player with sufficient army strength can simply march through governance territories faster than they can integrate. If the governance player is conquered before reaching the victory threshold, the path ends — there is no diplomatic resolution to a successful military invasion.

## VICTORY PATH 5: DYNASTIC PRESTIGE

### Core Philosophy

Dynastic Prestige is the victory of a dynasty that turned its bloodline into a legend. Every other victory path is about building something in the world — a military empire, an economic network, a faith architecture, a governance model. Dynastic Prestige is about building something in history. The dynasty that wins through prestige has been present at every great moment, has members in every major court, has married into or outmaneuvered every rival bloodline, and has accumulated a weight of honor, reputation, and cultural significance that no army can conquer and no currency can purchase. They did not win because they were the strongest, the richest, or the most pious. They won because they became, over the course of generations within the match, the dynasty that everyone else measured themselves against. The Dynastic Prestige victory is a victory of meaning — this bloodline mattered in ways that will outlast every empire, economy, and crusade on the board.

### Prerequisites

Before Dynastic Prestige becomes a viable primary path:

**Infrastructure prerequisites:**
- Dynasty Hall (capital-tier building for bloodline management and prestige events)
- Court of Honors (records and accumulates prestige points from qualifying events)
- Tournament Grounds (generates periodic prestige events)
- Ambassador Network (diplomatic infrastructure for managing inter-dynasty bloodline relationships)
- Chronicle Hall (architectural structure that converts accumulated prestige into permanent legacy points — the slow accumulation mechanism)

**Resource prerequisites:**
- Sufficient treasury reserves to fund prestige events (grand tournaments, royal marriages, ambassadorial missions — all expensive)
- Cultural production above 50 in capital territory (prestige requires cultural context — a dynasty that produces nothing cultural cannot accumulate meaningful prestige)
- At minimum Neutral territory stability (prestige reputation is undermined by visible internal dysfunction)

**Conviction prerequisites:**
- All conviction bands allow Dynastic Prestige, but each band accesses different prestige mechanisms. Moral players accumulate prestige through justice, charitable acts, and honorable conduct. Neutral players accumulate through competence, diplomacy, and strategic excellence. Cruel players accumulate through fear, dominance, and the prestige of strength (a fundamentally different flavor but mechanically viable).

**Diplomatic prerequisites:**
- At least two dynastic marriage agreements with founding house bloodlines (these are the primary prestige accumulation events in the early-to-mid game)
- At least three dynasties at Cordial or above state (prestige requires audience — an isolated dynasty accumulates prestige slowly)

**Military prerequisites (unique to this path):**
- At least one bloodline member at Veteran military rank or above (bloodline members on the battlefield generate prestige through personal valor — at least one must achieve Veteran rank before the path's primary accumulation mechanism is fully active)
- The dynasty's heir or a named bloodline member must have participated in at least one named battle (not just a skirmish — a battle significant enough to receive a battle name in the Chronicle)

### The Victory Path — Stage by Stage

**Early Game (0 to 30 minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 0 to 60 minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

The prestige player's early game is relationship building and bloodline positioning. Every marriage negotiated, every tournament attended, every diplomatic gesture — all of it is accumulating the social capital that will be converted into prestige points over the rest of the match.

The player is building:
- Dynasty Hall and Court of Honors as early capital investments
- Marriage alliances with as many founding houses as possible (each marriage provides ongoing prestige point generation as long as the alliance is intact)
- Ambassador presence in every major court
- Tournament Grounds for hosting events (hosting generates more prestige than attending)
- Positioning bloodline members in key military commands (personal valor in battle is a prestige multiplier)

The first meaningful threshold is 1,000 accumulated prestige points. Below this threshold, the dynasty is a participant in the prestige economy. Above it, they are a recognized prestige power, and founding houses begin soliciting marriages rather than being solicited.

The early mistake is avoiding military engagement entirely. Prestige players who do not participate in any conflict accumulate prestige slowly and miss the high-value valor opportunities that are only available on the battlefield.

**Mid Game (30 to 90 minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 60 to 180 minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

Mid-game is the prestige accumulation race. Multiple dynasties will be pursuing prestige events simultaneously. The prestige player must generate prestige faster than their competitors while also defending their bloodline members (who are high-value assassination targets) and managing the political fallout from prestige disputes.

The player is building:
- Prestige event calendar management (hosting grand tournaments, coronation ceremonies, diplomatic conferences)
- Bloodline member management (placing them in high-yield prestige contexts, protecting them from assassination, managing succession)
- Dispute War preparation (when a rival prestige player approaches the same threshold, a Prestige Dispute War is probable — the player must be ready)
- Chronicle Hall investments (every 10,000 prestige points converted through the Chronicle Hall becomes permanent Legacy — even if the dynasty loses a prestige event, the Legacy is permanently held)

The mid-game thresholds to cross:
- 5,000 prestige points accumulated (the point at which the dynasty's name is known in every court on the map)
- At least two bloodline members at Legendary military rank or above (Legendary rank generates 3x valor prestige from battlefield participation)
- Alliance network covering at least four founding houses (active marriage alliances — each provides ongoing prestige flow)
- 1,000 permanent Legacy points in the Chronicle (the safety floor — even in disaster, this Legacy is preserved)

**Late Game (90+ minutes in a 2-3 hour match / 180+ minutes in a 4-6 hour match)**

Late game is the prestige harvest. The dynasty is accumulating prestige rapidly from a mature alliance network, regular event hosting, and battlefield valor from positioned bloodline members. The primary threats are Prestige Dispute Wars from rival prestige players, assassination of bloodline members, and the emergence of a military conquest player who may see eliminating the prestige player's bloodline members as a clean path to destabilizing the path.

The decisive late-game action is the Grand Legacy Declaration — a formal announcement that the dynasty is claiming the prestige victory, converting all remaining accumulated prestige to Legacy, and challenging any rival to contest it through a Dispute War. The Dispute War is a structured military engagement with specific victory conditions rather than an open-ended conflict.

### The Victory Threshold

All five of the following conditions must be met simultaneously:

1. **Permanent Legacy:** 10,000 accumulated Legacy points in the Chronicle Hall (these are permanent and cannot be reduced by any in-game event — they represent the unassailable historical record)
2. **Prestige pool:** 15,000 current prestige points (the living reputation — can fluctuate, must be above threshold at declaration)
3. **Alliance breadth:** Active marriage alliances or formal friendship pacts with at least 5 of the 9 founding houses
4. **Bloodline distinction:** At least one bloodline member at Legendary rank OR at least two at Veteran rank present and alive at declaration
5. **Cultural production:** Dynasty cultural output at the top quartile of all active dynasties (your culture must be the world's most recognized to claim prestige victory)

The victory is declared by the player through the Grand Legacy Declaration in the Dynasty Hall. This opens a 10-match-minute Dispute War window during which any dynasty that has accumulated 8,000 or more prestige may challenge through direct military combat — specifically, the challenger must capture and hold the declaring dynasty's Dynasty Hall for 3 minutes, or kill or capture two Legendary bloodline members. If no successful challenge occurs, the win is confirmed.

### Visibility to Other Players

**Publicly visible at all times:**
- Dynasty prestige rankings (visible in the diplomatic interface — all players can see relative prestige standing)
- Alliance map (marriage alliances are visible to all dynasties at Cordial or above state)
- Which bloodline members have achieved Veteran or Legendary rank (battlefield accomplishments are public record)

**Visible at specific thresholds:**
- At 3,000 prestige: global notification identifying the dynasty as a prestigious power
- At 7,000 prestige: global notification "The [Dynasty] bloodline is becoming legend — their name echoes through every court in the known world"
- Grand Legacy Declaration: global event, full milestone checklist publicly displayed

**Hidden until crossed:**
- Exact Legacy accumulation rate (opponents see the prestige pool but not the Chronicle Hall conversion rate without espionage)
- Which specific alliances are the highest-value prestige generators (opponents can see the alliances but not the prestige flow rates)
- Bloodline member locations when not in field army formation (bloodline members can be moved covertly)

**Unambiguous path tell:** A dynasty that attends every diplomatic event, maintains more marriage alliances than any other player, and consistently positions bloodline members in high-visibility military commands is clearly on this path by mid-game. The prestige leaderboard (visible in diplomatic interface) is the clearest tell — a dynasty that is consistently top-ranked on prestige is the prestige path player.

### Counter-Play

**Optimal counter-strategy:**

The Dynastic Prestige path has two structural vulnerabilities: bloodline members and alliance integrity.

Bloodline member assassination is the highest-leverage counter. Prestige players depend on living, ranked bloodline members for both ongoing prestige generation and the final victory threshold (requiring Legendary/Veteran members at declaration). Assassinating a Legendary bloodline member causes a prestige collapse event that typically costs 2,000-3,000 prestige points and removes the valor generation from that member permanently. Three successful assassinations in mid-game can set a prestige player back by 30 match-minutes.

Alliance disruption is the second lever. Each of the prestige player's marriage alliances provides ongoing prestige flow. Turning an alliance partner against the prestige player — through fabricated insults, military threats, economic pressure, or counter-marriage offers — breaks the alliance and stops the prestige flow. A prestige player who loses three alliances in rapid succession will find their accumulation rate cut by 40-50%.

During the Grand Legacy Declaration window, the Dispute War challenge is the only remaining counter. Challengers need to either kill/capture two Legendary members or seize the Dynasty Hall. Well-positioned bodyguard infrastructure protects bloodline members, and the Dynasty Hall can be fortified. The challenger needs military concentration at a specific location under time pressure — a task that requires preparation and is very difficult to improvise in the 10-minute window.

**Point of no return:**

At 10,000 permanent Legacy points in the Chronicle Hall, the dynasty's historical record is established regardless of match outcome. Below the full victory threshold, this does not win the game — but it means the prestige player's progress is partially secured even in defeat. The path has no single point of no return because Legacy accumulation is permanent, but practically the path becomes nearly unstoppable at 12,000 current prestige with active alliances covering 5+ houses — the final threshold is within reach and the Dispute War counter is a difficult task.

**Natural coalition formations:**

There is no natural coalition against Dynastic Prestige — it is the path that other players most consistently underestimate and least urgently oppose. Military players view prestige players as non-threatening until the Grand Legacy Declaration. Economic players see prestige players as legitimate diplomatic partners. Faith players often align with prestige players because shared faith and dynastic honor are thematically compatible. The counter-prestige dynamic emerges most sharply when two players are both high on the prestige leaderboard and approaching the threshold simultaneously — at that point a bilateral dispute war is nearly inevitable.

**Fatal mistakes for the prestige player:**

- Neglecting bloodline member protection. A prestige player who sends their Legendary bloodline members into high-risk combat without bodyguard units is accepting unnecessary assassination risk.
- Allowing a single dynastic succession crisis to spiral. If the dynasty heir dies and succession is disputed, the prestige accumulation stops entirely during the succession resolution period. Succession planning is a prestige player's most important long-term management task.
- Pursuing prestige events that require military deployment without adequate home defense. Prestige players often have weaker home military than conquest players. A prestige player attending a distant tournament while their home territories are being raided is losing prestige on both ends.
- Making the Grand Legacy Declaration too early. The Dispute War window is a real threat. Declaring before the Dynasty Hall is fortified and bloodline members are secured invites successful challenge.

### Victory Path Interactions with Other Paths

**Dynastic Prestige and Military Conquest:**
The relationship described above — early synergy through battlefield valor, late conflict through bloodline security. A prestige player who also controls significant territory has a military capacity advantage in Dispute Wars. A conquest player who is also accumulating prestige through battlefield victories is on a viable hybrid path until the late game, when conquest's stability requirements and prestige's diplomatic requirements begin conflicting.

**Dynastic Prestige and Economic Dominance:**
Strong synergy throughout the match. Economic resources fund prestige events directly. A wealthy dynasty can outbid rivals for the most prestigious marriage alliances, host the grandest tournaments, and fund the Chronicle Hall investments that convert prestige to permanent Legacy. The dual path is viable and one of the strongest mid-game combinations in the game.

**Dynastic Prestige and Faith Divine Right:**
Strong thematic synergy, moderate mechanical synergy, as described in the Faith section. The shared moral authority of faith and dynasty creates reinforcing legitimacy narratives. Mechanically, a prestige player with Devout faith in their territories receives the Sacred Marriage doctrine (Highborne unique, but accessible to others through doctrine research), which allows faith spread through dynastic alliances — converting alliances into prestige events that simultaneously generate prestige and advance the faith coverage threshold.

**Dynastic Prestige and Territorial Governance:**
Weak mechanical synergy. Prestige players and governance players compete for the same Moral conviction bands without sharing much infrastructure. The governance player needs their resources in civic infrastructure. The prestige player needs theirs in court and cultural infrastructure. They do not conflict directly but they do not meaningfully reinforce each other.

### Conviction Constraints

**Moral conviction (positive axis):**
Moral prestige players access the highest-value prestige events: honorable judicial rulings (major prestige events), diplomatic arbitration successes (when the dynasty resolves conflicts between other dynasties, generating prestige from both parties), and the Patron of Arts designation (an ongoing prestige generation modifier available only to Moral players above +60). Moral prestige is the most consistent and predictable accumulation path.

**Neutral conviction:**
Competence prestige. Neutral players accumulate prestige through strategic excellence — brilliant battlefield maneuvers, well-structured treaties, long-game diplomatic positioning. Neutral prestige is slightly slower than Moral but more resilient to conviction shocks. Neutral players also have access to the Mercenary Valor mechanism (hiring prestigious mercenary commanders who generate valor prestige without requiring bloodline member battlefield risk).

**Cruel conviction (negative axis):**
Fear prestige. Cruel players cannot access marriage alliance and honor event prestige mechanisms in the same way, but they have unique access to Dominance Events — forced tribute ceremonies, public humiliations of rival dynasties, the capture and display of enemy bloodline members. Cruel prestige accumulation is the most volatile: high peaks when dominance events succeed, sharp collapses when dominance is challenged. Cruel prestige players are the most likely to trigger Dispute Wars both offensively (as challengers) and defensively (as targeted by coalitions). The path is viable but is the hardest version of the prestige path to sustain.

**Optimal conviction posture:** Moral (+60 to +80) for the most consistent accumulation. Neutral for a stable but slower path. Cruel for high-risk, high-reward accumulation that suits aggressive players.

### House Affinity

**Highborne:** The aristocratic lineage house. Highborne's entire cultural identity is dynastic prestige — their founding philosophy treats bloodline as the organizing principle of civilization. Their unique Sacred Marriage doctrine, Court of Legacy building, and starting prestige pool advantage make them the canonical Dynastic Prestige house. Highborne players start with 200 prestige points, the highest starting prestige of any house.

**Trueborn:** The Trueborn's role as world arbiters and neutral knowledge keepers gives them unmatched access to diplomatic prestige events — arbitration, treaty witnessing, historical chronicle keeping. A Trueborn prestige player accumulates prestige from every major diplomatic event on the map, whether or not they are directly involved, because the Trueborn are invited to witness and record everything. This makes Trueborn an extremely strong mid-to-late game prestige player.

**Whitehall:** The sacred law house. Whitehall's legal and theological authority generates prestige from judicial events — major rulings, theological debates, dynastic legitimacy disputes. A Whitehall prestige player is accumulating prestige every time any dynasty needs a legitimate adjudicator, which happens constantly in a multi-dynasty game.

### The Loss Condition

Dynastic Prestige fails through bloodline extinction and accumulated collapse:

**Bloodline fragility:** If all named bloodline members (Veteran rank or above) are killed or captured within a 15 match-minute window, a Dynastic Crisis event triggers. The dynasty loses 50% of current prestige points, all alliance prestige flows pause for 10 match-minutes, and the Grand Legacy Declaration mechanism is locked for 20 match-minutes. A dynasty that loses its bloodline leadership is not dead — but it is severely set back.

**Alliance collapse:** If more than half of the dynasty's active marriage alliances are broken within a 10 match-minute window (through death, diplomatic rupture, or conquest of the alliance partner), the prestige leaderboard ranking drops sharply and rival prestige players may overtake. Recovery requires 20-30 match-minutes of rebuilding, during which a rival prestige player on a stronger trajectory wins the path.

**Losing the Dispute War:** If a Grand Legacy Declaration triggers a Dispute War and the defending dynasty loses (Dynasty Hall captured for 3 minutes or two Legendary members killed/captured), the declaration fails. The dynasty loses 3,000 prestige points, the Dynasty Hall is compromised for 15 match-minutes, and a 30-match-minute lockout prevents a second declaration attempt. Losing a Dispute War with no second attempt window left (match time running short) is decisive defeat on this path.

## MULTI-PATH DYNAMICS

### Path Collisions — When Two Players Pursue the Same Victory Path

**Military Conquest Collision:**
When two conquest players are both seriously building toward the territory threshold simultaneously, a mid-game collision is inevitable. They will be fighting over the same territories, and the question becomes which conquest player can out-pace the other's garrison and stability maintenance. The collision mechanic is the Legitimacy War: when both players hold 30%+ territory simultaneously, a global event fires — the "Two Crowns" notification — announcing that two dynasties are contending for the same right of conquest. This triggers a forced diplomatic state shift: the two conquest players must formally declare War against each other if they are not already at War. The "Two Crowns" state cannot coexist — the game actively pushes toward resolution. The collision resolution is military: whichever conquest player destroys the other's capital territory first forces the loser into a partial territory release. Non-conquest players benefit from the collision because both conquest players are consuming resources fighting each other.

**Economic Dominance Collision:**
When two dynasties both reach 40% currency adoption simultaneously, a Currency War event fires. Each dynasty's currency begins competing in every active trade network. The competing currency standard is a zero-sum mechanic — market share gained by one dynasty is lost by the other. The collision mechanic is the Trade Network Bid: every dynasty currently using both currencies is prompted to formally choose one standard, based on a combination of adoption rate, reserve credibility (gold backing), and relationship with each dynasty. The currency war creates two distinct coalitions of trade partners, and the decisive mechanic is Trueborn City alignment: whichever currency the Trueborn City officially adopts as its trading standard gains a 20% adoption rate advantage and will typically secure victory in the currency war within 15-20 match-minutes. The Trueborn City alignment choice is based on a scored evaluation of both currencies' backing and the Trueborn's diplomatic relationship with each dynasty.

**Faith Divine Right Collision:**
When two dynasties both pursue formal Divine Right declaration, the collision is doctrinal. Two dynasties cannot simultaneously hold Divine Right — the theological architecture of the game treats it as singular. The collision mechanic is the Great Schism: when two dynasties both reach Devout intensity (Level 3) in their home territories and both have filed declaration intentions, a schism event fires. Both faith paths become competing theological movements, and the four ancient faiths must each formally align with one or the other (or neither). The Schism resolution requires each dynasty to accumulate 4 of the 9 founding house recognitions from separate pools — the same house cannot recognize both. The race for recognition becomes the decisive mechanic. If one dynasty secures 4 recognitions before the other secures 3, the slower dynasty's declaration is automatically delayed by 20 match-minutes. The first dynasty to complete a successful declaration window wins the path; the second cannot win through the same path and must switch.

**Territorial Governance Collision:**
When two dynasties both pursue voluntary integration and both reach 35% integrated territory, a Governance Competition event fires. The collision mechanic is the Migration Preference: every territory not yet integrated by either dynasty generates a population sentiment score for each dynasty, updated in real time. Territories adjacent to both integration empires see dual Attraction Aura overlap, and the population formally evaluates which dynasty offers better governance by a direct comparison of Prosperity Indexes, conviction bands, faith compatibility, and cultural output. The collision is resolved through the quality of governance rather than military force — the dynasty with genuinely higher Prosperity metrics wins each contested territory's petition. The only military element is that conquest players who see two governance dynasties competing can exploit the distraction to raid both simultaneously.

**Dynastic Prestige Collision:**
When two dynasties both reach 7,000 prestige points, a dynastic rivalry event fires. The collision mechanic is the Prestige War: a structured diplomatic crisis in which the two dynasties must compete for the same high-value prestige events — major tournaments, diplomatic conferences, royal marriages of neutral parties. For every prestige event that occurs during a Prestige War, the two rival dynasties bid against each other for participation rights. The winner of the bid gains the event's prestige; the loser gains nothing. The Prestige War ends either through a Grand Legacy Declaration (which triggers the Dispute War) or when one dynasty drops below 5,000 prestige (concedes the competition). Prestige War creates a visible, player-driven climax to the path race that non-prestige players can observe and exploit.

### Path Recognition — Observable Tells Per Victory Path

The following behaviors, when observed together, constitute a reliable identification of which victory path an opponent is pursuing. Each path has three primary tells.

**Military Conquest — Three Tells:**
1. Army size consistently exceeds the dynasty's territorial needs for defense. A dynasty with 20% of the map but armies sized to attack a dynasty with 50% is building for conquest, not defense.
2. Infrastructure investment pattern is exclusively military and administrative with no cultural or faith buildings visible in recent construction activity.
3. Diplomatic state with multiple neighbors at Hostile or War simultaneously, combined with visible territory expansion in adjacent zones over consecutive map checks.

**Economic Dominance — Three Tells:**
1. An unusually high number of trade posts in foreign territories. A dynasty with trade posts in five or more foreign territories before mid-game is building a trade network, not territorial defense.
2. No wars declared and no military buildups, combined with visible active engagement at every diplomatic conference and treaty negotiation.
3. A dynasty that offers favorable trade terms to everyone and never declines a trade agreement is building the trust network that the currency standard requires.

**Faith Divine Right — Three Tells:**
1. Temple and cathedral construction density in home territories that exceeds what is necessary for conviction maintenance. A dynasty building a cathedral in every territory quadrant is building toward Devout/Fervent intensity deliberately.
2. Faith mission activity visible in foreign territories adjacent to the dynasty's borders. Faith outposts in non-home territory are only useful for propagation — there is no other reason to build them.
3. A dynasty that files theological disputes against rival faith authorities and engages the four ancient faiths' doctrine systems directly is building toward declaration prerequisites.

**Territorial Governance — Three Tells:**
1. Civic infrastructure density in home territories far exceeding what stability requires. A dynasty building granaries, public courts, and libraries in every territory is building a Prosperity Index, not just stability maintenance.
2. Zero wars declared combined with extremely high diplomatic generosity — accepting unfavorable trade terms, offering asylum to diplomatic refugees, publicly mediating disputes between other dynasties.
3. Integration petition indicators appearing on their border territories in the map interface. Integration petitions are visible public events — a dynasty receiving three or more simultaneously is building toward governance victory.

**Dynastic Prestige — Three Tells:**
1. Alliance map showing more marriage agreements than any other dynasty on the board, combined with ambassador presence in every major court.
2. Bloodline members consistently appearing in high-profile military contexts even when the dynasty's actual military strategy does not require them — using Legendary-rank members as commanders in minor raids because valor accumulation is the goal.
3. Hosting a disproportionate number of diplomatic and cultural events (tournaments, summits, ceremonies). A dynasty that hosts more events than it has strategic need for is converting money into prestige.

### The Neutral Player — Spoiler Strategy

A player who deliberately pursues no victory path and instead dedicates their match to disrupting all other players' paths is employing the Spoiler Strategy. This is mechanically viable and genuinely dangerous in certain configurations — but it has hard limits.

**What the Spoiler can do:**

The Spoiler's core advantage is flexibility. Without being committed to any victory path, they can allocate resources to wherever disruption is most valuable at any given moment. They can raid a conquest player's supply lines, then pivot to assassinate a prestige player's bloodline member, then spend the next twenty minutes establishing a competing currency to slow the economic player. No other player can afford this kind of tactical breadth because they are committed to building toward specific thresholds.

The Spoiler can also function as a kingmaker. In late-game multi-player matches, the Spoiler's accumulated resources and military capacity, combined with their freedom from victory path constraints, make them the most valuable potential ally for any dynasty approaching a victory threshold. Spoiler players can effectively auction their support to the highest bidder.

**What the Spoiler cannot do:**

The Spoiler cannot win. This is the limit. There is no victory path for sustained disruption. A player who spends 90 minutes preventing other players from winning while building nothing toward their own victory threshold will eventually face a match in which they cannot prevent a victory they did not see coming, or will find themselves with insufficient resources to hold their own territory against players who have been building consistently toward specific strengths.

The Spoiler is also vulnerable to being targeted. Because the Spoiler has no path-specific defensive strengths (no garrison infrastructure, no faith authority, no prosperity-driven loyalty, no alliance network), they are actually easier to conquer than any path player. A conquest player who identifies the Spoiler as a persistent threat can dedicate a single campaign to eliminating them while their other fronts are managed defensively.

**The viability window:**

The Spoiler is most dangerous in matches with four or more players on separate paths, where disrupting any single player helps all the others. In a two-player or three-player match, Spoiler play is weak — there are too few path players for the disruption to matter, and the Spoiler will fall behind the two committed players in every measurable metric. In a 6-10 player match with multiple near-winners, the Spoiler can delay the match end significantly and extract very favorable terms for throwing support one way or another.

**The Spoiler's endgame:**

The viable Spoiler endgame is a late-game path pivot. A Spoiler who has accumulated strong military assets through flexible play and who pivots to Military Conquest in the late game has a viable path to victory IF they have preserved enough territory and haven't depleted their resources on pure disruption. The risk is that other players recognize the Spoiler has pivoted and can direct counter-effort specifically at the new path.

### Late-Game Path Switching

**The general rule:** Path switching at Level 3 (Ideological Expansion phase) is expensive but viable. Path switching at Level 4 (Irreversible Divergence) is almost always fatal unless the new path is closely related to the old path.

**Viable switches at Level 3:**

- Military Conquest to Dynastic Prestige: The conquest player has military strength and battlefield accomplishments. Pivoting to prestige means leveraging those accomplishments as a foundation, building out the alliance and cultural infrastructure. Cost: 20-30 match-minutes of reallocating resources from military to court infrastructure. Viable if the conquest path has hit a wall (typically the Reckoning coalition is impenetrable).

- Economic Dominance to Territorial Governance: Both paths share Moral conviction and prosperity investment. The economic player already has trade networks and diplomatic relationships that are compatible with governance. The transition requires replacing trade infrastructure priority with civic infrastructure priority. Cost: 15-25 match-minutes. The most viable switch in the game — the two paths are so behaviorally similar that the switch is mostly a resource reallocation.

- Dynastic Prestige to Military Conquest: The prestige player has battlefield veterans, existing military infrastructure from valor-seeking deployment, and alliance relationships that can provide military support. Pivoting to conquest means using those assets for territorial expansion. Cost: 25-35 match-minutes of building siege and garrison infrastructure. Viable if the prestige race has been lost to a more dominant rival.

- Faith Divine Right to Territorial Governance: Both paths benefit from Moral conviction. The faith player already has cultural cohesion infrastructure that partially overlaps with civic infrastructure. The transition requires redirecting faith propagation resources toward civic investment and integration bureau construction. Cost: 20-30 match-minutes. Viable if the declaration prerequisites are proving unreachable.

**Non-viable switches at Level 3:**

- Economic Dominance to Military Conquest: The conviction requirements conflict directly. An economic player who pivots to conquest must rebuild from a Moral conviction base while generating conquest conviction damage. The army buildup required from zero military investment takes too long at Level 3. Only viable in extremely long matches (8+ hours) where there is time to rebuild.

- Military Conquest to Faith Divine Right: The conquest player has neither the faith infrastructure nor the Moral conviction standing required for the declaration prerequisites. Converting 30+ match-minutes of conquest damage into Moral conviction while simultaneously building faith infrastructure from scratch is not achievable in time. Not viable.

**Level 4 switches — almost always fatal:**

At Level 4, the game state has committed. Resources, territories, and infrastructure have been built around a specific path for 60-90+ match-minutes. Abandoning that path means accepting that the investment is wasted. The only Level 4 switch that is not reliably fatal is Dynastic Prestige to Territorial Governance (because both require Moral conviction and high cultural investment — the infrastructure partially transfers). All other Level 4 switches are concession of the match.

## VICTORY CONDITION DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

The five paths are designed to produce genuinely different games within the same match. A conquest-oriented game feels like a military simulation: armies moving across provinces, siege infrastructure going up around enemy capitals, garrison chains extending into newly occupied territory. An economic game feels like a commercial network being slowly constructed: trade posts appearing in foreign cities, alliance meetings generating favorable terms, the currency ticker in the trade interface showing adoption creeping upward. A faith game feels like an ideological movement gaining weight: the map's faith overlay slowly shifting colors, theological debates generating legitimacy, the spread clock ticking down during the declaration window. Each path creates its own aesthetic and its own tempo. The design goal is that watching which buildings a player constructs in their first thirty minutes tells you their path, and that path determines the emotional character of their entire match experience.

The critical design constraint on all five paths is genuine late-game escalation rather than passive accumulation. Each path has a structural problem that prevents the player from simply building toward a threshold on a quiet path while the rest of the map fights elsewhere. Military conquest has stability as the enforced constraint — more territory means more suppression costs, which means the conquest player cannot simply expand passively without managing the rebellion dynamic. Economic dominance has the trust architecture as the enforced constraint — the currency standard requires ongoing positive diplomatic behavior, which means the economic player cannot accumulate passively without managing relationship health across multiple partners. Faith divine right has the declaration clock as the enforced constraint — the player must declare at a specific moment, cannot extend the accumulation phase indefinitely, and must survive a structured opposition window after declaring. Territorial governance has the prosperity maintenance requirement — more integrated territory means more civic infrastructure to maintain, and any gap in prosperity drives integration reversals. Dynastic prestige has the bloodline vulnerability — prestige cannot be accumulated safely from a protected rear position because the highest-value prestige events require bloodline member exposure. Every path has a structural mechanism that prevents pure passive accumulation and forces the player into meaningful risk as they approach the threshold.

All five paths remain viable through Level 4 because none of them is designed to be the obviously dominant choice. The game deliberately structures the early, mid, and late game so that the winning path is determined by player behavior, map configuration, and opponent decisions rather than by an inherent power ranking. A 10-player match in which 8 players are pursuing military conquest will produce a different power ranking than a 10-player match in which paths are distributed. Military conquest is strongest when there are few other conquest players and many path players who have neglected military investment. Economic dominance is strongest when the diplomatic landscape is stable and multiple dynasties have independent reasons to maintain positive relationships. Faith divine right is strongest when the map's cultural concentration allows for rapid propagation. Territorial governance is strongest when the match is long and conquest players have over-extended. Dynastic prestige is strongest in matches with many players, because the alliance network breadth requirement is easier to satisfy and prestige events are more numerous. No single path dominates all configurations. Each path has a set of conditions under which it is the optimal choice and a set of conditions under which it is a poor choice. The player who reads the map configuration and selects or pivots their path accordingly is playing the deepest version of Bloodlines.

The five paths also create a social dynamic that is as interesting as the mechanical one. Every player at the table has both a path-specific and a path-agnostic interest in every other player's progress. A conquest player needs the economic player to stay viable until it is convenient to eliminate them — because a healthy economic player funds the conquest player's mercenary recruitment. A faith player needs the prestige player to stay alive because prestige players host diplomatic events that generate faith propagation access. A governance player needs at least one conquest player to remain threatening because the contrast between the conquest player's brutal occupation costs and the governance player's prosperity makes integration petitions more attractive. The five paths are not just five ways to win — they are five roles in a political economy that needs all of its parts to function. The late game, when paths begin to reach resolution, is when that political economy begins to collapse, and the collapse itself is the match's climax.
