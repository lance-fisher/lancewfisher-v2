# Bloodlines — Conviction System

## Overview
The Conviction system models belief, morale, and ideological commitment as a tangible gameplay mechanic. Conviction is not just a passive stat. It is a resource that can be spent, invested, and lost. It reflects how deeply a faction's people believe in their cause, their faith, and their leaders.

## Design Intent
Conviction exists to create meaningful tension between what a player wants to do and what their people are willing to support. High conviction enables extraordinary actions (including Born of Sacrifice). Low conviction leads to desertion, unrest, and collapse from within.

## Open Design Questions
- How is conviction generated? (Faith actions, victories, leadership traits, sacrifices?)
- How is conviction spent? (Elite unit creation, morale in battle, political actions?)
- How does conviction decay? (Defeats, atrocities, broken promises, leader death?)
- Is conviction per-faction, per-territory, per-army, or per-population group?
- What is the relationship between conviction and faith alignment?

### Design Content (Added 2026-03-15)

Conviction is a behavioral morality spectrum shaped permanently by actions. It is not a cosmetic alignment label chosen in a menu. It is produced by what the player actually does during the match.

**Canonical Definition:** Bloodline = family. Conviction = philosophy. Faith = religion.

Conviction is distinct from Faith. Faith is chosen. Conviction is lived. Faith determines doctrine and worldview. Conviction determines what kind of civilization the player has actually become through action.

**Actions that influence Conviction:**
- Warfare practices
- Treatment of prisoners
- Enslavement
- Protection of civilians
- Pillage and destruction
- Sacrifice
- Governance decisions

Conviction is permanent and reflects the historical character of the civilization. Conviction represents the moral character of the dynasty's rule. Faith Alignment represents religious practice. These two systems influence each other but remain separate.

**Conviction permanently shapes:**
- Player abilities
- Faith alignment interactions
- Kingdom development
- Late game powers

**Examples of conviction directions:**
- Conquest
- Prosperity through trade
- Faith authority
- Territorial governance
- Dynastic prestige

### Player Manual Expansion (Added 2026-03-15)

**Dual Definition Preserved:** Conviction has two definitions that both matter:
1. Conviction is how your dynasty acts — the behavioral morality spectrum shaped permanently by actions and decisions. It does not drift automatically. It includes an endpoint described as cruel and morally bankrupt beyond simply cruel. It affects reputation, loyalty, sabotage risk, and willingness of populations to contribute.
2. Conviction is also the governing philosophy and world-facing strategic path the dynasty pursues — the "how we intend to rule and win" layer. It evolves over time and can shift as the dynasty adapts.

These are not conflicting definitions. They are two layers of the same reality: what you do, and what you become. The dynasty's behavior shapes its moral character and its strategic philosophy.

**Conviction in Practice:** Bloodlines watches for patterns, not isolated incidents. Repeated behavior matters. The realm remembers what kind of power you practice.

**Legitimacy Interaction:** A harsh act committed by a crumbling illegitimate dynasty may provoke revolt. The same act committed by a triumphant prestigious dynasty with strong religious and cultural authority may be interpreted as necessary or justified. Perception changes consequences. Bloodlines is not naive moral math.

### Faith and Conviction Independence (Added 2026-03-18)

## Faith and Conviction Independence (LOCKED)

Faith and Conviction are explicitly defined as independent systems. This separation is a CORE DESIGN PRINCIPLE and must never be violated.

**The rule:**

Faith is chosen. Conviction is lived.

Faith defines what a dynasty believes and practices spiritually. Conviction defines what a dynasty has actually become through the sum of its actions.

**Critical Rule — they must never be forced into alignment:**

* A player may follow a dark faith path (Blood Dominion dark expression, brutal sacrifice, blood rites) and still maintain positive conviction through just governance, protection of populations, and honorable warfare.

* A player may follow a light faith path (Old Light mercy expression, ceremonial worship, healing) and still accumulate negative conviction through cruelty to prisoners, pillage, enslavement, and betrayal.

The faith-conviction independence rule exists to prevent the game from becoming a simple moral axis. A player's spiritual practice does not determine their moral character. Actions do.

Faith influences conviction through mechanics (certain faith actions shift conviction), but the player can counteract those influences through other actions. The two systems interact but are never locked together.

**Design implications:**

The most interesting rulers are those whose faith and conviction are in tension. A dynasty that follows the Blood Dominion with genuine mercy creates a different civilization than one that follows it with cruelty. A dynasty that follows the Old Light as a tool of control becomes something different from one that follows it as genuine stewardship. This tension is a feature, not a conflict to be resolved.

### Conviction Axis Model and Three Pillars (Added 2026-03-18)

## Conviction and Faith — Axis Model

Civilizations in Bloodlines are defined by two independent axes:

**Faith Doctrine Axis:**
* Light Doctrine
* Dark Doctrine

**Conviction Axis:**
* High Conviction
* Neutral Conviction
* Low Conviction

These axes are independent. No position on the Faith Doctrine Axis implies any position on the Conviction Axis.

## Conviction — Design Intent

Conviction measures:
* integrity
* consistency
* treatment of others
* exercise of power

It reflects how a ruler behaves, not what they believe.

## Example Outcomes

**Dark Faith + High Conviction:**
* Structured, disciplined, internally respected societies
* Harsh but principled
* A civilization that practices blood rites with genuine conviction, internal order, and principled governance

**Light Faith + Low Conviction:**
* Corrupt, hypocritical, unstable societies
* Externally righteous but internally broken
* A civilization that performs ceremonial worship while abusing its populations

## Final Design Structure — Three Pillars

Every civilization in Bloodlines is defined by three pillars:

1. **Dynasty** — defines lineage and political identity
2. **Faith** — defines worldview and cultural systems
3. **Conviction** — defines moral character and leadership integrity

Dynasty defines who you are. Faith defines what you believe. Conviction defines how you rule.

These three pillars are independent of each other. Any combination is valid and produces a distinct civilization.

**Updated Conviction Axis:**

* Cruel Conviction

The terms "High" and "Low" are deprecated and replaced. "Moral" and "Cruel" are the canonical terms going forward.

The prior axis model entry (High/Neutral/Low) is preserved above as historical record. This entry supersedes it.

**Why this matters:** "High" and "Low" implied an abstract meter. "Moral" and "Cruel" name the actual character of the ruler and civilization. These are not measurements — they are identities. The population, the Trueborn city, and other dynasties do not see a number. They see a ruler who is either principled or merciless.

### Conviction as a Central Game Feature (Added 2026-03-18, Fourth Ingestion)

Conviction is not a background system. It is a prominent, featured aspect of gameplay with significant mechanical weight.

**Impact on Population:**
- Moral conviction generates population loyalty, volunteer recruitment, and willingness to contribute beyond what is conscripted
- Cruel conviction generates fear-driven compliance but increases desertion risk, suppresses birth rates, and eliminates volunteer contributions
- Population remembers patterns of rule — a sudden cruel act after years of moral rule carries different weight than prolonged cruelty
- The population does not react to isolated acts in isolation; it reacts to the accumulated character of the dynasty

**Impact on the Trueborn City:**
- The Trueborn city maintains a conviction record for all players across the match
- A dynasty with extreme cruel conviction risks triggering the Trueborn coalition response
- A dynasty with sustained moral conviction earns favorable treatment: trade bonuses, diplomatic access, potential coalition membership
- The Trueborn are not naive — a moral conviction record that is recent after a history of cruelty receives less weight

**Impact on Other Dynasty Leaders:**
- Other leaders perceive and react to conviction record throughout the match
- Moral rulers attract alliances, diplomatic respect, and the voluntary defection of enemy populations
- Cruel rulers face diplomatic isolation, heightened espionage risk, and accelerated coalition formation against them
- Conviction reputation is one of the components of a dynasty's standing in the world — it cannot be easily falsified

### Conviction Milestones (Added 2026-03-18, Fourth Ingestion)

Conviction milestones are unlocked at specific thresholds on both ends of the axis. Both directions carry genuine power. Neither is a trap.

**Moral Conviction Milestones:**
- Population morale bonus: troops drawn from a moral population fight with higher base morale
- Volunteer recruitment: population offers troops without conscription cost or loyalty penalty
- Trade network trust bonus: trade partners extend favorable terms to dynasties with moral conviction records
- Diplomatic favor with the Trueborn city: access to coalition membership, mediation services, and Trueborn trade routes
- Light doctrine faith amplification: moral conviction strengthens the effects of light doctrine faith mechanics
- Extreme moral threshold: potential divine favor manifestation (light-aligned entity interventions, healing amplification, morale events)
- Other leaders more likely to sue for peace rather than fight to the death against a moral dynasty

**Cruel Conviction Milestones:**
- Fear compliance: population suppresses internal dissent, reducing revolt risk in exchange for lower voluntary contribution
- Enemy morale penalty on contact: the dynasty's reputation for cruelty degrades opposing troop morale before battle begins
- Prisoner execution option: executing prisoners unlocks a resource return not available through ransom or release
- Dark doctrine amplification: cruel conviction strengthens the effects of dark doctrine faith mechanics
- Unbeliever sacrifice threshold: at extreme cruel conviction combined with high dark doctrine faith intensity, the sacrifice of unbelieving population becomes available (see Faith System — Dark Doctrine)
- Trueborn coalition preparation begins: the Trueborn city begins forming a response coalition as extreme cruel conviction is sustained
- Other leaders more likely to pre-emptively attack a cruel dynasty out of calculated fear rather than wait to be targeted

### What Conviction Offers the Ruler and People (Added 2026-03-18, Fourth Ingestion)

**For the Ruler:**
- Moral conviction builds legitimacy — the dynasty's right to rule is accepted rather than enforced
- Moral conviction opens diplomatic paths unavailable to cruel rulers: voluntary territorial integration, alliance formation, Trueborn support
- Cruel conviction opens power paths unavailable to moral rulers: unbeliever sacrifice, fear-based army composition, extreme dark doctrine mechanics
- Conviction record shapes which victory paths are viable — a cruel dynasty cannot win through governance attraction; a moral dynasty cannot unlock certain dark faith escalations
- Extreme conviction in either direction unlocks late-game powers that neutral dynasties cannot access

**For the People:**
- Population under moral conviction grows faster, contributes more willingly, and reaches higher faith intensity
- Population under moral conviction generates volunteer military units without recruiting cost
- Population under cruel conviction shrinks faster, requires enforcement infrastructure to maintain, and generates no volunteers
- Population under cruel conviction may comply — but the compliance is transactional, not loyal
- The composition and quality of a dynasty's population is directly shaped by the conviction of its rulers over time

### Light/Dark Faith Specific Conviction Interactions (Added 2026-03-18, Fourth Ingestion)

The four possible combinations of faith doctrine axis and conviction axis each produce a distinct civilization type with distinct mechanics:

**Light Doctrine + Moral Conviction:**
- Maximum faith intensity amplification
- Maximum population goodwill and loyalty
- Access to healing and restoration mechanics
- Volunteer recruitment bonus stacked with faith intensity bonus
- Potential divine manifestation: angelic or light-entity interventions, population morale events
- Strongest diplomatic position in the game

**Light Doctrine + Cruel Conviction:**
- Internal religious hypocrisy — faith intensity degrades over time as the population perceives the faith as a tool of control
- Reduced faith intensity caps relative to moral conviction
- Diplomatic damage among other dynasties following the same light faith
- Risk of religious fracture: a segment of the population may organize around authentic faith practice and resist the regime
- The gap between professed values and practiced cruelty creates instability that worsens over time

**Dark Doctrine + Moral Conviction:**
- Structured, principled practitioners of a harsh faith — rare and formidable
- Access to dark doctrine mechanics without the worst cruel conviction penalties
- Population is not oppressed but is part of a demanding, ordered system with genuine internal justice
- Unique diplomatic position: feared but respected
- Other dynasties cannot easily predict or dismiss this civilization — it defies the expected categories
- Faith intensity remains stable because the conviction and the doctrine are internally consistent

**Dark Doctrine + Cruel Conviction:**
- Maximum dark power amplification — both systems reinforce each other
- Access to unbeliever sacrifice at extreme thresholds (see Faith System)
- Fear-based control is maximized throughout the population
- Trueborn coalition formation risk is at its highest
- Enemy morale damage on contact is maximized
- Potential dark manifestation: demonic or corruption-aligned entity interventions
- The most recognizable "dark empire" civilization type in the game — powerful, but encircled

### Conviction Action Weight Framework (Added 2026-03-18, Fifth Session)

Conviction is tracked through a point system. Player actions generate conviction points (CP). Accumulated CP determines position on the Moral / Neutral / Cruel axis. The system tracks patterns, not isolated incidents. Repeated behavior compounds.

**Design Rule:** No single action locks a dynasty into any position. Every shift can be counteracted through subsequent behavior — but the accumulated weight of a pattern is harder to reverse than it was to build.

#### Actions That Shift Toward Moral

| Action | CP Shift | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Release prisoners without ransom | +2 | Minor; generous but economically negligible |
| Honor a peace agreement when breaking it would be advantageous | +5 | Significant; sacrifice of opportunity |
| Protect civilian zones during conflict (no pillage order) | +3 per territory | Moderate; consistent behavior required |
| Veteran retirement with land grant | +2 per cycle | Minor; cumulative across many Born of Sacrifice recycling cycles |
| Rebuild a destroyed territory (yours or conquered) | +4 | Visible investment in human welfare |
| Provide food aid to a starving allied or neutral population | +6 | High; genuine sacrifice of resources |
| Allow conquered population to maintain their faith | +3 | Tolerance exercised under power |
| Heal wounded prisoners before ransom | +1 | Small; basic decency |
| Execute a traitor through public trial (with evidence) | +2 | Justice performed visibly and legitimately |

#### Actions That Shift Toward Cruel

| Action | CP Shift | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Execute prisoners of war | -4 | Significant; eliminates ransom and diplomatic value |
| Pillage a territory | -3 per territory | Moderate; cumulative if done repeatedly |
| Discard veterans without recognition or reward | -1 per cycle | Minor individually; compounds through Born of Sacrifice cycles |
| Enslave conquered population | -6 | Major; clear act of dehumanization |
| Burn farmland during war | -5 | Devastating to civilian population; visible moral crime |
| Force faith conversion at sword point | -5 | Violates the faith-conviction separation mechanic; also destabilizes target faith intensity |
| Execute a bloodline member of a defeated dynasty | -8 | Very significant; eliminates diplomatic options permanently |
| Sacrifice unbeliever population (requires extreme threshold unlock) | -15 | Catastrophic single-act shift; the most powerful cruel action in the game |
| Conduct a raid specifically targeting water infrastructure | -7 | Attacks survival; universally condemned |
| Imprison an innocent (false accusation of treason) | -4 | Injustice against the dynasty's own people |

#### Neutral Zone Actions (No CP Shift)

| Action | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Ransom prisoners at market rate | Standard exchange; no moral weight |
| Conscript troops through family obligation | Expected; not cruel |
| Enforce taxation at standard rates | Governance; neutral |
| Execute a guilty traitor without trial | Edge case — shifts slightly cruel if it becomes a pattern |

#### Pattern Amplification Rules

Actions that cross a threshold into pattern behavior have their CP values amplified:

- **First occurrence:** Full CP value as listed
- **Second occurrence (same action within the same stage):** 1.5x CP value
- **Third and subsequent occurrences:** 2x CP value — the realm interprets this as policy, not incident

**The reverse also applies:** A moral action performed only once carries its listed value. A moral action performed consistently across many game cycles becomes the dynasty's character — and generates a reputation bonus that functions independently of the CP total.

#### Conviction Threshold Bands

Specific numeric thresholds are to be tuned in the data layer, but the structural design is as follows:

| Band | Label | Access |
|------|-------|--------|
| Very high positive | Apex Moral | All moral milestones unlocked; divine manifestation eligible; maximum Trueborn favor |
| Moderate positive | Moral | Moral milestones active; trade bonuses; volunteer recruitment |
| Near zero (either side) | Neutral | No bonus, no penalty; maximum diplomatic flexibility |
| Moderate negative | Cruel | Cruel milestones active; fear compliance; prisoner execution bonus |
| Very high negative | Apex Cruel | All cruel milestones unlocked; unbeliever sacrifice eligible; Trueborn coalition active |
