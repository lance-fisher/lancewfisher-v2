The polygamy question: only for Blood Dominion and The Wild.

## War Heroes and Lesser Houses — Unit Promotion System (NEW)

Exceptional commanders earn titles and lands, creating lesser houses under the main dynasty. Lesser houses remain loyal but become their own branches.

In battle, a swordsman or other combat unit not directly affiliated with the bloodline gets ranked up through combat for multiple stages, say 5 for example. This in turn can create advantages for the player. Beyond 5 promotions for a unit in combat they may be offered a promotion to command an army in a role similar to a general or commander. After multiple successful campaigns they in turn may be offered lands and titles and in turn become a lesser house affiliated with the bloodline dynasty.

Additionally for gameplay playability and strategy, for example an army of 5 promoted swordsmen would likely beat an army of 5 regular class axemen, despite situations where that edge would be given to the axemen if they were equally matched otherwise.

## Mixed Bloodline Dynasties — CORRECTION AND EXPANSION

**Existing text (to be corrected):**
> "If a bloodline repeatedly takes wives of another bloodline, children create complex inheritance and legitimacy dynamics. Children of mixed bloodline can branch off as own dynasty through blood and marriage. May announce loyalty to one bloodline or another. Creates emergent dynastic webs, rival claims, legitimacy disputes. Late game allows newly formed dynasties to emerge from royalty of older houses to make claims over current leaders."

**Correction and Expansion:**
There would be ample notification about the status of a member of the bloodline or children born seeking to branch off. Ultimately the head of the bloodline would make the decision.

There should also be a slider that helps keep the player aware of the intentions of the children to defect:
- Loyal to head of bloodline (vassal)
- Neutral (NPC behavior)
- Seeking to join a different bloodline entirely

Based on the decision of the head of the family this could result in forced combat between them.

This can happen on the reverse side as well where others may seek to join your bloodline. This should require some kind of diplomatic action being successful first to initiate.

## Early Game Design Philosophy (NEW)

A large part of the early game should be battling or taking over as ruler many of the NPC tribes in the area. Conflict between houses should be extremely minimal for the early stages of the game.

It is all too common in RTS games for "blitz" or mass buildups of forces and abandonment of every other factor necessary to build a successful bloodline. Cheesing should be discouraged by game development.
