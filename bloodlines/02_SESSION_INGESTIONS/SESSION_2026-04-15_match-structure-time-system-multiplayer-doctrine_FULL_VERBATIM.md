PART ONE: THE FIVE-STAGE MATCH STRUCTURE

Foundational Rule

Bloodlines uses a five-stage match structure. Stages do not advance by timer. They advance when the match has reached the relevant world-state and readiness conditions for the next stage. No fixed real-time duration governs stage transitions. The conditions are listed explicitly below for each stage. A fresh model or agent must never substitute a timer-based stage-advance system because there is no canonical timer. Readiness is the gate, not the clock.

The five stages overlay with three broader player-facing phases:
Phase One: Emergence spans Stage One through early Stage Two.
Phase Two: Commitment spans late Stage Two through Stage Four.
Phase Three: Resolution spans Stage Five.

Stages describe what the match has structurally become. Phases describe what the player is primarily doing within that arc. Both frameworks are canonical and should be stated together when briefing a fresh model or agent.

Stage One: Founding

Purpose: Stage One is the intimate, localized opening of the match. The player is establishing the home seat of the bloodline, securing the dynasty's immediate survival, reading the geography, and making first contact with the immediate environment. This is the most personal and least globally competitive stage. The scale of threat and opportunity is local. Major inter-dynasty competition is not yet the priority.

What happens mechanically in Stage One: The player founds the primary home base and establishes the dynastic anchor. Food and water security must be established. Basic defended borders must be created. Immediate local resource infrastructure including farmland, water access, and initial extraction nodes must be secured. Surrounding terrain is explored, including rivers, coastlines, forests, iron access, pass locations, and other strategic geography relevant to Stage Two decisions. Minor local diplomacy begins, including first-contact relationships with nearby tribes, settlers, and any available minor powers. Faith commitment should not be rushed during Stage One. The founding generation should still feel young and stable at the end of this stage. Survival, footing, and positional understanding are the objectives.

Stage One ends when all of the following are true: The dynasty has a stable, defended home base. Borders are defensible against the current local threat level. Resource production exceeds immediate survival requirements and provides a genuine buffer. The surrounding geography is understood well enough to make informed Stage Two directional decisions. Food and water are stable with buffer surplus, not merely at break-even.

Time feel in Stage One: Small skirmishes during Stage One typically declare 3 to 6 in-world months when concluded. The founding generation remains young and cohesive. Bloodline aging is minimal. The dynastic tree is shallow.

Stage Two: Expansion and Identity

Purpose: Stage Two is where the dynasty begins becoming a real civilization with a legible strategic identity. The player expands beyond the immediate homeland and begins committing to who this dynasty is becoming militarily, economically, religiously, and politically. Identity starts hardening here, and several of the most consequential match-defining choices belong to this stage.

What happens mechanically in Stage Two: Territorial expansion moves outward across the home continent beyond the founding perimeter. Farther resource nodes are contested and claimed. Forward positions are established beyond the original borders. Tribal and regional encounters become harder and more developed. Conviction pattern formation begins carrying real moral and dynastic weight. The faith covenant commitment arrives here and is one of the most consequential decisions of the entire match. Internal economy compounds and surplus production grows. Internal trade networks begin forming. Early first contact with rival dynasties occurs during this stage.

Stage Two ends when all of the following are true: The dynasty has committed to a faith covenant. Territorial reach has expanded meaningfully beyond the founding area. The economic base is capable of supporting later large-scale conflict. Initial contact with the other major powers on the continent has been made and those powers are understood as real forces rather than distant abstractions.

Time feel in Stage Two: Expansion battles and first regional conflicts typically declare 6 months to 1 in-world year. Children begin aging into adolescence. The bloodline tree becomes one generation deep in an active sense, meaning children born at founding are now capable of being assigned roles.

Stage Three: Encounter, Establishment, and the Neutral City

Purpose: Stage Three is where the match stops being primarily developmental and becomes an actual contest between civilizations. Rival dynasties are no longer distant possibilities. They are present, armed, positionally established, and politically consequential. The world map is now a contested space rather than an open frontier.

What happens mechanically in Stage Three: First significant military contact occurs between major dynasties. Border conflicts develop over contested regions, resource nodes, and strategic geography. Forward bases are created and longer-range logistical presence becomes necessary. Formal trade expansion crosses dynasty lines and generates both cooperation and conflict. Larger army structures develop. The Trueborn City becomes a real strategic variable. It can be cultivated, contested, or deliberately avoided, but it cannot remain background flavor. Defined alliance structures, rivalries, and diplomatic posture harden during this stage. The midgame political map takes its most visible shape here. The heir generation becomes fully active as young adults during Stage Three.

Stage Three ends when all of the following are true: Major powers have made significant military contact. Trade networks are active across dynasty lines. Forward military positions are established by multiple major dynasties. Alliances and rivalries are legible and function as real political constraints. The Trueborn City has been meaningfully engaged or deliberately avoided as an explicit strategic choice, not ignored by default. The likely shape of Stage Four conflict is becoming clear.

Time feel in Stage Three: First inter-dynasty wars typically declare 1 to 2 in-world years. The founding generation is entering middle age. The heir generation is fully active and beginning to accumulate history. Succession pressure begins becoming a real dynastic variable.

Stage Four: War and the Turning of Tides

Purpose: Stage Four is the stage of major war. Everything accumulated across the first three stages is now tested under sustained campaign pressure. Individual skirmishes give way to full campaigns. This is where battles become match-defining rather than provisional. Fortification doctrine, commander depth, supply stability, bloodline coherence, and faith conviction all pay off or fail visibly here.

What happens mechanically in Stage Four: Sustained campaigns replace isolated skirmishes as the dominant military form. Large-scale army-on-army conflict is the central activity. Faith divergence and conviction doctrine express themselves in fully visible battlefield and political consequences. Deep economic, loyalty, and dynastic strain emerges from prolonged war. Siege operations become central and serious undertakings rather than optional mechanics. The Great Reckoning and coalition pressure becomes possible here. The fate of the Trueborn City is usually decided in this stage. Clear sorting occurs between dynasties that are contending for victory and those fighting for survival, coalition leverage, or limited objectives. Fortresses become true campaign obstacles. Unfortified realms collapse quickly under serious and sustained Stage Four pressure.

Stage Four ends when all of the following are true: The major wars have meaningfully shifted power distribution across the map. It is clear which dynasties are positioned to pursue one of the recognized victory paths. Other dynasties are fighting for survival, coalition leverage, or limited strategic objectives rather than outright dominance. The Stage Five endgame contest has become visible and the likely contenders are identifiable.

Time feel in Stage Four: Major battles in Stage Four typically declare 2 to 3 in-world years. Sustained siege campaigns typically declare 3 to 5 in-world years. Aging, succession, and dynastic strain become visibly consequential. Named commanders and bloodline figures accumulate history, scars, and in some cases legendary status. Founding generation figures begin dying of age or being lost in war.

Stage Five: Final Convergence

Purpose: Stage Five is the endgame resolution stage. Every major system is operating at maximum expression. Every remaining contender is either closing on a recognized victory path or resisting one. This stage is not a cleanup period. It is where the story resolves violently and politically, with all accumulated systems bearing final consequence.

What happens mechanically in Stage Five: Apex faith structures and apex covenant commitments are active. Final Divine Right declaration windows open. Final coalition negotiations and counter-coalition resistance take place. Resolution battles over currency dominance thresholds, territorial control thresholds, or faith dominance conditions occur. Final major naval contests and secondary-continent control requirements can be decided here. Peak bloodline member power and legendary figure presence defines late battles. Full-force Trueborn City reemergence is possible if the city was not neutralized earlier. Decisive legitimacy contests determine which dynasty can claim recognized sovereignty. Final sovereignty decisions and end-match recognition drive the resolution.

Stage Five ends when one of the following occurs: A recognized victory path resolves for one dynasty. A decisive coalition or negotiated settlement forms that prevents any single dynasty from claiming solo sovereignty, producing a recognized shared resolution. The remaining sovereignty contest is conclusively broken or ratified, and the match reaches its declared end state.

Time feel in Stage Five: The accumulated years of prior declarations fully express themselves in legitimacy, bloodline depth, veteran commanders, named units, and dynastic standing. Late-stage battles carry the weight of everything that came before. The match should feel like a historical culmination, not a timed wrapup.

PART TWO: THE DECLARED DUAL-CLOCK TIME MODEL

Core Rule: Bloodlines does not use a single universal conversion ratio between real minutes and in-world years. It does not run on a universal time-speed multiplier. It does not pause dynastic time during battle. These approaches were considered and rejected.

Bloodlines uses the Declared Time Model. This is a two-clock system joined by an explicit declaration at the close of each engagement.

Clock One: The Battle Clock. The battle clock governs direct RTS engagements and live tactical combat. It is real-time. During a battle, the player experiences time as immediate tactical real-time. A major engagement lasts roughly 20 to 40 real minutes at full tactical engagement. The battle clock is the player's experience of presence and direct command.

Clock Two: The Dynasty Clock. The dynasty clock governs in-world historical time. It governs years passing, bloodline aging, succession pressure, the aging of children into adults, long-form operations, regional movement across the world map, diplomacy maturation, faith spread, and the broad evolution of the world between direct engagements. It does not tick at a fixed real-minutes-to-years conversion. It advances through the declaration seam at the close of engagements and through the ongoing internal processing heartbeat of the strategic layer.

The Declaration Seam: The declaration seam is the canonical mechanism that joins the two clocks. When a direct battle ends, the game presents a declaration stating how much in-world dynasty-clock time has elapsed as a result of the engagement. This declaration is not flavor text. It is the binding mechanism between the tactical RTS layer and the dynastic simulation layer.

The player fights a battle in real-time. The world interprets that battle as a campaign that consumed months or years. Both are true simultaneously.

Canonical declaration reference ranges:
- Small frontier skirmish: approximately 3 to 6 in-world months.
- Major pitched field battle: approximately 1 to 2 in-world years.
- Sustained siege campaign: approximately 3 to 5 in-world years.

Declarations in later stages, particularly Stage Three through Stage Five, should produce enough elapsed time that real generational motion becomes visible across a full match. The design target is that a complete long match can naturally produce two to three visible generations of dynastic history.

How the Two Clocks Interact: The dynasty clock does not run independently during battle in the same way it runs during the strategic layer. Ordinary dynasty management events, queue items, and routine decisions do not interrupt live tactical combat except in cases of specifically defined high-urgency tactical alerts. Everything queues and is surfaced after the battle resolves. The declaration seam provides the specific moment of clock synchronization.

The Canonical Session Rhythm: Battle, Declaration, Events Queue, Commitment Phase, Next Battle. During the commitment phase, the player assigns army movements, decides which fronts to personally attend, delegates commanders to unattended fronts, advances or pauses operations, sends diplomacy, makes bloodline assignments, commits or cancels building programs, and issues standing directives. This is not a pause menu. It is a core layer of play.

The Internal Processing Heartbeat: Separately from the declared battle-to-dynasty seam, Bloodlines maintains a recurring internal operational heartbeat, approximately every 90 seconds of game time, that processes systemic updates. These include resource generation, population shifts, supply consumption, trade yields, faith intensity pressure, conviction drift, and event triggers. This heartbeat does not replace the declared-time architecture. It is the ongoing operational simulation running underneath the player-facing session rhythm.

What Multi-Speed Time Means in Bloodlines: Bloodlines is not correctly described as using a classic RTS speed slider. The correct framing is mode-based temporal context. Tactical Real-Time Mode during direct battle: time is immediate, personal, and action-based. Declaration Moment at battle close: elapsed in-world time is stated and locked. Strategic Layer during the commitment phase: operational decisions are made with campaign and dynastic scale in mind. These are not speed settings. They are layers of play with different relationships to time.

PART THREE: THE LIVE STRATEGIC LAYER

The World Remains Active When Zoomed Out: When the player is in the regional or sovereign command layer, the world remains live. The match does not stop. Armies continue traveling across the world map. Caravans continue moving. Operations continue advancing. Diplomacy remains in transit. Faith pressure continues spreading. Delegated battles continue resolving under commander execution. The shared in-world date and time continue advancing.

Army movement on the large-scale strategic map is represented by an icon representative of the army and its composition traveling across the visible world, consistent with the Utopia-influenced movement model established in the canon. Unit animation is reserved for live direct combat. Icon movement on the strategic map is sufficient for conveying strategic operations.

Strategic Layer Pacing Controls: In single-player, pacing options include normal live campaign pace, slow live campaign pace, pause for orders, and auto-pause on major queue events, battle start offers, or bloodline-critical alerts. In multiplayer, different rules apply and are described in Part Four.

PART FOUR: MULTIPLAYER TIME AND ENGAGEMENT DOCTRINE

The Shared Match Calendar: In multiplayer, Bloodlines runs on a shared in-world match calendar. All players operate against one shared timeline. The match does not pause for any individual player's strategic-layer window. A player who spends more time in commitment planning is allowing more real campaign time to pass while the world continues moving.

No Individual Pause or Speed Control: In multiplayer, no single player may pause the entire match or unilaterally change the global simulation speed. If any multiplayer pause mechanism exists at all in Bloodlines, it must be one of the following restricted forms only: unanimous pause, majority vote pause, host or administrator pause in private custom lobby configurations only, or emergency pause tokens (small per-player allotment, non-chainable, disabled during certain high-stakes resolution windows).

Commanders and Delegation Are Not Optional in Multiplayer: Because the shared match does not pause, multiplayer makes commanders, directives, standing orders, and attitude modifiers more critical than they are in single-player. Overextension in multiplayer degrades outcomes on unattended fronts rather than creating a hard stop.

Imminent Engagement Warning System

Foundational Rule: When a hostile engagement targeting a player becomes inevitable and crosses the activation threshold, the defending player receives an Immediate category urgent alert rather than being silently thrown into an already-running battle with no warning. This alert does not pause the match. It does not stop the shared calendar. It creates a short live engagement-entry countdown window.

What Triggers an Imminent Engagement Warning: An imminent engagement warning fires when a hostile army, fleet, siege force, raid formation, or other valid combat group has committed to an attack and has crossed the final activation threshold. Triggers include: a hostile field army entering battle range, a hostile army reaching a settlement, a hostile force reaching a fortification, a hostile fleet making contact, a hostile attack converging on a bloodline member, a hostile attack reaching a forward operating position, a hostile interception of a moving army. Triggers do NOT include distant movement or early strategic approach.

The Engagement-Entry Countdown Window: The countdown window exists between imminent and active. Default countdown ranges by battle type:
- Open-field confrontation or border clash: approximately 10 to 20 real seconds.
- Settlement defense or fortification assault: approximately 15 to 25 real seconds.
- Siege breach or siege assault escalation: approximately 20 to 30 real seconds.
- Naval interception or coastal landing: approximately 10 to 20 real seconds, modified by distance and visibility.
- Ambush or concealed approach: reduced, as low as 5 to 10 real seconds.

Modifiers that increase warning time: watchtowers, patrols, sentries, fortifications, coastal lookouts, signal infrastructure, high scouting coverage, high local loyalty, high military infrastructure readiness, bloodline commander with high vigilance or readiness traits.

Modifiers that decrease warning time: ambush approach through concealing terrain, river fog, night conditions, active weather effects, rogue support providing enemy concealment, enemy deception operations, low scouting coverage, naval surprise or concealed landfall.

What the Defender Sees During the Alert: The alert shows at minimum: engagement name and battle category, named location and regional context, current date and time, attacking party identity, defending party identity, estimated enemy composition (based on scouting, not omniscient), player forces currently committed or eligible, named commanders and bloodline members present, current posture, reinforcement options, supply condition and morale and readiness and fatigue, terrain and battle context summary, countdown timer, and the exact player choices available.

Player Choices During the Countdown: Personally enter battle, delegate to commander, change posture (Hold, Defend in Depth, Withdraw if Possible, Delay and Buy Time, Commit Fully, Protect Bloodline Member, Preserve Army, Reinforce Center, Retreat to Fortification if Legal, Contest Crossing, Screen Withdrawal), swap or confirm commander, commit nearby reinforcements (bounded by travel time), protect or risk bloodline members (remain, protected command position, attempt withdrawal, personal guard priority, refuse withdrawal), enter now, or ignore and let it proceed.

The Countdown Is Not a Planning Chamber: During the countdown, only locally relevant battle actions are permitted. Actions NOT permitted: rework bloodline assignments across unrelated regions, renegotiate unrelated diplomacy, rebuild infrastructure on other fronts, alter strategic priorities for other theaters.

What Happens When the Timer Expires: The battle begins or escalates. The global match does not pause. Other players continue. The defending player enters live battlefield if they chose to, or the battle begins under AI commander execution. The player may zoom in afterward and inherit the current battlefield state in progress.

Attacker-Side Rules: The attacker commits by crossing the engagement commitment threshold. The defender's warning window begins according to visibility, scouting, and terrain context. The attacker is already in the opening phase of approach while the countdown runs.

Bloodline Priority Cases: If a bloodline member of significant standing is present, the alert must elevate in severity and visibility. This affects UI prominence, audio sting priority, queue position, potential default camera pull, and decision summary wording.

Audio Requirements: Imminent engagement warnings receive a distinct high-priority audio sting separate from ordinary notifications. Bloodline-elevated alerts receive a further elevated or modified sting. Audio design must be wired into the engagement state machine.

UI and UX Requirements: The warning panel must appear as an urgent overlay without fully obscuring the map region, visually highlight the battle location, show available forces and commanders, show countdown timer prominently, show primary choices (Enter Battle, Delegate to Commander, Adjust Local Orders), preserve the sense that the world is still live, support fast mouse and keyboard input, scale for readability, and use shape and icon differentiation.

Multiplayer Pause Does Not Apply to This System: The imminent engagement warning system is not permission for individual pause, slowdown, speed control, or hard freeze.

Relationship to the Notification Queue Model: The imminent engagement warning is a specialized branch of the Immediate notification class. It is not an ordinary dynasty event. Ordinary events still do not interrupt live tactical combat. The warning uses its own dedicated UI system and countdown logic.

Relationship to the Zoom Layer Doctrine: At the sovereign layer, a threatened region flashes and the warning panel can be opened from the world map view. At the regional layer, the engagement marker is highlighted. Selecting Enter Battle transitions the camera seamlessly into the active battlefield. If the battle has already begun under AI execution, the player inherits the current state. The transition must feel like changing the camera's position within one continuous world.

PART FIVE: IMPLEMENTATION GUARDRAILS

- Do not implement stage transitions as fixed timers.
- Do not implement a single universal conversion ratio between real minutes and in-world years.
- Do not pause dynasty time during battle.
- Do not interrupt live tactical combat with ordinary dynasty queue events.
- Do not treat the strategic layer as a menu or pause state.
- Do implement the canonical session rhythm: Battle, Declaration, Events Queue, Commitment Phase, Next Battle.
- Do implement readiness-condition stage advancement.
- Do ensure the time model supports visible dynastic aging and succession pressure across a full match.
- Do not allow any individual player in multiplayer to pause the shared match or alter global speed unilaterally.
- Do not allow the imminent engagement countdown window to function as a disguised pause menu.
- Do not restart active battles from the beginning when a player joins midstream.
- Do expose countdown durations, modifier values, and threshold conditions in configuration rather than hardcoding them.
- Do not condense, summarize, or simplify this system in future sessions.

PART SIX: MASTER PROMPT INSERT PARAGRAPH

Bloodlines uses a five-stage condition-based match structure: Stage One Founding, Stage Two Expansion and Identity, Stage Three Encounter Establishment and the Neutral City, Stage Four War and the Turning of Tides, Stage Five Final Convergence. Stages do not advance by timer. They advance when the relevant world-state and readiness conditions are met. The game uses the canonical Declared Time Model rather than a universal time ratio. Direct RTS engagements are fought in real-time on the battle clock. When an engagement concludes, the game declares the elapsed dynasty-clock time consumed by that engagement, typically 3 to 6 months for skirmishes, 1 to 2 years for major battles, and 3 to 5 years for sustained sieges, with larger declarations in later stages to support visible aging and 2 to 3 generations across a full match. After battle, the game surfaces the events queue and the player enters a commitment phase to issue directives before the next engagement. The canonical session rhythm is Battle, Declaration, Events Queue, Commitment Phase, Next Battle. Ordinary dynasty events do not interrupt live combat. The strategic layer is not a pause menu but a core layer of play where the world remains live. Armies move visibly across the world map on the strategic layer. The player sees the date advancing. In single-player, pacing controls including pause for orders are available. In multiplayer, the match runs on a shared live calendar. No individual player may pause the entire match or unilaterally alter global speed. Multiplayer pause, if present at all, is restricted to vote-based, host-authorized, or emergency-token systems only, agreed at lobby creation. When a hostile engagement becomes imminent against a player who is in the strategic layer, the game issues an Immediate Engagement Alert with a short live countdown, a pre-battle command panel showing available forces and choices, and a transition into live battle when the countdown expires, with the player either entering in person or the engagement proceeding under commander delegation. Players may zoom into any active battle in progress and inherit the current battlefield state rather than restarting from the beginning.
