# House Visual Identity Packs

Status: graphics-lane production reference

## Standard Pack Template

Each House pack records:

- visual status
- primary materials
- primary and secondary color direction
- armor style direction
- cloth style direction
- architectural identity
- military silhouette identity
- civic visual identity
- banner and heraldic direction
- command-unit appearance
- elite-unit distinction
- worker and civilian appearance
- favored structural motifs
- instant battlefield read
- homeland influence
- confusion guardrails

## House Trueborn

- Visual status: settled visual direction, gameplay expansion still open
- Primary materials: pale dressed stone, oak, muted brass, parchment, brushed steel
- Color direction: weathered silver primary, restrained gold accent, ash-blue neutrals
- Armor style: disciplined noble martial kit with clean lines and minimal flourish
- Cloth style: formal layered robes, council cloaks, oath sashes, archive-weight fabrics
- Architecture: administrative permanence, covered walkways, archive chambers, ordered courts
- Military silhouette: balanced infantry and command profiles; no exaggerated aggression
- Civic identity: clerks, mediators, and civic attendants look organized and established
- Banner direction: singular central crest with branching seal variants for offices and retinues
- Command appearance: capes or shoulder cloth with silver edging, visible sealwork, authority without theatricality
- Elite distinction: cleaner finish, finer trim, slightly taller shield and helm profiles, no fantasy ornament
- Worker and civilian appearance: stable prosperous tailoring, muted but well-kept garments
- Favored motifs: seals, thresholds, archive marks, light wells, disciplined stone framing
- Battlefield read: silver-haired command presence, pale institutional accents, calm geometry
- Homeland influence: river-valley administrative centers and trade-hub adjacency
- Never confuse with: Whitehall bureaucracy, which is leaner and more workmanlike; Highborne luxury, which is more aristocratic and ornate

## House Highborne

- Visual status: settled visual direction, strategic rebuild still open
- Primary materials: polished limestone, carved stone, dark wood, velvet, gilded bronze
- Color direction: dark gold primary, warm cream secondary, reserved deep plum or wine accents
- Armor style: refined aristocratic harness with elegant proportions and high craftsmanship
- Cloth style: tailored noble drape, layered sleeves, expensive weave, controlled richness
- Architecture: inherited wealth, tall windows, carved lintels, ancestral residences grown into keeps
- Military silhouette: elegant spears, crested helms, narrower cavalry profiles, composure over bulk
- Civic identity: attendants, tutors, and retainers appear hierarchical and well-provisioned
- Banner direction: heraldic repetition through house marks, rank marks, and marriage badges
- Command appearance: polished trim, cloak weight, visibly custom weapons and horse tack
- Elite distinction: more elaborate but still grounded cloth and plate composition; never flamboyant fantasy
- Worker and civilian appearance: upper-class retainers remain dressed above world average, common labor still cleaner than frontier houses
- Favored motifs: arches, window tracery, inherited seals, long gallery forms
- Battlefield read: aristocratic polish, dark-gold authority, vertical elegance
- Homeland influence: established estates and inherited civic cores
- Never confuse with: Goldgrave commercial visibility or Trueborn institutional restraint

## House Ironmark

- Visual status: fully locked primary visual lane
- Primary materials: charcoal iron, soot-dark stone, forge timber, worn leather, ember-stained cloth
- Color direction: charcoal iron primary, controlled ember red accent, dull steel secondary
- Armor style: utilitarian plate and brigandine, reinforced joints, visibly repairable construction
- Cloth style: short practical tabards, wrapped forearms, minimal trailing cloth
- Architecture: industrial function, forges, chimneys, stockyards, armories, hauling infrastructure
- Military silhouette: dense infantry mass, axes, square shields, smoke-and-fire utility
- Civic identity: miners, smiths, teamsters, and drill-yard workers read as labor-heavy and hard-used
- Banner direction: blunt field marks, forge brands, axe-based icon family, no villain coding
- Command appearance: heavier pauldrons, command cloak lining, embossed forge marks, authority through use not ornament
- Elite distinction: cleaner steel finish, deeper ember trim, heavier shield or weapon mass
- Worker and civilian appearance: aprons, work wraps, soot wear, forged tool visibility
- Favored motifs: rivets, braces, chimney stacks, hard corners, axle and chain logic
- Battlefield read: dark iron mass with ember accents and blunt tool-of-war silhouettes
- Homeland influence: iron ridges, foundries, quarry roads, militarized production zones
- Never confuse with: Stonehelm stone-defense warmth or Oldcrest age-worn gray layering

- Armor style: practical defensive civic-military gear, field-capable but not industrial

- Military silhouette: wardens, spears, polearms, shielded defenders, local levy with agrarian structure
- Civic identity: farmers, mill workers, herders, water tenders, and granary staff visibly shape the realm
- Banner direction: harvest marks, sheaf and heartland motifs, broad field banners
- Command appearance: warm cloth, polished but not courtly armor, land-steward authority
- Elite distinction: Verdant Warden lane uses heavier protective layering, pastoral-green support accents only where approved
- Worker and civilian appearance: healthiest and warmest rural civilian lane in the roster, with visible work tools and storage gear
- Favored motifs: terraces, wells, grain marks, orchard lines, storehouse beams, irrigation channels
- Battlefield read: warm agrarian civilization with disciplined local defense and visible abundance
- Homeland influence: river valleys, farm belts, managed water, productive lowland terrain
- Never confuse with: Goldgrave prosperity, which is commercial, or Wild covenant visuals, which are more untamed
