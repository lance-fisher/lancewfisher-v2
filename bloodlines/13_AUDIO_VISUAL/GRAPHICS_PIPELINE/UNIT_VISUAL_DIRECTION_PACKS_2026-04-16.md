# Unit Visual Direction Packs

Status: additive graphics-lane production reference

## Purpose

This document converts the House identity packs and current concept-sheet lane into unit-family direction suitable for future concept generation, placeholder creation, and Unity-side readability testing.

These packs do not declare new canon roster seams. They define how existing and planned unit families should look when they appear.

## Shared RTS Rules

- primary class read must resolve at gameplay height before House trim
- weapons, shields, banners, and horse mass carry class read
- House color belongs in controlled cloth, trim, shield paint bands, or banner marks
- helmets and shoulder shapes should reinforce House identity without fantasy excess
- elite or command units may carry slightly more detail, but never at the cost of silhouette

## 1. Core Infantry

### Universal direction

- Role: stable line-fighter read
- Primary silhouette anchors: shield plane, spear or sword line, torso mass, helm profile
- Material baseline: iron or steel, leather, cloth, wood
- Damage states needed: yes for later production candidate and final
- Upgrade states needed: yes where armor modernization matters
- Icon required: yes

### House modifiers

- Trueborn: balanced shield-spear forms, silver-blue cloth restraint, seal-driven trim, institutional calm
- Highborne: narrower, more elegant lines, aristocratic crests kept restrained, finer cloth layering
- Ironmark: heavier square shield and blunt weapon read, charcoal iron, ember accent discipline
- Goldgrave: professional contract-guard polish, cleaner kit, trade-town maintenance quality
- Stonehelm: broad shields, dense cloaks, low-center mass, garrison sturdiness
- Westland: lighter frontier reinforcement, travel gear, aggressive forward lean
- Hartvale: warm defended agrarian read, practical layered cloth, field-protector identity
- Whitehall: orderly official guard look, courier-escort discipline, office-mark trims
- Oldcrest: older shield patterns, inherited mixed-era harness, age and repair visible

## 2. Elite Infantry

### Universal direction

- Role: top-tier line or keep guard
- Primary silhouette anchors: taller shield or heavier weapon mass, denser torso armor, stronger command cloth
- Material baseline: cleaner metal finish, reinforced trim, better cloth quality
- Damage states needed: yes
- Upgrade states needed: yes
- Icon required: yes

### House modifiers

- Trueborn: seal guard or oath guard authority, cleaner geometry over ornament
- Highborne: refined noble guard silhouette, elegant polearm or blade treatment
- Ironmark: heavier forge elite, deep ember trim, brutal but disciplined mass
- Goldgrave: well-funded professional guard, contract authority rather than nobility
- Stonehelm: bulwark guard, tower-linked elite, defensive wall-holding identity
- Westland: veteran frontier retainer, scarred mobility, reinforced travel kit
- Hartvale: Verdant Warden or equivalent protected civic-guardian lane, warm and grounded
- Whitehall: council guard and escort elite, administrative authority, seal-heavy discipline
- Oldcrest: ancestral guard, memorial lineage treatment, older forms kept battle-worthy

## 3. Ranged Units

### Universal direction

- Role: bow, crossbow, or ranged support read
- Primary silhouette anchors: weapon arc, quiver or bolt case, lighter shield or none
- Material baseline: cloth and leather more visible than heavy plate
- Damage states needed: not usually separate from generic unit damage layer
- Upgrade states needed: yes when ranged weapon family changes
- Icon required: yes

### House modifiers

- Trueborn: escort bow discipline, ordered posture, restrained noble trim
- Highborne: elegant ranged line, crested but still practical
- Ironmark: heavier ranged supports, iron-rich trim, practical aggression
- Goldgrave: professional guard-ranged treatment, caravan-defense logic
- Stonehelm: tower-linked or wall-service ranged lane, dense weatherproof kit
- Westland: frontier skirmish posture, travel-ready clothing, lighter protection
- Hartvale: harvest-defense archers, warm cloth and practical farm-belt defense
- Whitehall: courier-road escort ranged lane, official restraint
- Oldcrest: older bow forms, patched but respected line, historical continuity visible

## 4. Cavalry And Scouts

### Universal direction

- Role: speed, flank, or authority read
- Primary silhouette anchors: horse mass, rider height, banner or lance profile, saddle kit
- Material baseline: leather, cloth, horse tack, moderate metal on elite riders
- Damage states needed: later production only
- Upgrade states needed: yes where cavalry family changes from scout to heavy
- Icon required: yes

### House modifiers

- Trueborn: orderly escort cavalry with balanced tack and no flamboyant capes
- Highborne: aristocratic cavalry with refined horse gear and elegant line
- Ironmark: heavier working-horse mass, minimal flourish, industrial tack logic
- Goldgrave: route-security riders, polished but practical commercial protection
- Stonehelm: compact defensive cavalry, sturdy horse armor accents, keep-response role
- Westland: outrider and frontier raid identity, wind-cut cloaks, rapid profile
- Hartvale: steward-rider and field-response lane, warm saddle cloth, agrarian authority
- Whitehall: courier cavalry and office escort lane, seal and dispatch logic
- Oldcrest: noble old-guard cavalry with inherited tack and age-layered presentation

## 5. Workers And Civic Units

### Universal direction

- Role: noncombat labor, building, carrying, tending, and governance support
- Primary silhouette anchors: tools, baskets, poles, ledgers, buckets, carts
- Material baseline: cloth, rope, leather, wood, utility metal
- Damage states needed: no separate visual state beyond general runtime feedback
- Upgrade states needed: not usually
- Icon required: yes

### House modifiers

- Trueborn: organized clerks, attendants, and civic workers with stable tailoring
- Highborne: well-kept estate labor and noble retainers, hierarchy visible
- Ironmark: soot wear, aprons, work wraps, hauling posture
- Goldgrave: merchant and route labor read, satchels, account and cargo props
- Stonehelm: masons, quartermasters, and keep workers with weather-ready gear
- Westland: pathfinders, builders, wagon hands, frontier-worn dress
- Hartvale: healthiest rural lane, harvest tools, baskets, water and granary supports
- Whitehall: scribes, envoys, and record staff with restrained official props
- Oldcrest: conservative dress, mended garments, old practical tools

## 6. Command Units

### Universal direction

- Role: battlefield authority, banner focus, and chain-of-command read
- Primary silhouette anchors: banner, cloak, weapon distinction, guard body mass, horse tack for mounted command
- Material baseline: strongest cloth hierarchy in the roster, but still restrained
- Damage states needed: later candidate and final
- Upgrade states needed: yes where command lane escalates with rank
- Icon required: yes

### House modifiers

- Trueborn: seal-bearing command, calm composure, authority without spectacle
- Highborne: aristocratic precision, long-line cloak forms, polished horse gear
- Ironmark: forge-mark authority, short heavy cloak, weapon mass and iron trim
- Goldgrave: contract-command and market-guard authority, polished practicality
- Stonehelm: greatcloak, bulwark shield boss, keep-linked command helm silhouette
- Westland: rider-coat leadership and forward pressure read
- Hartvale: land-steward command, warm cloth, defended-abundance authority
- Whitehall: office-mark sash systems, envoy or governor visual distinction
- Oldcrest: ancestral weapons, older cloak logic, lineage-heavy authority

## 7. Production Notes

- Every unit family needs a gameplay-height inset in future concept or generation sheets.
- Every House-specific unit pass must declare confusion-risk Houses before concepting begins.
- Elite and command lanes are the only places where extra trim should push above the normal roster detail ceiling.
- If a House treatment cannot be recognized without flooding the asset in color, the silhouette is not yet strong enough.
