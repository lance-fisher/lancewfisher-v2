# Building Family Direction Packs

Status: additive graphics-lane production reference

## Purpose

This document turns the visual bible, House identity packs, and current concept-sheet batches into practical direction for structure families that will later become concept prompts, placeholders, and Unity-prefab candidates.

## Shared RTS Building Rules

- a building must read its function before its decorative culture
- structure tops, entry points, roof form, and footprint shape carry primary purpose
- House identity should appear through trim, banners, roof treatment, stone color bias, and add-on structure logic
- damage and upgrade states must remain legible at gameplay height
- defensive structures must show threat and pathing consequence before ornament

## 1. Command Hall / Keep / Hall

- Primary read: seat of power and local control anchor
- Silhouette anchors: dominant central mass, visible entry or gate, banner point, secondary towers or wings
- Material family: stone, timber, tile or slate, iron brace accents
- Required states: construction phase, intact, damaged, heavy damage, occupied banner variant
- Icon required: yes
- Confusion risks: do not let the keep silhouette collapse into barracks or market read

## 2. Housing Tiers

- Primary read: population support and domestic density
- Silhouette anchors: clustered roof logic, chimneys or vents, fenced yard or storage traces
- Material family: timber, plaster, thatch or tile by House lane
- Required states: tier variants, damaged or raided variant if later needed
- Icon required: yes

## 3. Economy Support

### Farms

- Read: worked land plus support structure
- Anchors: field adjacency, small storage or windbreak logic, irrigation traces where appropriate

### Granary

- Read: food storage and local security
- Anchors: raised or reinforced storage mass, grain vents, storage doors

### Storehouse

- Read: general supply intake and output
- Anchors: wide doors, cargo platform, stacked-goods logic

### Well / Water Structure

- Read: life-support infrastructure
- Anchors: circular or framed draw point, bucket logic, damp ground, civic footpath wear

### Lumber / Quarry / Iron Structures

- Read: material extraction and haul support
- Anchors: supports, lifts, spoil, tool racks, ramp or cart logic

## 4. Military Production Buildings

### Barracks

- Read: infantry mustering and training
- Anchors: yard logic, weapon racks, long roof or disciplined bay rhythm

### Archery Structure

- Read: ranged training
- Anchors: target frames, narrow training lane, lighter yard treatment

### Stables

- Read: mounted unit support
- Anchors: stable doors, paddock or tack support, elongated horse-facing footprint

### Blacksmith / Forge

- Read: metalwork and military reinforcement
- Anchors: chimney, furnace or anvil mass, slag or tool traces

### Siege Workshop

- Read: heavy war-machine production
- Anchors: oversized bay doors, timber gantries, frame parts, wheel or beam mass

## 5. Fortification Family

### Walls

- Read: blocked path and defensive line
- Anchors: parapet rhythm, strong top edge, clear thickness

### Gates

- Read: controlled threshold
- Anchors: dark passage void, heavy flank masses, door geometry

### Towers

- Read: elevated watch and attack point
- Anchors: upward thrust, crown silhouette, arrow-slit or firing logic

### Keeps / Citadels

- Read: deeper defensive seat and dynastic survival point
- Anchors: dominant vertical or layered mass, multiple defensive layers, banner authority

Required state planning across fortification family:

- intact
- damaged
- breached where applicable
- construction phase
- occupied banner variants

## 6. Civic And Trade Buildings

### Market

- Read: exchange and route-facing commerce
- Anchors: awning logic, open frontage, stall rhythm, route attachment

### Neutral Trade Hub / City Support

- Read: larger civic-commercial density
- Anchors: broader footprint, signage or route logic, multi-entry frontage

## 7. Faith Structures

- Read: covenant presence and ritual authority
- Anchors: distinctive worship shape, shrine or pyre logic, processional or symbolic markers
- Rule: faith identity may be visually strong, but never so bright or magical that it breaks the grounded Bloodlines tone
- Required states: base structure, later tier variant where applicable, damage state if gameplay uses it

## 8. Ruins And Neutral Structures

- Read: former utility plus present strategic residue
- Anchors: broken wall logic, partial rooflines, recoverable footprint
- Rule: ruin assets must support pathing and recognition, not visual fog

## 9. House Overlay Rules

Use these as trim and material overlays across the building families above.

- Trueborn: pale dressed stone, calm silver-blue banners, institutional order
- Highborne: refined limestone, elegant stone detail, dark-gold restraint
- Ironmark: darker stone, forge timber, blunt ironwork, ember-mark discipline
- Goldgrave: polished timber and plaster, trade-facing trim, prosperous maintenance
- Stonehelm: warm brown stone, slate, heavy braces, fortress logic
- Westland: weathered timber, frontier red accents, extension-built pragmatism
- Hartvale: warm tile and granary logic, harvest cloth, water and field integration
- Whitehall: orderly plaster and pale timber, official seals, dispatch-tower clarity
- Oldcrest: layered old stone, repairs across eras, memorial continuity

## 10. Production Notes

- Every major building family should eventually have icon, damage-state, and construction-state planning before final integration.
- Future concept sheets should always include gameplay-height insets and scale references against units.
- If a building cannot be identified from roof and footprint shape alone, it is not yet ready for RTS use.
